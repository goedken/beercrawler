BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Thomas Hooker Brewing Company
Connecticut, United States
hookerbeer.com

Style: American Pale Ale (APA)

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 5.30%

Availability: Year-round

Notes / Commercial Description:
Thomas Hooker American Pale Ale is an extremely vivid, medium-bodied brew. Hooker Pale Ale stresses the crisp bitterness, lingering resin flavor, and aroma of American hops which are characteristic of the most distinctive West Coast Ales. The caramel sweetness of the malt balances the chock-full-of-hops flavor to yield a complex but quite refreshing brew.

35 IBU

I recently found this in the singles section at Country Garden Six Pack here in Lancaster, PA and I was curious to try it despite the "Born On" date being a smeary mess that reveals nothing. Given its low scores, it may not matter. As always, I will try to be as fair as possible.

From the bottle: "Troutbrook Brewing Company"; "Crisp Hop Flavor"; "Our True American Pale Ale"; "Connecticut's Beer".

My pour netted me just over a finger of tawny head with poor retention, falling quicker than I could type. Color was a slightly hazy deep-amber. Nose had a pleasant grassiness, which was reasonable for the style. Mouthfeel was on the thin side, not quite watery, but not much above it, either. The taste was of grassy hops, albeit muted. Finish was semi-dry with the malt negating the hops on the finish. It was not that it was bad, per se, but it was really lacking for the style. I have had MUCH better APAs and would steer others away from this punchless example.

It's drinkable. But really it's because it doesn't have any distinct characteristic that it is. A brew where if any of the flavors were stronger it may make it less drinkable.

It may be that I have a very undercarbonated or older bottle. There is no bottle on date. I tend to believe it couldn't be fresh but I can't say I would try this again.

4.03/5  rDev +19.9%

Poured from a 12-ounce bottle into a fluted pilsner glass.

A: Poured a clean, golden amber color with a one-finger head of tight, white foam. Carbonation is moderate and this brew leaves a pretty nice webby lace.

S: Smells of faintly sweet and lightly toasted bread as well as a slightly sweet, floral hop zest.

T: Taste is slightly astringent, becoming bittersweet with ample hops near the beginning. A mellowing with crystal malts takes place, followed by the additional malty, buttered biscuit flavor near the end. Aftertaste is so clean and minimal, you thought you'd just drank water ... is this a bad thing?

M: Mouthfeel is light-medium bodied, crisp, clean and refreshing.

D: A nice, if not slightly standard, APA. Very sessionable and well-crafted indeed. If you're looking for a balanced yet slightly unspectacularly flavored APA, Hooker is the one for you.

These Hooker brews are really starting to grow on me. Local brewers making quality craft brews and sticking true to style while offering it all at a reasonable price. That's a recipe for success.
