abita.com

Style: American Brown Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 8.50%

Availability: Limited (brewed once)

Notes / Commercial Description:
Bourbon Street Maple Pecan is a nut brown ale that is aged in Bourbon barrels. Our Maple Pecan is brewed with a combination of pale, munich, biscuit, and caramel malts. Roasted pecans are also added to the brew for a nutty flavor and aroma. We add maple syrup in the brewhouse as well to give the beer a sweet flavor and full body. After fermentation and aging the beer is transferred into the bourbon barrels. It is then aged for another 8 weeks to absorb all of the flavors from the barrels. The result is brown ale that blends the light flavors of nuts, maple syrup, and baking bread from the beer with the warming flavors of wood and vanilla from the bourbon barrels.

Added by jhartley on 01-12-2015

This beer is retired; no longer brewed.

Pours a very clear brown with reddish highlights. Head is good-sized, light cream colored, with good retention and very nice lacing.

Aroma keeps quite a bit in reserve...except for bourbon. That part shines through, while maple, wood, and caramel and brown sugar are definitely in the background. Definitely smells on the sweet side, but not overwhelmingly so.

A touch sweet up front, with a bit of pecan, caramel, brown sugar, and even touches of earthy, piney, and citrusy hops. Incredible, huge bourbon finish, carrying tons of coconut; it's like sipping on coconut milk (really!). That's ... not really a good thing. It's overwhelming. Zero presence from the maple, and no real depth or complexity.

Very light mouthfeel from the get-go; too light for my tastes. It really allows the bourbon to dominate far too much.

I've had other "lighter" brews (read: non-stouts) that are BBA; as an example, Stone's Arrogant Bastard. I think it's harder to really balance out these lighter brews when you age them in barrels, and this brew exemplifies that challenge. It's unbalanced, watery, and ultimately quite unsatisfying. Extremely disappointing.

donspublic, Sep 20, 2015
3.82/5  rDev +3.8%
look: 4 | smell: 5 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 2.75 | overall: 3.5

A- Ok, apparently I was expecting a stout when I opened this beer and was very surprised by the light color of the beer. Once I was over the initial shock, it actually pours a pretty good looking, medium reddish-brown in color. A big and frothy head forms and retains very well, lacing all the way down the glass. The body of the beer is very hazy.

S- Wow! The name of this beer doesn't let you down at all here! Huge, sweet, and very maple forward scents hit the nose first followed closely by the pecans in a nutty goodness. Really smells like a bunch of bourbon and syrup soaked pecans and smells freaking amazing.

T- When the beer first hits the tongue, the sweetness from the scent presents itself first. The sweetness on the tongue is muted compared to the scent and the same goes for the nuts that follow up the sweetness. In the middle of the drink, the nuts ramp up a bit to overtake the flavor and really make the base brown ale flavors known. Light notes of the barrels come in next, with a light oak flavor, but they are gone just as quickly as they arrive. Light bitterness of the barrels and nuts finish the drink and just a hint of the maple sweetness lingers on the palate.

M- For 8.5% ABV and some heavier flavors, this beer is very thin and rather light across the palate. The alcohol isn't prominent, but it is definitely noticeable in both the flavor and as a warming sensation as you swallow the beer.

Overall, there are two main issues I have with this beer. First, it needs more time in the barrels. The barrel flavors are barely present and mostly muted. Second, this needs to be a stout. The brown ale can't carry the flavors that are attempted or the alcohol level. All of that said, it still is among the best smelling beers I have encountered to date.

Appearance: It pours a tawny color into a deep reddish brown body that is hazy enough to only allow some shadows through. A large slightly off-white head lasts for a short while before fading to a thin layer across the surface of the beer. Some lacing on the glass.

Mouthfeel: This beer is very smooth and easy drinking. It's perfect for this beer.

What you are left with here is the perfect beer to enjoy in the autumn.
