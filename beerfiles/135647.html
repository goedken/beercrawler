Labatt Prohibition Series Apple Harvest Ale brewed by Labatt Brewing Company Ltd., with an ABV of 6.00% This brew pours out a nice deep golden amber color with a small white head. The smell of Labatt Prohibition Series Apple Harvest Ale, is rich with grains,malts,and hops. You also pick up a sweetness of apples in the smell. Not saying that is a bad thing. The taste of Apple Harvest Ale is bold with flavors. You pick up the bitter sweetness from the apples, you also pick up on grains and malts in this brew. The mouth feel of Labatt Prohibition Series Apple Harvest Ale, is rich, bold and flavorful. I like how smooth this brew goes down. You really can't pick up on the 6.0% ABV in this brew. There is nothing overpowering about this brew, Labatt Prohibition Series Apple Harvest Ale, has a nice balance to it. This is one of the better "Apple" brews on the market. Overall I'm giving Labatt Prohibition Series Apple Harvest Ale a 4.25 out 5. I would buy this brew again. Labatt Brewing Company Ltd., did a nice job with this one.

Labatt Prohibition Series Apple Harvest Ale
12 oz. brown glass bottle, coded "JOGR42 16:14"
$1.79 @ Total Wine & More, Cherry Hill, NJ

First I should note, as I've done for some of the other beers from Labatt USA, that this beer has nothing to do with Labatt in Canada except for the use of the name. It's neither brewed in Canada nor imported into Canada; and it's not a beer that has ever been brewed by Labatt's in Canada. It's not a revival of an old beer, it's just marketing, from the stubby bottle to the retro-looking label. The bottle I'm looking at was brewed and bottled in Rochester, NY by North American Breweries / High Falls (Genesee).

As far as apple beers go they've really nailed a perfect balance between apple flavor and actually "beer" flavor. You'll find the apple at first, with the first few sips being quite strong, but after that as your nose adapts the beer side will come out. It's light-bodied and crisp, but there's clearly a malt component to it, and a little bit of bitterness.

Poured into a lager-glass from a bottle.

Labatt's Prohibition Series Apple Harvest Ale is, despite the name, not a ale dating back to the prohibition era, nor would I really call it an ale. Rather it is a beer which is searching for the middle-ground between a lager and an extraordinarily sweet cider. The beer Labatt reminded me of most was probably some strong version of a German Radler. And if that's the expectations one should bring, Labatt is far from horrible. Similarly, it's also a beer that is quite unique for the American market, and a beer which I enjoyed to a degree - especially as a substitute to the extremely sweet Angry Orchard-esque ciders out on the market. Is it a great beer? Hardly, but occasionally it finds itself into my fridge.

57md, Sep 14, 2014
2.25/5  rDev -21.6%

When I see Labatt, I think drinkable and decent. This beer is neither. I enjoy a lot of Labatt's offerings when going to a party or barbecue from time to time so when I stumbled upon this case I did what I always do. Check BA for the score. It didn't have one, so I knew I was flying into uncharted territory on this one. I thought "It can't be that bad, it's Labatt." Bad call.

Look: Didn't poor it into a glass, I was drinking this at a party.

Smell: Smells like apple juice, but a bit more artificial, if that makes sense. It's the only way I can describe it.

Taste: Imagine a lighter Miller Fortune, but worse tasting. Like a bad Amber Lager, then a splash of apple juice, a bit sweet at the end that almost made me say apple cider, but that seems too fancy for this beer. A bit of the 6% alcohol content pokes through at the end, nothing too bad but the aftertaste is God awful. Completely off-putting. I willingly lent out a few at the party to a few of my friends and most of them struggled to even finish the bottle.

Feel: This is where the beer shines, I guess. Light-Medium mouthfeel with some decent carbonation, but nothing offensive. After you struggle through the first one of these, they become easier to drink and soon enough they'll be going down like water. So I guess it wins a drinkability point.

Overall: Not good. Really disappointing. And what's worse is that I go to school in PA, so I had to commit to buying a massive 24-pack of this stuff just to try it. I put my faith in Labatt, and was let down big time. I'd rather have a Shock Top Honeycrisp Apple Wheat.
