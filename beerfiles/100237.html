Davepoolesque, Nov 10, 2014
4.4/5  rDev +12.5%
look: 4 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

Poured from a 750 ml brown capped bottle into a clear glass Three Notch'd tulip. Batch 4 on side of label. Purchased from Wine Warehouse (Charlottesville, VA).

A: Slightly hazy amber with healthy 1-2 finger light beige head with good retention and several levels of lacing.

S: Wonderful rustic hoppy aroma with robust wet hops, grassy but pleasant with herbal, floral, and citrus overtones of orange and lime but with a beautiful rough wild hops type of edge to it. Malt is not totally left out with some light and medium caramel with a hefty dose of breadiness poking through. There are no significant yeast esters or alcohol notes present.

T: The flavor measures up to the nose with more orange and light pine hops character coming through with the same wild grassy but not quite weedy herbal, earthy hop notes in there too. The combination of the raw, edgy hopping with the light amber/malty APA style of malt profile works excellently. I think that more breweries should push wet hops toward a light amber style as the moderate caramel malt really plays well off of the orangey hops. I don't note any yeast. Alcohol is warming but not much in the flavor. Bitterness is firm and medium. A wonderful, unique beer with a hand-crafted signature to it. Easily Lickinghole Creek's best beer I have had to date.

M: Exceedingly smooth with delicate carbonation, on the low-medium end of the spectrum, with medium body really working together to leave a combo of both creaminess and some prickliness in the feel.

O: What a stellar beer! I was really pleasantly surprised by this beer and the low-ish scores, although I think that is batch related, as this latest batch (Batch 4) seems to be doing better with reviewers. Just a delicious beer with rewarding both delicate and raw edges. Very complex, and a very good example of using an amber ale as the palate for wet hops.
