if more people got to taste these types of barrel aged strong ales from lakefront, they would have a much better reputation among the beer nerds, pedestrian day to day beers aside, but i reckon they hold these pretty close to the chest, and i feel fortunate enough to have been able to have a few special beers on draft. this is a rad beer, brewed with the malt base of a dopplebock, european sweet malts in high proportion, and a lot more squash than spices which is awesome, and a ton of this brandy barrel character, which is decidedly not a whiskey barrel. really impressive depth of flavor here, and hold on, can we talk about the alcohol? this is a doozy, but not something that is readily perceptible to the drinker, its smooth and warming and autumnal and strong, but there is none of the fusel hotness that usually is there in beers this strong. nicely done there. tons of oak and brandy on the nose and in the flavor, sweet and strong and smooth, with the squash right there behind it, held up nicely but not overtaken by the grain. spices appear to be cinnamon and allspice, but its mild enough for the beer to be tasted and appreciated independently of them, which is not often the case in these pumpkin beers. great carbonation too, not common in beer this strong. i like everything about this one, and am sure its the best beer i have ever had from lakefront. i didnt even know they did this kind of thing. wonderful stuff here, a needed reworking of a played out seasonal style. bravo!

Slack, Oct 15, 2016
4.33/5  rDev +3.8%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

How intriguing that Lakefront is doing a barrel-aged, fruit-infused beer series! I love these guys ever since my first visit in 2006. Their re-use of old dairy tanks is such a Midwestern idea & their rescue of the original Bernie Brewer on the demise of County Stadium was equally inspired. I really hope to be able to visit MKE & its environs in 2017.

From the bottle: "Ale brewed with Pumpkin & Spices with Vanilla added & aged in brandy barrels"; "Bottled On: 08 Sep 2016 - B3".

This bottle is a part of the #PumpkinChallenge2016, beginning with Pop!ping the cap. I had noticed some lees in the punt so I went with a gentle pour in order to keep from rousing them. This was a strange beer in that I did not initially get any head, then I employed an in-glass swirl & still got nothing. Then, while I was typing, the inherent carbonation & its bubbles formed a skim of light-tan head that kept regenerating itself, like the Hydra! Wild, man. Color was Deep Amber/Light Copper to Copper (SRM = > 13, < 17) with NE-quality clarity. Nose smelled nicely of cinnamon, ginger, allspice & vanilla, all playing together nicely instead of being a discordant group. Mouthfeel was medium, but tingly on the tongue from all of the effervescence. The taste really caught me by surprise, though. The intensity of the spices combined with the woodiness of the barrel aging plus the alcohol heat almost caused me to literally do a spit take! I could not tell you the last time that such a thing happened to me. This is another one of which I may try to score another bottle for cellaring. Here's why - the finish was really hot, adding to the already spicy cinnamon flavor. It even caused my eyes to water. Wow! I think that it will really smooth out nicely given some time to do so. In the interim, it is a big, no-foolin'-around beer. Caveat emptor!

ZAP, Apr 03, 2016
4.67/5  rDev +12%
look: 4 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 4.75

12 oz bottle poured into a Lakefront tulip.
6.5% ABV, Bottled Aug 20 2016 (another beer from the future!)

A - Deep amber hued, this rich-looking beer pours quietly and has minimal sediment lightly sprinkled through the beer; it's ivory cap of foam doing little to cling to life.

S - While the beautiful bouquet of brandy escapes rather effortlessly from this beer, it's the spice aroma that's rather intoxicating! Vanilla underscores the big beer while cinnamon, roasted pumpkin, and namely nutmeg, dissolve into a bountiful malt base with undertones of brown sugar and molasses. Get in mah belly already!

T - Hearty bread-like malt provides a wonderful if quickly forgotten base for a plethora of pumpkin spiciness; hints of raw cinnamon stick, a hefty dash of nutmeg, and a pinch of vanilla extract though none overwhelming. It's their impeccable balanced brandy-soaked-oak finish that perfectly compliments the rest of the beer on the palate, however.

M - Despite it's robust aroma and flavor, Lakefront's Imperial Pumpkin maintains an exquisite level of balance through the entire performance. Opening sweet and malty, the beer transitions to an ensemble of spices that close with an encore of thinning, warming brandy that leaves the beer sans-syrupy.

O - Lakefront's 25th anniversary brandy barrel-aged imperial pumpkin went over so well, it made a triumphant return as a seasonal! Pumpkin flavored everything during the fall season is a-ok by me and Wisconsin has brandy so ingrained in it's culture, why not marry the two? A match made in heaven, this Imperial Pumpkin is a seasonal to die for - put down that pumpkin pie and pick up a bottle of a liquefied, alcoholic version.

A - A clear, orange golden hue with a slightly hazy transparency and little head or lacing on the tap pour.

T - Like the nose, the taste is quiet. The close is quick and with just enough bitterness to clear the mild sweetness away.

The remainder of the elements, from the spice and pumpkin to the barrel, are all in services to, rather than outshining, this beer's fundamental malt core. That at 9.5% this beer drinks like a 6% means this is one to drink carefully, not because it taxes, but because it can put you on your ass if you don't respect it.

Smells marvelous, like a caramelized kabocha glazed with maple and lightly dusted in cinnamon--one of the few pumpkin ales where I get good doses of both the spices and the gourds. The taste, however, does not follow through. Flavors are more straightforward; I have difficulty finding the doppelbock base, as the dominant taste is a boozy nutmeg. The use of brandy barrels is an interesting and welcome addition, imparting just a hint of tartness and juicy baked apple, but I wish that wasn't subsumed by boozy heat and a tingly mouthfeel; this could do with a bit less carbonation, as that would help to smooth out the experience and allow some of the aromatic complexity carry over into the flavor profile.

Absolutely a seasonal worth trying, and one of the better pumpkin ales I've tried.
