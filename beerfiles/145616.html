BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Prairie Artisan Ales
Oklahoma, United States
prairieales.com

Style: American Double / Imperial IPA

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 8.00%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
Phantasmagoria is an IPA that is low in malt flavors, but high in hops. We brew this beer as a nood to the big hoppy beers of the west coast. We use loads of citrusy and piney hops at the end of the boil and in the fermenter to make this beer a hop experience.

70 IBUs

Poured into a snifter. Moderately hazy on the pour with a golden-amber color. Not much head despite a fairly vigorous pour. The foam left quickly leaving a few tracks, but not much. Great aroma. No label information. Phantasmagoria is brewed in the style of an American Double IPA, available in 12 oz. bottles and on limited draft.

Poured from a 12 oz. bottle to a tulip glass.

(Appearance) Pours a crisp, foamy head of a pearl white color over a medium hazy golden yellow body with abundant carbonation. Retention is very good and lacing is light and patchy. 4.5

(Smell) Hop presence of tropical fruit, grapefruit juice, citrus peel, pepper spice and piney resins with a moderate bitterness over a comparatively subtle pale malt body with a slight sweetness. Very inviting and complex arrangement of hop flavors in this beer. Potency is moderate. 4

(Taste) The flavor largely follows the aroma, with juicy grapefruit, citrus pulp, spicy pine and tropical fruit subtleties building the brunt of the hop flavor, and a clean, mildly sweet pale malt profile keeping things simple otherwise, allowing for the subtleties of the abundant hop flavors to shine without much of a challenge. Finishes clean and moderately dry. 4

(Mouthfeel) Texture is slick, mildly residual, medium dry. Carbonation is full, generating a medium frothiness and a fully crisp finish. Body is short of medium for the style, medium+ overall. Balance is slightly hoppy bitter over sweet. Alcohol presence is low and there are no off characters. 3.75

(Overall) An enjoyable Double IPA for its accessibility through bright, flavorful hop profiles, although collectively their impact has limitations in depth compared to other west-coast style IPAs which have a more limited, but sharper focus on key hop profiles- this one is substantial, but very scattershot with a little bit of nearly every hop flavor coming to light. The light malt profile has just enough impact to keep things fairly balanced, and the bitterness is stronger than the malt base overall, but also mild. There's no doubting this is a respectable homage to the west coast IPA. Altogether, this is a very enjoyable DIPA with solid hop qualities all around, generating an overall positive impression without being memorable otherwise. It's truly a tease that I can only manage to get single bottles of beers like this as this is truly a highly drinkable beer and warrants a second helping. 4

Phantasmagoria 2016

Sweet honey, passion fruit, tropical, minor (peach) stone fruit in the entry. Fresh cut green grass, cedar, lemon grass-citrus and herbal notes. A slender touch of grapefruit in the finish. Big, clean bitterness in the finish, not overbearing in any way. Bitterness kicks up mid palate, then falls off fast. I get this fresh apple core, pear-like subtle flavor that lingers on the palate after the finish. Pineapple and slight dankiness becomes more apparent as the beer warms. Malts are clean and crackery. There's enough non-descript malt sweetness to keep the hops in balance. This brew is thin bodied, which makes this a very approachable DIPA and keeps the focus on the hops. The carbonation is firm and appropriate. I love, love, love this brew! The approachability and drinkability is great, especially when so many DIPA's are thicker and more malt-laden. I paid 4.99$ for a 12 oz bottle. It's a bit pricey. Yes, I would buy again. This is a complex double for sure. If you claim to be a hop head you have to try this. Prairie knows what they are doing.

12oz bottle from Minks (Marietta, GA). 3.98. No discernible bottling date. Served in a Wicked Weed snifter.

Pours a muffled, pale orange with a two and a half finger cap of white fluff. The head becomes a dense half finger crown that deposits large swaths of lace.

Grapefruit juice, oranges, dank pine and a little bread on the nose. Pleasant enough that I occasionally bring the snifter back to my nose to enjoy between sips.

Bittersweet tropical fruity hop flavors lead. Piney mango and vague pineapple succumb to grapefruit peel, orange pulp, some floral dankness, a hint of caramel and an occasional kiss of alcohol. Tasty and easy to drink.

Oily, slick medium full feel with moderate carbonation. The lingering, hop resinous finish has notes of grapefruit, mango and orange.

Phantasmagoria is a damn solid imperial IPA from a brewery that I typically associate with farmhouse type ales and imperial stouts. Let it be known that Prairie knows how to handle hops. This one is worth a try.

Clear golden pour. World class head volume, lacing and retention.

Outstanding complex nose of tropical hops (passion fruit, pineapple, orange and lemon) coupled with a subtle spicy spruce over a solid malt base.

Medium body with a nice oily texture. Normal carbonation level with a bit of effervescence in the middle and a fairly prickly finish. The carbonation / body balance is a bit weak.

All that said, this does have a delicious flavor profile of hops that were noted in the nose over a rich malt base.
