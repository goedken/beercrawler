Pours a pretty amber orange color and feartures a big, wet, creamy head that lasts a reasonable 6-8 minutes before capping out. Scattered lace dots my Sam Smith pint glass. Nose is grainy with herbal and citrus hops.

Lots of sediment, which is nice, but the taste is kind of all over the place. Yeasty and powered more by bread malts than aggressive west coast hopping. It definitely has more of an English feel to it than American, with gentle herbal and medicinal hops along for a malt ride. Like, I said, the texture and sediment are fine, and I can't detect ant burn at all from 7.4. If this were labeled an EIPA, it's an easy half point upgrade.

On tap at the Golden West cafe in the Hampden neighborhood of Baltimore.

Pours dark amber and medium hazy with a decent head. Smells of citrus hops and very pale malt. Tastes the same as it smells: straightforward, hoppy, and American. Not much by the way of malt complexities, but they're still present enough to prevent that aggressive hop profile from running roughshod over your tongue.

Impressively smooth for a higher-ABV IPA, with decent balance. It's flavor profile doesn't quite stand up to the paragons of the style (like Two Hearted), but it's still one of the best Maryland-brewed IPAs I've had.

Poured into a Double Barley can-shaped glass. Bottom of can says 07/19/16.

A- Pours a semi-hazy dark amber color with a 1/2 inch off-white head that retains well before becoming a thick ring around the edge of the glass and a full sheet of sheer surface foam. Semi-resilient lacing leaves large clots behind.

S- Good hop aroma of grass, citrus and dank with a decent amount of malt sweetness backing it. Slight booze fume.

T- Somewhat juicy hop flavor that's also got a bit of spiciness going on. Mild bitterness for the style and not as much malt presence as you would guess given the color of the beer. Pretty easy to drink for the ABV.

M- Slightly oily from the hops with medium carbonation and a body leaning toward the lighter side.

O- There's something missing from this beer. First of all, there's not enough bitterness for my taste. The hop character is OK but the malt is off-kilter both in terms of sweetness and ratio with regards to the hops. I guess all I'm saying is more "kick" is needed all-around. Decent, but in need of tweaking.

L - Pale orange body with a one finger thick head that rapidly shrinks down to a thinner but long lasting head. Nice lacing.

S - Sharp citrus and sweet biscuity malt. More bitter grapefruit smell than orange or tropical fruits like mango. Very Cascade-y but with some more kick.

F - Not an overly dry IPA. Smooth and maybe even a little slick. Medium carbonation.

O - Picked this up at the brewery hoping they would have Hyde or Pulp in their 32oz. oil cans but this was a wonderful consolation prize. Well rounded beer that tops other local flagships like Snake Dog and Lot 3.

markgugs, Oct 09, 2015
3.71/5  rDev -3.4%
look: 3.25 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 3.75

12 oz can (!) into a teku.

Appearance: Pours a murky copper color with bits of sediment floating around. The head is two fingers high and off-white in shade, maybe erring towards a pale yellow. Head retention is quite decent, aided by the thick, soft texture. A short but solid ring of lacing sticks to the glass.

Smell: Interestingly, it isn't very citrusy or tropical. Maybe I get a slight amount of melon, and that's it. No, this one smells piny and dank. In addition to being piny and dank, it also smells herbaceous and even catty. Aside from my disappointment in the lack of fruity notes, it shows off an impressive spicier/herbal bend to the hops, without overdone harshness or malt.

Taste: Wow, the taste is quite pungent. At first, the dank pine (bordering on garlic/onion) is a little strong, but it smooths out nicely after a few sips. There are some mild hints of lemon and grapefruit as this beer initially hits the palate, but like the nose suggested, this is mostly pine and herbs. But not pine, herbs, and bitter! This is not a bitter beer at all, despite all the pine. Instead, it's rather savory. Finally, the malts appear close to the back end with notes of sugar cookie and caramel.

Mouthfeel: This is where the beer takes off and succeeds. Its unfiltered nature makes it feel full and creamy...and yet it finishes with a dry crispness to it. No hints of alcohol appear, and carbonation levels are spot-on.

Overall: An unusual IPA - the hop profile takes a bit of getting used to, but the actual construction of the beer itself is top-notch. With a more citrusy flavor, this would be a true world-beater. Still, it's another rock-solid IPA from Maryland, one that will squeeze its way into my regular rotation.

3.71/5: I always love having more canned IPAs around, especially if they're really good (like this)
