bennyman12, Sep 06, 2016
4.49/5  rDev +2.5%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

Decadent, rich and built for the sweet tooth that we like to deny in all of us, the Odd Side masterminds embark upon a stout of legendary status then layered with coffee, maple and bacon before laying down in a long slumber in bourbon barrels. What emerges is simply lushness.

Black as pitch and capped with a lacy spiderweb of tar-stained froth, the stout mesmerizes the senses with a host of caramel, chocolate, vanilla, coconut, maple and espresso. Drawing the first sip, its immense burnt sweetness is something of roasted marshmallow, chocolate covered espresso bean and Somoas cookies.

Yea, wrap the mind around that! Yet as the middle palate is awash in the boozy-sweet, lavish sultry roast, and spicy masculine taste, the mind deconstructs the flavors and recognizes caramels and maple sugars early, then chocolate and coffee next, candied walnuts as the earthiness builds in, and then closes with the barrel and booze complexities of the bourbon influences that bring about those irresistible coconut, vanillan and oaken properties.

Absurdly full, lavish and deep, the stout slathers the tongue with robust sweetness. Even as the bitter coffee and spicy alcohol try to thin the malt viscosity, the grains will have none of that- finishing the session with a long, dense, luxurious sweet viscosity that extends well into the next sip. Don't worry about that dessert pairing- this beer replaces that dessert course without any apology.

bgold86, Jun 14, 2016
4.38/5  rDev 0%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.5

Reviewing from notes that I took on 2/6/16, although I had the final bottle from my stash on 4/10. If I remember correctly, they tasted pretty similar, but I'll use the notes for this review. Poured out of a 12 oz brown bottle from the 2015 release on Black Friday. Pretty sure it was bottled a couple weeks before that. Enjoyed out of an oversized brandy snifter.

Appearance (4.25) - Pours a very dark walnut brown bordering on black. The only reason I know it's brown is because of how it looked as I poured it into the glass. Head builds to a finger and a half of soft, pillowy khaki colored head with a slight pinkish twinge to the hue. Retention is moderate, with a few strings of lace on the sides of the glass after it begins to dissipate, settling as a fairly consistent film--with some breaks--across the surface of the liquid.

Smell (4.25) - The first thing I notice is that maple sweetness accompanying the more traditional notes of rich dark chocolate, roasted malt and caramel, presumedly from the barrel aging. In fact, the barrel is a little more present here than it was in BBIMM, for example. Maybe it just works better with the maple than it did with cinnamon and peppers. Still, it is a subtle touch--if you're looking for a bourbon bomb, this is not likely to be your beer of choice. I don't get much smoke or anything that I would expect from the inclusion of bacon. Just a little bit would be nice.

Taste (4.5) - Here there is a faint vein of that salty-smokiness that you get from fresh-cooked bacon, but it's exceedingly fleeting. If I wasn't actively looking for it, I don't know that I would have noticed. But the other elements are certainly still here, chocolate hangs in the background along with some nice roasted bitterness, but the maple really plows through and makes this a sweet beer--not Black Tuesday sweet, but sweet nonetheless. I'm not sure I would want more than 12 oz at a time, so the format is a blessing. Aftertaste hints at some light bourbon and oak, but it, like the smoke, is very fleeting.

Mouthfeel (4.0) - Medium body and moderate carbonation, with a lingering kiss of sweetness on the finish. Nothing spectacular, but enough body to carry the flavors. Plain and simple.

Overall (4.5), this is a beer where the whole is better than the sum of it's parts. Until I had Hazel's Nuts, I would have told you that this was Odd Sides' best stout. It's still quite good, and you can see where the reputation comes from, but here's hoping that they get whatever issues some of their bottles had figured out so that I can continue to buy this in the future with confidence.
