jzeilinger, Jan 28, 2016
3.77/5  rDev +3%
look: 4 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

Going from curious and intriguing to simple and fruity, this installment of the Voodoo Doughnut series lands Rogue Ales at the precipice of tropical flavors in a pure and dedicated taste.

Mango Astronaut Ale pours a few shades peachier than your standard golden ale and it carries a tightly woven head with surprisingly persistent longevity. Spilling over with bright and vivid mango perfume, its scent is heavily tilted toward the fruit but not without glimmers of apple, orange, nectarine, apricot and white all providing complementary roles. And once the taste hits the tongue, the flavors explode like fruit jam.

Little changes over the middle palate as the strong fruit and light syrup flavors bathe the tastebuds in its tropical and orchard fruit medley. Suggestions of fruit roll-ups, cream sickle and sherbet come to mind until the light pectin bite of fruit seed and skin aid the bitterness in hops to offset just enough of the sweetness to fuel palatability, but careful not to rival the candied fruit.

With the malty and fruity-sweet grip lessening slightly in finish, the beer's exit is juicy, tropical and highly remiss of hawaiian punch. Just off of refreshing, or dry, or crisp, the beer's candied fruit flavors are intent to give drinkers a dessert-like but fruit-forward succuence- perfect to close a thai entree, ...or to start the day.

Svingjo, Nov 04, 2015
3.86/5  rDev +5.5%
look: 3.75 | smell: 4 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 4

750ml, usual Pepto-Bismol pink painted bottle - this one is apparently inspired by Voodoo Doughnut's Mango Tango fancied-up and fried confection offering - I guess the 'astronaut' part was added by the space cadets at Rogue, eh?

This beer pours a clear, medium copper amber colour, with three fingers of puffy, finely foamy, and somewhat creamy ecru head, which leaves a bit of craggy tree branch lace around the glass as it quickly abates.

It smells of pungent pureed mangoes, a bit of apricot and peach, grainy, slightly doughy pale and caramel malt, real, sugar-free vanilla, and an indistinct earthy bitterness. The taste is bready, grainy malt, a still heady underripe mango, peach, and apricot fruitiness, a twinge of edgy yeast, some free-range dark sugars, cotton candy (it might just be the bottle in front of me), and a growing earthy, leafy, and faintly herbal - I'm gonna say hop-borne - astringency.

The carbonation is fairly straightforward in its mostly supportive frothiness, the body a decent middleweight, and plain in its smoothness - nothing else helping or hindering the cause. It finishes semi-sweet, the mango character doing well to hold onto its dominant stance, alongside a not particularly vanilla-flecked maltiness.

As far as these Voodoo Doughnut things go, this is probably the simplest, and therefore the best rendition - it's pretty much just a mango-ed up amber ale, with the suggestion of a vanilla adjunct thing that is just that, the beer not really doing well to reflect that part of the doughnut recipe. Whatever, this is tasty, and agreeable to drink, but not all that easy to swallow, given the north of $15 shelf tag.
