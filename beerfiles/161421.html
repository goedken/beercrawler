Poured from a 12 oz. can into a Snorkel glass. Canned on Sept. 22, 2016.

Appearance: Clear, bright golden body with a finger's worth of head. Small islands of lacing left behind in the glass.

Smell: Like a hop cone dipped in honey and sprayed with citrus zest! Pine, grapefruit, and more pine.

Taste: Wow! This is delicious with a touch of honey sweetness up front and then a mouthful of piney hop cones. Nice amount of bitterness to this one; just enough malt to level it out.

Feel: Medium mouth feel, with an easy drink-ability that is just to easy. Not too high of an abv helps.

Overall: This is fantastic! A DIPA that drinks so easy and is so tasty I like it more than their Top Cutter. The aroma just comes through in the taste, ya gotta love that. The taste just lingers and is pleasant.

Thanks to beertunes for this one.

Poured into a Seattle Beer Week pint glass. Pours a medium golden amber with a half finger white head with good retention and lacing. Aroma of caramel malt, citrus, dank hops, light tropical fruit and grapefruit. Flavor is caramel malt with light biscuit, piney, dank and citrus hops, very smooth and so well integrated that the resinous piney alpha oil hops come as almost a surprise in the finish, with is lingering and herbally bitter. Medium bodied with a pleasant light creaminess. This imperial IPA is so integrated and mellow at the start that I was almost concerned with my first sip, but the building nicely fresh herbal hop flavors and strong, but appropriate alpha oil bitterness slowly builds until it's a minute after the sip when I got the full effect of the hops. There are good herbal flavors to support the hop onslaught. This does have a substantial caramel malt body to it, but that is my favorite style of IPA, so I like this a lot. Despite the malt, the hops stand for themselves here. Just what I expected and wanted from this hop centered brewery. Nice, glad it's canned now.

As one can see, this is a mighty fine looker with a creamy creme head which leaves behind plenty of lacing on top of the mostly clear, orange beer.

As mentioned, great aroma. Sharp, pointy grapefruit that gets smoother around the edges thanks to sweet maltitudity.

Straight up old school mouth punishing DIPA. Not a sweet "could be a barley wine with age" specimen at all. Just a put up or shut up beer. Very nice.

On tap at the brewery the other day.

The beer pours an amber orange color with pretty decent head retention and lacing. On the nose, I really didn't pick up all that much. There was some noticeable tropical fruit and light citrus, but the beer smelled a bit boozy as well, which surprised me a bit. It was OK, but I wasn't hugely impressed by what I smell. On the palate, my initial impression was that the beer has some good hoppy bitterness, as you would expect. However, the sweetness from the malt really comes on strong in the mid palate, and the alcohol on the finish was hot and off putting. There were still some interesting flavors in this beer, but I was surprised by the level of sweetness and heat from alcohol I picked up. Mouthfeel was medium bodied I guess, but it was hard not to be distracted by the heat on the finish. There was some decent bitterness as well, but again, the harsh heat from the alcohol on the finish was off putting.

Mehhh... while not awful, given the brewery, I found this beer to be a disappointment. There are many excellent DIPA's in the NW, and IMHO, this is definitely not one of them.

16oz glass consumed at Barley's Brews Pub in Kennewick, WA, for approx $6. Finally got a shot at this beer.

Gorgeous, clear golden amber color, with a big, fluffy off-white head. Said head took it's time dropping. Lots of lace.

The nose was full of grapefruit and pine, but backed up with mango and pineapple notes. Not much in the malt department.

Big fruit juicy flavor with a big piney backbone. Seemed like a nice melding of a PNW DIPA with the tropical fruitiness that has become so much the rage. Things finished on a dry yet resiny note.

The body was somewhere between medium and full. Big, sticky, resiny, oily mouthfeel to this beer.

A modern hophead's dream version of this style. Unlike a lot of DIPAs I've had, it didn't seem like a normal IPA with amped up, boozy maltiness. In fact, the fairly high alcohol level wasn't very obvious. Instead, tasted like a metric fuck-ton of hops were utilized. I look forward to having this again and, hopefully, having cans of this in my fridge before too long.
