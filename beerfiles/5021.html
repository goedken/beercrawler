12oz bottle into a Duvel tulip. Reviewed from notes, which were taken without the benefit of a specific style designation; it tasted much more like an English Bitter than an APA, and my scores reflect as much.

A: Cloudy medium amber body under an inch of creamy off-white head. Good retention and lacing. Solid.

S: Smells very English-y, with biscuity malt, floral hops, and lots of overripe apricot yeastiness. Really fruity, a little buttery, and not that great.

T: Follows the nose: more sweet than bitter, with butterscotch + ripe stone fruit being the dominant flavors. Clean finish. Not terrible, but far from great.

M: Fairly light, moderately carbonated, and clean. Pretty god.

O: This is drinkable enough for what it is, but don't expect the hoppiness of an APA.

Cheers!
