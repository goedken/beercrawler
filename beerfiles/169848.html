Copper/amber with a fluffy, uniform white head across the whole surface. Minimal spotty lacing. No legs.

Somewhat bland nose, and what's there isn't great. Overly sweet. Reminds me of Red Bull, akin to liquid Smarties. Light spice found in Belgian yeast, and a little bit of caramel malt.

Flat out gross. Like 40% poorly aged dubbel mixed with 60% tap water. Malty and minute spice. Light tartness on the back end, but it's somewhat of a sharp, astringent sour. Little to no plums present, but the bottle is getting up there in age, so I can't fault that aspect. The vomit notes mentioned in other reviews are way overblown.

Low-ish carbonation. Light body, but kind of syrupy. Bready and dry finish.

Bad sour, bad Belgian, bad beer. Complete waste of money.

750 ml bottle was poured into a snifter.

Taste: Awesome plum and fig notes, brady, and caramel malts, tart notes, and Belgian yeast. Some chewy notes in there too.

Mouthfeel: Light bodied, a little chewy, a little yeasty, medium to high carbonation, a little dry, and a little tart.

Overall: More of an abbey ale with plums than a wild or sour ale. Still enjoyable and I look forward to next year's anniversary beer.

russpowell, Jul 25, 2015
3.91/5  rDev +9.2%
look: 3.5 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Thanks Emma for sharing.

A- Orange to Amber body from the 750ml to a tulip. A fizzy off white cap rises up to a finger with fair retention falling down to a small ring. Lacing is spotty and sparse.

S- Dark fruits in general obviously from the plums with a definite spiciness going along with that. Plum flesh and earthy pits. Tartness is hinted at.

T- Plums first and foremost and as a huge fan of them I am impressed by how they have captured the flesh and pit notes of the fruit. Tartness is medium low and feels like that of a plum and lactic. Some phenols bring a generally spicy hint. Earthy and plummy.

MF- Creamy and foamy consistency to a medium overall body. High end carbonation provides a crisp finish. Medium tartness and lingering plums on the palate.

Must be some plum haters or vomit bottles out there. This is full of fresh plums and tartness. Kind of one note but refreshing and captures a tough fruit flavor.
