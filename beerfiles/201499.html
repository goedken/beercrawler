superspak, May 19, 2016
4.55/5  rDev +4.4%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

Enjoyed out of a 12 oz brown bottle from the February 2016 release. I've had this beer close to a dozen times now, so while this review is based on my most recent experience last night, my overall opinion is colored by all those other experiences. Poured into a basic tulip style glass.

Appearance (4.25) - A moderately aggressive pour yields a finger or so that resembles the color of wet sand. Retention is moderate, with the head hanging around for a little bit before slinking back into the liquid, stabilizing as a smattering of film across the surface of the liquid and a ringlet on the edges. Sparse strings of lace are visible at points along the sides of the glass. The body is a deep near-black shade of brown with no light penetrating through.

Smell (4.75) - Wow, this beer is just overpoweringly full hazelnut in a deliciously good way as it relates to the aroma. It's so dense and rich, it feels like I'm sticking my nose down into a freshly-opened jar of Nutella. There is so much nutty characteristic that it almost smells like straight hazelnut butter--like a sweeter, less salty version of peanut butter. Underlying the huge nutty smell is that base of cocoa and dark chocolate that I get from most of Odd Side's barrel-aged stouts--almost like a house flavor, but limited to the stouts. There is very little booze--just some hints of vanilla and caramel that are very likely from the barrels in which this was aged.

Taste (4.5) - Unfortunately, the taste is not as aggressive as that intoxicating smell, but I will note that it improves and becomes more hazelnut dominant as the beer warms up. There is also a fantastic, lingering hazelnut flavor on the back of my tongue that hangs around well after the beer has gone. As with the smell, there is a base of sweet chocolate, caramel and vanilla, with the chocolate being more cocoa-like and sweeter than the roasty baker's chocolate that is often present with imperial stouts. And while the barrel is definitely not overt, to me it comes through in how the flavors all meld together, providing a subtle depth of character that wouldn't be there otherwise. It's really a case study in how to use a barrel to influence a beer without actually tasting the barrel individually.

Mouthfeel (4.5) - Body is on the full side of medium, with moderate carbonation that doesn't overpower with prickliness. Finish is long and slow, lingering for some time after the beer has been swallowed.

Overall (4.5), this is likely my favorite beer from Odd Side. I may have been lucky to avoid any bottles of mine falling victim to the reports of infection and other off flavors that have popped up around the interwebs. So the result is the best hazelnut stout I've ever had (full disclosure: I've only had this and Grey Monday), and a beer that I wish I could drink fresh all year round.

12oz bottle, poured into a stemless wine glass. Enjoyed on 4.17.2016, reviewed from Tasting Journal.

The beer pours a nice cola brown color, thick and oily with a khaki head, about a finger or two high with good retention and light spotted lace. The aroma is great, loads of coffee, mild roast, light hazelnut with lots of chocolate, oak, bourbon and a bit of vanilla. Super rich nose, lots going on and the interplay between the coffee and hazelnut is great, a very inviting beer. The flavors are bursting with chocolate and hazelnut, great coffee flavor is layered nicely that helps cut down the roastiness of the chocolate and bittersweet nature of the chocolate, the bourbon character is really mild, light oak with just a hint of vanilla and only a smidge of stone fruits. The mouthfeel is nice, coating with a full body but it doesn't quite have the legs of many imperial stouts, higher carbonation that anticipated and a warm finish.

Verdict: A nice adjunct stout from Odd Side Ales, interesting combination of coffee and hazelnuts but it works well. The balance is nice, good complexity, definitely an enjoyable dessert beer, nice to sip on.
