I picked this up when I was out of town, and afraid there wasn't going to be any good beer available. Fortunately a 7-11 had this and Sierra Nevada Celebration, so I was covered. Poured into a pint glass from a bottle. Pours a hazy dark-straw color with a two finger head that leaves an inch of lacing and dissipates slowly. Aroma is of citrus and dank west coast hops. Similar look and smell to Little Sumptin' Ale. Taste is cimcoe, cascade, and floral hops with enough wheat backbone to keep it from getting bitter. The hops work well together and none overwhelm the others. Light body and medium carbonation with no bitterness or alcohol aftertaste.
This is an extra hopped and smoother version of is weaker brother. I much prefer this to Little Sumptin' since it provides better hops and less bitterness. More IPA than double IPA, but no need to squabble about something good.

brewandbbq, Oct 25, 2015
4.18/5  rDev -0.2%
look: 4 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

A: Pours a clear gold with a bubbly, thick white head that laces deep into the glass.

S: Aromas of caramel, wheat toast, mandarin orange, tangerine, lemon, lime, grapefruit, herbs, kumquat, pine, and cedar fill the nose.

T: A surprisingly gentle entry quickly reveals well-integrated notes of caramel, brown sugar, wheat toast, crackers, mandarin orange, lemon, lime, kumquat, white grapefruit, tangerine, herbs, pine, cedar, and juniper. The finish is smooth, yet bitter, offering a long fade of tart citrus, herbs, wheat toast, and pine.

M: On the lighter side of medium in terms of body, and smooth with only a hint of initial stickiness. Carbonation is airy and active, yet still quite delicate for the style. Alcohol is very restrained and is only detectable briefly on the fade.

O: As with most Lagunitas beers I have tried, I would prefer a slightly heavier body and a little more malt presence and complexity, but overall, this is a very good double/imperial IPA. I really like how well-integrated the aromas and flavors are, and I appreciated the smooth texture of this ale on the palate. This one should definitely appeal to fans of the brewery's products, as well as to fans of this particular style.
