Brewed by:
Two Roads Brewing Company
Connecticut, United States
tworoadsbrewing.com

Style: Bière de Garde

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.00%

Availability: Winter

Notes / Commercial Description:
Two Roads Holiday Ale is inspired by the little known Biere de Noel style, on subset of Biere de Garde, both of which originated on small farmhouse breweries in the north of France. Biere de Noel, which translates as “Christmas Beer”, tends to exhibit a more malty profile than a typical Biere de Garde.

In its native France this special type of ale is brewed by just a handful of small breweries and is only available for the few weeks leading up to Christmas.

22 oz bottle was poured into a snifter.

Appearance: A one finger off white head was poured that reduced to a thin layer. It is an amber color with good clarity and vision of bubbles coming up through the glass. Spotty lacing down the glass as the beer was drank.

Mouthfeel: Smooth, chewy, like eating bready, malty, medium to full bodied, light bitterness, and medium carbonation.

Overall: Not familiar with this style, but it reminds me of a mixture between an altbier and doppelbock. I'd revisit this again.

Pours a dark chestnut brown color with golden copper/mahogany edges and a three finger frothy, off-white head that slowly settles into a thick, lasting ring. Excellent retention, lingering for the duration of the pour and completely coating the glass with sheets of soapy lacing. Impressive looking beer.

Slightly toasted dark malt aroma with earthy spice, floral hops and vinous fruit. Delicate roast with hints of blonde coffee and lightly charred toast followed by lighter malt sweetness. Some caramel, toffee, treacle and hazelnut. As it warms, the earthy spices and a slight medicinal/syrup-like presence comes forward. Cardomam, allspice, clove, nutmeg and cinnamon followed by cough syrup, sweet grape, raisin, fig and other dark berries. The nose is a little earthy and spice heavy for my preference, but the toasted malt character and vinous sweetness are pretty nice.

Full body with tight carbonation. Very dry and earthy with soily hop bitterness and musty spice. The spices are really strong, overpowering the pleasant malt and fruit esters found in the nose. They are still there, just buried underneath. Prominent zesty pepper with allspice, clove and cinnamon. After warming up a bit the malt bill shines a bit more. Light toast and faint coffee along with caramel, toffee and light chocolate sweetness. Faint vinous fruit presence and boozy sweetness in the finish yet the ABV is decently masked. Unfortunately, the dark fruit esters are somewhat lost. The medicinal character found in the nose is present toward the back of the palate but not quite as strong/harsh as the nose suggests. Overall, I wasn't a big fan of this beer. Not offensive just somewhat harsh and unbalanced. Wouldn't want more than a few ounces.

Poured from a bomber into a White Birch tulip glass.

The smell is a strong dark fruit flavor in the nose that is leaving hints of raisin, plumb, fig, and grape aroma. The is a dark biscuit aroma that leans into the back of this beer and hugs the walls as there is more fruit making a presence. There is a light bubblegum aroma here with an overripe strawberry as well.

The taste is fruity with that same dark fruit flavor but there is more here then in the nose. A really great raspberry flavor splashes the tongue and lets a sweeter fruit flavor takes hold for a while as little bits of bread flavor point out their presence. A hello from a nice malt bit and then a shower of strawberry, raspberry, and the darker fruits such as grape, raisin, and fig. There is even a tease of banana that I thought was there briefly but it just lasts long enough to reach for it and then it’s not there anymore.

The feel of this beer is really pleasant. There is a really juicy feel to this beer that helps it smooth out and go down easy and helps really nail that great drink-ability that was better then first expected. It is quite an enjoyable beer.

Mouth is med to heavier bod, a bit syrupy sweet, decent carb, too much hot booze though.

Overall meh, probably my least fav beer from them yet. Nothing really fun about this beer, too sweet syrupy with way too much booze and the spices are OK but not great for what it is. There are so many better places this beer could have gone.
