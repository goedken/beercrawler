Style: American IPA

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.50%

Availability: Year-round

Notes / Commercial Description:
Velveteen Habit boasts a vibrant floral nose with a juicy Citra hop intensity. The rich malt spine lends a hint of sweetness to balance the crisp hop bite in the finish, making it an IPA that challenges your taste buds without fatiguing them.

TX-Badger, Jun 07, 2015

look: 4 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.25

A big thanks to Jeff for this one. 12oz. bottle into a Darkness snifter.

Dense pour yields a .5 finger light cream colored head with good retention. Beer is a slightly cloudy muddy orange. Lacing is thick and layered with great stick. Very nice except for the color.

Nose is outrageously aromatic. Big hints of citrus, cane sugar, pine and fresh cut grass. A bit of fresh hop bite in the back. Really impressive.

Opens citrus, grass, pine and a bit of pineapple and tropical fruit sweetness in the back. A mellow hop bitterness in the middle is balanced by a fruit-forward and citrus-y malt backbone. Ends with a whimper of grapefruit hop presence. Same aftertaste. Nice complexity. I'm digging the great balance in this one too.

Light bodied with moderate carbonation. Vibrant and lively in the mouth and goes down crisp, sharp and accented. Finishes rather mellow with an aggressive, slightly lingering aftertaste. Very smooth and drinkable.

An IPA that drinks like and has the flavor of a IIPA. Need I say more? Incredibly under the radar.

SpeedwayJim, May 25, 2015
3.06/5  rDev -25%
look: 3 | smell: 3.25 | taste: 3 | feel: 3 | overall: 3

BOTTLE: 12 fl oz. Brown glass. Branded pry-off pressure cap. Decent label art.

7.5% ABV.

Served cold into a pint glass at high altitude in Castle Rock, CO. Reviewed live as an IPA per the label. Expectations are high given this brewery's reputation.

No bubble show forms as it's poured.

HEAD: About 3 inches wide. White in colour. Foamy. Frothy as hell. Coating. Retention is good - about 8 minutes.

BODY: Translucent transparent pale copper of average vibrance. Clean; no yeast particulate or hop sediment is visible.

Appears well-carbonated. It's a nice looking IPA, but isn't unique or special looking.

AROMA: Pale malts. Vague citrus. Floral hop character. Mild juiciness. A soft aroma which suggests a nice balanced brew with an underwhelming hop profile.

Aromatic intensity is average.

No yeast character, off-notes, or booze is/are detectable.

TASTE: Floral hop character. Pale malts. Has no vibrant citrus, grapefruit, resins, pine, etc. The hop profile is underwhelming, and the malts edge out the hops anyway. Poorly put together, but not entirely imbalanced. I like it generally, but it's somewhat shallow. Needs intricacy, subtle, nuance, or something. The choices of hops seem poor.

Not gestalt. Simple and plain.

TEXTURE: Overcarbonated. Smooth, wet, medium-bodied, unrefreshing.

There's no harmony of texture and taste here.

OVERALL: Underwhelming. It's drinkable, but doesn't stand out amongst the myriad of IPAs available readily on shelves. I honestly wouldn't nab a second bottle if I came across it. A disappointing introduction to a heavily hyped brewery. Above average at best.

Low C+ (3.06)
