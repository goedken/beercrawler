Style: Cream Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 6.70%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
Electric Mullet is a clear bright yellow Imperial Cream Ale with big doughy yeast aromas. Sweet cakey malt and hearty milled grain flavors cleanly transition to a consistent piney hop bitterness, leaving a lasting dryness in the finish.

A: Pours a hazy medium straw yellow in color with moderate amounts of active visible carbonation rising from the bottom of the glass and some faint honey orange + golden yellow highlights. The beer has a finger tall creamy foamy bright white head that slowly reduces to a couple of medium sized patches of medium thick film surrounded by a large patch of thin film covering the remainder of the surface of the beer and a thick ring at the edges of the glass. Moderate amounts of lacing are observed.

S: Moderate aromas of grassy + floral hops with light aromas of citrus/grapefruit pith hops, There are light to moderate aromas of grainy + bready malts.

T: Upfront there is a moderate flavor of grainy + bready malts with a light hint of sweetness. That is followed by moderate flavors of grassy. floral. and citrus/grapefruit pith hops which impart a light to moderate amount of lingering bitterness.

M: Medium bodied with moderate amounts of carbonation. Smooth and creamy with a light amount of dryness in the finish.

O: I just had to buy this beer based on the name and label but I also have had a couple of other cream ale/IPA hybrids which I enjoyed... Enjoyable but not quite as good as the others in this hybrid style that I have tried because it does not have as big a fruity hop aroma/flavor. Alcohol is well hidden and this one is pretty easy to drink.

Pours a hazy straw color with a couple fingers of rocky foam. Been on a cream ale kick for the last few months, so I'm curious to see Short's take on the style.
Aroma is caramel, biscuit, cracker, grapefruit, and floral hops. Taste is sweet and light as a cream ale should be, but with a bitter kick of grapefruit rind on the finish.
While this is a decent cream ale I wish Short's had given in to the temptation to India Style everything. This could be a real thirst quencher in the summer save for the bitterness. Shortsicle cream ale would be a good alternative release.

We recently began getting distro of Short's B.C. beers here in south-central PA & I am pretty excited about it! I have known those guys for years, between BA fests in BOS & my (usually) annual excursion to Madison, WI for the GTMW. I don't care if they are in bottles since I never expected them to come to me!

From the bottle: "Handmade by people who care. (tm)"

As I Pop!ped the cap, it gave me a second or two of pause in appreciation for probably my favorite set of caps, ever! They are bone-white with the Short's logo of a pair of short trousers with legs and feet hanging out of them, overlain by a hop vine. I may have to get my craft person to make me a special coaster ... Speaking of special, my pour was nothing special, just a gentle decanting as it seemed to have a haze in the bottle. It had a haze in the glass, too, but it also netted me two fingers of dense, bone-white head with excellent retention that showed no signs of dissipation. Color was a lightly-hazy Pale-Straw (SRM = 1 - 2) & I was reminded of my time as a command urinalysis coordinator. Interestingly, the haze began to clarify as it warmed. Nose was, well, bizarre. It smelled of bread, corn and grass or hay. As a hophead, I love the addition of hops to most beers, but there are some styles in which the hops are wasted, like the Cascadian/Black IPA as a glaring example. This smelled like they overhopped a perfectly fine Cream Ale. Mouthfeel was kind of thin, slightly watery, but not of keeping with the style. The taste was VERY bitter, not grapefruit, but grapefruit rind, pith & seeds! I found myself making the Bitter Beer Face for the first time in a long while. Oof. This is NOT what I want to taste in a Cream Ale! Hey, I still have an affinity for Genesee (Genny) Cream Ale, it having been one of my starters as an underage drinker. This had little to no similarity to a Cream Ale as I know them. The thing is, Cream Ale is a flawed style with its inherent adjuncts, but it is its own unique style. I generally like Cream Ales, especially out of a CAN on a hot summer's day, but this was not making it for me. I appreciate Short's B.C.'s willingness to experiment, but this was j-u-u-u-u-s-t a bit outside, as Bob Ueker might say. Finish was dry, bitter, overhopped & just unsatisfying.

Love anything from Shorts so when I saw their Limited Edition Variety pack in Massachusetts had to pick it up. This was one of 4 beers and since its one of my favorite styles (cream ales) I had to review this puppy first. But ahhh, this aint no regular runnadamill cream ale. This is an "India style" cream ale.

On the nose this has the typical bready malts, yeasty, cracker like characteristics like a delicious cream ale, in addition to that there is a waft of hoppy, floral magic. Like the aroma of a good cream ale but grassed up a bit.

The taste....before I sipped this, I was like, will it be a cream ale followed by an IPA or an IPA followed by a cream ale or an integrated masterpiece? Well, its neither of the above. This is more like one of those nonsensical celebrity name combos. Like Bennifer or Brangelina. When you hear it for the first time, its familiar but then combining the names like that doesn't really make sense. This ale is kind of like that. On paper a combination of one of the most popular styles in the US with the underrated cream ale was an exciting prospect for me but then its just so damn bitter. My last sip was when I started typing this review and it still lingers on. IMHO this is a miss from a terrific brewery. But I hope they will make some changes to the recipe if they plan on releasing this next year.

12 ounce bottle into tulip glass, bottled on 9/28/2015. Pours lightly hazy/cloudy deep golden yellow color with a 2 finger dense and rocky white head with great retention, that reduces to a nice cap that lingers. Nice dense soapy lacing clings around the glass. Aromas of lemon zest, pineapple, guava, apricot, orange rind, pepper, pine, cracker, corn, light honey, herbal, and floral/grassy earthiness. Nice and pleasant aromas with good balance and complexity of citrus/tropical/piney hops and moderate pale malt notes; with solid strength. Taste of big lemon zest, pineapple, guava, apricot, orange rind, pepper, pine, cracker, corn, light honey, herbal, and floral/grassy earthiness. Large amount of pine/citrus rind/spicy bitterness on the finish; with lingering notes of lemon/orange rind, pineapple, guava, apricot, pepper, pine, cracker, corn, light honey, and herbal/floral/grassy earthiness on the finish with a while. Damn nice complexity, robustness, and balance of citrus/tropical/piney hops and moderate pale malt flavors; with a nice malt/bitterness balance and no astringent bitterness after the finish. Moderate dryness from bitterness as well. Medium carbonation and body; with a very smooth, sticky/resinous, and fairly creamy/bready balanced mouthfeel that is nice. Alcohol is very well hidden with minimal warming present after the finish. Overall this is a very nice Cream ale/IPA style. All around good complexity, robustness, and balance of citrus/tropical/piney hops and moderate pale flavors; and very smooth and easy to drink. A very enjoyable offering.

I picked up a bottle of Short's Electric Mullet last week in their new Winter variety pack. I've been meaning ot try this one and shorts does a pretty good job when it comes to hoppy beers, so lets see how it goes. 2015 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a imperial pint glass.

A- The label looks good, it goes well with the name and it's eye catching. It poured a hazy slightly pale yellow color that took on more of a golden hue when held to the light with a little over two fingers worth of creamy white head that died down to a thin ring that stayed till the end and it left some nice patchy lacing behind. This is a good looking cream ale, but I wish it wasn't so hazy.

S- The aroma starts off with a slightly higher amount of medium sweetness with the malts being the first to show up and they impart those typical cream ale aromas with some creamy, grainy and sweet malt aromas coming through. Up next comes the hops which impart a earthy, piney and light citrus rind like aroma. This beer has a pretty nice aroma, but it's a little on the sweet side.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma, but more intense and it starts off with a lower amount of high sweetness with the malts being the first to show up and it imparts those same cream ale aspects that I got in the aroma, but this time it's much sweeter. Towards the end comes the hops which impart more of an earthy and pine aspect this time with a lower amount of high bitterness with a very sweet and very bitter aftertaste. This beer is just ok, the sweetness and bitterness are too high.

M- Not smooth, creamy, a little crisp, on the thicker side of being medium bodied with a medium amount of carbonation. The mouthfeel is decent, but I wish it was smoother,

Overall I thought this beer was just ok and I thought it was pretty close to an average example of the style, I like the idea of the a hoppy Cream Ale, but when it came to this beer, the bitterness and sweetness were too dominating and it hurt the drinkability for me. This beer didn't have good drinkability, one glass is more than enough for me. What I like the most about this beer is the appearance and aroma, they were both good and the highlights of this beer. What I like the least about this beer is the taste and drinkability, they just weren't that good and they needed some work. I don't think I would get this beer again, it's just nor for me and I can't say I would recommend it. All in all I was a little let down with this beer and I think it still could have been a pretty nice beet if the sweetness and bitterness weren't so intense. It's close to one of my least favorite shorts beers and it's average at best for the style. Better luck next time guys, this one could use some work.
