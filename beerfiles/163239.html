Boulevard Brewing Co. "Tasting Room Blonde Ale"
12 oz. brown glass bottle, "BEST BY 09/04/15"

Tasting Room Blond delivers a solid, sweetish and malty ale that's livened by a bright apple-like fruitiness. It's crystal clear and deep golden in color beneath a frothy head of white. The nose is clean and grainy-sweet with some background herbal hops. In the flavor the malt has a slight touch of toastiness to it, and a subtly tart apple and citrusy fruitiness off-sets that. It remains sweetish but it's more grainy. A firm bitterness anchors it, and grassy and herbal hops help to balance it. Apart from the fact that it's a bit stronger (5.8% abv) and fuller bodied than most, it remains a fairly basic blonde ale. The interesting apple/lemon-like fruitiness however lends it a touch of uniqueness.

Overall: Solid little blonde. I've had so many boring blonde's in the past that I wasn't really expecting anything from this. Definitely drinks more like a Lager. Bright malts, low to no hop presence, but this one has probably a lighter body than most lagers. I'm diggin' it.

A simplistic but well made blonde ale, part if the tasting room series. For some reason I thought this series was meant to be a way to experiment and try new radical stuff, I know their strange but highly acclaimed Radler came out of this program. It baffles me then how a straight up blonde is experimental in any way. Unless there is something going on here that my senses are numb too, it's as basic as blondes can be. I suppose there is a bit more of a hop element than the style usually offers, but nothing way outside the box, that's for sure. That said, it's a delicious, refreshing, and very well made example of the style, with a really delicate and fresh malt flavor, a bright mouthfeel, and a quick finish. Great in this Kansas City humidity. I suppose every brewery has to have a beer like this these days, and this is a good one, but it always look for a bit more out of this tasting room series...
