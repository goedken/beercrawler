11.2oz bottle, poured into an unlabeled 10oz snifter glass. Enjoyed on 7.28.16, shared with friends, reviewed from Tasting Journal.

The beer pours a pale gold color, hazy and cloudy with two fingers of white head, cloudy and fluffy with nice retention yet yields little lace. The aroma is great, really tropical, fruity, citrusy, juicy, a touch of funk, peppercorn earthiness, bready and grainy sweetness, but very light, delicate, easy, a bit floral and herbal as well. The flavors are great, pale grain, citrus zest juicy tropical fruit, a touch of funkiness plays nicely with an earthy spice and grain as well as the fruity hops, pretty yeasty as well, stronger citrus and mosaic presence at the finish. The mouthfeel is great, light body, delicate, lively coating, crisp but smooth finish.

Verdict: An awesome dry hopped farmhouse beer from De Proef and Trillium. Juicy, hoppy, tropical, bright, refreshing but with a nice earthy and spicy saison backbone to balance things out that has just a touch of pleasant funk. Really impressive beer, excellent summer ale.

The beer is mildly hazy even when being careful to leave out the lees. Nice head retention with a foamy ecru cap. Scent has mild phenols from the yeast that come through as black pepper and clove. Hops push through the yeast contribution nicely with an aroma of lemon, blueberry, and some grassy notes as well.

In the flavor the hops are more subdued. Mildly spicy finish. The yeast carries the flavor with more of the black pepper. The clove is less apparent and is replaced with a general herbal quality reminiscent of thyme. Mouthfeel is light to medium with carbonation that comes of as smooth and creamy. Well done with the bottle conditioning.

Overall a nice marriage of American hops and Belgian yeast. The two come together in harmony rather than each trying to scream over the other sad can be had in some hoppy Belgian styled beers.

StoutElk_92, Jul 17, 2016
4.7/5  rDev +11.6%
look: 5 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.75

Drank from an 11.2 fl.oz. bottle purchased at Pemberton Farms, Cambridge, MA
Served in a tulip

The nearly clear, golden colored body was topped by a rocky, two-plus finger tall plume of off-white sea foam. Outstanding retention. The head slowly dropped to a thick, frothy, lasting cap. Large chunks of lacing coated the glass. As the beer sat, the chill haze dropped to reveal a crystal clear body.

The nose was just as amazing as I had hoped it would be. Both the saison qualities and the hops came through strong, yet neither overshadowed the other. The beer was peppery and slightly grassy from the saison base. Crisp and clean. Somewhat lemony and tart. The hops lent some bright, tropical fruit notes. Loads of peach, mango and guava. Touch of musty passionfruit. Amazingly fragrant and complex, yet also well balanced.

The flavor profile was excellent as well, but the Citra and Mosaic hops definitely played a smaller role here. Nice pale malt base on the front end. There was a subtle, fruity quality. Perhaps some citrus and tropical fruit notes. Peppery in the center. Lemony and wheaty as well. Somewhat tart. Earthy hops were noticeable on the back end. Slightly bitter. Tart, lemony notes lingered in the finish.

Excellent smooth, fluffy mouthfeel. Spot-on for the style. The liquid contained a super fine, zippy effervescence that frothed up nicely on the palate.

I was a bit skeptical about a traditional Belgian saison being dry hopped with punchy American varietals, but this beer was truly amazing. The saison base was exemplary and the Citra and Mosaic hops complemented the beer better than I could have ever imagined.
