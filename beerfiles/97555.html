BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Arcadia Brewing Company
Michigan, United States
arcadiaales.com

Style: American Pale Ale (APA)

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 4.50%

Availability: Year-round

Notes / Commercial Description:
If you're looking for uncomplicated fun, look no further, you've got a Cheap Date. Whether you're relaxing shore side, hitting the competitive lawn game circuit, or just enjoying the seasons in all their splendor, there's never a bad time for our refreshingly hoppy session ale. Keep it casual and just enjoy the moment with this standby of high drinkability. Pairs well with grilling out, hanging out, and being out - all the "out"s.

Cheap Date is a pale gold ale with a fine white head and a gorgeous aroma mirrored by its flavor, overlooked with tropical Citra hops, citrusy Simcoe hops, and the perfect malty balance.

KarlHungus, Jul 29, 2016
3.67/5  rDev -0.8%
look: 4 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

Look: It pours an opaque yellow-orange with a nice frothy white head (1 cm in thickness) up top. The pour was not too aggressive as I only leveled out the snifter at the tail end, and left about an ounce in the bottle. Head retention is modest, but good, with decent lacing.

Aroma: The aroma has a citrusy punch to it. Other notes include pine hops, orange, and biscuit malts buried at the back. While the citrus is nice, the rest of the aroma feels lacking.

Taste: The flavor profile works through citrus, orange, pine and sticky hops, and a biscuit malt base. The taste has a bit of an overarching lactic, sour feel to it. No single note stands out in the forefront, as this beer remains well balanced and painfully tame.

Feel: As the beer hits the palette, it expands a bit, which feels reminiscent of a champagne de bier. Otherwise the carbonation is a bit abrasive and forward, giving this beer a rough feel. A medium consistency gives this beer good body and feel otherwise.

Overall: A meh beer. The mouth feel is certainly interesting. Overall, Cheap Date is certainly kept at a session-able level, but not something that would be too fun drinking throughout a session. The label art and beer name are fun.

Served in a 12oz snifter.

I have an affinity for Michigan beers and when I spotted this one as a single at The Foodery (Rittenhouse Square) during a recent foray to PHL, I snapped it up. I also like the labels on the Arcadia beers, so that was a bonus.

From the bottle: "1% to Kalamazoo River Valley Trail". That's nice of them to do that.

I Pop!ped the cap and gave it a heavy-handed pour, creating three-plus fingers of dense, rocky, bone-white head with excellent retention. Color was Straw-Yellow (SRM = > 2, < 4) with NE-quality clarity. In total, it looked like a slice of lemon meringue pie in my glass. How pretty! Nose was an interesting mix of grass, tropical fruit and fresh mint. Mouthfeel was medium. The taste was more towards papaya and mango, but there was also a cool mintiness to the taste. The finish was dry, kind of biscuity, more minty than fruity, but very pleasant. I generally eschew "session beers/ales", but if they were to put this in a CAN, I could see putting it in the summer cooler.

This one is a pale yellow in color with a mild murkiness lending itself to translucency in the body. The slightly tinted head is a moderately sized fluffy one that takes some time to fade to a heavy layer. Pretty good lacing is dropped as it goes.
The smell is mild at first, then puts in a good dose of flowery and earthy hops with some dryness. The malts are crusty if not crackery. This all translates to the flavor, and it offers a dry bitterness akin something I might expect from English hop varietals, and the same can be said of the malts. A little juiciness comes out before a certain chalkiness follows, and there's a bit of a spent grains taste underneath. It's not enough to take away too much. There's a certain mineral aspect underlying as well.
This beer is well carbonated, fluffy, and crisp. It gets a bit foamy toward the middle but holds steady, running semi-dry into a dry finish with a light medium body.

rapayn01, Aug 22, 2015
3.85/5  rDev +4.1%
look: 3.75 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4

Serving: 12 oz. bottle poured into a Founder's 2012 Harvest Party Pint. Bottled on 5/5/15, making this almost 2.5 months old.

A: Pours a somewhat hazy, murky, golden straw color. Fizzy white head that dissipates quickly.

S: Not too much going on here. Faint hop presence. Subtle malt notes in the background.

T: Definitely soft on the palate. Some wheat, some orange.

M: Very mild, even for a session ale. Good carbonation. Nice amount of hop bitterness in the finish though, quite dry.

O: So the bottle says Session Ale, and this actually drinks more like a Pale Ale or Wheat Ale. Definitely doesn't drink like a session IPA like Go To or All Day. This is truly a session ale in the sense that it's very light and drinkable, almost to the point that it's flavored hop water. It's certainly better than adjunct lagers as it has a nice taste and decent hop presences, it's just not that boisterous. Most session ales nowadays pride themselves on packing a hop punch in a light, low-ABV beer. This is just a light, low-ABV beer.

Recommendation: I'll leave this one for big fans of Arcadia or Michigan beers in general. Outside of that, this is entry-level stuff you should pass on. Cool label though!

12 ounce bottle into pint glass; no bottle dating, but is a recent release. Pours lightly hazy bright golden yellow color with a 1-2 finger dense white head with good retention, that reduces to a small cap that lingers. Nice spotty soapy lacing clings down the glass, with a moderate amount of streaming carbonation. Aromas of lemon zest, grapefruit, tangerine, peach, pineapple, orange peel, pine, cracker, biscuit, and floral/grassy earthiness. Very nice aromas with good balance and complexity of bright citrus/tropical/piney hops and light-moderate bready malt notes; with good strength. Taste of lemon zest, grapefruit, tangerine, peach, pineapple, orange peel, pine, cracker, biscuit, and floral/grassy earthiness. Good amount of pine/grassy bitterness on the finish; with lingering notes of lemon/orange peel, grapefruit, tangerine, pineapple, peach, pine, cracker, biscuit, and floral/grassy earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Damn nice complexity and robustness of bright citrus/tropical/piney hops and light-moderate bready malt flavors; with a crisp malt/bitterness balance and zero cloying flavors after the finish. Medium carbonation and light-medium body; with a very smooth, crisp, and moderately sticky mouthfeel that is nice. Alcohol is very well hidden with zero warming present after the finish as expected of 4.5%. Overall this is a damn nice session IPA style. All around great complexity, robustness, and balance of bright citrus/tropical/piney hops and light-moderate bready malt flavors; and very smooth and crisp to drink. A very enjoyable offering.
