Style: Belgian Strong Pale Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 8.50%

Availability: Limited (brewed once)

Notes / Commercial Description:
Valencia Gold is an aromatic Belgian-style ale that celebrates the vibrant and pioneering history of San Francisco’s sunny Mission District. This imperial ale is brewed with fragrant orange blossom honey and fresh Valencia Oranges from California’s Central Valley and dry-hopped with tangerine-scented Mandarina Bavaria hops. Pour this pure California sunshine into your favorite tulip glass and enjoy with fresh Dungeness Crab or winter citrus salads. ¡Salud!

Poured from the bottle into a tulip glass.

A deep lemon/orange body, almost pineapple yellow with a dark orange hue for the body color. Not much noticeable carbonation, quite opaque and solid. Two finger head is creamy and bright white, with some decent soapy retention and staying power.

Very inviting bouquet. Deep spritzy and sweet orange oil zest on the nose. Quite a real nice balance going on there, and a very unique orange aroma. Controlled sweetness, but doesn't seem to come from Belgian candied sugar either. Real nice smelling brew.

Palate brings a lot more interesting character. Sweet but tolerable, and slightly soda cream quality. Hints of vanilla soda fountain sweetness, and classic orange brightness. Juicy body, a hint of boozy but controlled flavor kicks in as well. There's a real interesting green like spice to this as well which makes the beer incredibly refreshing, hints towards thyme and sage. Slight mineral quality too, but with a much thicker malt body.

This is a real nice take on a Belgian with orange character. Every other ingredient seems to accent it, in such a real wonderful and unique way as well. The sweetness while not overboard, is just a little much, and slightly comes off a little candy store like strangely. Otherwise great brew.

4.02/5  rDev +10.1%

This beer starts out floral, citric, and dry. Obviously the orange/citrus comes through. I also get some peach and orange pith that makes an appearance from time to time. Once your palate settles in there is a subtle sweetness that is apparent, most likely from the honey. A pale almost chalky flavor associated to the fermented honey comes through as well. Floral turns to soft earthy notes and orange oil (as if derived from the peel) is now noticeable. Belgian esters and spiciness make their presence known along with some hints of clove. Slight cereal graininess and perhaps some white wheat (?) in the finish. Dry Belgian candy finish cleans it all up. Thin, light body. Carbonation is a little punchy and abrasive. Alcohol is perceptible and seems more exaggerated with the dryness and floral aspects of the beer.

In the beginning drinkability seemed like it would be an issue. As the beer warmed the complexities came together to make a reasonably enjoyable beer. After having a handful of their beers, I'm not 100% sold on Almanac just yet, even some of their sours. This is a good beer from them, not great. Had this been a 6.8-7.5% beer, I would have enjoyed much more.
