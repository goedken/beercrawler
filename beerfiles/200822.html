"You started with cream in your coffee, now have coffee in your cream ale. We took blonde beer, then calibrated it with roasty coffee and a touch of creaminess sweetness. Start and end end the day with craft brew. CREAM! It's Mad Science." Brewed in the style of a cream ale, available in 12 oz. canned 6 packs and on limited draft.

(Appearance) Pours a frothy foam head of a creamy white color over a moderately hazy copper amber body with fine sediment in tow and moderate carbonation. Retention is average and lacing is light and spotty. 3.5

(Smell) Aromas of pale and soft caramel grains forward with a bold impact of mildly roasted coffee beans, generating light fruitiness and moderate acidity. Potency is moderate. 3.5

(Taste) The flavor largely follows the aroma, with a more rounded coffee character. Clean, accessible malt aroma of pale, light wheat and some caramel grains with a slightly more residual sweetness. The impact of coffee beans generates moderate roast, fruit and acidity for a well-rounded bean flavor. Finishes clean and smooth. 3.75

(Mouthfeel) Texture is slick, moderately residual and oily, smooth, medium dry. Carbonation is modest, generating a medium frothiness and a medium crisp finish. Body is medium+ for the style, medium overall. Balance is nearly even sweet to roasty and acidic coffee bean bitterness. Alcohol presence is light and there are no off characters. 3.75

(Overall) An enjoyable coffee-infused cream ale that does a good job at fine-tuning the line between an ever-too-roasty bean and the raw subtleties of fruit and acidity. Altogether, it's a fitting dessert-style beer with a bit of residual sweetness but a lightness which makes for high drinkability. Another enjoyable ale from Sixpoint. 3.75

Review from BeerAdvocate magazine issue #112 (MAY 2016)

Noisette, Dry, Clean, Unique
You have to love the boundaries that have been pushed with coffee infused beers. While the dark days may not be behind us yet, it is refreshing to see lighter colored beers of this sort. It also helps when the brewer stays within even modest style boundaries, which Sixpoint has done here with its Cream Ale. A cracker-like maltiness meets the coffee’s slight roastiness and the finish unveils a mild citrusy hop character. The great use of coffee stays with the palate throughout, making for a very enjoyable beer. [Jason]

I have been sidetracked from The CANQuest (tm) for a couple/few days, but now, it is right back to what matters most. Who would have imagined that an offhand remark on a snowy evening in an LNBA forum would blossom into my being at the tip of the sword in the craft CAN revolution? Not me!

From the CAN: "Cream Ale Brewed with Coffee"; "You started with cream in your coffee, now have coffee in your cream ale. We took blonde beer, than calibrated it with roasty coffee and a touch of creamy sweetness. Start and end the day with craft brew. CREAM! It's Mad Science."; "Craft rules everything around me."; "Beer for Beasts Please Recycle".

At last, a Crack! I was so excited by the sound that I performed an inverted Glug, but I was getting a LOT of foaming action & I had to back off. I ended up with two-plus fingers of dense, foamy, tawny head that held its own in the face of tonight's heat & humidity before falling away to wisps. Color was Amber to Deep Amber/Light Copper (SRM = > 9, < 13) with NE-quality clarity. Nose had a faint coffee scent, but frankly, I was disappointed. I am a retired U.S. Navy sailor & I take my coffee very strong! I was hoping for more than this. Mouthfeel was big, full and (dare I say it?) creamy! The taste had a definite coffee bent, but the inherent sweetness of the beer's style was cutting into the coffee's bitterness. Understand, I drink coffee. That is the hot, black liquid that comes straight outta the pot or spigot. Anything else is merely a variation on a hot milkshake. This had the same problem, in my eyes. Other flavors were getting in the way of the coffee & dumbing it down. Perhaps I am wrong-headed and looking at it from the wrong direction, but I wanted more. Finish was in that increasingly present netherworld of semi-sweet to semi-dry. I am so CANflicted! I like Genny Cream Ale & I like coffee, but I was just not digging what this was giving me.

12oz can courtesy of that New Jersey beer ninja, SammyJaxxx. According to the stamp on the bottom of the can, this is best by July 12, 2016.

Poured into a Sierra Nevada pint glass, this was a chill hazed, dark, golden amber color, taking a more brighter, golden color when held up to light. There was about an inch or so of off-white foam, which receded fairly rapidly to very light covering that hung around for some time.

Initially, there was a light, roasty coffee scent, but it was quickly abetted, then consumed by a grassy hop note.

Very unusual flavor. The coffee is not dominant at all, but compliments what otherwise tastes like a fairly straightforward blonde ale/cream ale. Not as hoppy as the nose led me to believe, and the coffee gives this beer a depth I wouldn’t have expected without dominating things in the slightest.

Fairly light bodied, with a light, slightly creamy mouthfeel.

Not a session beer at all, but it drinks like one. Cream ales and blonde ales are something I find rather bland, but I’d happily drink this again. Thanks, SammyJaxxx!
