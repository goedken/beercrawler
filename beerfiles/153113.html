Pours a murky amber with some colorful, bright accents. Solid 3 finger bone white head lingers for a while. Aroma of wheat, hay, some musky yeasts, and corn. Slightly jammy, but not huge blueberry come at you like I had hoped. Flavor is slightly acidic, a subtle wave of tartness, salty, and a mild touch of blueberries. Body is sublime. Perfect spring ale as it is light, prickly on the tongue, and goes down really easy.
Overall, pretty good. A solid upgrade from their first. Not world class as it still misses the mark on complexity, but much more true to the style.
$14 for 750 ml

A: poured from bottle into sniffer. mild carbonation. murky red/pink. Not quite blueberry as I imagined

N: There is blueberry in this? I swear all I smell is the cooled water after you boiled 20 lbs. corn (with husk) in a big pot

T: does not stray too far from the nose. I can hardly distinguish the fruit, except for that bitterness taste after you are chewing on a grape/blueberry skin for a while

F: tingle on the tongue; partially from the carbonation, partially from the sour bite

O: I would've been more impressed with this had I not tasted other blueberry sours, e.g., Cascade Blueberry, etc. Not disappointing, perhaps just expected too much

look: 4.75 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

L- Mauve with pink hues, very nice brilliant white head that reduces to a skim, no real lacing. Looks very attractive, rich color

S- Jammy sour berry, tart blueberry with a load of funk. Very, very nice

T- Very reminiscent of blueberry skin, tart while still not too acidic/out of balance. A long note of cereal grain on the back end.

F- Very effervescent, lively, clean, and soft. Very nice, just about perfect.

O- A very strong effort. I really like the balance and nuance in this one. It's not overly sour but still nice and tart. The more it warms the more jammy it becomes; super nice.
