Appearance: Dispenses a clouded, dulled gold body with a steep head of near white foam.

Smell: A crackery, subtly bready (sans breadcrust) malt base scaffolding for the tropical, resiny, floral hops and slight brett mustiness.

Taste: A varied parade of the lighter malt tastes along the Malt Rainbow, from hewn hay to biscuits, white bread and crackers, though there are perhaps some hints of cookie-ish sweetness to be had. Resinous, tropical hops, mostly citric white grapefruit and papaya backed by the freshest of florals. Moderate bitterness. Brett infused funkiness throughout. Finishes dry, a bit citric with a final flush of suds.

Mouthfeel: Medium-bodied. Well carbonated, leaving you a bit gassy.

Overall: Though I found it to be fairly good, the lady proclaimed the beer was "like I stuck my tongue in liquid soap." Can't please 'em all, I suppose.

cosmicevan, Mar 26, 2015
4.3/5  rDev +1.2%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

Wild IIPA brewed with local (NC) ingredients and finished off with Brettanomyces. Poured from a bottle into a Wicked Weed snifter. The pour is slightly hazy and pale gold-amber with an impressive head for a sour beer. It's two fingers thick, clumpy, and pretty persistent. I definitely get the hops on the nose as citrusy notes cover up the funky brett. Grapefruit, lemon, pineapple, and just a tiny bit of mustiness.

Light body and a delicate feel that gets firmer as it warms and degasses. Light, grainy, cracker-like sweetness up front and some light, herbal, hop notes. Lemon rind, sage, grapefruit juice, peppercorns, and dry white wine. Not sour at all, but there's an underlying mustiness that complements the dry, herbal hoppy notes very nicely without being too distracting. The finish is dry and slightly citrusy, like a gin and tonic with mild, stony, quinine-like bitterness and juniper berry tart-n-sweet notes.

This is a really interesting beer. Very light in flavor, yet it can fool you into thinking it's "bland" because every flavor here is subtle and gentle, yet when you contemplate the beer, you realize there is a very high level of complexity here. Part of me wants it to have more of a hop character, but then it would just be an Imperial IPA. Part of me wants it to have more funky brett notes, but then it would just be a sour. I bought one more bottle of this (which I'm saving for a trading partner), but I really could see stashing this beer in the cellar and just letting it go to see what happens to it. Personally, I feel like I just caught a brief glimpse into what this beer is. A snapshot in a long and unpredictable life. I am also starting to really appreciate the fact that Wicked Weed puts its sours into bottles that are about 2/3 the size of a bomber and charges $13. To me, it's the ideal size for a sour. Large enough to fully appreciate the beer (and share the bottle with a friend or two), yet not large enough to push the price point up to where you feel guilty about buying it.

Sizable fizzy head, large and white. Good retention, fantastic leasing. Brew is almost perfectly clear and golden. Very active. Extremely hazy and nearly opaque once the yeast from the bottom of the bottle enters the glass; also develops more of a trademark Brett fluffy head.

Huge and fruity tropical aroma. Ruby red grapefruit, pineapple, lemon, and citrus peel. Slight mustiness, and a bit of biscuit.

Flavor is a little more subdued at first, representing biscuit malt and vegetal bitterness. Then, the hops smack you in the face. It is a literally like chewing on a grapefruit. Unbelievably good. Very little presence from the Brett.

Very light bodied, especially for a double IPA. Easy to drink, long better finish.

A very impressive, fantastic double IPA. I would love to see what age would do to this, to see if it brings out the Brett significantly.
