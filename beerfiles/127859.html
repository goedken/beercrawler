A best by date on the bottom of the can of 21 Sept. 2015, so still pretty fresh. Can notes state a fusion of Bravo, Cascade, Centennial, and Lemondrop hops with an abv. of 6.2%.

Look- Pours a sparkling gold with lively white foam. I'm glad I poured some out from the '360' open top can into a glass. This beer is truly a beauty to be admired.

Smell- Pine, some mild citrus and bread.

Taste- Floral hops followed by pine with a bit of malt making an appearance in the back. There is a citric lemon aspect to this beer that becomes apparent after consuming a few ounces.

Feel- A bit oily to start then finishes rather dry with citric acidity.

Overall- A very enjoyable beer and all around solid IPA that would be a regular in my rotation if I had access. Love the tall boy can with the flip top, great for outdoor enjoyment.

Pours a deep orange color with good clarity. White head forms and leaves good lacing.

Hoppy aroma of pine and tropical fruit. Some malt sweetness and faint alcohol smell.

In the taste, hoppy citrus, pineapple, pine and pith. Really nice bright hoppy flavors making my taste buds do a 360. Pale malt foundation adds a balancing quality to the beer, yet still hop forward in a satisfying manner. As it warms more toasty malt flavor comes out along with leafy hops.

Medium bodied, well carbonated ale that drinks like a pale but definitely tastes like IPA.

Overall, this is a very good IPA by Sly Fox. The canned reviews seem more disappointing, but on draft this tasted very bright and fresh.
