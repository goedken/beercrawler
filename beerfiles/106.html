Poured from a 12oz. bottle into an imperial pint glass.
Since this beer is retired, I was very surprised to find this bottle mixed among a bunch of other singles on the bottom shelf of a cooler in a tiny local liquor store.

A: The beer is a medium gold with an orange tint, has a short white head that fades quickly and doesnt leave much lace on the glass

S: The aroma is of caramel, honey, spice and hops.

T: The taste is very sweet with flavors of honey and spice. You can taste a decent, but not strong hops background.

M: Crisp and smooth with a light-to-medium body, light carbonation with a clean finish. Most beers that have the type of sweetness that this one does, will leave some stickiness behind but Im not feeling that here.

D: Its light and goes down very easy. Like many other Sam Adams beers, its one that I could go with for a while.

Given that this beer has been retired and replaced as the Sam Adams spring release by the White Ale, I can help but compare the two, even though they are from completely different styles. While this is a decent beer, I prefer the White Ale.

TheManiacalOne, Jul 09, 2006
3.97/5  rDev +15.4%
look: 3.5 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Samuel Adams Spring Ale pours to a deep golden hue with just a touch of amber. It also offers a large, healthy head that remains intact as I slowly drain my glass. The aroma is of a sweet, malty sort. After a second sniff, I can detect a hint of spicy hops and a slight earthy smell. The magic of Samuel Adams Spring Ale is in the taste, however. When this exquisite brew flows through your lips the initial flavor, like the smell, is earthy and reminiscent of peat. A delicate, fruity taste then presents itself and coats your mouth with a unique tart sweetness. As you swallow this tasty ale, you will notice a slight bitterness on the back of your tongue as a result of the hops. The overall finish is exceptionally clean; one of the many positive aspects of this outstanding beer.
