This appearance is just amazeballs. Oil black out of the can, forming the same oily obsidian color in the glass, not flinching a bit as it pools, and forms a head the color of malted milk balls. No doubt, an all-time favorite of mine.

Aromas of roast malt and layers of stiff chocolate, with a boozy coffee aroma garnished with slivered figs and licorice sticks.

Flavor is ashy to start, followed by burnt booze, with tons of roast malts, peppermint, cocoa, and then coffee. The finish is chocolatey and the booze forms a tidal wave and then sets on the tongue. Just booze and more booze as it warms.

Feel is just...yeah, what you'd expect from a 13% beer and then some. Don't expect to feel or taste anything after finishing this beer, it's just total palate destruction with a syrupy thickness, an oily feel, and a dry roastiness underneath.

Overall, this beer is IMPRESSIVE but NOT particularly ENJOYABLE. The booze is the star of the show, and the beer ends up tasting like a really badly bourbon-doused chocolate-coffee milkshake after awhile. Expect to take 30 minutes or more finishing this one. I'd dump some of it but I'm fearful it would plug the drain.

Pours dark, black and oily. Dark mocha colored head. Head dissipates leaving a ring of mocha colored foam along the outside of the beer.

Smell is a mild unremarkable array of roasted ashy malts, licorice, anise, and chocolate.

Taste is again roasty almost ashy malts up front. Some licorice and anise come through with some dark cocoa. Bits of tobacco and roasted coffee also come through. Maybe a hint of dark fruits on the back end with a lingering dry bitterness. This is a really robust stout that stands out. Touch of sweetness, but only enough to cut the smoky, roast of the beer. Without that touch of sweet it would almost be too ashy. Lingering oaky flavor starts to develop in the mouth as you drink the beer.

Feel is really thick and viscous. looks like motor oil. Smooth and silky in the mouth. Mild to medium carbonation provides a nice pop. For an Imperial stout, this is a really stand out feel on the beer. So rich and robust.

Overall this is an incredible sipper. Wonderful appearance and feel and the taste is really nice. This would pair so well with a cigar on a cool night.

Can dated 7/6 (BB 6/2018):
Man, this poured thick and black. This may be the closest pour to actual motor oil (really dirty at that) that I've had ever, but at least in a while. The head grows a dark brown, as you might expect, and ends in a thick, creamy ring. Streamers of dark brown lace remain on the glass as I sip it down.

The nose is a generic but dark roasted malt, with a slight bit of char. With a swirl, there's a sort of dark cherry and a scorched wood tart note mixed in as well.

The taste is on point if you expect it to follow the nose fairly well. That cherry note does come across as a little more tart (but not as much scorched) than the nose indicates, but this aspect of the taste fits the nose in my mind. There is a certain level of sweetness to this beer, giving the overall impression to me of a chocolate covered cherry (minus the sugary syrup in the middle). CArbonation has trouble pushing through the thick body of this beer, but does make it. A light coating of slickness lingers, allowing the flavor to be sensed for a few seconds after the sip.

This is a very solid beer. It leans to the sweeter side, but that sort of tart note helps balance it nicely. There could be a little more to the roast and a little less sweet/tart for me to go to the highest categories, but I am splitting hairs, some of which might have already been split.

On the nose is a strong profile of bourbon and coffee. The heat is definitely present from this imperial stout. They make no attempt at hiding its potency. There are subtle hints of pure cocoa which help to tame the heat a bit.

The flavor profile is that of equal amounts of chocolate. espresso, and bourbon with hints of tobacco and cherry. However, there is more bourbon than there should be. The heat is a bit intense. There is enough sweetness to balance the bitter so that neither really takes center stage.

The mouthfeel is perfect. A nice full body imperial stout with enough carbonation to keep the tongue ringing long after it's left the palate.

Overall this is a good imperial stout but they could do more to balance the heat. I have had many Imperial Stouts around 13% that have done a better job at hiding this heat.

timmy2b, Sep 15, 2016
4.46/5  rDev +7%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.5

Pours a pitch black color. The head is a finger high and tan in shade. Retention isn't as fantastic as other monster stouts up in this gravity, but the texture is at least nice and creamy. Small waves of lacing stick to the glass.

I could just summarize the aroma with the two words "roast bomb" and be done with it, but I won't. Still, it has a fantastically roasty nose that's beautifully embellished with additional notes of vanilla, dark chocolate, nuts, maple syrup, and espresso. Like many Scandinavian-style imperial stouts, the roast is so intense that it creates a powerful umami quality - this is also aided by strong notes of peat smoke. Also, there's no real alcohol presence. Honestly, this is close to my ideal for the style, and that's the only other thing that can be said.

This stout is more or less roast heaven. Strong notes of umami, roasted malt, and espresso open it up, aided by a peaty middle. Some of the sweeter notes show up on the close - primarily dark chocolate, vanilla, and maple syrup. Some fringe notes of dark fruits pop up every once in a while. It's not infinitely complex, but it does an excellent job of balancing the handful of notes it does feature to near-perfection.

There is a gentle prickle of alcohol that can be felt, especially going down the throat. Still, this is remarkably smooth for 13%. Despite the massively thick texture, it's surprisingly agile due to the dryness. Carbonation is a bit low, as expected.

This stuff is seriously on fleek, and I can't believe I actually said that...

4.46/5: Everything a non-barrel aged imperial stout should be

4.78/5  rDev +14.6%
look: 5 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 5

If ever there was a motor oil stout in appearance, this is it. On Fleek is topped with possibly the darkest caffe mocha head I've ever seen, and it is easily rejuvenated throughout the entire lifespan of the pour. The fluffy, dense foam, dark brown head is so rich it nears red velvet and purple hues. The beer itself greases and stains the glass with dark brown legs.

Intense roast explodes on the nose, with coffee, tons of vanilla, soft creme cherry, chocolate, and almond paste. OMG is this beehotch enticing.

On the palate raisin and molasses come forth with supreme prejudice, followed very swiftly by coffee, toasted walnut, and a stick to your ribs, savory, almost meat-like umami flavor from the heavily roasted malt, lending an almost salty flavor.

Feel is just god damn luscious. Thick and velvety, very substantial. Just what the Doctor ordered. There is a touch of heat on the backend, being 13% what would I expect, except that it is almost unnoticeable with all the other attributes just pounding your palate into blissful submission.

Despite its dumbass name, this beer is in rarefied air with the likes of Marshal Zhukov, DORIS, and even Dark Lord (albeit less sweet with more body). All this, in a can!? Wow, I didn't know this kind of WMD was possible in this small a package!
