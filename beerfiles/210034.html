BEER INFO

Brewed by:
New Belgium Brewing
Colorado, United States
newbelgium.com

Style: Belgian Pale Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.00%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
A deliciously uncommon springtime beer, our Golden Ale brewed with Hof Ten Dorsal blends modern Mosaic hops and malted sunflower seeds with Old World ingredients such as Saaz hops, spelt, and wild carrot herbs. It’s New and Old Belgium together in a bottle.

Tastes more like a Belgian Blonde than a Belgian Golden to me (and yes, there is a difference). Typical Belgian Blonde aroma of banana and clove, with some citrus and pepper from the Mosaic and Saaz hops. The taste has the same elements, but is also a little grainy, nutty and earthy. 4.12/5  rDev +7.9%
look: 3.5 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

Light clear golden color with generous white cap and lacing.

Aroma at first is of layered spice infused ripe yellow fruit with a light touch of citrusy hops.

Taste is great. It's Belgium with a bump like the accent touch of a lemon twist in a perfectly made martini. Just enough to be present but not at all intrusive. The usual flavors of banana, clove, citrus and white pepper we often find in Belgian blonde ales are there but in a more subdued way, as if the conductor directed the choir to sing sotto. Malt serves as a foundation but seems present more in the body than the flavor. Aftertaste brings something like a mild nuttiness with just a hint of juicy rather than sharp hops.

Texture is not at all heavy which belies the 7% alcohol.

Overall this is a contemplative combination of elements that makes the complex seem simple like the shape of a perfect tea rose where all the different parts combine together into a most pleasing whole.

Poured from a 12 oz. bottle into a New Belgium Globe glass.

Taste: Like it smells, a spicy and fruity Belgian style taste with notes of yeast and spices. Belgian yeast with notes of peppers, clove, and farmhouse funk. Blend of additional herbs and spices with some earthy hops. Sweet notes of candied sugar and bubblegum. Bready malt notes of wheat, grains, and cracker. A pretty good taste.

Mouthfeel: Medium bodied with a moderate amount of carbonation. Crisp, spicy, and pretty smooth.

Overall: A very solid spin on a Belgian style ale. Nice blend of spice, herbs, and fruit undertones.

Poured from the bottle into a tulip glass.

Bright and nearly crystal clear, a big lemon and brass gold yellow worth a polished shine appearance for the body. Real nice raising middle column of carbonation, real bright white and creamy consistency to a nice simple two finger head. A small bowled puck is always present. Real nice looker of a brew.

Aroma has some American yeast like character for a Belgian but with nice touches. White wine grape slightly, mild sensing sweet pastry bread from Belgian candi sugar sensations. Might be a bit of small coriander slightly but it's pretty tiny this green herb scent like character. Solid.

Juicy mid palate on first sip, with a small of tropic fruit but gets taken over by sucrose sweetness. Slight tasting Golden Monkey like flavors and an odd bit of banana ester making an appearance on the tongue. Light bodied, but with a far thicker but easy drinking palate. A little bit of minty spice hits on the end, making for some interesting flavors and is nice with the sugars.

American hop angle doesn't seem to come out that much, and classic Belgian flavors are more in the sweeter and American vein. Funny how that turns out, at it's most American it's not quite, at it's most Belgian, it's more American. A decent brew though.
