I picked up a bottle of Oddside's Scallywagger for $4.49 at Valley Produce. This one really peaked my interest, so lets see how it goes. 2015 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a snifter.

A- The label looks nice, it goes great with the name and it's eye catching. It poured a cloudy amber orange color which took on more of an orange color when held to the light with a finger and half worth of bubbly off white head that died down to a thin ring that eventually faded away and it left a little bit of spotty lacing. This is a pretty nice looking beer, I have no complaints.

S- The aroma starts off with a higher amount of sweetness with the rum being first to show up and it imparts a caramel, rum, light vanilla and oak flavor which leads into the pineapple which is noticeable and not to overpowering. Up next comes the hops which impart some typical hoppy aromas with a little of fruity citrus and tropical fruit like aroma. Towards the end there's a little more caramel and sweet aromas with a noticeable boozy aroma. This beer has a pretty nice aroma that lives up to it's description.

T- The taste seems to be fairly similar to the aroma and it starts off with a higher amount of sweetness with the rum still being the first to show up, it's not as dominant as it was in the aroma, but it imparts all the same aspects. Up next comes a noticeable boozy flavor followed by the pineapple which shows up a little more than it did in the aroma and it imparts a nice natural pineapple juice like flavor. Towards the end comes the hops which impart the same hop profile that they did in the aroma and on the finish there's a lower amount of medium bitterness with some more booziness. In the aftertaste there's some rum and caramel flavors. This is an ok tasting beer, the flavors are just as advertised, but it's very intense and too sweet and boozy for me.

M- Not the smoothest. boozy, not too crisp, a little cloying, a little creamy, on the thicker side of being medium bodied with a slightly lower amount of carbonation. The mouthfeel is decent, but I wish it was smooth and not as cloying and boozy.

Overall I thought this beer was just ok and I thought it was pretty close to being average for the style, I like the concept, but in the end it's the boozy rum and the sweetness that really stands out and it starts to feel like i'm drinking one of those flavored rums for captain morgan. This beer doesn't have good drinkability, one is more than enough for me. What I like the most about this beer was that it actually delivered when it came to the rum, ipa and pineapple flavors. What I like the least about this beer was the booziness and sweetness, they kind of took of the beer. I wouldn't buy this beer again, I might turn one down and I wouldn't recommend it unless sweet, boozy and fruity is your thing. All in all I liked some parts, but i really didn't like others, so it kind of fell right in the middle. It;s for sure not a favorite oddsides beer. Thanks for taking a chance and making a really unique beer, but I think it still needs a little more work.

Bottle dated 11/16/2015:
I used to be a rum and Coke fan back in the day, but I fell out of really enjoying rum. I don't dislike it, just easy to pass it over. Also haven't had many rum-barrel beers I've had, but let's se:

Beer pours a weak brown - almost a mix of brown ale and Pilsner. The head is not voluminous, but does sit nice and steady atop the pour - a fuzzy white. Decent head, but not near "pale".

Definitely get the rum in the nose. Initial sniffs it was too strong and leaned towards isopropyl in nature. So I let it rest to warm a tad. The harshness eased a tad, and the pineapple note became noticeable. They started to blend more and more, and I could even sense a piña colada even though I wasn't getting any coconut.

Taste starts out with a little tartness over a crackery grain. This tartness grew into a definitive pineapple taste. The beer has a tingle from the get go. The pineapple starts to yield to a woodiness as the spirit flavor begins its ascent. The rum hits full bore mid-taste. It's strength falls off decently fast, but the flavor lingers. The end is actually sonewhat piney to me - with maybe even a light resinous quality. Strange.
Warmth softens this beer a sit does many. The tart opening bite fades, the pineapple remains but is more subdued, and the rum loses some of its sting. The proportions seem about the same, though.

That's one good word to describe this beer. This tastes to me more like a strong mixed drink than a beer. For me, the rum needs to come down quite a bit and accent the beer more than being the beer. Fun to try, no need to retry.

Bottled 11/16 and poured 12/16. $5 for a bottle. I'm cautiously optimistic going into this beer. The Odd Side Pineapple Derelict or whatever it was called was one of (if not the) first beers I've had from Odd Side, and I wasn't too impressed by it. Hopefully this one's better.

Appearance: Hard pour into a Sam Adams Perfect Pint gives two fingers of a creamy white head that dies down to about half a finger. Lacing is really nice. This beer has quite a few yeast chunks in it. It's a hazy copper brown and very hard to see through with floaters all around... kind of like a diarrhea filled snow globe. Whatever, looks good to me without that analogy.

Smell: First smell was an intense, intense, IN-TENSE rum aroma -- really just like straight rum. As it breathes, it's still super strong, but some barrel comes through with a coconut vibe that plays incredibly (and unsurprisingly) well with the pineapple. Very sweet, and quite boozy on the nose. Super unique. Those aromas are most dominant, with only a little bit of accentuating hops coming through to my nose: piney and resinous, and likely contributing a bit to the awesome tropical fruit aroma. A little heavy on the rum for my liking, but you get what's advertised with this one. Strong, potent, awesome smelling beer.

Taste/feel: Follows the nose perfectly with an in-your-face presence of rum, pineapple, barrel, and a little coconut on the end. The finish is some woodiness, rum, sweet pineapple... just a jumble of awesomeness. This is very boozy, sweet, and all-around delicious. If that's not enough, the feel is absolutely outstanding with a nice heft to it and an incredible silkiness with just enough carbonation. As the beer warms slightly, the pineapple comes off as a bit tangier and it literally makes my mouth water. So many levels of flavor to this beer, I can't stand it. While I want to bitch that I think the rum is too "in-your-face", because it's super present, all of the other flavors with it... wow. Holy hell, wow.

As usual, kicking myself for only getting a couple of bottles of this stuff. I had opportunities to get several bottles but I stuck with two because I wanted to try a bunch of different beers... bah!

I'm not huge on pineapple on its own as a fruit, but I enjoy the flavor, and no more than in Scallywagger. This is one of the hottest and booziest beers I've ever had, so know that much, but the flavors coming from the barrel and pineapple are unreal -- I think the coconut flavor I'm picking up is what's putting this over the top.

I've always adored Odd Side ... with all of their more recent releases, they are truly becoming an all-star brewery, and one of my favorite breweries anywhere.
