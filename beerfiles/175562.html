Style: English Brown Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 6.00%

Availability: Year-round

Notes / Commercial Description:
You’ll go totally walnuts over this oak-aged, walnut-seasoned brown ale. Made with a brewing style that dates back to 16th-century England and using a blend of six choice malts and two English hops, Goodwood Walnut Brown Ale features notes of caramel and chocolate with a finish that is – you guessed it – pleasingly nutty.

Lukass, Sep 16, 2015
3.79/5  rDev +8%
look: 3.75 | smell: 4 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.75

Not as sweet as the historic brown ale of southern England, yet lacking the dryness and crispness to qualify for the northern English brow ale- Goodwood's Walnut Brown Ale strikes and even-keel balance between the two and adds its own curious overlay of walnut wood aging to the style's subtle but robust complexity.

Deep, dark and lusciously brown, the ale settles into the glass with a creamy viscocity that patiently settles underneath and dense blanket of froth. Above the rim, the overcast of chocolate, walnut and mid-intensity coffee saturates the nose while is savory sweetness appeases the taste. Toasted caramel, bittersweet chocolate, columbian-blend coffee and roasting walnuts round out the initial taste.

Its creamy malt sweetness slathers onto the middle palate with caramel that develops into toffee and sorghum as its sweetness slowly relaxes and the earthen toastiness of the ale rises. Coffee, cocoa and walnut all offer their robust bitternesses while the hops join in with their own broad woodsy taste.

With the ale closing with a long and malty-sweet linger, its medium-full body remains full and lush. While the whimsical thoughts of s'mores come to mind, the wood spices from walnut make their mark with a supple dryness to match that of the alcohol. Walnut Brown Ale is simply what Newcastle Nut Brown Ale wants to be when its all grown up.

BEERchitect, Sep 02, 2015
3.58/5  rDev +2%
look: 3.75 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

Ale aged on oak with walnuts. Dark reddish-brown pour with about a finger of tan head. Faint nose (common for this style). Some toasted malt, bread, and a touch of vanilla.

Mild flavor and medium bodied. Mouthfeel is firm, but a bit flat as well. Some good malty toffee-like goodness up front, but then the beer just kind of fizzles out. Flavors are missing completely at mid-palate, then come back a bit on the finish. A little bit of an herbal hop note along with a hint of walnuts arrive at the back of the palate and linger briefly.

This is not a bad brown ale. In my opinion, any failings of this beer are the result of the limitations of the style. I realize not every beer can be a monster and that brown ales are, by their nature, mellow and mild. This brewer clearly put a lot of thought into this beer. The flavors of the oak, walnuts, and toasted malt go well together, but there just isn't enough there. If I was a brewer in today's craft beer market, I wouldn't even waste my time brewing a brown ale unless it was a big one. If this brewer took this beer and made it really beefy (like Triple Digit's ``Chickow!'' brown ale), they would have something special.
