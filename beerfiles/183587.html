Dragginballs76, Dec 05, 2015
2.94/5  rDev -11.2%
look: 3.25 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 2.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 2.75

Poured from bottle into a pint glass

Appearance The beer pours a deep copper-golden color with a one finger head of off white foam. The head fades relatively quickly leaving just a little bit of foamy lace on the sides of the glass.

Smell The beer has a big smell of chai spice with cardamom, cinnamon, clove, and coriander all there and quite strong. Along with these aromas comes some smells of a bit of caramel and some aromas of a more bready lightly yeasty smell.

Taste The taste begins with a super chai spicy taste with the spices detected in the nose (cardamom, cinnamon, clove and coriander) hitting the tongue. There are some malty tastes of a bready nature upfront as well and with some sweeter caramel and brown sugar which manage to meld well with the spice. As the taste advances however, the spice (especially the cinnamon) increases, all while the sweeter and malty flavors dwindle. With these transitions that occur in the taste to the end, one is left with a very spicy chai, and rather unbalanced taste to linger on the tongue.

Mouthfeel The body of the brew is on the slightly thinner side with a carbonation level that is on the slightly higher side. For the heavy chai spice flavors of the brew the feel is somewhat appropriate, helping to calm the massive spice a little bit.

Overall The brew is way too spicy without and lacks a nice balance of malt and sweet to balance it out. The big spice really takes away from the overall enjoyment. I have to say however, I am not a chai tea fan, so if you are a chai tea drinker then you may actually enjoy this one

lwillitz, Sep 15, 2015
2.57/5  rDev -22.4%
look: 3.75 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 1.75 | feel: 1.75 | overall: 2.25

This beer hits the glass with a mostly clear body that is an orange-copper in color. A small to medium sized heads forms and is around just a little while and leaves just a touch of lace. Even when you open the bottle you can smell the spices. There is obviously a ton of chai right up front, combining cinnamon, cardamom, and just the slightest hint of coffee. The scent finishes with just a slight touch of pepper.

Underwhelming is the best way to describe this beer after it has hit your tongue. After the relatively decadent scent, the flavors translate to the tongue in a watered down, half-strength version of themselves. The first flavor on the tongue is a mashup of all the flavors from the scent that is light and thin in comparison. The spices really ramp up in the middle of the drink and overtake the other flavors, leaving a cardamom-cinnamon combo dominating the tongue. There is really no lingering flavor on the tongue as it washes away. As I mentioned in the taste, this beer is very watery and light on the palate.

Overall, a pretty good scent on this beer doesn't translate to the taste of the beer...at all. Don't be fooled by the enticing nose of the beer, the flavors on the tongue leave you scratching your head.

Was lucky enough to find a single of this instead of having to buy the whole seasonal case.

A : Rosy, peach iced tea colored body with a short lived 2 finger head. Slight mossiness on top from the head.

S : Rich clove, cinnamon, with a sharp chai spiciness and cooked rice. The nose on this is actually pretty impressive for being a beer. I know I couldn't make a beer smell like Chai.

T : Melon-like fruitiness, cinnamon, and slight hop spiciness with again the cooked rice and some clove. Some tea bitterness on the backside, along with black pepper and more of the faint melon.

F : Very light bodied, lots of carbonation and just a hint of creaminess. It ends with the sizzle of black pepper on the tip of the tongue.

O : It's not bad, but not fantastic. A typical Blue Moon offering. I'd drink it again, wouldn't have a problem helping someone get rid of them.
