Margarita meets gin & tonic with Grimm's exploritory sour ale that celebrates the salinity of northern German "salt" beer and the lime and pine character of gin for a combination of tastes that are highly agreeable together in the glass.

Super Spruce pours the pale canary-yellow hue and modestly hazed look of traditional gose, along with a short frothy sheet. Its scent is chocked full of citrus and spruce with tangy cider and white wine tingle on the nose. Supple sweetness greets the palate with light grain and wheat in shortbread fashion.

Crisp, clean and sharply lactic, the beer's overcast of sourness quickly overtakes the malt and brings a host of lemon, lime, crabapple and sour white grape to the middle palate. Playful saltiness brings a brisk seawater character while giving a robut and juicy fruit flavor. Sprinkled with coriander and a heavy dose of spruce, the beer's high juniper berry closes the taste with muddled sappy pine needles.

Light, crisp and clean, the beer's efforless body and piquant sourness becomes a highly drinkable, thrist quenching affair that promotes a quick rate of consumption. Brisk acidity washes the palate and closes with an echo of spruce and lime.

i am a sucker for all things spruce, so with such an authentic pine tree flavor in here, i have to just fawn over it. what an amazing beer this is, a perfect but entirely original additional ingredient in a very well made base beer. hazy pale, the spruce doesnt change the color, it looks the part, very active carbonation. the nose here is fine too, spruce and lemon and coriander, herbaceous and like the springtime, not overpowering, but still bright. the flavor is similar, definitely led by the spruce, but not dominated by it. its obvious and readily identifiable, but not one dimensional in any way at all. the saltiness and the spruce really work together flawlessly, and there is a nice big fluffy wheat base for these flavors to ride home. moderate tang to this, but its not particularly sour. i appreciate these days a sour style of beer that is still very drinkable, and this one most certainly is. the spruce is amazingly fresh, and it seems to love this new canvass of a beer style, as is creeps and grows into every pocket of aroma and flavor and feel, while still being respectful and aware that it is a guest here. impressive is an understatement. refreshing too, dry and well carbonated, these guys are killing it right now. one of the most unique spruce beers i have ever had.

Drank from a 1 pt. 6 fl. oz. bottle purchased at Cambridge Wine & Spirits, Cambridge, MA
January 2016
Served in a tulip

A tight, fluffy, two-finger tall, bone white crown topped the hazy, bright gold liquid. The head fell to a small cap. Patchy lacing trickled down the glass. With time, the chill haze eased up to reveal a nearly clear body.

The nose was really interesting and very unique for the style. The first thing hit me were the hops. They were really bright, fruity and exotic. Overripe tropical fruits came to mind. Passionfruit dominated. Some guava too. Touch of candy-like sweetness. Hint of mustiness. There was a dry, powdery, yeasty quality as well. Lemon and lime notes came through big time as the beer warmed. The mustiness increased significantly as well.

The flavor profile was more typical for the style but still impressive. Loads of tart lemon and lime notes. The beer was anchored by a crackery, pale malt base. Bit of candy-like sweetness. Big dose of salinity on the back end. This reminded me a lot of a margarita. Spices came through as well. Touch of coriander. Slightly floral. In addition to the lemon and lime, there was a slightly funky, bretty tartness. The beer finished with intense lemon and lime rind-like notes and an acidity level that fell just short of puckering. Beautifully balanced.

Nice crisp, fluffy, frothy mouthfeel. The liquid contained a lively, spritzy effervescence. Spot-on for the style.

Though I picked up absolutely no spruce tip or barrel notes, this was still an excellent gose. As the warmer weather months approach, I would love to revisit this.
