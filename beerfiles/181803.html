Sabtos, Oct 16, 2016
4.36/5  rDev +13%
look: 4 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.25

Poured form a bottle into a shaker pint glass.

L: Gorgeous dark brown coffee color. A few bubbles. One finger of light tan head that dissipated quickly; very little residual lacing.

S: Sweet coffee and chocolate with the slightest hint of caramel.

T: Purists might criticize the taste, as it more closely resembles a milk stout, but (since I love stouts) I found it to be downright delicious. Coffee (obviously), cocoa, and caramel tastes prevail, and the milky sweetness lingers. Further, it doesn't taste like a beer a 7% alcohol content.

F: Creamy and full--a small sip feels like a mouthful. Not as hoppy and effervescent as most ales. A faint malty feel lingers.

O: It's not what I thought it would be, as the sweet creamy character is much more like that a of a milk stout, but I found it to be very pleasant. I will absolutely buy it again.

akolb, Oct 09, 2016
3.66/5  rDev -5.2%
look: 3.5 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

I picked up a bottle of Alaskan Brewing's Heritage Coffee Ale from their Pilot series a couple weeks ago for $1.69 at The Lager Mill. I'm in the mood for a coffee beer today and I'm interested to see what they can do with this one, so lets see how it goes. 2016 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a imperial pint glass.

A- The label looks nice, it's got a nice design to it and it's a little eye catching. It poured a fairly clear crimson brown color that took on more of a copper hue when held to the light and it had a fingers worth of creamy lighter tan head that died down to a thin ring that eventually faded away and it didn't leave much lacing behind. This is a nice looking beer, but I wish it had better head retention and lacing.

S- The aroma starts off with a higher amount of medium sweetness with the coffee being the first to show up and it just a little bit on the light side and it imparts more of a creamy black coffee like aroma with a those sweet malts really coming through to make for a sweet coffee. Up next comes the brown ale base which is noticeable and it imparts some nice caramel, toasted malts and nutty aromas. This beer has a pretty nice aroma, the balance is pretty good.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma and it starts off with a lower amount of high sweetness with the coffee being the first to show up and it's still a little on the lighter side like it was in the aroma, but this time more of those caramel flavors mixed it to give it a flavored iced coffee like flavor. Up next comes more sweetness along with the brown ale base shows up enough to give it some really nice balance and it imparts the same aspects with those nice nutty aspects coming through just enough. This is a pretty nice beer, i really like the balance, but the sweetness is a little too high for me.

M- Smooth, slightly crisp, a little creamy, a little cloying, on the thicker side of being medium bodied with a lower amount of medium carbonation. The mouthfeel is nice, but i wish it was a little less sweet.

Overall I thought this was a nice beer and solid example of the style, but it seemed to be just a little rough around the edges and that held it back from being as good as it could be. This beer has ok drinkability, one is enough for me, it would get too sweet for me if I have more than one. What I like the most about this beer is the balance, it's the highlight of this beer and it's really nice. What I like the least about this beer is the sweetness, it needs to be toned down a little to make it more drinkable. I might buy this beer again and I would recommend it if someone wants a coffee beer on the sweeter side. All in all liked this beer, but i didn't love it and it was the sweetness that held it back. It's not a favorite Alaskan beer and it's a solid example of the style. Nice job guys this is a well balanced, beer keep it up.
