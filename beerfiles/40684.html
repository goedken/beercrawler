look: 3.5 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

reviewed from notes, this one was serve in a pint glass during a magic hat tasting i had with my brother in law down at his apartment.

The pour was decent. A nice ruby tinted amber color coming through, with a nice caramel like hint to it. Some light touches of frothy white head on the top as well as a good bit of carbonation coming up to the surface throughout the session.

The aroma was a malt bomb and this was evident really from the very first sip. But the nose was leading that way as well. Hints of caramel, some bready touches and a musky sort of aroma were dominating the day here. Nice touches of earthy hops coming through as well, but very light, more of an accent then anything. The flavor started out with a nice rush of caramel malts, syrup like in the feel almost with some soothing carbonation underlying it but not enough to push it off the map. Nice bready and biscuit like mid palate was then replaced with a somewhat chewy and earthy hop in the finish, before melding back into the sweet profile that held me at the beginning.

Overall not bad at all for the style, a little thick to really be a session able beer here as I found the carbonation a touch lacking, but the flavor was pretty darn good and I would have no issues with trying this one again any day.

It's gone. It's gone. It's long gone. Magic Hat makes great beers when they try.

Odd Notion takes the cake as my favorite variety of Magic Hat. Here's the breakdown.

Appearance: Deep, rich Irish red with a 2 finger tan head. I wish i could post of pic of my glass held up to light. With the sunlight passing through it, it appears almost crimson. It's a sight to behold for the beer enthusiast.

Smell: Malty, with some sort of flavor that I don't think I've run across before. Quite unique.

Taste: The malts are the dominant force here, with the hops and added flavorings taking a backseat to them. Again, there's something there that I can't exactly put my finger on, and I like it a lot.

Mouthfeel and Drinkability: Its thick, but not too thick, and its heavy, but not too heavy. Magic Hat Odd Notion strikes a phenomenal balance, and I could definitely session these if only I could find them outside of a variety pack.

Overall, one of my favorite beers. The variety pack containing them has been on sale at my local Liquor store (State Liquors, Worcester MA) for some time now, and I'm on my fifth or sixth twelver. If you see this around, pick it up. You won't regret it.

Medium brown color, capped with a thin coating of beige head. Holds a thin collar, but no lacing.

Medium bodied, with mellow carbonation. Very much caramel malt driven, with a good bit of roast. The ginger carries all the way through, lasting long into the finish.
I'm not sure if this was the most enjoyable beer I've ever had, but it certainly is different. Very glad that Magic Hat is still doing strange, off beat things. However, I don't think I could drink more than one bottle in a sitting.
