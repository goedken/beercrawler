12 oz. bottle, bottled 04/13/16 stamped on the label. Another new one to me from Odd Side Ales; acquired a single bottle of this stuff from the local bottle shop. This one a Dry Hopped Lager. No abv listed on the bottle anywhere.

Poured to a Unibroue Maudite snifter.

A - Pours a pale straw yellow body, with lots of chill haze. Semi-translucent; hazy. Good finger of frothy white head that stays a while, leaving nice scattered fluffy lacing. Apt carbonation.

S - Aroma comes off dry and funky, initially. Smells very yeasty, with notes of earthiness, grass, hay, citrus & lemon / orange peel.

T - Taste follows the nose: lots of dry grassy, herbal hops, light notes of funky yeast, and notes of citrus fruit - orange, mango, lemon zest. Very interesting stuff.

M - Feel is smooth, crisp, clean, fluid. Semi-dry & bready finish, with good lingering herbal / piney hop bitterness. Feels very fresh & floral. Light-bodied with good carbonation; sessionable stuff.

Overall, a unique and flavorful dry hopped lager from Odd Side Ales. Very enjoyable stuff.

Odd Side Ales Chillindamos -----4/5.

OddSide Ales Chillindamos.

Y'know, some beers just catch your eye. A dry hopped...lager? Lmfao. But, at a buck 'o nine a single I figured I could take the risk on it without too many complaints. Even if it ends up sucking I didn't lose much :).

Look - Pours a murky honey comb golden with a three finger bubbly white head that actually sticks around and pays more than its security deposits. Carbonation appears to be pretty damn high, but no big deal for the style. Lacing is spotty although noticeable. Lacing in a lager...never though I'd see the day. 4.25.

Smell - Wow...you mean a lager that actually smells like some work went into it?! dry mosaic hops without a touch of some ridiculous cheap adjunct garbage?! Unheard of! Until now. 4.25.

Taste - Yep those Mosaic hops just keep on rolling in. Very floral and a touch bitter with a orange peel like aspect though nowhere near overwhelming like a lot of brews. No sign of any lager esque garbage. Tastes more like a dry-hopped pale ale. 4.25.

Feel - Body is actually moderate though with the usual amount of large carbonation. Sits very easy and swallows even easier. Creamy as well due to the retained head. 4.25.

Overall - If this is truly a lager...It's the best damn one I have ever had . Certainly on par, if not better than Lagunitas DayTime and a few other dry hopped Pale ales I have had. A highly session able easy drinker. Really shows what the ' big guys ' cut corners on to turn out maximum swill at minimal costs. Hell, even beats out Mosaic Promise by a slim margin. Realllyyyyy makes me wonder about some of the shit I read on bottles these days... at this point, combined with all the other brews I've ha out of them, OddSide are some solid mofos. 4.25..

Yikes -- bottled 2/23 and poured 7/16. I mean I guess that's not horrendously old, but I likely wouldn't have purchased it if I saw that before I got it. $2.19 single poured into a, all boo, shaker pint.

Appearance: Hard pour into the glass, this is a classic looking lager with a haze. Yellow with a finger of a bright white soapy head on top. Very little lacing. Well, it passes the first test. I also love the name of this beer... Chillindamos I'm assuming is a reference to one of few Kid Rock songs I like, "Cowboy."

Smell: Gentle floral hop notes coming from the glass -- also a little, for the lack of a better word, powdery. Classic lager biscuity aroma is there underneath. Really a delicate smelling beer: modest, clean, and makes me thirsty.

Taste/feel: Not what I was looking for, though I don't know what the ABV of this beer is, so I'm a bit handcuffed. If I had to guess, I'd say 4.5%-5%. This is just a decent lager. The bits of hoppiness I got in the aroma are basically all gone in the flavor and are only really traceable at the end, where there's a bit of a bitterness. The lager base is alright, if lacking a little flavor. It's not as "crackery" as I'd like and it just doesn't have a ton of flavor. It is drinkable though, and what's there definitely isn't bad. The feel of Chillindamos is pleasant, with nearly a full body, surprisingly. Carbonation is about moderate with a slow prickling carbonation.

I'm confident that this beer's hops have faded more than Odd Side would like, but it's still a pretty tasty lager that warrants a purchase.

strohme2, Apr 02, 2015
3.78/5  rDev +5.6%
look: 3.75 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 4 | overall: 3.75

I picked up a bottle of Oddside's Chillindamos last week for $1.89 at The Lager Mill. I enjoy a nice hoppy Lager every now and then and I'm interested to see how this one turns out, so lets see how it goes. 2015 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a imperial pint glass.

A- The label looks nice, it goes with the name and it's slightly eye catching. It poured a hazy yellow color that takes on more of a golden color when held to the light with almost a finger and a half worth of creamy white head that died down to a thin ring that stayed till the end and it left some patchy lacing. This is a pretty nice looking beer, but it's a little hazy for a Lager.

S- The aroma starts off with a low amount of medium sweetness with the hops being the first to show up and they impart a nice mixture of fruity and spicy hops that seem to be a pretty even mixture. Up next comes a slight yeasty aroma followed by some malts with some pale malts, grainy and a slight bit of sweet malt like aromas. This beer has a pretty nice aroma, but I wish the fruity hops showed up a little more.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma and it starts off with a lower amount of medium sweetness with the hops being the first to show up and they impart the same mixture of hops that they did in the aroma and the fruity hops seems to show up slightly more than the spicy hops this time, but as a whole they are a little lighter than they were in the aroma. Up next comes that clean lager yeast that goes right into the malts which don't show up as much as they did in the aroma, but the impart the same aspects with the pale malts seeming to stick out the most. On the finish there's hardly any bitterness with a hoppy aftertaste. This is a pretty nice tasting beer, the hops add some nice flavor and hardly any bitterness which I think suites this Lager well.

M- Very smooth, a little clean, crisp, on the thick side of being light bodied with a medium amount of carbonation. This beer has a good mouthfeel that works well with it.

Overall I thought this was a pretty nice beer and I thought it was a good example of the style, the hop profile was nice and I think dry hopping is the way to go when it comes to Lagers, I find the bitterness can quickly overpower them and the dry hopping lets the hop flavors shine without the bitterness coming through, This beer has good drinkability it's very smooth, a little clean, crisp, not filling and the taste does a nice job at holding my attention, I could drink a few of these, especially in the summertime. What I like the most about this beer was the hop profile, it was pretty nice and it was just what this beer needed. What I like the least about this beer is that the fruity hops didn't show up a little more in the taste and aroma. I would buy this beer again when in the mood for the style and I would recommend trying it if you like Lagers. All in all I was happy with this beer, I really think that more breweries should take this approach when it comes to Lagers, I think dry hopping works great with the style. It's not my favorite Oddsides beer, but it is up there as one of the better hoppy lagers I've had. Good job guys this was a tasty beer, keep it up.

12 ounce bottle into lager glass, bottled on 2/23/2015. Pours slightly hazy golden straw color with a 1 finger dense white head with good retention, that reduces to a small cap that lingers. Nice spotty soapy lacing clings down the glass, with a fair amount of streaming carbonation. Aromas of big pineapple, grapefruit, tangerine, peach, mango, passion fruit, melon, lemon zest, orange peel, berry, cracker, light honey, floral, and herbal/grassy earthiness. Damn nice aromas with good balance and complexity of bright citrus/tropical hops and light pale malt notes; with good strength. Taste of pineapple, grapefruit, tangerine, peach, mango, passion fruit, melon, lemon zest, orange peel, berry, cracker, light honey, floral, and herbal//grassy earthiness. Fair amount of herbal/grassy bitterness on the finish; with lingering notes of pineapple, peach, mango, lemon zest, melon, berry, cracker, light honey, and herbal/grassy earthiness on the finish for a short bit. Damn nice complexity and balance of bright citrus/tropical hops and light-moderate pale malt flavors; with a great malt/bitterness balance and zero cloying flavors after the finish. Medium carbonation and light-medium bodied; with a very smooth, crisp, and slightly sticky mouthfeel that is great. Alcohol is well hidden with zero warming present after the finish as expected of 4.5%. Overall this is an excellent hoppy pale lager style. All around great complexity and balance of bright citrus/tropical hops and light-moderate pale malt flavors; and very smooth and crisp to drink. A very enjoyable offering.
