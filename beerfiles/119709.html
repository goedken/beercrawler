New England's worst kept secret has gotten out! Encroaching on Pennsylvania, their north east IPA that's ridiculously hazy, premierely fruity, tantalizingly juicy and creamy, and then shockingly smooth with bitterness now graces the brewhouse of Brew Gentlemen.

Absurdly turbid, the beer's shocking opaque gold renders its appearance akin to tapioca, with perhaps extra pulp. But with one supple sniff, the beer instantly wins over the senses. Juicy with a citrus and tropical fruit medley, an exotic bouquet wraps around a fresh grassy, herbal scent and begs a quick first sip. Just as juicy as the fragrance suggests, a rounded fructose-like sweetness with angel-food cake, light honey and dough sweeps the front of the tongue.

And the beer simply blossoms from there. White grapefruit, under-ripened orange, light lemon all roll gently into a tropical punch taste from pineapple, mango, passionfruit and papaya. While a drift of melon, kiwi, nectarine and lime come and go, the mind can't help from going to creamsicle or sherbet-like evocation. Smooth bitterness ensues and the ale dries and its grip on the finish is soft with parsley, fresh grass clippings, muddled chive and a kiss of cannabis as the beer flutters into finish.

Creamy, medium and smooth, the beer's patient separation of foam from ale in the appearance manifests in texture as well. Juicy, fluffy and yet with an arid sensation, the beer covers remarkable distance smoothly, softly and completely; en route to a malty-dry finish with a coaxing resinous grip. Sensual, firm and dry, General Braddock certainly has a way with the tastebuds.

On tap at Brew Gentlemen in Braddock, PA.

This one pours a super hazy golden orange, with a small head, and a good amount of lacing.

Smells like pineapple, mango, and grapefruit, with a slight bit of malt, and some pine.

This is firmly in the NE-style IPA tradition. This has a firm bitterness, that provides a minimum of balance to the huge juicy blast up front- nectarine, peach, and mango, with a hint of grapefruit. There's maybe a tiny bit of malt, but it's barely noticeable.

This has super soft and juicy mouthfeel, with a good level of carbonation. It's again quite drinkable.

This is the best beer I had from Brew Gentlemen on my visit. It's almost all that I want in a NE-style IPA.

750ml growler, 4 days old. Awesome. Served in a willi becher glass.

Pours a hazy, super high attenuation and cloudy golden yellow body, one and a half finger white head with great retention time. Thick, effervescent head with lots of lacing. Looks really nice and turbid. Smells of pineapple, grapefruit, light cereal grain, touch of malt sweetness, but overwhelmingly hoppy in the NE tradition. Flavour is a bit of grapefruit, more of pineapple and papaya; light malt sweetness makes a half-hearted attempt to balance things out, and it's just enough. Lots of juicy tropical hops here, with just the minimal amount of malt. Light zesty bitterness. Mouthfeel is medium bodied, creamy, smooth without much prickliness but plenty of carbonation. Overall, a very nice execution of the NE IPA paradigm, as far as non-NE region NE-style goes, this is pretty similar to Narrow Gauge in St Louis, and not that far off from the likes of Trillium and Tree House. Queue the hype...

These guys are making some amazing beer in Braddock PA, I hope everyone in western Pennsylvania knows how lucky they are to have a young brewery of this caliber in their neck of the woods.

This is a big juicy hazy tropical east coast IPA. The kind you could drink for days that turn into years and never grow tired of. Pours the color of grapefruit juice, lightly carbonated with excellent lacing. The best of any Brew Gentlemen beer I've had.

Aromas of orange, a faint touch of lemon, and lots of peach and ripe mango. So well balanced, no alcohol heat at all, malts are soft and bready but definitely in the background. Some of what they're doing seems heavily influenced by Tree House in Monson MA, which is comparing them to one of the absolute best breweries in the north east. I have a new can't miss spot on my next trip to Pittsburgh.
