Enjoyed this at the Armsby Abbey Stoutfest. Reviewed from notes.

A - Poured into a pint glass a hazy yellow with a one finger creamy white head that fades slowly to leave sticky lacing and a great foamy blanket.

S - Aromas of super sticky candy hops. This seriously smells like a brew day! Wort, grain, hop leaves. Really, really never thought Cascade hops could smell like this, or that a brewer could get this type of aroma into a glass. Shocking.

T - Taste is more of the same. Really didn't think cascade could translate like this. More of the great grain build, lightly floral hops, orange zest, pale bitterness. This is the epitome of a brew day.

M - Mouthfeel is smooth, lightly carbonated, fluffy, juicy.

O - Overall this was a knee buckler. This beer deserves MUCH higher marks, however, I can understand why some would rate it so low. The smell and taste of cascade hops aren't what's "in" right now. Everyone wants super tropical citra, or excessively bitter...and this beer is none of that. This beer smells, and tastes, like what it is like to brew an APA. Go to a brewery on brew day, inhale deeply, what you smell IS this beer. Masterfully done.

Serving Type: On tap

Hill Farmstead Cascade

Three Penny Taproom draft. Full notes taken May 22, 2015.

A- Slightly hazy golden yellow bodied pour from draught to pint glass. A small white layer but no head, seems like a glass issue. Large lace webs despite head issues.

S- Cascade hop aroma with a backdrop of grainy yeast bread and pale barley. Oranges, floral, herbal and general citrus.

T- Floral and citrus makes up much of the flavor from those Cascade hops. Oranges, big tangerine, flower meadows, lemon zest, herbal tones, pale cracked cereal grain, yeast bread and mineral water hints.

MF- Creamy and puffy light-medium body that foams up quite a bit to be airy. Carbonation is at a medium level and bitterness is smooth and on the lower end.

Floral and citrus Cascade profile exemplified in a smooth and easy drinking Pale. A nice break from the tropical juice that has become so popular.

On tap at the Positive Pie in Montpelier.

This is probably the least assertive HF beer I've ever had. It might sound like a strange complaint, but I was really expecting this to taste more like Cascade hops. Which isn't to say that it doesn't taste like Cascade--it does--but it's surprisingly malt heavy. The result is a super smooth and drinkable IPA with a flavor profile that's stable throughout.

This would be an excellent intro IPA, and were it readily available around where I live it would be a go-to session IPA. So it's very good, but just not "OMG I have to trade for that"-level good.

Acquired 3 hours ago. Poured in Hill Farmstead stemware. Split with angrybabboon.

Appearance: Hazy bright orange-gold. A bit more orange than is typical for the HF pale ale. Head is white, fluffy, and creamy. Moderate retention leaves a few clumps up cap with a loose creamy collar. Sticky splotches of lacing. Minimal to moderate carbonation on the pour.

Smell: Cascade as expected. Grapefruit pith and juice tempered by a solid floral push. A minimal hop spiciness in the back as well. Malts decidedly bready with some biscuit dough and a soft bit of cracker. Warming adds something that almost says marmalade. Leafy and clean hop profile. Some Hill Farmstead ale yeast runs through the back adding a soft powdery yeast profile. Slight bitterness rounds it out with some mild woody elements.

Taste: Bitter up front. Snaps to straight Cascade almost immediately. Loads of grapefruit pith, zest, and juice. Deeply floral with dried flower petal. Slight hop spiciness creeps through the back. Malt backbone is slightly sweet with some caramelized malt. A touch of marmalade. Biscuit dough - slightly undercooked - and white bread. Some orange pith as well.

Mouthfeel: Medium body with a moderate carbonation. A touch of astringency. Typical slight fluffy creaminess on the initial sip.

Overall: A case study in Cascade.

Jamming on Cascade is a good thing. Fill it. Crush it. Repeat.
