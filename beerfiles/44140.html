Appearance:
Incredibly clear, deep bronze body. Fluffy, bone white head hangs around for a minute before fading away to a simple ring around the glass. Light lacing and carbonation.

Aroma:
Piercing sweetness hits upfront, but there is plenty going on here! Toffee, toasted malts, brown sugar, raisins and other dark fruits. This one definitely pleases the nose!

Taste:
The sweetness is present here too, but brings some light grassy hop bitterness with it to balance things out.. and I love it! Tart cherries, burnt biscuits, and plenty of malty goodness. That second one might seem weird (and it does) but it works here! Notes of figs and caramel sneak in every now and then. Light, burnt character on the finish with a bit of alcohol presence.

Mouthfeel:
Plenty of bubbly and on the lighter side of medium bodied. Semi-sweet finish.

Overall:
I have thoroughly enjoyed everything I have had from this small brewery located in Saint Paul, and Ovni Ale is no exception; it's great! Easy to drink, plenty of character, and refreshing. If you find yourself in Minnesota, do yourself a favor and seek this one (and any Flat Earth beer for that matter) out, you won't be disappointed!

Derek, Aug 08, 2009
3.47/5  rDev -9.2%
look: 3 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

Ovni is an acronym and is French for 'objet volant non-identifie', which is the equivalent of UFO, or 'unidentified flying object' in English. See the Ovni label for related fun facts about flying saucers, mysterious electrical discharges and the Eiffel Tower.

Ferric oxide orange with bright pumpkin edges. It appears as though this biere de garde is filtered, which is an odd design choice if you ask me. The beige colored crown is nowhere near attractive, especially for a Belgian/French-style ale. The rapidly shrinking foam is mealy and unsticky, leaving the glass virtually untouched. Not an impressive start.

The aroma gets things back on track because it fits the style pretty well. The beer smells toasted-earthy (Caramunich is one member of the malt bill), sour fruity and a touch spicy. The latter is probably due to yeast esters rather than added spices.

Ovni Ale does not put me in mind of the world's best bieres de garde. Nor of the country's best. It's significantly tart, more so than some lambics. Granted, the bottle is approaching one year of age. On the other hand, a well-brewed 7.1% biere de garde should be able to go three years at least. And it should improve with age.

The choice of malts isn't to my liking, but it isn't a deal-breaker. There's too much toasted-earthy-nutty caramel flavor. I feel like I'm drinking a sour bock. The fruitiness is akin to little green apples and lemon juice. Sweat sock funk is low-medium, hops are essentially AWOL and alcohol is barely noted.

This isn't bad beer at all, it just doesn't come together as well as it might have. It's hard to believe that much has changed since bottling, so it's doubtful that a fresh bottle would be significantly better. Just not my cup of tea, I guess.

The mouthfeel tries its damndest, but can't quite get over the hump. Abundant bubbles are fine if they result in a smooth creaminess. The beer is acceptable in that regard, but the carbonation is too sharp and biting; not well-integrated like it should be.

Ovni Ale is the first Flat Earth beer that is less than solid. The flavor profile is too tart and too scattershot. Some unifying theme, some flavor focus, would do wonders. Sorry guys, but this flying saucer of a biere de garde fails to reach orbit when compared to the style's best.
