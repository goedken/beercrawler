Brewed by:

stonebrewing.com

Style: Belgian IPA

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 8.90%

Availability: Limited (brewed once)

Notes / Commercial Description:
In 2008, Stone Brewmasters Steve Wagner and Mitch Steele traveled to Belgium, where they enjoyed a number of hop-forward Belgian tripels, pale ales and IPAs—beers that had taken a cue from the U.S. craft brewing movement and inspired us in return. It’s a big reason 2008’s summer season came to be known at Stone as our “Summer of the Triple.” Upon their return, they crafted Stone 08.08.08 Vertical Epic Ale, the seventh beer in the 11-beer Stone Vertical Epic Ale series. Packed with floral, spicy essence from Belgian yeast plus plenty of hoppy New World character, it’s our West Coast take on hop-driven Belgian beers.

Added by ClavisAurea on 09-26-2015

Poured into a Gulden Draak tulip. Pours a medium orangey gold amber, with a one finger off-white head with good retention and lots of lacing. Aroma of Belgian yeast, apple juice, light stone fruit, coriander and light citrus hops with characteristics of both tripel and Belgian IPA. Flavor is light stone fruit, apple, Belgian yeast, coriander, drying out to finish with a significant spice bitterness with light citrus hops. Medium bodied with a pleasant creaminess. An interesting hybrid of tripel and IPA styles, with the tripel spicing and Belgian base character dominating the hops. The finish is bitter from both the spicing and hops with the coriander (and clove?) seeming to provide the bulk of bitterness, but with great residual fruit flavors to support. Nicely done. I almost didn't pick this up as I had tried it before; I see now that I only reviewed this during my half vertical in '12 (I was able to try aged '06-'12), so it was great to try this fresh. I liked the aged one, and like this as well. The tripel character is stronger in this fresh bottle.
