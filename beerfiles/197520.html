12 ounce bottle into pint glass, bottled on 11/18/2015. Pours slightly hazy/cloudy reddish brown color with a 1-2 finger fairly dense light khaki head with good retention, that reduces to a small cap that lingers. Light spotty soapy lacing clings down the glass, with a moderate amount of streaming carbonation retaining the cap. Aromas of caramel, brown sugar, toasted brown bread, nuttiness, light chocolate/cocoa, herbal, grass, light pepper, and roast/toasted earthiness. Nice and pleasant aromas with good balance and complexity of dark/roast/bready malt and light-moderate earthy hop notes; with solid strength. Taste of big caramel, brown sugar, toasted brown bread, nuttiness, light chocolate/cocoa, herbal, grass, light pepper, and roast/toasted earthiness. Light herbal/spicy hop and roast bitterness on the finish; with lingering notes of caramel, brown sugar, toasted brown bread, nuttiness, light chocolate/cocoa, herbal, grass, light pepper, and roast/toasted earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Very nice complexity, robustness, and balance of dark/roast/bready malt and light-moderate earthy hop flavors; with a great malt/bitterness balance and zero cloying flavors after the finish. Light dryness from hop/roast bitterness as well. Medium carbonation and body; with a very smooth, moderately creamy/bready, and lightly sticky/chalky balanced mouthfeel that is nice. Alcohol is very well hidden with zero warming present after the finish as expected of 5.3%. Overall this is a very good American brown ale style. All around good complexity, robustness, and balance of dark/roast/bready malt and light-moderate earthy hop flavors; and very smooth and easy to drink. A very enjoyable offering.

arizcards, Jan 31, 2016
3.85/5  rDev +4.1%
look: 4 | smell: 4 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 4 | overall: 3.75

Goose Island Winter Ale.

It almost doesn't feel right drinking winter beers this year seeing as here in South East Michigan we haven't received shit for snow and it's probably about 40 out right now. I remember back when I started into the craft world, there was an odd though pleasant feeling surrounding drinking anything wintery during a bout of some falling snow. Let's hope it isn't too late in the year to still receive. Anyway, Goose Island. I've only had a handful of beers outta these guys and I wasn't impressed, even before they sold out or whatever the hell the recent spike in hate is for. Let's drink it.

Look - From the twelve ounce bottle hard poured into a pint glass. I can't believe it, a brown ale that actually pours a true shade of brown. Go figure. Yeah the beer pours a darker brown with a finger and a half of light mocha esque head that dies down to a thick ring of froth amongst the outer edges and leaves a thick cloudy cap upon the top of the swill. While light penetrates, it is still hard to read the carbonation level. No lacing or stick amongst a finished glass.

Smell - As you can often come to expect out of a brown ale there is a high level of nut. Kind of hazelnut like in nature though partially roasted over an open fire it would seem. The promised chocolate notes are also there. It isn't a complex nose but it is pleasant.

Taste - It follows the nose for the most part. As expected the nuts remain quite dominant though the chocolate does turn up. There is a fruity like sweetness going on as the beer has warmed up a bit. Almost like an almond Hershey bar perhaps with some kind of cherry filling. The chocolate aspects are definitely on the darker side since milk chocolate seems to be pretty rare in my experience, just thought I should mention that ;).

Feel - The body is on about the lower side of thick though seemingly just moderate on some sips with a moderate amount of carbonation. The flavors remain all the same though the sweetness clearly turns up a touch more. Swallows easy and the brew is exceptionally drying.

Overall - This was a proper brown ale that I feel would have done better without the sweetness. This would have likely scored 4s across the board had it not been for that. But oh well. Not much of a winter beer, more or less for those cool fall nights that dip into the forties around a fire outside. As always, try it for yourself and come to your own conclusions..
