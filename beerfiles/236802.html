look: 3.75 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4

22oz Bomber
$10.49
Steve's Liquor (Mineral Point) - Madison, WI

Gose Gone Wild - Phuket is about as light golden as you can get. When backlit it takes on an almost green hue. A touch of haze runs through the body, nothing crazy. The foam is spritzy and pure white. It pops, crckles, and snaps on the pour but quickly fizzles down to nearly nothing after a handful of seconds. No head = no lace. Still, it looks good.

The nose picks up the little slack that the appearance displayed. It's mango off the bat, and that runs from start to finish, while a slightly earthy combo of chili and lemongrass join the fray as a mouthwatering compliment. The lemongrass brings with it a citric, almost Fruity Pebble like quality that I thoroughly enjoy. A quick swirl of the glass brings forward the soft wheat tones and a touch of citrus rind along with a welcomed lactic acidity . I really appreciate how each individual adjunct compliments the next.

It's amazing how well rounded, how well integrated the ingredients are in this beverage. It's bright and lemon forward off the bat and that's before the lemongrass even kicks in. The middle is where the mango, lemongrass and chilis show themselves. The chilis bringing a touch of earthiness but no heat. The wheat is a little on the damp side with a touch of acidity and the sea salt is noticeable a few glugs into the bottle.

The body is light and effervescent with carbonation a little on the prickly side. A soft acidity bites at the inside of the cheeks and a long dry finish helps accentuate that tartness. It's hugely drinkable and quite the thirst quencher, if I do say so myself.

I like the idea of these World Tour editions. The original was delicious and while the Phuket version doesn't quite live up to that beauty it sure is fun to drink and delicious to boot. Excellent use of the adjuncts in a way so as not to take from the original beer. I would recommend picking up a bottle of this if you run across one.

Drank from a 1 pint 6 fl. oz. (651 ml) bottle purchased at Craft Beer Cellar, Belmont, MA
Served in a stange

The ever so slightly hazed, pale straw yellow body was capped by a billowing, three-finger tall crop of fluffy, bright white froth. The head quickly fell to a film of tiny bubbles. A patchy ringlet of lacing trickled along the glass. As the beer sat, the haze dropped completely and the liquid turned a crystal clear, exceptionally pale yellow. On the second pour, some of the yeast sneaked into the glass and clouded the liquid.

The nose was really interesting. It was quite spicy with strong notes of coriander. There was a dry, musty, yeasty quality. Big passionfruit presence. Tropical and exotic. Touch of lemon peel. Slightly sour and also a touch sweet. The lemon intensified as the beer sat. This reminded me a bit of Italian lemon ice. After the beer was finished, I poured the dregs at the bottom of the bottle into my glass and it smelled strongly of mango.

The flavor profile was solid but definitely not as interesting as the nose. Sour lemon and lemon peel notes dominated. Lots of lime too. Touch of pithy, rind-like bitterness. Crisp and wheaty. Slightly salty on the back end. Some chili heat appeared in the finish. Initially, the heat was subtle but it became more noticeable with time. Pithy, dry, lemon rind notes came through as well.

Lighter, softer mouthfeel. Slightly watery. The liquid contained a subtle, larger bubbled carbonation. Wet and frothy on the palate.

I really enjoyed this "world tour" version of Gose Gone Wild, even though it was only slightly different from the original. Hopefully future iterations will be more unique.

Stevedore, Jul 27, 2016
4.25/5  rDev +1.9%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

With the acclaim of the Gose Gone Wild well in the rear view, it's high time that we dust off the recipe and tinker with it a bit. At least that's what Stillwater brewers think as they use the Thailand Phuket region as inspiration to the sour German-style wheat ale with additions of mango, lemongrass and Thai chilies.

Hazy canary-yellow in color, its foreshadows its acidic and sour tone that eventually defines the beer. But first, its foundation of bread, fresh-kilned grain and wheat take hold in a wafer-thin taste. But that's all short-lived as the sweetheart candy flavors, tropical mango and herbal grassiness all grow rapidly and provide a lemon and lime tang.

But tart turns to full sourness as the confectioner's sweetness rapidly dissolves, leaving the taste of lemon, lime, peach, mango and apricot to carry a burlap musty earthiness across the middle. Its palate-stripping crabapple and white grape vinous acidity is accompanied by the characteristic mineral and saline fullness. Together, a notion of sweat, spice and tin elevate the beer's sourness, its light peppery heat, and its yogurt-like lactic tartness.

Its finish is greatly crisp, clean and politely piquant; leaving the palate refreshed with a simmer of lemon, herb and verbena tartness. After a brief delay, an echo of dry wheat and mango revisit the palate deep in aftertaste.

Though many of the classic gose are long gone, comparisons to American attempts are few and far between. But with recent brewing advancements in place, Gose Gone Wild doesn't only compare favorably, it likely surpasses many of them in quality and crispness.
