Toasted nuts, spiciness, light-medium crystal grains, breadiness, raisins and earthiness come to mind.

MF- Puffy and silky with a hint of creaminess for the texture. The body itself is medium or a shade thicker than that and the finish is slightly bitter.

I'm just not sure how the blood oranges were work out here as there are clear toasty deeper colored grains than I'd expect to pair with citrus; thankfully not too sweet. Decent but the oranges seem to fade away after a few sips and the malts take over.

98green, Feb 14, 2016
3.47/5  rDev -0.6%
look: 3.5 | smell: 3.25 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.5

Dark Horse Rain in Blood.

Bought this one on suggestion from a new guy at one of my local craft shops. Not sure why it's labeled as retired on here since the bottled on date is 1/26/16. Nice homage to Slayer. Time to review a few beers.

Look -From the twelve ounce bottle hard poured into a dimpled pint glass. Brew is a darker orange mixed with just a touch of copper. The head stands about a finger and three quarters in height and is frothy and densely packed. Basically looks like an upside down liquid creamsicle of sorts. Carbonation appears to be about moderate as expected. Noticing the reviews aren't all that great some I'm hoping they likely changed the recipe and reintroduced the beer. Lacing and stick after a finished glass is moderate.

Smell - The nose is mostly dominated by a heavily fronted wheat aspect aspect and the orange definitely seems to be skin like instead of juicy in nature. No typical hop notes nor any detectable malts aside from what has already been mentioned. 75/25 wheat to orange ratio in this department.

Taste - Again the orange doesn't stack up as very balance in relation to the wheat. This is a VERY wheaty beer. WHEAT. The orange does come through just a squeeze more and again remains very skin like in flavor. Basically the flavor profile is like putting orange skins on white bread and eating it. But since it's in beer form it likely tastes better than what the aforementioned concoction would.

Feel - Arguably the best part of the whole beer. It sits a touch acidic and moderate to thin with a nice pillowy texture on the tongue. Carbonation is at the bare minimum. Orange skins become prevalent here even more so at about 35/65 in relation to the malt. Swallows easily with some tartness and a surprising showing of alcohol.

Overall - It wasn't quite what I wanted but it is still solid enough. As I've already states multiple times, the wheat is the driving force within this beer,Not the oranges. As for drinkability it's a one and done for me. A little too acidic for your wife on a hot summer day, but could surely accompany you to the beach in the summer. As always,try it for yourself.

SometimesIfart, Feb 13, 2016
3.03/5  rDev -13.2%
look: 3.5 | smell: 3 | taste: 3 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 2.75

I picked up a bottle of Dark Horse's Rain In Blood Orange Pale Ale a couple days ago for $2.09 at Boyne Country Provisions. I've been meaning to try this beer and I figured now was the time to do it, so lets see how it goes. 2015 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a imperial pint glass.

A- The label looks nice, it's goes well with the name and it's a little e ye catching. It poured a very cloudy dark orange color that took on more of a brighter orange hue when held to the light with a finger and half of creamy off white head that died down to a thin ring that stayed till the end and it left some patchy lacing behind. This is a nice looking beer, but it's very cloudy for the style.

S- The aroma starts off with a medium amount of sweetness with the malts being the first to show up and they impart some grainy, caramel malt and slightly toasted aromas followed by a weird perfume and floral like aroma that's a little soapy. Up next comes the blood orange which is on the light side and it impart a decent citrus aroma. The aroma was ok, but I didn't like the soapy aspect and I was hoping the blood orange would show up more.

T- The taste seems to be very similar to the aroma, but this time there's no soapy aspect and it starts off with a medium amount of sweetness with the malts being the first to show up and they impart the same aspects that they did in the aroma and they seem to be the aspect that that sticks out the most in the taste and it's also a little grainy with some doughy yeast showing up in the background. Up next comes a slight hoppy flavor that's hardly detectable and it goes into the finish with hardly any bitterness with the blood orange showing up right at the finish and it goes into the aftertaste imparting a very light blood orange flavor. This beer tasted ok, but I thought it was too malty and it needed more blood orange.

M- Smooth, crisp, a little clean, on the light side of being medium bodied with a medium amount of carbonation. The mouthfeel is nice and it works well with this beer.

Overall I didn't think this beer was that great and I thought it was just a little below average for the style with the Pale Ale base just being too malty and the blood orange not showing up enough. This beer didn't have the best drinkability, one is enough for me, I have no urge to drink another one. What I like the most about this beer is the appearance and mouthfeel, to me those were the highlights of this beer. What I like the least about this beer was the Pale Ale base and the lack of blood orange flavor and aromas. I wouldn't buy this beer again and I wouldn't recommend it, it just doesn't live up it's name. Overall I wasn't into this beer, I was expecting more out of it and it just feel short in almost every single aspect. It's one of my least favorite Dark Horse beers and it's one of my least favorite examples of the style. Better luck next time guys, this one could use some work.
