BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Two Roads Brewing Company
Connecticut, United States
tworoadsbrewing.com

Style: American Black Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.50%

Availability: Winter

Notes / Commercial Description:
A hefty Black Ale with profound malt and hop complexity. Neither Stout nor Porter, it will be its own thing – full-bodied, bold and black. Flavor characteristics of dark chocolate, mocha, molasses and dark fruits. Balanced by piney citrusy notes of Pacific Northwest hops. Full-bodied and uncommonly satisfying at 7.5% alcohol.

Opaque black liquid and a robust, thick tan colored foam. Slow retention and big lace.

Molasses, dark oats, dry, earthy hops, dark chocolate and peanutbutter aroma in a big way. Reminds me very much of a Courage Imperial Stout nose. The hops are earthy and very piney. Later, as the liquid warms, subtle dark fruits pop out, especially blackberry.

A big, hearty and bitter flavor , bakers chocolate, dark oats, massive dark malt complexity and even bigger hops. The hops are very earthy, bitter and dry. Many wild flavor's seem to battle eachother for supremacy. The balance is pretty crazy with malt and hops going crazy. Later on, tangy and fruity flavors pop out in a subtle way.

The feel is a medium body, not thick or smooth really, but grainy and dry for sure. A bit zippy at first, finishing dry and tart.

Very unique, rides the rail of any specific style. 12 oz. Can.

From a 12oz can, dated 10/05/16. Served in a tulip.

Pours an opaque chestnut brown with an uncontrollable soft head of cafe au lait suds. Retention is excellent and leaves vast wads of lacing sheeting down the glass.

Smells of nuts and dried flowers and leaves, toast and clean laundry right out of the dryer (the laundry, not the toast).

Taste is soft, roasted bitterness, not coffee, exactly, more like chicory or something nutty. Herbs, flowers, cocoa, molasses, blueberries and copier paper. Finishes nicely balanced, with a long, soft, lightly-lingering, drying bitterness

Feel is soft, smooth and creamy, medium bodied and amply carbonated. Drinks like cream soda.

Overall, I enjoyed the bejeesus out of this beer. Looking back over what I've written, I've used the word 'soft' four times - I might just as well have said smooth and creamy. A most excellent tasty beverage.

I forget where I picked up this CAN, but since my previous review was of Two Roads' Road Jam, this seemed like a logical follow-up in support of The CANQuest (tm).

From the CAN: "Take the Road Less Shoveled"; "Dark Malt, Big Hops [-] A Unique Winter Ale"; "Seasonal Collection"; "This is to taking the road less traveled, in life and in beer!"; "A natural, harmless sediment may appear".

Upon Crack!ing it open, I got a squirt across my fingertips due to it being a brimful craft CAN! A gentle Glug still lifted two fingers of dense, foamy, deep-tan/light-brown head that slowly fell to wisps, leaving sticky lacing in its wake. Color was Very Dark Brown to Black/Opaque (SRM = > 34, < 44) with garnet highlights at the edges. Nose had a combined dark chocolate-mintiness, like a liquefied York Peppermint Patty. Mmm. Mouthfeel was medium-to-full, not quite creamy, but relatively big in the mouth and on the tongue. The taste was somewhat smoky, very dark chocolaty and just minty enough to be cooling. I liked it more than most in this style, but it still seemed like the hops were getting wasted. Because the dark malt was so prevalent, I am going to characterize the finish as semi-sweet, as in semi-sweet or dark chocolate, since that is what I was really put in mind of. It's not my favorite style, but it was a decent example of an ABA.

Poured from a 12 oz bottle.
Color is a rich, dark brown but not quite black. Held to the light shows a nice ruby underhalo at the bottom of the glass. Pleasant brown head, a bit over carbonated as it foamed over and out of the glass during the pour.
Smell is of dark malt, slight roast, grassy hops and every inhalation makes my stomach rumble in anticipation.
Flavor is a little sharp, there's a fruity quality but a dry and maybe slightly medicinal aspect. The hops are more a bitter than floral/grassy on the tongue, which surprised me since the aroma seemed to be there. A bit of a molasses/licorice flavor in there which I think seems adjunct rather than yeast profile in nature. Not as smooth as I would prefer.
Leaves a bitter aftertaste that is a bit of fruit/cigar tobacco in nature to my taste buds. Similar in some respects but short by every measure to the Long Trail Unearthed that I had immediately prior.
Would benefit from lower IBU or different hop selection, and I'd factor in some oatmeal to smooth it out. Just not a stout, definitely not a porter, but not quite a black IPA either. Not sure what the mark was, but I think they missed it in any event.

4.38/5  rDev +19.7%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.25

Two Roads Brewing Company "Route Of All Evil"
12 oz. brown glass bottle, "BTLD 10/27/15"

I won't go into too much detail on this one as it's a fairly straightforward black ale, black IPA, Cascadian dark ale, or whatever you want to call it.

It's hoppy in both the aroma and the flavor with delicate pine and citrus, and the malt is chocolatey and softly roasty. The additional note of molasses is fairly unique however, and the dark fruitiness (raisin, plum, currant) gives it an additional layer of complexity that's not usually found. Beyond those things it's the balance that really makes it shine. It's neither too bitter nor too sweet, and the pine and citrus notes are held back so that they don't stand out too far from the chocolatey, burnt sugar, and charred malt.

Many beers in this vein appear simply as IPA's with darker malts thrown in, but this comes off as the opposite, a strong dark beer with Pacific northwest hops thoughtfully integrated into it. Another winner from Two Roads!

Bottled on 10/27/15, consumed on 1/7/16.

A: Poured into a Duvel tulip, the body is black with a small tannish head.

S: Some roasted or even burnt barley malt. Pine needle hops.

T: Follows the nose, with char, roasted barley malt, some cocoa powder underneath it all and a piney hop finish. Clean on the immediate aftertaste, though there's a lingering battle between the piney hops, roasted/charred barley malt and a faintly sweet, chocolatey sensation. The bitterness ultimately prevails, as it should for this style. Overall, a good flavor profile for a Black IPA.

M: A wee bit thin in heft/feel. Call it lighter side of medium. Carbonation is on the modest side of things - i.e., not too sharp - but it doesn't lead to a creamy feel at all; it still has some bite to it.

O: Good stuff. As it warms up a bit I get a little more char and ash lingering on the aftertaste, reminding me more of a porter in some ways, but the piney hop bitterness brings me back to the American Black Ale style.

Bottled 10/27/15: Pours black color with light coming through at the edges of the glass making it look mahogany brown; 1-2 finger foamy tan head that was full of tiny bubbles and faded very slowly, leaving foamy lace rings as it went down.

Smells of fresh resiny piney and citrus hops up front, like a fresh hopped IPA. The nose then shifts to roasted malt, molasses, peat moss, cocoa powder and coffee grinds. Balance is quite nice as it's a fine blend of juicy hops and charred grains - I like the simplicity.

Tastes like it smells with fresh piney and citrus hops up front, like a fresh hopped IPA. The second wave is charred wood, roasted malt, coffee grinds, molasses, peat moss, cocoa powder, earthy notes and chalky yeast. Balance is nice with more smoked grains, and like the nose a blend of juicy hops and roasted malts. It's almost ashy with brown sugar and lite bitterness in the finish.

Mouthfeel is creamy and smooth across the tongue, with a thin and almost watery body that feels light and trails off slightly bitter with a nice mix of smoked malts and sweet, juicy hops. Overall a nice black ale.
