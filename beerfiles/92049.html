I purchased this CAN during a recent BCCA CANvention in furtherance of The CANQuest (tm) & it never ceases to amaze me how many beers are being CANned! I really dig the graphics, especially on one like this, with its outer space theme and vibrant colors.

From the CAN: "Proud Sponsor of [the] Chicago Comic & Entertainment Expo (C2E2)".

The Crack! was very loud, but as the day is in its waning moments, I did not feel like dealing with any potential aftermath of an inverted Glug & so I went with a rather gentle one, instead. An in-glass swirl raised two fingers of dense, foamy, tawny head with decent retention. Color was a slightly-hazy solid Amber (SRM = > 7, < 9). Nose smelled strongly of tropical fruits, like mango, papaya, guava and tangerine, underlain by a caramel sweetness. I should have a swab CANstantly at the ready to mop up all of the drool. I am to the point of being the hophead equivalent of one of Pavlov's dogs. A mere whiff and I begin to drool unCANtrollably. Mouthfeel was medium. The taste was very much of tropical fruits with a little bit of grapefruit citrus and some caramel sweetness seemingly thrown in just for good measure. Finish was dry, but very fruity. It was literally breath-taking, initially, whether a product of the ABV or the mlange of hops, or a CANbination thereof. It left me with a taste like a roll of Tropical Fruit Life Savers as well as a slight burning sensation. Phew! I should do this more often.

Appearance - Pours about 2 fingers of off-white dense foam, body is a slightly hazy golden amber color which gentle carb rising through the body, great thick lacing left after each quaff.

Smell - huge nose of floral hops along with strong tropical fruits of mango and papaya.

Taste - Just a floral explosion on the first sip, with delicious and juicy tropical notes of mango and papaya joining the dense feeling of liquid floral pollen filling the mouth, which quickly transitions into semi-sweet drying citrus piths and tropical fruit stones. The finish is slightly dry and light floral chalk, with juicy tropical notes in the mouth corners.

Mouthfeel - A perfectly medium carb filled with bright edges keeps this IPA feeling just short of medium, and scrubs just enough of the slight juiciness of the tropical notes to allow the florals and piths to assert themselves firmly with a light bitter dryness, begging the next sip.

Overall - A VERY good DIPA and one that the floral hop lover must try. There are just enough tropical notes here to widen the appreciative audience here, but this floral bomb ends with some bitter drying pucker that may catch a few off guard - but I love the transition. A floral hops lovers dream.

schoolboy, Mar 15, 2016
4.17/5  rDev +0.2%
look: 3.5 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.25

12oz can poured into a tulip. Pours a clear gold tone, with a foamy, matrix of head that rises 3 fingers from the beer, and descends leaving good lacing with solid retention. Dry pine hops on a thick malt backbone best sum up both the nose and palate. Citrus notes are somewhat prominent on the nose, but sour to an an almost kumquat like flavor on the palate (a welcome transition). Mouthfeel is crisp and thin-medium, like most of Revolution's IPAs.

Revolution again makes a beer that somehow elegantly blurs the lines between east and west coast. The beer is nothing jump-out-of-the-glass incredible, just a thoroughly drinkable, solidly balanced beer. I tend to skew away from maltier IPAs, but Revolution seems to mix in just the right amount of dry hops to find a brilliant middle way. This beer is clearly the spiritual sequel to Anti-Hero and Unsessionable...or perhaps differently put, Revolution has a clear style, and this beer doesn't stray far from it, but when that style is a winning one, why change?
