JackieTH, Nov 04, 2015
2.62/5  rDev -26.2%
look: 3 | smell: 2.75 | taste: 2.5 | feel: 2.75 | overall: 2.5

A: Pours a weak white colour head that recedes within 30 seconds, leaving no lacing. Body colour is a clear vibrant copper of average vibrance. Clean, with no visible yeast.

Sm: Copper malts/Caramunich. A glimmer of spices - allspice? It's quite a reticent aroma, and never suggests any overt pumpkin. Very dull and uninteresting for an ostensible pumpkin ale.

Aromatic intensity is average.

T: Follows the aroma. Shallow, reticent, and even insipid to an extent. Copper malts, a hint of allspice...but no pumpkin. Poorly built; is it too much to ask that a pumpkin ale prominently feature pumpkin flavour?

Mf: Smooth, wet, medium-bodied, unrefreshing. Has apt thickness and weight on the palate.

Not oily, gushed, hot, boozy, astringent, harsh, rough, or scratchy.

O: There's no harmony of texture and taste. This build isn't gestalt. Balance is mediocre, drinkability is average. I hoped for better from Alaskan, but this is really lackluster. I wouldn't trifle with it again, and will relegate the 5 beers that remain in the 6 pack I bought to consumption solely for intoxication. Average at best, with no interesting characteristics and little flavour.

C- (2.54) / BELOW AVERAGE

I picked up a bottle of Alaskan's Pumpkin Ale last week for $1.79 at The Lager Mill. I've never tried this beer before so I thought I would give another new pumpkin beer a try, so lets see how it goes. 2015 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a imperial pint glass.

A- The label is nice, it's got a nice design and it's just a little eye catching. It poured an amber red color that took on more of an orange color when held to the light with a little under a fingers worth of off white head head that died down to a nothing and it didn't really leave an lacing behind. This is a decent looking beer, but I wish it had better head and retention.

S- The aroma starts off with a higher amount of medium sweetness with the spices being the first to show up up with the cinnamon sticking out the most and there's some allspice and nutmeg showing up in the background and the combo of the cinnamon and sweetness make for a cinnamon candy like aroma. Up next comes the pumpkin which is noticeable, but it's on the lighter side and there's just a little bit of malts showing up in the background. This beer has a nice aroma, the candied aspect makes it a little unique for the style.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma and it starts off with a slightly higher amount of medium sweetness with the spices still being the first to show up, while they aren't as strong as they were in the aroma, it's still the cinnamon that sticks out the most with the other spices showing up in the background. Up next comes the pumpkin which was even lighter than it was in the aroma and it only imparts a slightly pumpkin flavor. On the finish there's a little bit of a grainy malt flavor with more sweetness malts and a little bit of bitterness with the spices showing up in the aftertaste. This is just an ok tasting Pumpkin Ale, it's a little too sweet and unbalanced for me.

M- Not the smoothest, crisp, medium bodied with a slightly higher amount of medium carbonation. The mouthfeel is ok, but i wish it was a little fuller.

Overall I thought this was just an ok beer and I thought it was a pretty average example of the style, it did stick out a little with the candies aspect, but to me it just made thos bee a little more sweeter than it had to be. This beer didn't have the best drinkability, one is enough for me, I wouldn't want another one in a row. What I like the most about this beer is the aroma. it was the highlight of this beer. What I like the least about this beer is the sweetness and the lack of balance, those aspects need some work. I wouldn't buy this beer again and I can't say I would recommend it. All in all I wasn't a big a fan of this beer, it was going in a unique direction, but it didn't come together as well as it could have. It's one of my least favorite beers from this brewery and it's average at best for the style. Better luck next time guys, this one could use some work.

Moderate dryness after the finish. Medium carbonation and body; with a very smooth, crisp, lightly creamy/bready, and slightly sticky mouthfeel that is good. Alcohol is very well hidden with zero warming present after the finish. Overall this is a nice pumpkin ale. All around good robustness, complexity, and balance of pumpkin/spices, bready malt, and light earthy hop flavors; and very smooth and crisp to drink. 3.12/5  rDev -12.1%
look: 3 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3 | feel: 3 | overall: 3

Alaskan Brewing Co. Pumpkin Ale.

As if I haven't already had my fill of fall beers despite the fact fall has just begun J decided to add another to the fray. Perfect Michigan late September night with temps in the lower fifties and a clear moonlit sky over the tree line. Sometimes the nights around here are just too beautiful. For a fall night sky, I shall drink a fall night beer.

Look - Pours a crystal clear copper with a half finger of soap bubbly white head that turns into a small island cloud formation floating around the top of the glass within only ten seconds or so. The carbonation appears to be electrified and in a trance of zombified states flowing in four single file lines to the top and popping upon impact. Lacing shall not be a factor as the head didn't retain well enough for it to even be considered. Kind of like filling out a joke job application to become a state representative despite the fact you just got released from the state penitentiary. 3.

Smell - The nose is exactly what you would expect out of a lower ABV pumpkin brew. Cinnamon is present like the inevitable death we all have to face, followed by some more generic fall spicyness and an ending of pie crust. Breadyness is apparent as well, almost sour dough in nature. 3.5.

Taste - This beer is too clean for its own good. Cinnamon dosage on the initial hit of the tongue followed once again by a few other generic fall spices. Flavors disappear as quickly as they come and the back of throat revelation is of white breads. Really isn't a whole lot going on here. 3.

Feel - The beer is medium bodied and surprisingly full with a large dosage of prickly though not annoyingly so levels of carbonation. No creamyneas due to the head not retaining. 3.

Overall - It really reminded me of the Blue Moon Pumpkin Ale offering. This isn't one to get all up in a fuss about and have a twenty page discussion on the forums over. Instead, this is an introductory level pumpkin brew that won't warm you on a cool late October/Early November night. Sessionable, but not really worth the time. Pass in the future. 3..
