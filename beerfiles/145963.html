Bottle (unknown date), adjusted review 9/2015:
Pours a hazy slightly dark golden with maybe a hint of orange. Head builds up grandly and fades slowly, providing a large amount of lacing, especially for a lager (although somewhat expected for a wheat). The head and lacing make up for the mildly ugly, cloudy brew.

The smell is a bit stale to me, with a wet cardboard scent being a part of it. Beyond that smell, however, I do note some tropical fruit like papaya, some melon, and a hint of green (fresh cut grass in some, mint in other whiffs). Some decent scents, but too muted.

The hoppy nature of this beer comes right off the bat, definitely hop-forward. The carbonation adds some spiciness up front as well when the beer is cold, it mellows slightly with some time in the glass. Dry from the get go, but the first tastes are canteloupe and some tropical notes, but give way to fresh cut grass and a very green feel. Body is actually at least medium, this beer is not a light lager, perhaps its the wheat adding some body. In some respects, this beer reminds me or a rye IPA.
On re-review, the hops were more muted, yielding a smoother beer, perhaps.

Personally I would have liked a little less hops and a little lighter beer. I think fans of West Coast IPAs should give this a try at least. The hop-head I was with really enjoyed it.

Immortale25, Dec 16, 2014
4.3/5  rDev +5.9%

Exploiting what is the arguably the single most seductive hop on the market, The Evil Twin brewers commandeer the brew kettle once again and create a hoppy masterpiece. Showcasing the "Mosaic" hops, an abundance of tropical, fruit and botanical qualities simple ooze from the beer's pores.

Bright but with a tinge of dry-hop hazing, the beer nestles into the snifter like honey- both in its color and its quiet calm. A slow release of cotton froth starts to release its wonderful bouquet of tangerine, blood orange, mango, apricot and passionfruit. its bursting glory is met with the malty sweet undertow of thin caramel, honey and floral honeysuckle.

As the taste really opens up across the middle, the malty support of caramel, biscuit and honey retreats and the hops intensify. What was tropical and citrus begin to morph into herbal grasses, resinous bitterness and the citrusy bite of orange peel and grapefruit pith.

Trailing dry with those piney, sprucy and spicy wood bitterness, the beer shows its IPA roots extremely well. But in the imperial vein, a slight semblance of sweetness and dextrin body remains and give the hops a proper balance in the end.
