This now has about a year of bottle age.

The beer foams some out of the bottle after being opened. Roughly 2/3 of the beer remains after the foam is under control. The remainder pours out into an Imperial pint glass a golden orange color with a light haze and a full 3" ie new dense fluffy white head some of which has to be discarded in order to complete the pour. The head recedes quite slowly to a 1/8" lasting cover over the liquid and several large patches of foam clinging to the sides of the glass. Sipping creates some patches on the side of the glass, some slide back down to the head some remain. Even to the end of the beer there is a thin layer of foam on the surface of the liquid.

The aromas are both fruity and spicy, and nicely blended together. There's also seems to be a definite bit of mustiness which should increase over the next few years.

The flavors pretty much follow the nose and with some spice that is coreiander and white pepper. Theres also some malt sweetness. Some green orchard fruits show in the background.

The mouth feel is medium bodied with a light crisp touch from the persistent carbonation. The moderately long finish begins with the sweetness fading as the other flavors emerge more strongly. There is also a persistent light hop bitterness as the flavors all fade to dryness.

Overall this is a nicely made beer which is starting to show the Brett character I'm looking forward to seeing what this beer is like over the next few years.

Sep 9, 2016

This year's bottle still had a bit of foam over, but the brett character has definitely begun to show and there's not ambiguity about it. While the beer is still recognizably a Tripel there's now a bit of that earthiness to the aromas and flavors that wasn't there last year.

2015 vintage served at cellar Temps in a teku

Appearance: Brownish orange. Very hazy, tons of sticky tan head.

Nose: very funky, barnyard, stone fruit, apricots, pineapple, peaches, bready malts

Taste: very tropical and fruit forward, slight hop character but nothing huge. Pineapple, peaches, ripe oranges and tangerines, and mango. Quite funky and has a strong barnyard character. Very nice. Very low carbonation and Mouthfeel. There is also quite a bit of the solvent alcohol as it warms up. A bit off putting.

Overall: super impressed with this beer. The tropical played well with the funk and the malty backbone provides a lot of body to a beer that probably will dry out in time. The solvent nature of the alcohol is a bit much. If the beer was Brewed to say 7% instead of 9% it may have turned out a bit better. IMO, wild ales are at their best around the 7% mark. This beer desperately needs carbonation. More time in the bottle will certainly make that happen but I fear it will be an epic gusher. This was bottled 4/2015 and has a BB of the end of 2019. It very well may explode by then. Even at 15 months old I peyed the capnoff and it started to exit the bottle. Oddly there are 2 BB dates: 4/3/2020 and BB end of 2019. Weird and crazy Belgians.

I happen to favor fruity tripels.
While I smell and taste the fruit in Bruges Wild 2015, what is distinctive is its dry finish. And the well-concealed alcohol also helps me like this. It was a bit too dry and carbonated to complement Bob's garlicky World Famous Vegetable Soup, so next time I find this I will drink with a dry cheese.

MJ's "Great Beer of Belgium" covers De Halve's story well... and he seems to like them above his average. I took a day trip to Brugges and admit to its charm... but it was on Sunday and the brewery was closed.
Next time I visit Belgium, De Halve won't be closed and I will take the tour... an interesting brewery in a great Flanders destination.

Poured from a 11.2 oz. bottle into a St. Bernardus goblet glass.

Appearance: Pours a hazy amber orange with a moderate amount of bubbles. Solid finger of off white head that slowly settles into a thin creamy layer.

Smell: A yeasty, funky, and fruity Belgian Tripel aroma with good hints of Brett, spices, and fruit. Belgian yeast with a scent of clove and peppers. Big funky scent of Brett yeast giving sour hints of cherries and vinegar. Lots of fruit esters with hints of banana, green apple, pear, lemon, and orange. Malt hints of straw, grains, wheat, and cracker. Subtle earthy hop hints. A pretty good aroma.

Taste: Like it smells, a funky, yeasty, and fruity Belgian Tripel taste with notes of Brett yeast, spices, and fruit. Belgian yeast with some good spice notes of pepper and clove. Funky and sour taste of wild Brett yeast with some vinegar and cherry. Good fruit notes of banana, pear, green apple, lemon, and orange. Malt notes of wheat, grains, straw, and cracker. Also some light earthy and floral hops. A really solid taste.

Mouthfeel: Medium bodied with a high level of carbonation. Creamy, fairly puckering, and pretty smooth. Alcohol is masked well.

Overall: A pretty solid spin on a Tripel. Good mix of funky Brett with the Belgian fruit esters.
