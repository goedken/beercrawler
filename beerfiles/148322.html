BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Two Roads Brewing Company
Connecticut, United States
tworoadsbrewing.com

Style: English Barleywine

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 12.00%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
When you turn a 100-year-old factory building into a brewery, it comes with a few "bonus" features, like a 20-ton overhead crane and hook! This beer honors our hefty inheritance-an English-style barleywine ale so big your taste buds will need hard hats! Brewed with specially sourced heirloom malt and highly hopped, this ale has a malty sweetness that's nicely balanced by its fruity character and intense hop aroma.

Added by ThatsThatDude on 12-03-2014

Bottled 12/03/14, some age should settle it.

Pours a little small fizzy head that fades almost instantly, crystal clear honey orange color.

Nose tons of sweet candy, caramel toffee notes, a honey nougat candy (I forget what that candy is called), a touch of fruity ester notes, with a little candied apple and dried tree fruit, plenty of blonde malts a little toasty and almost wheat like, light booze and touch of phenol, with a bit of hop character still left mostly some generic fruity hops.

Taste brings plenty of toasty reddish and grainy blond malts, a little wheat like but not much, big blond malt profile and tons of it, with some sweetness, a big syrupy profile, caramel syrup and toffee notes, biscuity as well. Into booze warmth and a bit of tingle, hasn't died down as much as I'd hoped, a little phenol and ester profile from the booze, candied dried apricots and pear/apple tree fruit. Also some spicy hops, a little fruity citrus notes, faint pineapple tropical hop profile but that seems mostly attenuated. Finish is sticky sweet but not overly, a little more warming slight tingly booze, reddish malt and caramel, and dried candied fruits again tree fruits and even a bit of pineapple.

Mouth is heavier bod, syrupy, plenty of booze warmth and a bit of tingle, and decent carb.

Overall not bad, hops have died out a bit but I could see where they were going and it was good, booze hadn't died down which I really had hoped, but decent malt profile and nice hops, need to get it fresh and try it again.

(bottled 10/20/15... let this sit out for a good 20+ minutes before pouring into Chouffe tulip)

L: blonde I would not call this - this is about where an English barleywine should be - not sure why the American versions are red... this appears to have a crazy chill haze... the foam is loose and doesn't want to hold - I'm calling quick turnaround here... lees bottom of the bottle - seems to be miscalculation

S: hops out front: predominantly orange-y, with an underlying bubblegum and caramel with flowery overtones... mellow, satiny booze caressing the nose holes... smells like an American beer, despite how y'all bill it... for sure...

T: oooh, shit - wasn't expecting that lusciousness... lemon oil and honey with a meekly-workmanly-malty, Absolut Citron chaser... delicate yet firm tannic bitterness carrying through... there's some delightful flowery herbal (chamomile?) highlights sprinkling stardust here - hoping it's hop-derived, but likely some fusels interplayin'... it's got a pleasant fruitiness that is (due to the yeast?), decidedly British-yeast-and-blond-ale derived

F: quite lip-smackingly dryly-silky and only phantom viscous... it ain't climaxin' my soul, but this is a rarely-pleasant shoulder massage - kudos!

O: as an American barleywine looking to England, it's not all that bad really... not that much character, but the end result taste is balanced well enough and rather enjoyable... I'd have to say, though, this may be one of the most quintessential beers in this style that tastes of both barley and (white) wine... I'd suggest them either hopping this more with the more characterful British hops (Challenger and First Gold, for starters), or just hopping this to shit with EKG - no, Fuggles - and ageing it 6 months plus before release

Beautiful golden brew, pale and slightly cloudy.

Aroma is pleasant, but lacking in complexity. It's primarily sweet, from both the malt and yeast.

The flavor brings a massive sweetness; more so than yeast and grain, it's a very sugary sweetness. That said, it's neither unpleasant nor particularly difficult to drink. The high ABV is exceptionally well hidden.

Full bodied, smooth feel. Just lively enough to keep it from being syrupy. Remarkably drinkable, but definitely a sipper.

My only two complaints: a lack of complexity or depth (it's mostly a one note sugar bomb) and a real lack of hoppiness. That's not a problem if this is a strong ale, but I expect some amount of hop presence in a barleywine, even a blonde, ostensibly English one.

A: The beer is clear amber yellow in color and has a light to moderate amount of visible carbonation. It poured with a quarter finger high dense white head that quickly died down, leaving a thin film of bubbles on the surface and a collar around the edge of the glass.
S: Light to moderate aromas of citrusy fruits are present in the nose—oranges seem to stand out in particular—along with hints of caramel malts.
T: Similar to the smell, the taste starts out with citrusy flavors but there are strong notes of malty sweetness with associated hints of caramel and toffee. Only a light amount of bitterness is present, and this is most noticeable towards the finish. The alcohol is well masked from the taste, although a little more starts to creep in once the beer warms up.
M: It feels medium- to full-bodied and somewhat smooth on the palate with a moderate amount of carbonation. There are some warming sensations from the alcohol, but there's enough hops in this beer to prevent the malts from contributing stickiness to the mouthfeel.
O: Considering its strength, this beer is very drinkable because it does a good job masking the alcohol from the taste and the hops help to cut through the mouthfeel and prevent this from being overly sticky.
