BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Stillwater Artisanal Ales
Maryland, United States
stillwater-artisanal.com

Style: American IPA

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.00%

Availability: Year-round

Notes / Commercial Description:
Stillwater IPA?? WTF, right? Well truth be told we love a good IPA just as much as the next guy and besides, what's more contemporary then a American IPA? The lean base of Pilsner malt with the lightest touch of caramel support this high definition blend of 6 intricately layered hops gathered from around the world. Bright citrus, stone fruit, pine, earth, dank, crisp & fucking delicious.

Added by jhartley on 12-19-2014

Poured from the bottle into a Spiegelau IPA glass.

Bit of a cloudy appearance, and tangerine orange body. Very small amounts off carbonation that meet up with a solid three finger off white head. Lots of staying power and retention, not even budging hardly. A bit of a few floaties but nothing major.

Aroma manages some unexpected things. Spicy orange citrus in that same tangerine vein as the body color which is nice, but a small grainy almost adjunct seems to feel like its coming off the nose. Mild soft caramel, almost butterscotch like adds a nice dimension as well. Luckily subsequent checks on the nose provide more hop pleasure while drinking.

Palate is quite large. Big dry amounts of astringent hops on first sip, and continuing with controlled pine and lime qualities. Slightly citrus and sweet caramel a bit, but a real dry lingering but controlled bitterness. A little bit of caramel on the finish and lots of accentuating woody and earth dryness on the finish.

A pretty different aggressive take on an IPA that forgoes giant aroma for a sharp but controlled palate. Solid brew.

bubseymour, Feb 06, 2016
4.12/5  rDev +6.5%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4

Picked this up a few weeks back over at the Whole Foods on Houston St in New York City. I enjoyed the other two beers that I've had from Stillwater and even though this was an IPA, I was eager to give this a go. It didn't stand head and shoulders above the leaders in this category but this beer was on par with their other offerings and went down relatively easily given the harshness of the hops used here!

Loved the pour and appearance to this - the hue was a cloudy resin, the head was two fingers thing and frothy, and boy, what nice rings of lacing this left as I took the first few sips. Those were present throughout my session and so was the dank, amber look to this that resembled a cross between a lava lamp and something fossilized from eons ago. Lots of citrus pith and earthy hops to this with a dank mouthfeel on top of it all. Good carbonation, a fair amount of resin, and rind in the aftertaste made this addicting while being dual at the same time: Juice and honey up front and dry in the back.

There was a fair amount of booze here too but never did this feel too strong or overpowering. A herbal undertone tied everything together and I have to admit that the bottle design was catchy and indicative of what was inside - a beer with lots going on that subtly worked together in the end. Hopheads need to give this a go with a shot of water as a chaser as the beer is bound to grow on them before it's finished!
