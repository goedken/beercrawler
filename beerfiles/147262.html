look: 3.5 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 4

I've had a 22 oz. bottle of this sitting in my cellar that I forgot about until a few days ago when I came across it. This is a bottle of the batch, that I believe is the first batch, that was released in February or so of last year. Poured into one of my pint glasses, this beer pours a very dark brown, almost black, color with a brown head that's about an inch thick when poured. Unfortunately the consistency of the head is like that of a head of very fizzy foam that you get when you pour soda pop into a glass, and not like the consistency of foamy heads that are found on most beers. This head made a sizzling/fizzling sound as it very quickly faded to a medium sized ring lining the inside of the glass when first poured along with a cluster swirl of a film layer of surface foam floating around in the middle of the beer's surface. Aromas of toasted caramel malt sweetness and hints of coffee along with a nice chili pepper presence. Has a light to medium roasted caramel malt sweetness flavor along with the aforementioned coffee hints along with perhaps a touch of chocolate as well. These all give way to the chili pepper spicy burn, which is quite pronounced here and is certainly the dominant flavor. It isn't overpowering though. Also, no discernible hop presence here that my taste buds could detect. For a chili head /hot sauce aficionado like myself the level of heat from the peppers here is perfect. This is exactly what I like in a chile beer, and this aspect of this beer reminds me a lot of what I liked so much about the Chipotle Ale that Rogue used to make. Most other chile beers I've had have either too little or to much heat from the peppers. The balance here is great. Has a crisp, somewahat prickly, yet somehow also fairly smotth, light to medium bodied mouthfeel with moderate carbonation. The body/mouthfeel is too light/thin for a stout for me. It clearly says on the label "Rogue Sriracha Hot Stout Beer". The mouthfeel/body reminds me a hell of a lot more of a brown ale than any decent stout I've ever had. Still, this is a really good beer flavorwise if you are a chili head/hot sauce lover and or a fan of chili beers.

Eric

Austin32, Sep 14, 2015
3.51/5  rDev -0.8%
look: 4.25 | smell: 2.5 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 4 | overall: 3.75

750 mL bottle from the LCBO; packaged Mar 6 2015. As is so often the case, morbid curiosity once again trumps prudent financial decision-making. Served slightly chilled.

Pours an inky brownish-black cola colour, generating two fingers of frothy, tan-coloured foam at the surface. It recedes evenly at a steady pace for the next five minutes or so, caking the glass with a curtain of lace as it is slowly replaced with a smooth, foamy cap and collar. It's a reasonably attractive-looking stout, but I have to admit that the nose turned me off a little. Garlic and spicy chile peppers are the most noticeable features, with a slight vinegary twang also coming through. The more 'stout-like' traits are buried beneath this - I can pick out hints of roasted malt, coffee, caramelized sugar and dark chocolate, but the one-two punch of garlic and chile is pretty overpowering.

"Weird" - that's the most succinct way to sum up this beer's flavour profile, but I've never been into the whole 'brevity' thing. Hints of molasses sweetness, roasted malts, salt and cocoa are steadily overtaken by a pointed cayenne pepper spiciness that does not relent quietly. Notes of coffee grounds and charred ash astringency linger briefly into the aftertaste; however, the spicy chile heat and savoury garlic salt flavours manage to outlast everything else. Medium-bodied, with relatively low carbonation levels - the mouthfeel is quite smooth and soft, with the peppery heat doing a lot more palate-prickling than the bubbles themselves. More drinkable than I expected it to be, but finishing off the bottle will still take me a few hours.

Final Grade: 3.51, a passable B grade. Rogue's Sriracha Hot Stout is an interesting novelty, but one should be (more than) enough for most consumers. I don't find it to be notably palatable, but at the same time, it's also not nearly as awful as the concept first sounded in my head. The level of heat is not to be scoffed at - I doubt hot sauce aficionados would break a sweat over this, but for the average BA, I would suggest that you keep some snacks on hand to counteract the searing effects on your palate. You'll also want to have an open-minded beer geek friend around, because 750 mL is probably too much for any one individual to comfortably consume.
