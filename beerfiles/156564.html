jpro, Aug 02, 2015
3.62/5  rDev +4%
look: 4 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.5

The trend of the American pale is is to become slightly more hop-forward, crisper, cleaner and to have less of a malt influence. In doing so, the Highlands brewers choose pilsner malt and low fermentation; perhaps giving way to a new style- the hoppy Kolsch.

Edging closely to the session IPA style, the pale ale pours a strikingly bright golden color with brilliant clarity and streaming effervescence. The nose is bright with citrus, herb and grain while its short and fleeting sweetness is partly malty with cracker-like breadiness but is also pleasantly grainy.

With the ale traversing quickly over the palate, its sweetness dissolves rapidly and is easily ushered away by its turbulent carbonation. What's left behind is a developing hop flavor that's turning from grapefruit and orange peels and into a spicy, grassy and herbal taste to translate into a bitter finish. Slim malt structure lingers after the sweetness and continues to support the moderate hop bitterness and associated flavor.

Its medium-like pale body is crisp and clean like well-crafted lager, and its refreshing finish is aided with the dryness of hops and its playful piney character. Lost Cove is a session pale/IPA hybrid that borrows the dryness and crispness from lager in its taste, effects and technique. Unquestionably, this is these are the trends that will replace the microbrewing darling, the American pale ale.

This seasonal APA from Highland Brewing Company has a beautiful crystal-clear, pale-straw-colored sparkling body; an average rocky white head does not decrease its attractiveness. The nose is predominately strong lemon notes with more subdued resinous pine and florals joined by fleeting alcohol notes. The flavor hops carry through to the palate, where they are overtaken by an increasingly strong bittering hops presence. The finish is bitter citrus with hints of pine and a strong clinging hops bitterness that lingers a good while for the style. Light-bodied, thin-textured and fizzy, this beer goes down easy but, due to the IPA-like closing bitterness, sticks around for awhile. (12 oz bottle, 53 days after pkg.)
