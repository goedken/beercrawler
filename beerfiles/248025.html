Cburnette23, Oct 26, 2016
3.08/5  rDev -25.6%
look: 3 | smell: 3 | taste: 3 | feel: 3.25 | overall: 3.25

"Belgian style quadruple ale" "malt beverage aged in used oak bourbon barrels" (as opposed to all of those fresh oak bourbon barrels).

BOTTLE: 12 fl oz. Brown glass. Tan label. Black pry-off crown cap. 2016 vintage. Purchased at a bottle shop in New York City.

HEAD: Pale khaki colour. ~4-5 minute retention. Decent consistency and complexion; it isn't as full or robust as it could be, but it's not thin or watery.

BODY: Dark amber/ruby. Nontransparent but translucent. I see no yeast particulate or hop sediment within.

It's a decent look for a quadrupel (bourbon barrel aged or otherwise). I can't say it's on par with the best beers in the style.

AROMA: Raisin, molasses, rum-like sugarcane, faint plum and faint gooey fig, caramelized date, Belgian candi sugar, and a bit of muted vanilla (from the bourbon barrel). Elsewise, I get little in terms of the bourbon barrel - which is odd in a beer coming from Central Waters. Maybe some barrel sugars, but no rich coconut or toasted white oak. A blip of leather; possibly tobacco.

Suggests a sweet quadrupel with decent depth of flavour and mild alcohol warmth. Aromatic intensity is average.

TASTE & TEXTURE: A little too sweet for my tastes, even by quadrupel standards, with a lackluster attenuation yeast-wise. Indistinct notes of vague dark fruit amplify the amber malt, caramalt, and candi sugar induced sweetness, hinting towards raisin, plum, dates, and maybe prunes with heavy caramelization running throughout. Molasses. Caramel. Booze-soaked fruit.

Its nods towards sugarcane indicate rum barrels more than bourbon when coupled with the faint vanilla/vanillin presence. I don't get any coconut (which would only draw me more towards rum) or toasted white oak, but there is some barrel sugar sweetness. I honestly doubt I'd know this was bourbon barrel aged if I drank it in a blind tasting, and that's not something I ever thought I'd say about a bourbon barrel aged beer from Central Waters (who usually put out some of the most well executed bourbon barrel beers in this country).

The balance isn't great on account of the high sweetness, and the build isn't stellar since the dark fruits don't ring all that distinct. The mouthfeel could also use work; it's a far cry from the fragile delicate soft feel of a beer like Westvleteren 12. It's well-carbonated, though, and the chewy gooeyness of it accentuates the fruits nicely. Heavy and thick on the palate, with a full body and robust backbone.

A jammier fruit presence and more bourbon barrel depth would be steps in the right direction, helping this shallow brew approach the quality of proper Belgian brewed expressions of the style. It's not a simple brew, but it is far simpler than one would expect of a bourbon barrel aged quadrupel. Flavour intensity and duration are low/brief considering it's bourbon barrel aged. There just isn't a lot here to latch onto.

OVERALL: I chose this beer to celebrate good career news with, expecting it to be excellent; so many Central Waters bourbon barrel aged beers are. But frankly it isn't a good quadrupel whether one regards it in terms of tradition or with leeway given its atraditional bourbon barrel aging approach. While I'll finish the bottle and I do enjoy it for what it is, ultimately it's too shallow and too sweet, and the bourbon just doesn't punch through enough to really make much of an impact. Selecting a better bourbon, aging this for longer, and resolving issues with the base beer are all in order for this to be more memorable fare.

Serve chilled (at the coldest). This is really muted when cold.

C+ (3.08) / ABOVE AVERAGE
