Reds green Apple ale.

Tonight on this episode of Palate Washers, Tyler brings you a bottle of Reds Green Apple Ale left in a lonely floor cooler off of interstate seventy five for a meager dollar. Will it be good? Maybe even GREAT?! OR will it suck like a Chinese who're trying to get a green card? Find out on the next episode of Palate Washer Z!...

Or right now.

Look - I will be very honest. One of the few times in my career that I haven't even bothered pouring the thing into a glass. But it is a clear bottle so I can at least give you some reading. Looks a slightly marked yellow with a thin cap amongst the top. Carbonation seems to be quite low. Small lacing inside of a finished bottle.

Smell - Well...it definitely smells like green Apple. Almost to an extent that I could swear on my left hand to Lord Satan that there were fresh green Apple juice within this bottle. Bits and pieces of malt alongside just a touch of alcohol. Actually smells a bit tart as it should. Not a bad nose in the least to be quite honest.

Taste - I'll first start by saying the flavor is fine. But..it doesn't live up to the nose. The green Apple mentioned in the nose is all present but there is a bit of a stale factor you must take into account here. The malts become even more prevalent in this aspects. Bits and pieces of crab Apple as well along side a touch of the skins.

Feel - Thin bodied with a moderate level of carbonation. About what I had expected in this category. Straight apple juice flavors as it sits and lingers around the mouth. Swallows easily and a bit malty without a second thought. Not particularly good, surely not impressive but clean and easy without offense and a nice flavor retention.

Overall - Is it a craft beer? Nope. But I've been around the block a time or two and rating this purely to it's intended purpose is what I do best, especially given my college malt liquor rising from the ashes background. A fine brew for when your goal is to get drunk simply to see where the night takes you. Wait...made by Miller?!Ermehgerddd a big brewery?! They took errr jerbssssss! 1/5!.

Nah kidding I'm not a complete snob.

Poured from can into a pint glass

Appearance The beer pours a light golden amber color with a one finger head of fizzy white foam. The head fades almost instantly, leaving just a trace of lace on the sides of the glass.

Smell The beer smells almost dead on to a sour green apple jolly rancher. It has a sweet green apple taste mixed with a good deal of caramel and candied sugar sweetness. Other than these smells there is just a hint of a yeasty smell.

Taste The taste is rather strong sweet but lightly soured green apple taste. There are also some flavors of a candied and sour sugar taste at the start, which again, give the overall flavor profile a jolly rancher/sour patch kid like apple sweet/tartness. With a little bit of a floral flavor and some light grain coming at the very end, one is left with a candied green apple taste to linger on the tongue.

Mouthfeel The body of the brew is on the thinner side with a carbonation level that is on the slightly higher side. For the sweet and tart green apple candy flavors of the brew the feel is rather appropriate, making it a rather soda like drinker.

Overall Again, like the rest of the reds this really isnt too much of a beer, but resides much more on the sweet cider side of things. It is sweet and green apple jolly rancher like in flavor which makes for a good brew as a desert or a sweet snack, but if looking for something more beer like in nature I would say this is not for you.

Appearance : Redd's Green Apple Ale pours a golden apple yellow color, about 80% transparent. Forms a 1 finger head that dissolves and fizzles within literal seconds. A small ring of bubbly lace is left sitting on top.

Aroma : From a distance it smells like Green Jolly Rancher. Upon closer inhalation the trademark miller corn smell permeates and destroys everything. In fact I'd say it smells closer to fermented apple skins and corn mash. Not too appealing.

Flavor : The initial taste is more of beer than apples, with malts and corn. Right after that first punch of malty beer, It settles into a surprisingly convincing crisp sliced Granny Smith apple. It follows through to the end with a pleasant pucker.

Mouthfeel : Nonexistant, carbonated, thin. No finish. Typical of all of Redd's offerings.

Overall : I'd have it again but it doesn't quite stack up to the original. Perhaps not even the Strawberry. A pleasant puckered beer with malt and apple skins. About what I expected.
