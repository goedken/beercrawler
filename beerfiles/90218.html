I am off to Philly to see Mac Sabbath, a McDonald's character-themed band that does Black Sabbath covers, here in a little while, but I also want to CANtinue The CANQuest (tm).

From the CAN: "A traditional sour wheat ale inspired by the German original. Made with Weyermann malted wheat and 2-row malted barley, this beer is refreshing and quaffable with crisp acidity."

The Crack! was just the thing to kick off the holiday weekend & so I followed it up with an inverted Glug. The massive, puffy, foamy, bone-white head rose up in the glass, finally forming a two finger cap above the liquid before falling away to wisps. Color was Pale-Straw (SRM = 1- 2) with NE-quality clarity. Nose could be smelled from a distance. The tartness was already causing me to pucker my lips in CANticipation of what was to come. A good whiff really gave me a sense of the sour aspects, also. Mouthfeel was a bit thin, slightly watery. The taste was not as intense as it had been on the nose. In fact, it even had a honeysuckle breadiness to it that helped to mellow the tartness. I really felt a jolt on the finish, though, right at the hinges of my jaw! Phew! Man, I got a full-body tingle off of that one. I will be the first to tell you that I am not a tart/sour beer fan, but this was very drinkable and highly refreshing. If you are hesitant, I enCANourage you to give it a try.

Overall this was a huge disappointment, my first beer from this brewery and it falls flat in every single category.

ordybill, Nov 15, 2015
3.62/5  rDev +6.5%
look: 3.5 | smell: 3.25 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

My fourth can of this one from the six-pack; can't find a canned-on or best-by date anywhere on the cans, but upon closer inspection the plastic can holder for the pack is marked with "PKG 06/19." I take it that this means the cans were filled on June 19th of this year, since I remember them appearing on the shelves toward the end of the summer. So these are about 4 months old, it turns out.

The color of this beer is incredibly pale yellow -- might just be the palest I've ever seen, maybe even a shade lighter than something like Bud Light. Very slight haze to it, but mostly clear. Lots and lots of little bubbles rising up constantly from the bottom. When initially pouring there's a good bit of bubbly white foam on top (especially if you pour a bit quicker than usual), loud fizzing sounds along with that, but this fades to a thin coating within a minute and then away to nothing several minutes after that, leaving just a thin ring around the inside of the glass and a few stray traces where it receded down the sides.

Smell is lemony-sour, acidic, a bit of something else I can't quite find a word to describe -- maybe a bit grain-like but also very slightly veggie-ish? I recall the smell of this one being a bit stronger the first time I had it, but it's coming across as a little faint this time around.

Flavor is lemony-sour right away! And then, toward the end, a surprisingly rich multi-grain sort of thing going on, hanging around just a bit as an aftertaste. As a Weissbier this would be made with both wheat and barley, so that makes sense. I'm always surprised at how strong the flavors are in this one considering it's only a 3% ABV beer -- certainly blows any "light" beer out of the water, and stands up very nicely even to many of the 5-6% ABV beers I've had in the past. Also, I've found that this beer goes very nicely with all sorts of food -- I've had one with macaroni and cheese before, and I'll be having the last bit of this one with some fish tacos. The one downside is that I occasionally (it's happened once with the glass I'm currently drinking, and a couple times in the first one I had, but not at all in the other two) pick up a slight hint of what I can only describe as a vomit-ish flavor. That's not as gross as it sounds, thankfully, but that is what it reminds me of when it pops up.

Feel is light, a little fizzy, and very drinkable. The fizz, though very much present, is not enough to come across as unpleasantly prickly, which really helps.

Overall, this has been a nice introduction to the Berliner Weisse style, and a tasty beer in general. I'll definitely be getting this again if I find more six-packs in the store in the future -- this one came from the discount bin, so I'm not sure if any store in my area currently carries it.

A- Can poured into a glass with a ever-so-slightly hazed, uber-pale golden body and a tiny white crown. The head dies out almost immediately to a tiny belt at the edges with lace rarely sticking to the glass.

S- General lactic sourness with hints of lemon and a touch of whole wheat. Green apple and pale grapes also comes to mind (not in the off flavor sort of way though).

T- Based on the aroma I expected a bit more tartness and overall the acidity is rather low with just a hint of lactic lemony tarts. Finishes more raw-grainy and wheaty than the aroma suggested with notes of diluted lemon water lingering.

MF- Very much light bodied with somewhat of a seltzer feel due to a lack of smoothing textures; ever so slightly foamy. High end carbonation and just a touch of tartness.

Solid sort of middle-of-the-road example of the style with a light tartness and general lack of lacto complexity. Quaffable indeed but also lacking an oomph from both the wheat and souring agents.

Poured from the can into a wheat ale glass.

This has got to be the most palest Berliner I have ever seen. Bright faded hay straw body color, with an emphasized clarity and long streams of cola bubbles. Almost Zima looking here, with a one finger bright head, minimal retention and very thin collar that dissolves quickly to next to nothing.

Very tart nose for the aroma. Strong fruit notes of lemon, hiding powdered flour like grain in the back behind it. Hints to a bit of crab apple with some good puckering sensations, seems very large.

Palate comes with a good emphasis on tartness but in a very different angle. Clean finishing, slow bready aromatics on the aftertaste which is a good plus that really builds. Mid palate though is very wet, and has a very cidery feel and character taste to it as well. Mid palate has more apple than fruit like qualities and also some gaseous mild dioxide like character.

Overall a very odd berliner, coming off more like cider than beer, some good malt and bready finish luckily saves it from being a complete bust.
