A: Pours a translucent golden yellow with a frothy, two finger off white head. The head settles to a finger, but sticks around the whole time. This beer has serious legs. Minimal carbonation.

S: Piney, bitter citrus rind, sweet dried apricot and a hint of melon. Prominent bready malt profile. A touch of alcohol on the nose.

T: Relatively muted flavor profile. Some dried fruit sweetness of apricot and raisin. A
Hint of pine and citrus.

M: Minimal carbonation, almost none. Syrupy.

D: Not too drinkable. There's some citrus and pine at the beginning, but as I sit here and the beer approaches room temperature it seems to fall apart. Flat, bitter and flavorless. The body is relatively thick, almost syrupy. I would suggest drinking this one ice cold for the highest and best level of enjoyment, underwhelming at best with some unpleasant aspects.

Pours a yellowish copper and clear, with a sudsy head that retreats and leaves a nice but brief lacing. Smell is typical IPA, pine with citrus and flowery notes but little else. The taste echoes the aroma, a surprising amount of hops flavor that isn't tamped down by by a lot of sweet malt. It's sweet, mind you...it's just more of a light, fresh, watermelon sweetness rather than an overbearing and syrupy fruit punch type of sweetness. (At least that's the best analogy I can come up with.) Although I don't find it overly flavorful, it is quite refreshing and carries its alcohol well. Not too bad, hopefully this is sticks around in the summer, where its profile would be much more appreciated.

Color is the orange shade of amber, topped with a fine-grained head in the Affligem chalice. It's quite lovely. Aroma has some piney hops and a whiff of fermenting pineapple. Quite spritzy in the mouth with a distinct tang, though less acidic than a lot of IPAs.

Mellow malt with a sweet undertone and lots of fruit. This tastes fairly strong, though the alcohol doesn't seep through. Quite aromatic. Even a taste of fruitcake. Very carbonated, which adds to the refreshing quality. It's kind of a hop soda sensation. This would be good on tap with a little less concentrated carbonation. Long mango taste with a touch of passion fruit and sharp unripe pineapple. Sweet apple juice and the bitter aftertaste.

This is a thoroughly drinkable brew, deeply imbued with hop flavors. Personally I don't see anything wrong with an IPA that's not horribly bitter, though this might not appeal to the hopoholic seeking more punishment. From the 22 oz bottle purchased at Bottle Works in Seattle.

On tap at Melrose Grill in Renton WA, poured into pint glass.

Copper-tan in color, the brew appears medium-bodied with light carbonation, as a small wispy head quickly dissipates. Toffee-sweet grainy malts at the front of the nose, as a range of floral notes describe the hop profile. The sweet notes run to the front of the palate, unusually sweet for the style. Hop notes punch back a bit before the sweet aspects become overly cloying, but this is one sugary-sweet IPA. Finishes long and sweet.

IPA's are hit and miss in the Northwest...this is a miss. It's full-flavored, and might not be a bad intro-brew to someone that isn't used to hoppy beers, but if hoppy brews are already refreshing and satisfying to your palate, look elsewhere.
