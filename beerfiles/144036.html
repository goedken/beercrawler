BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Georgetown Trading Co.
Kentucky, United States
georgetowntrading.com

Style: American Brown Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 10.20%

Availability: Year-round

Notes / Commercial Description:
Like most whiskey aficionados, we also love a great beer. And in particular we became very fond of barrel aged beers, which from time to time had been aged in our whiskey barrels! So, we decided to have some fun and make our own beer. We partnered with a great brewery (the Beltway Brewing Co. in Sterling, VA) and began "working" with their master brewer to create a unique recipe with a flavor profile that would develop best in barrels that previously held our award winning James E. Pepper 1776 Rye Whiskey. No more than 30 days after our whiskey barrels are dumped in Kentucky, they are at the brewery being filled with beer. This ensures that every batch gets a big, boozy Rye whiskey finish and notes of toffee, chocolate, oak & vanilla.

Added by Jason on 11-02-2014

James E Pepper's 1776 Rye-barrel aged Brown Ale. 10.4% ABV.

A: A bit over a finger of tan head. Black in the glass, with a red-brown inner glow. Honey brown in the dimple. Solid sheeting; decent lacing.

S: Rich chocolate cake and rye. Slight oak and faint tobacco. Slight toffee. Not overly complex, but nice.

T/MF: Taste is very different. It's a weird conglomeration of coffee, sweet char, licorice, and a slight graphite note. Toffee backing. Slight chocolate cake. Not I would expect of a brown ale, even rye-barrel aged, but good none the less.

Poured from a 22 oz. bottle, into a Belgian tulip glass.

Keeping bourbon synonymous with Kentucky, the re-pour-pose of those spent rye whisky barrels now become home to a new breed- craft beer lovers! The Georgetown Trading Co. devises a hearty brown ale that stands up to those whisky flavors nicely.

Deep dark and gentlemanly brown, the beer's mild haze foreshadows a creamy espresso-like froth that amounts above. Lavishly chocolaty and robust with hazelnut, the nose is saturating with caramel and toffee notes to evoke a dedicated dessert-like scent. Equally as lavish is the beer's initial taste as its sweet sticky toffee pudding-like taste bridges toward chocolate and hazelnut seamlessly.

Across the middle, the beer's boisterous sweetness slathers on the tastebuds. As its caramel and toffee relax, the beer's more toasty, delicate coffee flavors mount as the nuttiness trends toward walnut bitterness once the tepid hops begin to balance. But its the sweet vanilla, charred oak and caramelized woods that ultimately steal the show.

Finishing deep, long and sweet, the full-bodied beer is creamy and lightly syrupy- easily satisfying those with the sweetest of teeth. Peppery rye and alcohol counters the sweetness, yet the caramel and hazelnut reign supreme as they seep easily into the next drink.

BeerAdvocate, Aug 25, 2015
3.69/5  rDev -7.5%
look: 3.75 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

Poured into a Fremont small snifter. Pours a dark mahogany brown with a fine, but thin light brown head with good retention as a thick band around the glass and large patches. Aroma is dominated by rye whiskey, with light caramel malt, brown sugar and brown bread. Flavor follows, with strong rye whiskey and oak dominating a surprisingly supportive, but almost neutral malt body. Faint hints of molasses, nuts and vanilla. Finish has mild grassy hops and woody rye whiskey. Medium bodied with good carbonation. An odd take on a brown ale; not surprisingly this seems to have the ale body exist only to support the whiskey barrel flavors, which it does well. Missing are any distinctive brown ale flavors; they are there, but only as light afterthoughts. The barrel flavors are good, though and redeem this idiosyncratic brown ale. I'd put this in the strong ale category because its strength is its high point. I enjoyed drinking this, but would gravitate towards an actual imperial brown ale in the future. Not sure how this would age, the malt does not seem to be robust enough to support extended cellaring. Interesting and unusual.
