Poured into a Thirsty Monk snifter. Label says MFG 08/01/16.

A- Pours a dark amber color with a 1/2 inch beige head that retains well before becoming a thick ring around the edge of the glass and a mostly full sheet of sheer surface foam. Semi-resilient lacing leaves small clots behind.

S- Earthy hop aroma with a substantial malt backing that's slightly sweet. Also an afterthought of citrus. Hint of cookie dough.

T- Equal parts citrusy hop flavor, malt sweetness and assertive bitterness. Has a flavor profile I like to describe as aggressively balanced since the ABV is so well-hidden but the taste is still so intense.

M- Sharp from the hops with medium carbonation and a body leaning toward the fuller side.

O- Quite good. It's obviously starting to drop off a bit since it's approaching the two-month mark but still has a good amount of life in it.

BreakfastStout, Dec 25, 2015
4.08/5  rDev 0%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Bottled 11/12 and poured 12/20. A single bottle was between $3-$4, I don't remember. Poured into an over-sized Sam Adams Perfect Pint.

Appearance: A very hazy orange with a slim, creamy white head on top. Bits of lacing. Great looking beer. (4.25/5)

Smell: Juicy/fruity/tropical describes this beer well, thanks to the citra -- especially tropical. The malt adds even more sweetness to the aroma, which I'd hope for, considering it's a DIPA. Smells like fruit juice with a little resin from the hops. Very nice. (4.25/5)

Taste/feel: Big mouthfeel on this one somehow masks some of the flavor. It's strange. As it warms up a bit, as with most beers, more flavor comes through. A "juicy pine" flavor is what I notice primarily on the front, with tropical flavors (pineapple, mango) being in the middle. On the end it's pine and overripe tropical fruit with actually quite a bit of unexpected bitterness. The carbonation is unexpectedly high, and the feel is pretty full, which is an atypical combination, generally. Finish is really dry and resinous. There's malt and a little alcohol that lingers. The bitterness seems to intensify the longer I wait between sips, which is great, and only makes me want to take another sip. (4/5, 4/5)

Nice beer with quite a bit going on. I've read a few reviews that say they're bummed that the Citra doesn't "shine" as much as it could, but I get plenty of it -- this is a big DIPA, and one that definitely is on the more bitter side of the style. This beer's a little rough around the edges (mostly in terms of mouthfeel), but I actually enjoy that. Worth a try and some buys after if you see it on the shelf.

(4/5)

Feel is a little rough, carbonation feeling like lots of tiny pin pricks. Ending never gets too dry, a sign of the pale ale designation over an IPA to me.

Really enjoyed this as a pale ale, drinkable and flavorful, and fairly balanced.

I picked up a bottle of Oddside's I Want MY HTV a few days ago for $2.19 at The lager Mill. I didn't know anything about this beer till I saw it on the shelf, I'm a big fan of Citra Pale Ale so of course I had to try it, so lets see how it goes. 2015 Vintage, only 9 days old. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a teku.

A- The label looks nice, it goes with the name and it's a little eye catching. It poured a nice orange/amber color that took on more of orange color when held to the light with a little less than a fingers worth of bubbly off white head that died down to a thin ring that stayed till the end and it left some spotty lacing behind. This is a very nice looking beer, I have no complaints.

S- The aroma starts off with a higher amount of medium sweetness with the citra hops being the first to show up and they impart a huge citrus and tropical fruit aroma with some great pineapple and citrus rind like aromas seeming to stick out the most and it has a very juicy aspect to it. Up next comes the malts which impart a little bit of grainy, sweet malt and bready aroma with just a slight alcohol aroma showing up in the background. This beer has a very nice aroma, the Citra hops are showcased nicely.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma and it starts off with a higher amount of medium sweetness with the citra hops still being the first to show up and they are the aspect that stick out the most in this beer while imparting the same aspects that it did in the aroma, but this time no single fruit seems to stick out the most. Up next comes the malts with the sweet malts sticking out the most with some bready and grainy flavors showing up in the background along with a little bit of doughy yeast. On the finish there's a medium amount of bitterness with a light alcohol with a citra hop heavy aftertaste. This is a very nice tasting beer, it's just what I would expect an Imperial version of Citra Pale Ale to taste like.

M- Fairly smooth, not too crisp, medium bodied with a lower amount of medium carbonation. The mouthfeel is god and it works well with this beer.

Overall I thought this was a very nice beer and a very nice example the style, it was just what I thought an Imperial version of CItra Pale Ale would taste like and I really liked how well the citra hops were showcased. This beer had nice drinkability, I could drink a couple of these, but after that it might start to be too much. What I like the most about this beer was how well the citra hops were showcased and how true to style it was. What I liked the least about this beer was the mouthfeel, I wish it was a little more crisp. I would for sure buy this beer again and I would recommend this to anyone who likes DIPA's. All in all I was very happy with this beer, I know Oddsides has some skills when it came to this sty le and the really comes through in the taste and aroma. It's one of my favorite beer from this brewery an d it's a great example of the style. Great job guys, this beer is awesome, keep it up.

Oddside Ales I want my HTV.

A BIG South park esque I'M BACKKKK so champions and poobahs rejoice! Or complain! Do what ya do!. Heard about this one and knew I had to have it. $12.99 a sixer and hoping for the absolute best which I'm nearly certain I shall get outta OddSide. Now SHUT UP, I WANTTT MYYY H TEEEEEVEEEEEE.
