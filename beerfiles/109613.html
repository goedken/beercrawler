From notes taken 04/17/16:

Semi-clear bright orange-amber, dense gelatinous body of finer suspended flecks. Light creamy-tan head, fluffy head. Fine here, rocky there. Retention and lacing both superior. Hop aromas of pine, resin, mango, sweet orange, stone fruit, pleasant perfume, rind, at different intensities, at different points. Very perfumey palate throughout, but sweet and tart with tropical fruit as well, tea leaves in the middle, spice, pine, and peppercorn in the finish. Don’t know exactly what the grain bill is, but color-, aroma-, and flavor-wise this is practically an Imperial IPA. Oh yeah, and the ABV as well. Medium-bodied, sloshy but then nearly creamy, medium-level of tingly, then softer sudsy carbonation, moderately astringent and warming.

Look: bleached slice of wormwood cut from a fallen cloud floats atop an eerily transparent orange pekoe body refusing to remain in a single spectral category.

Smell: a verdant green branch oozing luscious sunny, sticky sap swung by a juice stained peddler of grapefruits. disturbed by our intrusion, his cart tips over spilling caramelized peaches and a mosaic of nearly ripe, ripe and exceedingly ripe strawberries.

Taste: felt coated mandarin oranges instantly dissolve into grapefruit skins which clothe an ever undressing crowd of dark, friendly lemons. toasty brown sugar malts lurk almost unseen among the Edenic cornucopia of citrus, fruity hops.

Feel: prickliness quickly taken over by a soft thickness finishing as bright airiness in the south of the tongue.

Overall: this beer drops fruit, both exotic and citrus, from pine limbs as if it were rain nourishing a parched earth. the malts are subtle, never cloying as in some red ales, and yet play an indispensable part in the song.

Medium body. Creamy texture. Average carbonation. Long, smooth finish. (Feel - 4.50)

Appearance – The beer pours a golden orange color with a one finger head of white foam. The head has a great level of retention, slowly fading over time to leave a fantastic level of foamy lace on the sides of the glass.

Smell – The smell is big and potent. It is strongest of a sweeter caramel smell mixed with other sweet aromas of tropical fruits (tangerine, mango, and pineapple). Taste- The taste begins with a strong caramel sweetness with a rich lightly toasted doughy taste. These flavors remain very strong throughout and are joined by some lighter notes of a tropical fruit sweetness as the taste moves forward. All the while a little bit more roast comes to the tongue as well as some light pine and earthy hop tastes. In the end a sweet and lightly hopped malty taste is left to linger on the tongue.

Mouthfeel – The body of the brew is thick and chewy with a carbonation level that is rather low. For the sweet and rich malty tastes of the brew the feel is quite nice, creating a thick medium for the rich flavors to bath the tongue.

Overall – Not really strong on the hop at all like I was expecting, but overall a rather nice, sorta sweeter brew. It definitely has its place and is certainly worth at least a try.

A- Stubby 12oz pours into a tulip with a copper-amber hue to a mostly clarified body. Rising above the surface of the liquid is a thick off-white, creamy cap finishing at a finger high. Good head retention leading to thick sheets and webs of lacing.

At first the aroma made me think I was in for a somewhat malty brew with fruity esters disrupting the hops but on the taste buds the hops come off as clean with citrus and pine at the forefront. Minimal sweetness allows the hops and malt flavors to work in unison.

How do I start this review? Oh yeah - by saying it's not very reddish in appearance. This is a lighter copper to possibly amber in shade. Clarity is excellent, however. The head is two fingers high and white in shade, featuring good retention and texture. Webby streaks of lacing stick to the glass nicely.

I pick up some Mosaic hops on the nose. Not the freshest take (it's within the best-by date, so it's fair game), but I definitely pick up some nice grapefruit and tangerine qualities. Even better, no sweet onion! I also get a lot of malt backbone, and it brings plenty of caramel and toffee - even a bit of earth. While nothing bold or explosive, it comes across very well-balanced and even delicate.

Yep. This tastes much more like a red ale than the look suggested. It opens up with some light grapefruit, transitioning into some mango and melon flavors mid-palate. Malts appear strongly throughout. Plenty of caramel and toffee clustered around the start slowly transitions into a finishing note of table sugar and bread. This comes together incredibly well, though the flavor profile isn't to my subjective liking.

I notice the slightly full body of this beer. Its texture isn't that creamy, more slick - thankfully not that slimy. There is a gently crisp bite (though no real bitterness) to ease it down the palate some. Carbonation is somewhat powerful as well.

Red ales are never my favorite style, but this is executed well. Balance is bang-on, and the bitterness is very low. Red ale lovers will really dig this, since it shows off new-school hops nicely (without compromising the malt backbone). I won't go seeking this out since I'm not a fan of this style, however.

3.93/5: A damn fine red ale, just not my thing
