A: Pours a hazy very dark reddish brown in color with light amounts of active visible carbonation and moderate mahogany highlights. The beer has a half finger tall tan head that reduces to a couple of small patches of very thin film and a thin ring at the edges of the glass. Very light amounts of lacing are observed.

S: Moderate to strong aromas of dark fruits (plum, raisin, and prune) with light to moderate aromas of rum + oak from the barrel aging.

T: Upfront there are moderate to strong flavors of dark malts with a moderate amount of dark fruit sweetness - specifically prune, plums, and raisin stand out. There are moderate to strong flavors of rum + oak from the barrel aging which become more prevalent as the beer warms up. Light amounts of lingering sweetness in the finish.

M: Slightly heavier than medium bodied with moderate amounts of carbonation. Light to moderate amounts of alcohol warming in the finish.

O: The beer is fairly strong in ABV and intense in flavor/aroma which makes this beer a definite slow sipper. Enjoyable and definitely worth grabbing if you get the chance since the rum adds to the BSDA flavors.

Picked up a caged and cork 375 ml bottle at the brewery in mid-May for $12.99. No bottling date visible.

clayrock81, Apr 10, 2015
3.38/5  rDev -16.1%
look: 3 | smell: 5 | taste: 3 | feel: 2 | overall: 3

Appearance: Black body with a thin fizzy cola-like head that quickly disappears leaving barely a ring and no lacing at all. The lack of head is forgivable given the ABV, but it's not impossible for a beer of this volume to maintain a head.

Aroma: Without even approaching the glass I get a strong rum aroma. Upon closer approach I pick up very strong aromas of plum, a rich cherry-like scent, and a mellow banana aroma. It has a very strong aromatic presence.

Taste: There is a very faint caramel flavor right up front mixed in with a bit of bread. The warmth of the alcohol comes through immediately, but It doesn't give off an unpleasant flavor. There is a faint date flavor and a bit of a light earthy after-taste. Notes of rum are present throughout the entire drinking experience.

Mouthfeel: The mouthfeel is surprisingly thin. There's not much to say about how this feels in the mouth, and I don't want to ramble on about what does not exist.

Overall: I wasn't too impressed with the beer. I suppose it had a lot to live up to given that it is an anniversary ale, a Belgian Strong Dark Ale, and one that was aged in rum casks. It just didn't deliver anything memorable. The biggest let-down for me was the mouthfeel. The beer tasted good with rum, but so does Coke. I would opt for a better Belgian Strong Dark Ale over one with the extra bells and whistles.
