twage04, Aug 23, 2015
4.35/5  rDev +5.8%
look: 4.75 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 5 | overall: 4.5

Lagunitas Imperial Red.

One of my favorite styles from one of my favorite easy to find breweries. Fingers crossed on a big hit with this one.

Look - Pours a very dark, nearly impenetrable amber/ruby color with a three finger off-white head that has exceptional retention. Carbonation appears to be on low with this one. Looks quite spot on for the style. Leaves some great lacing. 4.75

Smell - Very toasty on the forefront. Like toasted bread with a dash of raspberry jelly. Smells a touch soapy, and the hops come out to play once the beer warms up a bit. 4.

Taste - Before I get into flavors I'm going to first comment on how smooth this beer is. It's as smooth as a 1970's deep voiced smooth talking black pimp trying to pick up your momma. Usually I'm not a huge fan of malt forward beers, but this one is tasty. Malts everywhere with a balanced hop presence and a dash of sweet candies on the back edge. The well retained ultra creamy head makes this one too much fun and too easy to drink. Gulp after glorious gulp goes down smooth as silk. 4.25

Feel - The mody is heavily creamy as I stated and because of that I have a hard time getting a read on the general body of the beer itself. Carbonation feels light. 5

Overall - All smoothness and creaminess aside, it could have done with a more robust in your face flavor profile. Aside from that short coming, this is one of tge best red ales I have ever had the pleasure of consuming. And at $5.99 a bomber, you will not go wrong picking this guy up. 4.5.
