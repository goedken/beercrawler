Poured from the bottle into a wee heavy thistle glass. May 2015 listed on the label.

Murky, cloudy a little but with some clarity, and swamp brown looking body, with fine visible carbonation along the sides slightly. Head creation is quite minimal, about a finger, a bit of fizzy retention that dissolves but manages a shallow puck at least of faint tan color.

Aroma is pretty interesting. Cherry sourness and almost oud bruine character with a classic malt scotch ale thickness. The combination works out pretty good, if the cherry aroma quality seems to hint on the sour side slightly. A bit of brandy warmth is noticeable as well, but doesn't dominate strongly. Quite a bit varied and unexpected but also solid.

A bit of a discerning palate on first impressions. Some good rounded body but also a little out of character as more of that twinging cherry character hints to more Belgian Flanders Oude Bruin to Red thoughts. Very mild tartness luckily, but also subdued malt thickness of toffee and caramel in the mid palate. An interesting almost juiced cherry quality, but even some distracting prickly carbonation a bit that seems to linger on the tongue and really come out of nowhere. Moderate boozy barrel aging warmth, that does add a bit of nice flavor but also takes away from the base style.

Solid beer and a neat experiment, but lots of the flavors also seem to compete and clash with the style and pull you in opposite directions. Decent brew and a neat experiment, but needs to get the basics nailed down. No complaints that's for sure.

jdhilt, Sep 27, 2015
3.59/5  rDev -6.3%
look: 4 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

Appearance: Dark garnet red. Head is off-white/light khaki with minimal retention. Leaves a small slightly creamy and bubbly collar that persists for the duration. Small splotches of lacing. Minimal carbonation.

Smell: Cherry leather oak. A mix of tart cherry flesh with some sweeter bing cherry as well. Damp oak which is now a hallmark of Grimm beer. A touch of cardboard and oxidation. Brandy adds a sweet caramelized booziness to the back of the nose. Nicely sweet with some deeply cooked and caramelized malts. Some caramel and hints of almost toffee with a soft undercurrent of vanilla and almost smoke. Some dark fruit - raisin mostly with even more cherry as it warms. Soft spiciness. Some deeper red wine.

Taste: Slightly smoky caramel cherry leather. Sweet and caramelized malts. Caramel, touches of coffee, and a slight breadiness. Alcohol creeps through the back adding a soft spiciness. Some yeasty spice as well. Brandy barrel adds a damp wood with some darker almost burnt fruit flavors. Hints of raisin over a base of tart cherry skin. Some sweeter cherry flesh. Some slight oxidation as well. Minimal hop base. So much raisin. Some solid leather, especially as it warms. A bit muddled and unclear.

Mouthfeel: Medium body with a moderate carbonation. Surprisingly clean with a prickly carbonation.

Overall: No need to fear the moon.

It's a unique beer. Not many scotch ales sitting in barrels. It's good but a bit messy in the long run. Carbonation is a touch high but those are really the only complaints. Brandy is a nice compliment to the base beer as is cherry.

S: Interesting and weird - Scotch ale aromas with some cooked malts, caramelized malts, brown bread, cooked sugar, brown sugar, molasses, toffee - some funky barrel, with some boozy heat - musty, damp, wet, lactic barrel weirdness - slight acetone - dark fruits and fruit esters, fig, raisin, and some apple - cherry flesh, cherry skin

T: About as weird as the aroma, but it kind of works - fairly malty, with caramel malts, amber malts - cooked, caramelized, chewy malts, dark bread, molasses, and lots of toffee - lightly toasty, perhaps a bit roasty - lots of dark fruits - figs, raisins, and dark cherries - some barrel funk, lacto, and perhaps some powdery Brett dryness - some off-flavors, presumably from the barrels, with acetone and phenols - medium level of bitterness - some brandy heat - old, musty, woody barrels - straight-up booze in the finish, a little sticky

M: Medium body, nicely carbonated, a bit sticky in the mouthfeel but mostly smooth

O: Ouroboros?

Barrel-aging a Scotch ale is often a fun idea. I'm not sure where the cherries fit here, though. And the barrels lend some odd off-flavors. This is interesting, but definitely not among Grimm's best.

May 2015 bottle shared, notes from last night. ABV listed as 8.3%.

M - Good body, definitely on the hot side, good amount of alcohol coming through for the ABV, it is decently implemented though. Carbonation a bit on the high side. A pretty decadent sipper.

O - I suppose my main complaint would be the boozy hotness, but it does kinda fit in and amp up the decadence. Plus everything here comes through surprisingly well, scotch ale base/brandy barrel/tart cherries mingle nicely and make for a unique and deep taste. Probably best split 3 or 4 ways.

Poured a caramel color with a 2F head in a Gordon Thistle glass. Starting temp: 45F

Initially, the aroma is sour cherries, which is very different for the style.

Very tart and piquant mouth feel- a very different approach to the style.

At a colder serving temp, the beer tastes like a sour: cherries, oak, and malty.

Warming changes the beer: caramel and the Scotch Ale style become more apparent along with a maltier mouth feel.

It's best to start drinking this beer at a colder temp, and slowly savoring the changes at higher temps. This is a singular approach to a Scotch ale, one of my favorite styles.
Unique, a slow drinker, and a wonderful beer for the adventurous.
