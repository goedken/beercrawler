stortore, Jun 17, 2016
4.09/5  rDev +1%

I picked up a bottle of Jolly Pumpkin's Some of MY Best Friends are PInchadiscos for $12.99 at The Lager Mill. I'm always down to try a new JP beer and this one sounds like an interesting collaboration, so lets see how it goes. 3016 Vintage. Poured from a brown 750ml bottle into a Teku.

A- The label looks good, it's got a nice design to it and it's a little eye catching. It poured a fairly clear golden orange color that took on more of a golden color when held to the light with two fingers worth of fluffy white head that died down to a thick ring that stayed till the end and it left a good amount of patchy lacing behind. This is a very nice looking beer, I have no complaints at all.

S- The aroma starts off with a slightly higher amount of medium sweetness with that signature JP yeast being the first to show up and it imparts a wet straw and horse blanket like aroma that goes into a nice citrus, fruit, lemon and lime aroma with a little bit of a floral and herbal aroma coming through. Towards the ends there's come the malts with a little bit of a pale malt and grainy aroma. This beer has a very nice aroma, all the aspects work well together.

T- The taste seems to be fairly similar to the aroma and it starts off with a slightly lower amount of medium sweetness with that funky yeast being the first to show and it imparts a little bit more of musty flavor than most JP beers. Up next comes some fruity flavors that are very citursy with that lemon grass and lime coming through. Up next comes the malts which impart a pale malt flavor that goes into a ;little bit of hops towards the end with a slightly lower amount of medium bitterness that lingers just a little with a funky and fruity aftertaste. This is a good tasting beer, but I wish it was a little less bitter.

M- Smooth, slightly refreshing, on the lighter side of being medium bodied with a slightly higher amount tof medium carbonation.

Overall I thought this was a good beer and a good example of the style, all the flavors were pretty much just as advertised and for the most part they worked well together. This beer had ok drinkability, I think one glass would be my limit, anymore than that and the bitterness might start to wear on more. What I like the most about this beer is that all the flavors showed up just as advertised. What I liked the least about this beer is that the bitterness was toned down a little. I would buy this beer again if they release again, but only one bottle. I would recommend trying this beer, but you got to be able to take a little bit of bitterness. All in all liked this beer, but I didn't love it and if it had a less bitterness it would be very good. It's not a favorite JP beer and it's an above average example of the style. Nice job guys, this is a tasty beer, keep it up.

Poured gently at 46°F from a 750ml bottle (bottled on 11Aug15) into a tulip. Consumed on 17Apr16.
LOOK: A fingers worth of white foam is formed after pouring. The foam head recedes to a quarter fingers worth within 25 seconds. Yellow in color with an extreme amount of cloudiness. Rising bubbles are small in size and high in quantity. Lacing is thin and moderate in quantity.
SMELL: Strong aromas of hay and lemongrass are supported by mild aromas of rose hips, sour green apple, oak, and lime along with subtle aromas of white grapes, earthy yeast, and peaches.
TASTE: Strong lemongrass and hay flavors first caress the taste buds. Mild oak, sour green apple and subtle rose hip flavors immediately trail. Mild flavors of lime and earthy yeast along with subtle flavors of peaches and white grapes finish off the taste theatrical as all previous flavors persist, except for rose hips, which grow slightly stronger.
FEEL: Medium-bodied with moderate levels of carbonation. The beer goes down somewhat smooth and ends with a mild but pleasant dryness.
NOTE: The rose hips and lime were a great addition for this style! The rose hips contributed a nice tang to the overall taste while the tartness from the lime wasn't over the top and got along well with the other flavors.

4.1/5  rDev +1.2%
look: 4 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Built around those who know their way around sound selections and master mixing, the fine brewers of Jolly Pumpkin and Stillwater Ales join forces for a beer that stems from those sensory sounds to the equivalent in sensory taste.

Pinchadisco pours a hazy straw with frothy white creme. Stern perfumes of citrus, straw, grain and spice tingle the nose while its taste is lightly of grain and bread for an earth and spice upstart.

But the beer really reigns in on the taste buds as the flavors begin to saturate, delivering on the high citrus flavor of lemon and lime. As green apple and green grape fold into the mix, the flavors trend spicy, dry and winey. Peppercorn, citrusy hops and a strong earthen taste of wet cork and burlap impede the late palate before the gritty ale finishes.

Dry and champagne-like, the ale shows its more rustic roots of straw, hay and leather as the flavors come to a close. Its spicy finish is decorated with citrusy hop bitterness and also the unadulterated taste of dried lemon and lime peels in a gin-and-tonic kind of way.
