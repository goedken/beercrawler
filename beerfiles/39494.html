claspada, Oct 25, 2010
3.3/5  rDev -6%
look: 3.5 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

Beer review from BA Mag Dec 2007.

Otter Creek's latest "Special Release" brew with crystal and chocolate malt and "a touch of raspberry."

Dark, rich brown with garnet hues topped with a nice, creamy tan lace. A bit metallic in the nose at first, with pleasant aromas of chocolate, soft toffee and raspberries. Smooth up front and malty with sweet notes of caramel, honey and molasses--a touch syrupy. Jammy raspberry flavors meld well with the sweetness, without overpowering, while the slight tartness that it offers makes for a nice segue to a gently placed smacking of limey hops. A strong toffee forms at the end as the beer warms, providing that "sucking on a candy" experience. Gummy here and there. Crackery and bone-dry in the finish.

Interesting release, but nothing overly exciting and a bit too sweet and cloying at times. That said, this will no doubt appeal to fruit-beer lovers and maltheads, and provide some crossover potential for newbies to beer.

This beer doubles up as both a winter beer, and a New England local, as I am in the process of reviewing as much of each as possible.

A: Nice fluffy 2 finger head that slowly fades with large bubbles floating on top. A semi clear copper dark brown color, like melted brown sugar, adds a nice touch.

N: Nose has lots of caramelized brown sugar, and raspberries. A slight sweet darker malt, some very light roasted aromas, start the nose. That is accompanied with a big raspberry aroma, the kind you get from raspberry lemonade when the bartender went trigger happy with the raspberry flavoring. A slight crisp ale aroma tails it.

T: Starts with a big roasted malt taste. Its a lot more like a porter than anything. A slight brown sugar taste comes along with the toffee and roasted malt flavor. A dominant raspberry flavor goes right along with it. A slight creamy flavor with touches of chocolate add to it.

M: Medium body, somewhat thick and roasty, slightly velvety, and decent carbonation.

F: Finishes semi clean. Semi dry with a little sweetness. The lingering roasted flavor just lingers a bit. The fruit flavors, namely raspberry, fades pretty quickly. A little chocolate covered raspberry really hangs around giving a nice flavor that reminds me of the candy bars my grandmother used to give me. A nice clean beer, drinks well, but the raspberry might be a little too much, and the roasted flavor is a nice surprise that tastes pretty good.
