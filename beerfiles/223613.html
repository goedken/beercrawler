BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Trillium Brewing Company
Massachusetts, United States
trilliumbrewing.com

Style: American Double / Imperial IPA

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 8.00%

Availability: Limited (brewed once)

Notes / Commercial Description:
Scaled Way Up features heavy doses of Galaxy, Mosaic, and Nelson Sauvin in multiple dry hops. Straddling the aromatic line between dank/earthy and sweet/fruity. The flavor profile is jammy and fruit forward, consisting of candied peach and orange notes, and has powerful, sticky resin on the palate. Light in body with a bold bitterness upfront and crisp, bone-dry finish, Scaled Way Up is explosively aromatic.

MALT: Pilsner, White Wheat, Flaked Wheat, Dextrine, Dextrose, C-15

HOPS: Galaxy, Mosaic, Nelson Sauvin, Columbus

TomMorris, May 02, 2016
4.87/5  rDev +5.4%
look: 4.5 | smell: 5 | taste: 5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.75

Bottled 4/8/2016.

Appearance - Pours a hazy, somewhat turbid look with a very pale yellow. The lighter shade is a little unusual for a beer of this magnitude, but it melds smoothly with the pure white head. The head itself is fizzy and bubbles up quickly before rapidly dissipating.

Aroma - The nose is incredibly pungent and fruity. This may be one of the most aromatic and tropical IPAs that I've ever smelled that didn't have any fruit added to it. Huge bursts of pineapple, peach, and passion fruit lead the way with a nice hint of malt biscuit underneath to add a bit of balance.

Taste - Much like the nose, the taste is straight juice. The tropical notes are fresh and light. I wish there'd be a little more complexity, but the hops do their best to sustain the profile. The peach definitely moves to the foreground and sets the tone.

Mouthfeel - The mouthfeel is medium-bodied with lowish carbonation. It is a little soft on the palate but I probably would've liked some more heft to it to beef up the flavors.

Closing Arguments - Overall this is a fantastic IPA and worthy of praise. The hops are in the spotlight and live up to the billing. It's a rare feat to get this much aroma and flavor without being bitter or grassy, but Trillium nailed it.
