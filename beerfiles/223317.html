Colorado, United States
newbelgium.com

Style: Fruit / Vegetable Beer

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.40%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
No need to jet to Antwerp when you can open up a bouquet of Flowering Citrus Ale. Thanks to our friends at De Koninck Brewery, we brewed up a summery ale bursting with key lime and whole lemons that mingle with the fragrance of hibiscus, rose petals and strawberry-tinged Mistral hops. Open up and let Belgian summer bloom.

From notes taken 08/24/16:

Perfectly clear straw gold or golden amber. Looks about like a “White IPA,” maybe a little darker. Fluffy and delicate pure white head, with great retention and patchy legs. Grass, lime (as much sweet as sour) aromas, even notes of yeast and an alcoholic burn. Very citrus-tasting, but so much more than a glorified shandy. Lemon to lime throughout, both sweet and sour, perfect balance, nothing artificial, not unnecessarily sweet. Pasta suggestions. With grainy, yeasty retro that works well with heat. Like eating a slice of oven-fresh french bread. Hops are there, rindy if anything but easy to ignore. Before reviewing, I tried to guess what they might classify this as. Sure doesn’t taste much like a Belgian Strong Pale Ale, but that was my guess for lack of other options. American Golden/Blonde is a close second, but with collaboration beers, things are different. Apart from all-natural, quality citrus, and presumably the brewing process, the reasons this isn’t just a shandy, are the alcohol levels and the mouthfeel. Plenty warm, medium to full in body, finest spritzy suds.

Billolick, Oct 04, 2016
3.96/5  rDev +5%
look: 3.5 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

Light limony yellow color with white cap and lacing.

Aroma of fresh cut flowers from a bed next to a citrus grove. Flowers and lemons aren't the heavy hitters though, I find the yeast and hops most prominent yet neither really show up later.

With the first sip I thought I was drinking flowers. Citusy, floral, slightly tart, bright acidity, lightly malty with an uplifting sense of airness. Very pleasant brew that to me doesn't really taste like beer but that's not what it's designed to do. We don't expect the floating notes from a violin to have the deep percussion of a piano so we accept things for what they are. This is different and I applaud the effort. There is a slight nutty roundness in the aftertaste ending with just a prick of hops which is really the first time I noticed hops in the flavor.

Enough texture to call it medium, there is malt body rising up on gossamer wings.

Sort of the antithesis of a heavily flavored stout, this is an exceptional brew from the other end of the spectrum. In appearance, flavor and follow through it has the lightness of an airy summer day but at 7.4% it's a baritone singing the lyric tenor line. This brew combines skill, complexity and an almost audacious presentation like the mailed fist of an armoured knight presenting a bouquet of flowers to his lady love. Tenderness without weakness served up with gentle strength.

Had again in late October (after the Best By date) and I again enjoyed it so much that I now will write a review.
The first time in June, Flowering Citrus was more than pleasant. It went well with pasta with pesto and a mozzarella and tomato salad. Obviously, a great summer beer with seasonal food.

So nice to have a beer that plays nice with all aspects. I look forward to this being released again next summer.
And Bravo to New Belgium for collaborating and cross-fertilizing with De Konnick, a classic.

Overall, a good beer and worth having during a hot summer's day. The uniqueness of this beer definitely should make everyone's try list.
