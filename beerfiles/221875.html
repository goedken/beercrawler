Style: American Wild Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 4.50%

Availability: Limited (brewed once)

Notes / Commercial Description:
Tropic Punch Ale is a joint effort of Stillwater Artisanal and Dugges Ale in Sweden. This sour ale is brewed with Mango, Passion fruit and Peach, and fermented with lactobacillus.

Drank from an 11.2 fl oz bottle purchased at Fort Point Market, Boston, MA
24-05-2018
Served in a tulip

Hazy, bright straw yellow appearance. The beer was capped by a frothy, finger-tall, bone white crown that quickly fell to a thin collar. Spotty, streaky lacing trickled along the glass.

The nose was amazingly fruity and fragrant. Super tropical and exotic. Peach notes came through strongest. This almost smelled like straight up peach nectar. Mango was noticeable as well. Good amount of lacto-like sourness. Hint of lemon too.

Lots of intense fruitiness also came through in the flavor profile. Here, however, the fruity notes seemed somewhat more muddled, but in an interesting, fruit punch-like way. Peach was definitely noticeable. Bit of mango. I might have guessed strawberry and some type of citrus too. Somewhat wheaty and lemony. The beer finished with a pleasant lacto sourness and just a touch of funk.

The mouthfeel was medium bodied for the style and contained a fine and subtle yet spritzy effervescence. Crisp and somewhat prickly on the palate.

Fruit infused wild ales, Goses, IPAs, etc. are becoming an almost all-to-common thing today, but there are a few examples that definitely stand out from the pack. Tropic Punch Ale is definitely one of these. The fruit infusions add amazing tropical flavors without overshadowing the base beer. I really hope this is brewed again next summer and that it makes it into larger format bottles or four packs.

GreesyFizeek, Apr 09, 2016
4.35/5  rDev +1.4%
look: 3.75 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.25

A quick-soured, fruited wheat beer clocking in at under 5%... hmm, without even opening this I can tell this is a designer summer beer. Though it has a hefty price tag for a little 11.2 oz bottle (I paid almost $6 for this li'l guy!), it could be very worth it.

Pours a completely hazed yellow-gold color with a rowdy, rocky, white-foamy head that dissipates over the course of a minute to absolutely nothing. Lacto-soured beers without Brett tend to have crappy head retention, and this is no exception. It basically just looks like juice in the glass, which is quite apt...

... because this just smells like fruit juice! Tangy peach candy and brightly-tropical fleshy mango are the main notes here and they sing positively perfectly. I'm almost amazed this is a beer and not a wine cooler or something. This isn't my first rodeo with this kind of idea and I'm still taken aback. This melds so many tantalizing aromas together with just a slight kiss of wheat malt... I'm just incredulous as to how they managed to squeeze this much true, unadulterated fruit into this one. Well done, guys!

Flavor is perfectly thirst-quenching with a wonderful acidity and semi-sweet malty base backing up the tropical fruit punch as advertised by the beer's name itself. Tart berry notes also seem to puncture through the hazy tropical and citric depths here, bringing to mind white cranberry, gooseberry and raspberry as well. Peach is less prominent on the tongue than in the nose, I think, but it trades off with the mango and passionfruit here quite nicely.

Sharp and clean feel with prominent acidity and semi-long-lasting linger on the tongue. Acid profile is very well-rendered with a sourness that comes off as supportive to the fruit flavors and not just dull and present like many "kettle-soured" lacto-ales these days. It feels like a nice player in the action here, working the palate, along with the spiny and quick-acting carbonation, to discover new and exciting flavors. This just seems very well-done. Syrligt was also quite good so I'm thinking these guys at Dugges have quite a future ahead of them if they can break into the US craft beer scene more, and I'm thankful of Brian @ Stillwater for allowing us to enjoy some of their work... looking forward to more!
