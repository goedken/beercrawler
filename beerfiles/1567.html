A: pours to a 1 finger head with some lingering froth over an amber body.
S: subtle pumpkin notes with an light touch of nutmeg. Very light on the spice smell.
F:Soft small bubbles make for a smooth drink of medium body.
T:Some pumpkin seems to strike first but very well integrated with the malt/grain the follows. A trace of nutmeg through out like a guide. Slight tart flavor from hops or pumpkin? despite that finishes clean no lingering flavors, very tidy.
O: I feel this is a decent beer with a touch of pumpkin and nutmeg. It is not sweet or a spice bomb. They give you a beer with a light touch of something different. I would perfer a stronger roasted pumpkin flavor but this isn't bad.

look: 4 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

12oz bottle poured into a pint glass.

A: Red-orange color with good clarity. Bubbly off-white head stands at a half inch and leaves behind clumps of lacing.

S: Gourdy pumpkin, cinnamon and nutmeg, light grain and caramel, floral hops. Simple, but not bad.

T: Cinnamon and nutmeg are more forward, pumpkin flavor and sweet malts mingle. Barley and hints of candy shell and caramel. Soft hop bitterness. Grain and fading spices on the finish.

M: Medium body. Soft and creamy. Moderate carbonation. Smooth and easy.

O: It really isn't bad. It's well balanced actually, with the spices and pumpkin flavor feeling natural and well-integrated. Easily drinkable too. I think I've just hit the point where it's impossible for me to care about a pumkin beer at all.
