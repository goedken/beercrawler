Mpontiakos, Aug 28, 2016
4.69/5  rDev +3.8%
look: 4.75 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 4.75

Picked this up in New York not too long ago, as the "canned on" date on the bottom was 6/17. Of course, I rolled this before opening up today, and thankfully, there wasn't anything on the bottom of this as the hop particles were suspended! Instructions aside, this was a fantastically wonderful beer that outshone nearly every other northeastern IPA that I've recently had, with the hop and juice flavors meshing wonderfully together over an incredibly smooth, slick, and wet mouthfeel.

Really wonderful pour to this as the beer had a glowing, hazy apricot hue to it with a wonderful shine in spite of the translucency present here. Nice, big bubbles in the head that slowly settled down although the lacing wasn't as voluminous as I would have been led to believe. Wonderful aroma of orange, tangerine, mango, and passionfruit with so much more in the taste to augment those flavors. Apricot, stone fruit, nectar, and a smooth and full sweetness underneath that really made this beer shine as the hop profile ensured that this had enough booze, earthy bitterness, resin, and burn to counterbalance everything else that was going on here.

This felt a bit stronger than it was in actuality, because there was so much going on here. Can't say that I can think of a beer that had a better fruit profile to it than this one and a sweetness that didn't feel too overt or forced. As close to nectar as one could find, this was easily Grimm's best offering and worthy enough to stand toe-to-toe with the best from Tree House, Trillium, or any other IPA that tops most lists. Shame I only found one can of this!

2016 release
Growler fill, no date available, but released in mid June so this is roughly 1 month old

Appearance- Pours a bright, deep golden yellow color, very hazy. Finger thick, frothy white colored head, tightly packed bubbles. Lots of spotty head lacing.

Aroma- Bright citrus juices, mostly orange juice, sweet and creamy. Tropical fruit notes of pineapple, mango, papaya. Strawberry kiwi. Zesty, lemony sweetness. A touch of dankness.

Taste- Loads of citrus, orange juice, tangerine, sweeter grapefruit, orange creamsicle, touch of zesty orange peel and lemon. Smooth, juicy pineapple, mango, peach. Bright berry notes, juicy blueberries. Zesty, dank, herbaceous on the finish.

Feel- Light bodied feel, but very soft, smooth, and creamy. Just a mild zesty bitterness on the finish, but doesnt linger on. Moderate carbonation level.

Overall- Just an excellent IPA. Juiciness on top of juiciness. Citrus comes through the most, but its just wave after wave of citrus and tropical fruit notes, and then a dash of lemon. Has the classic soft mouthfeel. Zestiness of the finish provides a great balance.

ClavisAurea, Jul 01, 2016
4.73/5  rDev +4.6%
look: 5 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 4.75

16oz can into a NEBCO chalice.

Appearance: Fully hazed orange/yellow with a longlasting frothy white head and sweeping lacing.

Smell: Guava, Citra-smokiness, lime, some sweet stonefruit. Bright and inviting. Mildly pungent but not dank. As usual with Grimm, more succulent citrus mixed with berries and flowers than greenhouse/marijuana. Smells awesome. Melon, some banana. Every time I bring the cup to my lips and pick up the scent, I'm taken aback by the strong, well-integrated aroma--it's like the various hops coalesce into some kind of mega citrus fruit, characterized by lime, banana, and melon.

Taste: Follows the nose, big hit of banana-tinged melon, big Citra lime, same Citra smokiness that I think I first noticed in NEBCO Fuzzy Baby Ducks and am always happy to find in Citra beers. Fruity and soft, VERY juicy, with an ever changing chorus of berry and citrus notes. Some grainy pale malt in the finish, welcome.

The taste is excellent. Maybe not as "world class" as the aroma, but that's very nitpicky/subjective. Definitely without any off flavor whatsoever. Could be a touch more bitter for my taste, though I get the whole "Northeast style 100% late hopping" thing. This is a beer made by and for people who like Heady Topper, no doubt.

Mouthfeel: medium bodied, low side of moderate carbonation, semi-dry finish. Honestly, I consider the carbonation level and the not-dry-enough-ness to be flaws, but it's *so* satisfyingly juicy that it gets enough extra credit to push the minuses back up to a 4.75. This juiciness is just perfect with this particular flavor and beautiful aroma; it really makes the beer sing.

Overall: Grimm just keeps getting better and better. My only complaint is that there are still more 4 packs at the place I got this, and they cost $20 each. How am I not supposed to stock up on this?
