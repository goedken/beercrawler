rblib, Dec 19, 2015
4.96/5  rDev +10.5%
look: 4.75 | smell: 5 | taste: 5 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 5

Appearance: One of the lighter looking showers IPA's and pretty damn crystal clear. Small white head that thins out fairly quickly. But Jesus, seriously, this yellow gold brew looks like a pilsner to me right about now.

Aroma: Holy Moly Guacamole. No, that isn't the aroma stupid ass, this smells like vehement amounts of guava / guayaba juice, nectar, everything in the name of Guava. After a while you start smelling a slight whiff of piney green action but nothing like other Mosaic hopped IPA's such as St. Archer's Mosaic and Hangar 24's Betty.

Taste: Ultra sweet melon and just an intense hoppyness to it all over the palate. Sweet ass juicy fruit finish all over the tip and sides of the tongue and just awesome guava flavor all over the middle of the mouth. mmmm. Again, I say this is a subdued bitterness all around as w/ many of the Noble Ale Works showers series beers (such as galaxy and citra). I receive very little mango flavors although the bottle describes it being prominent but I do detect that cantaloupe essence and a sort of generic sweet syrup or sugar or sweetner type thing but not in an off-putting malty bullshit kind of way. I'm still searching that damn watermelon but I may sense like a watermelon bubblegum thing in the aftertaste.

Mouthfeel: Definitely makes it to about medium mouthfeel for the Double IPA style. The carbonation is fresh, bubbly and effervescent throughout. It's a pleasant mouthfeel and feels refreshing despite the pretty strong ABV.

Overall: This is a sexy, amazing and delicious double IPA, amazingly flavorful and complex for being hopped with only Mosaic. Im trying super hard to think of constructive criticism. the only thing I can think of is that its slightly cloying at the back end but its barely anything negative when you consider the abv, and the amount of hops that are probably put into this. I was also thinking that the beer could've used a bit more of a luscious and velvety mouthfeel but as I keep drinking it I notice the mouthfeel is top notch and super ideal. This is a world class brew and deserves many awards and a serious reputation.

4.4/5  rDev -2%
look: 4 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

22 oz bomber to pint glass

A: Classic copper-tan with a chilled haze, a small wispy head quickly dissipates without a trace.

S: Hop-forward bouquet with intense tropical suggestions. Mosaic hops give off all sorts of unique nuances, with green apples, melon and mango all rolled into one, floral profile. A bit of a sweet, grassy snap at the edge of the bouquet, masking much of the alcohol.

T: The flavors are more singular - hop-forward and fruity, but there aren't as many nuances as in the nose. There's still a strong tropical undertone, met nicely by caramelly malts and a drying booze. Everything harmonizes well, making this really easy to quaff. Medium finish with little lingering residuals.

M: Medium bodied, nicely oily-slick.

One of the better big IPA's I've had in a long time. I'll be seeking this out whenever it comes around.

I've had Nelson and Galaxy and both were good, favor goes toward Galaxy though. Let's see what Mosaic is talking about. First she pours that classic Californian Sunset orange with the nice white clouds crowning her glory! Hazy as all get out yet looks phenomenal to me! Let's get with the aroma. Huge floral notes step up first with notes of under ripe stone fruits. It's a mesh of Lavender and Mango/Peaches with some hop spice in the background. Malts are sweet in nature with that crackerish vibe. Very, very entertaining! Now for the taste. The first wave is of floral nature causing bitterness to creep on the palate. Then flows with malt sweetness that's carrying some dried mango fruitiness. There's a cracker flavor from the malts toward the finish. This finishes semi-dry with a clean bitterness that lingers for a while. Aftertaste of Floral and Hop Resin with faint booze. The body on the brew is medium-light and carbonation is on point helping the flavors to pop on the palate.

Overall: Color me a fan of Mosaic Hops! The aroma is off the fugging charts! The flavor is just as great except some booze, yet there's enough going on in the beer that makes you forget about it. I enjoy this brew, Noble nails a hop variety with a passion. Next, I want to see Noble do a DIPA with Mosaic and Galaxy hops. With all this said, this brew is Recommended!
