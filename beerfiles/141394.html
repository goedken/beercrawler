Kurmaraja, Aug 20, 2015
5/5  rDev +9.2%
look: 5 | smell: 5 | taste: 5 | feel: 5 | overall: 5

I was so looking forward to trying this beer. Over the past year, I've driven to Perennial many times for different events, and I've always talked to the locals about what their favorite Perennial beer is, and many of them have said Maman. So understand the excitement when I learned that bottles of this highly touted beer were actually going to be sold to go.

This beer pours the blackest I've ever seen. Even before filling the glass, I thought to myself how dark it was coming out of the bottle. No real head to speak of. An initial smell fills the nostrils with a strong bourbon impression. As it warms, the chocolate scent blends well with the bourbon characteristics. The tasting was very similar. The first sip gives a strong bourbon presence that gently flows into a dark chocolate aftertaste that lingers on the palate for quite some time. Throughout the drink, the flavor profiles would ebb and flow. Some sips would be chocolate forward, while others would have the initial bourbon profile.

Overall this is a masterpiece of a beer. There are plenty of barrel aged stouts, and many more that have other flavors on top of the barrel aging, with varying degrees of improvement over the base. This is just a "base" barrel aged stout, and the flavors are outstanding.

4.72/5  rDev +3.1%

On tap at Brasserie V in Madison, Wisconsin. Full pour served in a snifter glass. Also another pour from the 2015 bottle at a later tasting.

Dark black body, ominous and oily. Has a fair amount of legs. Minimal carbonation as expected, a small wispy collar remains after a while. No lacing. Smells of rich deep dark chocolate, oak, whiskey, milk chocolate, cocoa. No char or much roast to it; just very clean chocolate and whiskey-forward aromas. No heat. Flavour is similar; rich chocolate fudge, whiskey without any heat whatsoever, oak, minimal nutty flavours, milk chocolate. Again, very fudge-forward with a fair amount of whiskey flavour without any hint of heat or char. This reminds me a lot of yellow wax Assassin in many ways; an extremely clean and well-executed top shelf barrel aged stout. Mouthfeel is almost perfect; thick, creamy and coats the entire tongue in whiskey fudge goodness. I think this is the strongest aspect of the beer. Overall, the closest comparison I can make is yellow wax Assassin, and this may or may not be better than that. Anyway, this is an excellent barrel-aged stout, and easily one of the best I've had.

4.41/5  rDev -3.7%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

On tap
Pours dark black with an oily tan head that settles quickly without lacing. The nose is straightforward for a BA stout from perennial - raisins when cold, but a slight fruitiness that persists, marshmallow-like sweetness as it warms, cocoa & vanilla notes, booze in the background - but definitely hits hard like a bcs compared to other BA stouts on the market looking for that one night stand with your palate. This beer drinks with the barrel in mind bringing a carmelized sweetness upfront and finishing with a punishing amount of oaky tannic bitterness in the finish. It drinks with a deceptive full-bodied, lowly carbed, velvety smoothness, but has just the right amount of ethanol burn to complement the oaky bitterness that lingers. A fantastic beer, glad to get pours on draft.
