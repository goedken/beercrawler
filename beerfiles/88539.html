Best red IPA I've ever had.

Dan_K, Nov 04, 2015
4.02/5  rDev -0.7%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

This IPA is a nice ruby brown color with just the slightest of hazes; it is capped by a giant pebbly ancient-ivory-colored head that has reasonable retention and leaves nice rafts and ladders of lacing. It has a strong nose: bright, tangy grapefruit shines over a less-intense blend of pine and floral notes, with hints of bready malt and antiseptic alcohol. Up front, the palate is very bitter pine, and quite dry. By mid-palate the floral notes emerge, combining with the pine to create a very assertive, very bitter IPA soapiness, and a less pronounced bittersweet nuttiness and peeks of the bready malt foundation are seen as well. The finish is fairly clean, with just a bit of soapy nuttiness left behind, and is prickly from the strong carbonation. After the finish, a very assertive resinous hoppy bitterness sets in and clings for ages. The mouthfeel is a bit biggish for the light-medium body, and the texture is moderately creamy and very peppery from the carbonation. On the dryer end for a red IPA. (11.2 oz bottle, 69 days after pkg.)

Poured from the bottle into a Spiegelau IPA glass. Bottled August 15th 2015.

Deep mix of ruby and orange red body colors, excellent amounts of fine carbonation coming up from the glass in fine threads. Head is light tan almost yellow with a big clumping four fingers. Has a real creamy top, nice and four fingers tall and a soupy retention quality, but with good staying power. Creamed whipped up icing, sliding top, and soapy prospects with great appearances.

Nice mix of soft caramel, almost getting into toffee realm. Hints of strangely smoke and bbq but ever so slight. IPA angle for hops seems to be fairly closed though, perhaps a bit of woody pine but really reaching. Great aroma regardless.

Real hard to describe palate. This really travels in odd directions. Mid palate starts out light and classic malt amber, then gives to almost hints of caramel smoke and char. Controlled sweetness comes in a bit on the swallow but then a big giant slow creeping hop blasting finish of earth and pine needles just sneaks up on you. It nearly feels like a five alarm fire. Light and pale ale bodied like feel for easy drinking.

Definitely feels tyrannic.. And very subversive.. Neat take on an amber IPA.
