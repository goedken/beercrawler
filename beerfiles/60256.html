muttyd, Apr 18, 2011
3.68/5  rDev -1.1%
look: 4 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Sunny honey with a swirling cloud of yeast being buffeted by multiple streams of energetic carbonation. The pearl colored crown is a thin disk of cream that lasts long enough and leaves enough lace to deserve the good score.

The nose features golden grains, golden fruit and less spice than anticipated. A bona fide Belgian strain was used, but it doesn't smell like a very estery sort. Here's hoping that doesn't have negative implications on the taste buds.

Belgian Style Golden Ale is not a humdinger of a Belgian-style golden ale, but it's a damn fine effort for the boys from St. Paul. It's a bit rough around the edges, even with about 8 months of aging. Then again, that gives it a sort of rustic charm that is appealing to big beer lovers like me.

It would be grossly unfair to compare this Unchained Series offering to malt liquor, but it does have a sweet grain-like quality that is usually covered up by fruit and spice in the best versions of the style. Again, more fruit and spice yeast esters would be great.

Czech Saaz and Styrian Goldings are still contributing a healthy amount of grassy-herbal flavor, especially in the second half of each mouthful. In addition, it tastes and feels like most of the original 30 IBUs are still alive and kicking. It's mighty tempting to pull the trigger on 4.0 for flavor.

The mouthfeel is solid. It's lightly lush, never flags for an instant, and has zesty bubbles that are never in danger of crossing the line into annoyingly buzzy. A tongue tingler for sure.

Summit Belgian Style Golden Ale doesn't quite rival the standards from Belgian, but it's an enjoyable ale that gives its all in each and every mouthful. A little tweaking and this could be something special. Hey Summit, you are going to brew this one again, right? Thanks to feloniousmonk for the bottle.

ZAP, Aug 13, 2010
4.18/5  rDev +12.4%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

Transparent (though, clearer than I'd like) bright, golden hued, huge head, well-carbonated, lush and creamy foam, like a good dollop of ice cream, slow to descend.

Aroma: light hops, some spice, and delicate fruit. Lemon, pear, apple, even. Fresh, crisp, vibrant. Bright and beautiful.

Taste: some initial grab and grit playing on the palate, then all is smooth and clean. Pilsner malt easily discerned, some small amount of graininess. Body is light, and the mouthfeel grows in heat and complexity. More and more spice and gentle fruit, growing in flavor.

For this tasting, of a beer I've had several times on tap, and gone through a six-pack of at home, I decided to crack open a bottle of Duvel, standard for the style, and compare. More carbonation in the Duvel, bigger yeast action, but, oddly, the Summit has a more pronounced portion of the same flavors the Duvel has. Could be good, could be bad, depending on your point of view. Duvel's appeal is it's simple complexity and deep drinkability. The Summit lacks the spritzy carbonation, the champagne effect, but gives slightly more in the flavor department.

What's the difference? I'm no expert, but maybe a little more malt, a little more yeast, a lot of freshness, given that this was just brewed from over the river, rather than shipped over from Belgium.

This beer does not surprise me as a Summit beer. It tastes like a Summit beer. And that's great. I still wait for the Summit beer that takes me totally by surprise. It'll happen.

But, what really surprises me is that I can enjoy this so much better than a Duvel. They're two different takes on a similar style, but I find I favor the flavor of the Summit more. More pilsner-y, yet more flavorful. Lacks the carbonation and zest, but
flavor wins over.

And the drinkability is really high, despite the 9% abv.

So, cheers for Summit and the Unchained Series, keep them going, can't wait for more!
