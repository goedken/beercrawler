Rogue Ales
Oregon, United States
rogue.com

Style: American IPA

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.50%

Availability: Year-round

Notes / Commercial Description:
Rogue Cold Brew IPA is a hoppy IPA blended with 200 gallons of Stumptown Coffee Roasters’ famous Cold Brew Coffee in every batch. Brewed to awaken your senses, Cold Brew IPA opens with a huge hit of coffee aroma that is balanced by a not-so-subtle hop punch.

StonedTrippin, Aug 12, 2016
4.21/5  rDev +6.3%
look: 4 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

650ml bottle poured into a sniffer.

Hazy deep amber brown in color, it tops with thick creamy off-white head that endures with good retention before gradually subsiding to some frothy film and sheet on the surface along with plenty of lingering and refined lacing ring and patch.

Really nice coffee forwards in the aroma, you will also get sweet caramel from malt as well as brown bread and biscuit together with hazelnut, vanilla, somewhat nutty, and cinnamon. Mild fruity and citrus from hop can also be sensed through carefully notice under the wave of coffee. Very rich in coffee, it is obviously a kill for coffee lover.

Rich and bold coffee still predominates in the flavor like a cover sheet over the taste buds with its greatness providing a unique astringency, creamy, roasted, and bitterness profile. Hop tries to poke through in the middle layer of the taste and it does rather well with a citrusy and resiny bitterness. Malt is the last and most inner layer providing a sweet bready note as well as caramel, hazelnut, some nuttiness, woodiness, and cinnamon. Finish is all what you can expect from good IPA with a long solid pleasant bitterness, pungent, and astringent from both coffee and hop.

Medium body perhaps with a bit chewy mouthfeel, medium carbonation through the mouthfeel but has some crisp quality to well carry its taste and body, finish is very dry from the astringency.

Bold coffee greatness mixes with good hop quality and quality malt in support, the result is that excellent brew. If you like coffee this one is for you, if you like IPA this one is for you, and if you like good beer this one is also for you.

Coffee is definitely the dominant player here. however, it finishes clean to reveal more of the hoppy notes and some nice bready malts. Notes: This is not for everyone. The contrast might be a bit much for some but it works for me. The beer profile is exactly as labeled. Result/Rating: Very good. 4 out of 5.
