12oz bottle @ Rishi's in Grapids. No specs per the website or bottle. "Dark ale made with candi sugar and orange peel".
Pours out to a brilliantly clear deep brown, forming a small white head with minimal retention and no lacing. The aroma is quite mild and reminds me of that fruity golden ale you get at every brewpub, in other words, more yeast esters than hops or malt. Pink bubblegum, with the suggestion of crystal 120, that's it. The mouthfeel is cola-like, with a medium-light body. The taste starts with the pink bubblegum and then is interrupted by a vapid oxidized burnt rubber, phenols, with a very mild bitterness and a dry finish. Weird cider and higher alcohol tang. More burnt rubber in the aftertaste.

Basically, this beer was an afterthought and a mistake. This is the first bad beer I've had from oddside. Definitely pass on it. Down the sink it goes.

3.68/5  rDev +21.5%
look: 4 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

12 ounce bottle into pint glass, bottled on 11/24/2014. Pours fairly crystal clear dark ruby red color with a 1 finger dense light khaki head with good retention, that reduces to a small cap that lingers. Light spotty soapy lacing clings on the glass, with some moderate streaming carbonation. Aromas of caramel, brown sugar, toast, brown bread, orange peel, raisin, plum, light chocolate, light banana, clove, pepper, and herbal/yeast earthiness. Nice and pleasant aromas with good complexity and solid balance of dark/bready malt, spice, and yeast ester notes; with solid strength. A bit too spice forward in the aromas, but not bad. Taste of caramel, brown sugar, toast, orange peel, brown bread, light chocolate, clove, pepper, light raisin/banana, and herbal/yeast earthiness. Fair amount of earthy yeast spiciness on the finish; with lingering notes of caramel, brown sugar, toast, orange peel, brown bread, light chocolate, clove, pepper, light raisin/banana, and herbal/yeast earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Nice complexity and robustness of dark/bready malt, spice, and yeast ester flavors; with a good malt/yeast/spice balance and zero cloying flavors after the finish. Medium carbonation and body; with a smooth, fairly crisp, and lightly creamy/bready mouthfeel that is nice. Alcohol is well hidden with zero warming present after the finish as expected of 5%. Overall this is a nice Belgian brown ale style. All around good complexity, balance, and robustness of dark/bready malt, spices, and yeast ester flavors; and very smooth and crisp to drink. A nicely enjoyable offering.

superspak, Jan 03, 2015
3.57/5  rDev +17.8%
look: 4 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.25

I picked up a bottle of Odd side Ale's The Merry Elf last week for $1.99 at The Lager Mill. Well since it's getting close to Christmas I thought I would break out the holiday beers and this one seems like a slightly different take on a winter warmer, so lets see how it goes. 2014 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a snifter.

A- The label looks nice, it goes well with the name and it's a little eye catching. It poured a fairly clear mahogany color that took more of crimson and ruby color when held to the light with a finger and half worth of creamy tan head that died down to a thin ring that stayed till the end and it left some spotty lacing behind. This was a good looking beer, I have no complaints.

S- The aroma starts off with a slightly higher amount of medium sweetness that has a light candied sugar like aroma to it and it's followed by some dark fruits with no single fruit seeming to stick out the most. Up next comes the malts which impart a caramel malt, sweet malt and toasted malt like aromas with some doughy yeast showing up in the background along with a somewhat lighter orange rind aroma. This beer had a pretty nice aroma, no single aspect felt too overpowering.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma, but a little lighter and it starts off with a lower amount of medium sweetness that has just a slight candied sugar aspect to it with no dark fruits showing up this time. Up next comes the malts which impart the same aspects that they did in the aroma with the toasted malts sticking out the most, but there's also a grainy flavor that shows up along with some doughy yeast in the background. On the finish comes just a slight orange rind flavor, but it's hardly noticeable and it has a lower amount of bitterness with a candied sugar and toasted malt like aftertaste. This was a nice tasting beer, but the flavors were lighter than I wanted them to be and the taste wasn't as complex as the aromas.

M- Smooth, fairly crisp, a little clean, on the lighter side of being medium bodied with a medium amount of carbonation. This beer had a pretty nice mouthfeel that worked well with it.

Overall I thought this was a decent beer, BA has this as a Winter Warmer and i find it to not really fit that category, but Oddsides called it a Dark Ale and as a Dark Ale it's pretty solid, but I don't think the featured ingredients brought much to this beer. This beer had ok drinkability, it was smooth, fairly crisp, a little clean, not filling and the taste didn't do the best job at holding my attention, I could drink more than one, but I would start to get bored with it so one is the perfect amount for me. What I like the most about this beer was the appearance, this was a good looking beer. What I liked the least about this beer was that the featured ingredients didn't bring much to the table. I might buy this again next year, but I think the three bottles I got are enough for this year and I would recommend this if you like Dark Ales, but if your looking for a Winter Warmer I think you will be disappointed. All in all I thought this beer was solid, but it left me wanting more from it and by the end of the glass I really started to get bored with it, it's nice for what it is, but it doesn't really stand out as a winter seasonal. This isn't close to my favorite beer from Oddsides and for a dark ale it's above average. I like that Oddsides decided to distribute a holiday seasonal this year, but in the end it wasn't that memorable, better lick next time guys.
