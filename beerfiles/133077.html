tone77, Jul 26, 2016
4.07/5  rDev +2.5%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

The beer pours out into a Duvel glass a lightly hazed brright golden color with fluffy white head that almost spills over the rim of the glass. The head recedes slowly leaving batches and long legs off lacing on the sides of the glass and a nice thin layer on the top of the liquid. Sipping cleans the side of the glass leaving behind just a few small scattered speckles

The aromas include some citrus, some fruity and floral notes all backed up by a spicy, almost peppery aroma from the yeast.

The flavors are much as expected from the nose with the citrus, fruity and floral notes supported by a white pepper of the spicy yeast. There is also a slight herbal character in the background.

The mouthfeel is medium bodied and slightly prickly with moderate carbonation. The finish begins about midway through as the lemon, fruity and floral flavors begin to fade a bit allowing both the spicy yeast to become more prominent before fading into the lightly herbal finish that eventually becomes dry.

My overall impression is that this is a very well made saison that is quite good and I could easily have another. While I won't make a special effort to seek out more of this one I'll certainly keep it on the "fine to buy" list and be happy to get some--a nice change of pace beer.

BigGold, Jun 11, 2016
4.21/5  rDev +6%
look: 3.5 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 4

I think that I recently picked this up at Hunger 'n' Thirst, especially since it is so fresh. It is a really beautiful addition to The CANQuest (tm) with its metallic gold color and wild graphics.

From the CAN: "Don't Look Back"; "The beautiful music of Orpheus had power over the living, could move inanimate objects, and even hold sway over the gods. Lyric Ale is an ode to this profound beauty, which guides everything we do. We use a blend of hops from three [CAN]tinents and our house saison yeast to strike this harmonic balance of fruit and spice flavors."; Art by Peter Ferrari.

Since Saisons CAN & may be cloudy, I agitated the beer while still in the CAN to put any lees back into suspension. The Crack! was a bit messy, though, as I got some immediate foaming through the vent. A quick Glug formed massive amounts of dense, rocky, bone-white foam that slowly CANverted back into liquid with just over a finger remaining before it, too, dissipated to wisps. Color was a cloudy Golden-Amber (SRM = > 5, < 7). Nose had a fruitiness like peaches, apricots and tangerines along with a light funk from the yeast. Mouthfeel was medium, but with a god bit of carbonation in the mouth. The taste was very fruity, very peach-forward, but I also got apricots & tangerine as well as a tangy bitterness, like biting into pith, probably from the yeast. Mmm. I usually do not like Saisons, but this was really yummy! Finish was dry, mostly as a result of the yeast having done its job, but it is also worth noting that the fruitiness never faded, which was a large part of its attraction for me.
