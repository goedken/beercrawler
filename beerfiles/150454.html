Poured from bottle into stout glass...slowly. This one's a gusher for sure. The brew had barely slid to the bottom of the glass when the head exploded into being, where a third bottle pour became a thick, fluffy head that grew and expanded near an inch above the rim of the glass. Orange with a pinkish hue, lots of bubbly carbonation that lasts and lasts.Head dissipates slowly into a rocky finger with a crazy amount of lacing. Out of curiosity I bit into the foamy head (yes, a proper bite) and it was thick, chewy and piney.
Aroma is pine and floral hops, definite red wine and something else, like wood or coconut husks. Most of the hop aroma I find is in the head, as it disappears, a sweet fruity aroma takes the forefront. Strawberries and bubblegum wrapped in grapefruit.
Once the head settles and I'm able to sample the drink proper, the bitterness that I was sure would be there is no where to be found. This is smooth and sweet, floral and malt sweetness with a red wine warmth that slides in smoothly on the back end.

TreyIsWilson, Jan 23, 2015
3.95/5  rDev -5%
look: 4.5 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 4 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 4

Bottling date rubbed off. $5 single. Poured into an over-sized Sam Adams Perfect Pint.

Appearance: Since I had a huge glass, I poured from basically a mile up straight into the glass -- the beer all got in, but the head almost reached the brim of the 24oz (?) glass. Super hazy/murky burnt orange brown-ish color. There's an initial head of about two or three fingers that slowly bubbles down and leaves lacing on the glass.

Smell: Quite unique with a good amount of stuff going on. There's a really nice -- somewhat unexpected, if I'm being honest -- bright grapefruit, grassy, and herbal hoppiness on top, and then a slightly-more-than-subtle red wine quality and bit of barrel underneath that imparts a bit of a coconut aroma. Very interesting and nice smelling beer, but I can't say my jaw's hitting the floor.

Taste/feel: Hits the tongue feeling a bit "flat" in flavor, in my opinion -- this brew is surprisingly... dare I say... subtle? Mellow? It tastes like a hoppy pale ale poured into a dry-as-can-be wine barrel. I know that's not a helpful description, but that's what it is. Hops on the front with a little malt, a lot of woody barrel qualities (with a slight coconut note that I also smelled), and just a little bit of red wine to make things a little more interesting. The finish has a biscuity malt flavor that works well. The carbonation on this beer is prickly and moderately high, and it's medium-to-full bodied.

My natural comparison to Beautiful Disaster is Lucid Dreams, which is also by Odd Side ... but a Dubbel. Then again, that beer had more of what I was expecting with the wine characteristics. That beer had more wine flavor, and Beautiful Disaster, I think, has more wine BARREL flavor. That's not necessarily a bad thing, I just came in expecting something a bit different. While Lucid Dreams had a significant wine character, Beautiful Disaster is more subtle. Worth a try, but I wouldn't say it's worth five bucks for a bottle.

SNAP EDIT: As it warms, the wine flavor comes through a little bit better, and the beer gets a little bit sweeter as a result. I've come to realize I drank about 3/4 or so of the bottle way too cold... it's tasting very good at whatever temperature it's currently at. I find the smell slightly less appealing, but the taste is wonderful -- the hops seem to suddenly have become less intense, and the wine flavor has snuck in. Bumping taste and overall by .25. I don't like this brew more than Lucid Dreams, but those who like more subtle wine flavors in their beers will probably love this one.

superspak, Jan 03, 2015
4.12/5  rDev -1%
look: 4 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

I picked up a bottle of Oddside's Beautiful Disaster yesterday at The lager Mill. I'm a big fan of both the beers make up this one and I'm very interested to see how it works with red wine barrels, so lets see how it goes. 2014 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a snifter.

A- The label looks ok, but it's a little plain looking and it isn't too eye catching. It poured a hazy orange color that took on more of a brighter orange and red color when held to the light with a fingers worth of slightly off white head that died down to a thin ring that stayed till the end and it left some spotty lacing behind. This was a good looking beer, I have no complaints.

S- The aroma starts off with a higher amount of medium sweetness with a big fruity aroma, I think most of it's from the hops which are very similar to the how they were in El Dankerino with a little more of juicy cirrus fruit aspect and it even has the slight coconut aspect that El Dankerino had and there's also some green and fresh hop aromas showing up in the background. I think some of that fruit comes from the red wine barrels which impart a noticeable fruity wine aroma, but it isn't real strong. Towards the end I get some sweet malts with just a slight doughy malt aroma. This beer had a great aroma, it was full of great fruity aromas and I really enjoyed it.

T- The taste seems to be pretty similar to the aroma and it starts off with a higher amount of medium sweetness with the hops still being the first to show up and they impart the same aspects that they did in the aroma with those juicy citrus fruit flavors sticking out the most with some tropical fruit and green hop flavors showing up in the background. Up next comes the wine barrels which are light and they impart more of a fruity wine taste than a barrel flavor. On the finish there's just a slight malty flavor with a higher amount of medium bitterness that doesn't linger for too long with a very hoppy aftertaste. This was a good tasting beer, I really like the hop profile in this beer, but i wish the red wine barrel showed up more and imparted more of oaky barrel aspect to this beer.

M- Fairly smooth, slightly refreshing, a little crisp, on thicker side of being medium bodied with a lower amount of medium carbonation. This beer had a good mouthfeel and it worked well with it.

Overall I thought this was a good beer and good example of the style, both the beers are good on their own, but when blended together it's a straight up onslaught of fruity hops and I really liked that, but there was still just a little room for improvement, especially when it came to the barrel aspect. This beer had nice drinkability, it was fairly smooth, slightly refreshing, a little crisp, not too filling and the taste did a pretty nice job at holding my attention, I think two would be a good amount for me. What I like the most about this beer was the aroma, it was great, I loved how fruity it was and it was the highlight of this beer. What I liked the least about this beer was the wine barrel aspect, it wasn't bad, but i wish it showed up more and imparted more of an oaky barrel aspect. I would buy this beer again and I hope they release this beer next year. I would recommend this to anybody who likes IPA's and DIPA's with fruity hops, don't let the wine barrels scare you off, they aren't that strong. All in all I was happy with this beer, the two beers worked together nicely and while the wine barrels didn't make a world of difference they for sure didn't hurt this beer or take away from the base. This is another good Oddsides beer and in the end it tastes pretty similar to DIPA. Good job guys, this was a pretty tasty beer, keep up the hard work.
