IMHO, Proclamation appears to be gaining more experience and skills with each batch. This is an outstanding IPA. To make a long story short, try this and any Proclamation beer.

Consumed 11/3/16

A: Golden at its core with some light orange and straw - hazy - pours with a small white head, fades to a small collar and a wispy cap

S: Unique hop profile, with some citrus but mostly green aromas - some orange and grapefruit flesh, floral citrus - leafy, herbal hops, with some resinous hops and grass - mint - in a word, green - on the mild side, though - pale malts, light bread - very clean

T: Follows the aroma, with some citrus flesh, orange juice, grapefruit zest - more of a pronounced pine flavor, with some pine and hoppy resin - herbal, leafy, grassy, minty - medium bitterness - light, pale malts, touch of bread dough - fairly neutral malts, very clean overall

M: Medium body, very smooth, medium carbonation, refreshing and very drinkable

O: I'm pretty sure hops don't have tendrils, but whatever

Tendril is an interesting IPA. It's an interesting blend of modern West Coast and East Coast influences. I would enjoy a bit more fruitiness, but at the very least it's ridiculously drinkable. Definitely worth a try.

Bought in an 16 oz. can dated 8/1/16 in Wakefield, RI. Poured into a tulip glass.
Hazy yellow, with little head that fades to naught. Some lacing.
Tangerine, citrus, and pine in the nose.
An appropriately piney, bitter, hop flavor dominates with an interesting and pleasant hint of sourness. As others have stated, no malt flavor here. But I like it.
This beer has a solid, chewy mouth feel I relish. Surprisingly drinkable for 7.5% ABV (perhaps because I just completed a full meal?). While I like and would certainly drink more of this, I don't (yet) agree with others who've asserted that it bests Grey Sail Captain's Daughter, one of my favorite American IPAs. I need a side-by-side comparison and will pursue this quest to be certain. (Life's tough!)

Rhode Island we have a gem here. Do the right thing. Drink local.

irishtdw, Apr 19, 2015
4.49/5  rDev +8.7%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

Updating this review and ranking, as I've had a few different batches of Tendril now. They've mostly been consistent, though recipe tweaks have me liking the new batches slightly more.

Appearance: hazy, slightly turbid looking yellow, with a fat, sudsy head atop. Lots of lacing.

Aroma: all hops, no malt here for me. Lots of tropical scent, but more of a dank, weedy scent than anything else. Like orange creamsicle mixed with dank weed.

Taste: Some tropical flavor, with peachiness, and a touch of citrus, though it is more rind than flesh for me. Not sure of the hop bill on this beer, but I'd assume simcoe is a part, given the bitter rind flavor, along with the piney, weed-like flavor present. Again, no malt at all really, the hops dominate the flavor here and it is delicious. Tons and tons of orange character, with peaches and weed mixed in. Kind of like a fruitier version of Bissell's substance.

Feel: medium bodied and sticky, this beer coats the palate and leaves a lingering dankness. Moderately carbonated.

Overall: I really like this beer. It's probably my favorite IPA out of RI, overtaking Captains Daughter with the most recently bottled batch. Seek it out!

In saying that I have high hopes for Proclamation and will continue to support them. I just won't be purchasing this enigma of a brew anymore.
