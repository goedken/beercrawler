jda007, Apr 11, 2007
3.89/5  rDev +3.5%
look: 4.5 | smell: 3 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.5

Wow! After a disappointing encounter with Young's St. Nick's Barley Wine, I had low expectations of what I thought would be a straightforward ale. But this one knocked my socks off - it would be a wonderful crossover to the English style for someone weaned on American ales.

16.9 oz bottle purchased at Draeger's in San Mateo, CA. Label updated from the photo currently shown on BA. "Best Before 01 Feb 07" (so I'm 8-9 weeks late, but that doesn't matter at all).

Poured chilled (very - just over 40 degrees) into a standard English pint glass an orangey brown, mostly clear, with a two finger head and a delightful pervasive effervescence - almost like a Belgian in an engraved tulip.

Initial aroma was wonderful - very full, almost aggressive English hops - I couldn't wait to spend half an hour experiencing it. But after a few minutes it got unpleasantly dull and musty - that hop edge vanished, leaving an almost soap-like character. Taste lives up the promise of the initial nose - loads of (English) hops leading. Not overly full on the malt, but still the right proportion. Finishes beautifully, lingers a bit, but no stickiness, leaving you clean for the next drink. And at just 5.2%, you could have several.

Kudos to Young's. A very nice beer - not a wimp at all - and a great introduction to the English style.
