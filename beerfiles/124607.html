From the CAN: Special Release [-] Limited Pick".

The Crack! was really loud, leading me to wonder if my hearing has changed since Friday night when I went to see MacSabbath. Hmm. I was able to CANduct an inverted Glug, bringing it right to the rim of the glass before being forced to back off. I had a brief finger of fizzy head, but it was almost pop-like and dissipated as quickly as it had risen. Color was Amber to Deep Amber/Light Copper (SRM = > 9, < 13) with NE-quality clarity. Nose smelled more like strawberry than cranberry. Hmm. The taste was lightly tart, but I was still getting strawberry as opposed to cranberry. I just re-read my initial review and I wonder if they did not mis-package this one. I will grant you that I have not had their Strawberry Ale, but this does not seem to be as tart as my original bottle review would indicate. I am not CANplaining, either. This was tasty without being cloying. Mouthfeel was thin-to-medium, just out of watery territory. Finish was semi-sweet and I would be willing to revisit this in a CAN if I could find a Strawberry version to drink as a CANterpart.

I was out & about this past week, scouring for new & different beers when my travels & travails took me to Highland Pizzeria. Score!

From the bottle: "Special Release [-] Limited Pick".

That may be the case, but it seems to be a rotating limited release.

No Pop! as it was a screw-top bottle, followed by a heavy-handed pour. No head to speak of, although it was very fizzy & effervescent initially. Nose smelled like Ocean Spray CranApple juice drink! Ha! I used to really like drinking that at my maternal grandmother's house as a kid. Color was Amber (SRM = >7, < 9) with a faint pinkish hue. Mouthfeel was kind of thin & watery. The taste was reminiscent of candy, like SweeTarts, where you get the commingling of the sweetness of the apple along the tartness of the cranberry. It's hard to fault them for truth in advertising! Poured from a 12oz bottle into a US tumbler pint glass.

A: The beer is a light pinkish gold color, with an extremely thin white head that fades almost instantly and leaves no lace on the glass.

S: The aroma contains mostly cranberries as you would expect along with some light caramelized malts.

T: The taste starts out with a sweet cranberry flavor and not the tartness that you typical get with cranberry-flavored beers. Then some mild breadiness comes in from a thin malt character. There is almost no hops presence and very little balance. The after-taste is sweet.

M: A little crisp and a little smooth, light body, medium-to-high carbonation, finish is slightly sticky.

O: A little flavorful due to the cranberry, goes down ok, not filling at all, mild kick, not the best representation of style, if youre in the mood for the cranberry flavors its not too bad but there seems to be a lack of beer-like qualities to it.

Poured from bottle into a pint glass

Appearance The beer pours a clear golden amber color as is seen through the clear bottle. Upon pouring there is a scant fizzy white head that fades in an instant leaving no lace on the sides of the glass.

Smell The aroma of the beer is pretty much of an apple cider like smell. With this overarching cider aroma are some light hints of grain and a little bit of cranberry.

Taste The taste begins with a strong apple cider taste that is on the rather sweeter side. As the taste advances a little bit of a cranberry flavor works its way onto the tongue with some of the sweetness fading ever so slightly. With the loss of the sweet and the cranberry coming to the tongue, a little bit of a more bitter and sour flavor balances some of the sweet. In the end, one is left with a rather sweet, but somewhat sour apple cider flavor to linger on the tongue.

Mouthfeel The body of the beer is very thin and light with a carbonation level that is average to a little bit on the higher side. For the cider like characteristics of the brew, the feel is rather appropriate and makes for a more crisp drinking style.

Overall Pretty much a lightly cranberry flavored cider, here with the cranberry hitting just at the end of the taste. If you like a somewhat sweeter but lightly soured cider then this may be for you, but if not looking for a cider, then you may want to avoid this one.
