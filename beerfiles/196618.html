16.9 oz. bottle poured into weizen glass
(born on Oct. 18, 2015 ... "291 15" printed on glass)

L: pale gold with slightly darker (amber) highlights, 90%-plus clear, only a slight mistiness; massive foamer ... almost fills the weizen glass with white/offwhite foam, a head 5 to 6 inches tall but dissipating fast and leaving no traces, no stick, no skin, only a hint of collar; there is some activity in the glass, bubbles in bunches of up to maybe 50 bubbles at a time rising fast throughout the column

S: musty dusty salt water; slight citrusy sweetness, lemonade or tart orange; as it warms, has an herb/garlic yeasty pizza crust note, the coriander adding something; wants to smell like pickle juice but pulls up well short ... it's not that sour

T: salty tart lemon/orange, grain, wet earth, lingering spice note from the coriander; yeast and grain; pinch of lime/lime salt

F: lightweight but filled out by the carbonation

O: fantastic gose with an interesting array of notes that change as the palate adjusts and the beer warms; it smells like a barn-musty farmhouse ale at first but the classic salty tart gose aroma takes over, while the flavor allows more coriander in as it warms; it's not cheap, but it's good enough that I'll grab another bottle or two

Smells like lemon, orange peel, lactic sour, and a bit of wheat.

This feels a little "dead" if that makes any sense, it just falls a little flat. The sourness is hidden behind a surprisingly forward malt character, bready and sweet. The sourness is pretty lemon forward, with a bit of a wheaty bitterness.

This is lighter bodied, pretty crisp, with a normal level of carbonation.

Not too bad, but definitely not one of Prairie's best.
