chicagoish, Feb 10, 2016
3.86/5  rDev +6.6%
look: 4.25 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

A- 12oz bottle into a tulip with a deep brown body with slight cuts of deep red at the edges. The off-white cap is very thick with a creamy texture and great retention. Very thick webs of lacing coat most of the glass.

S- Brown malt aromatics with an overall feel just beyond toasty but still not nearly Porter like. Deep caramel sweets, toasted nuttiness, citrus and pine hops, milk chocolate, roast barley and a hint of raisin come to mind. Balanced between hops and grains.

T- While citrus and pine were present in the aroma the hops really jump out to me here with orange, resin, floral and earthy character. The malts become slightly secondary but also deliver a strong punch of caramel, toasted nuts, brown sugar, cereal grains, chocolate and medium sweetness.

MF- Medium bodied coming off as fairly big with a chewy creamy and puffy texture. Carbonation at a moderately high level but the texture really does provide a padding and a base for the bold flavors. Hoppy bitter finish.

Bright citrus and pine hops layered onto a bold mix of brown to slightly roasty malts. Smooth coexistence between the hops and grains as sweetness and over-caramelized flavors aren't allowed to disrupt the balance, well played.

A: Poured from the bottle into a Piraat tulip/snifter-type glass. Body is hazy dark brown - more of a chestnut brown than chocolate brown - with a small light tan-colored head that leaves behind good sheets of lace.

S: Not much on the nose - though I may have poured this one a little too cold: modest grainy and toasty malt, some piney or earthy-type hops.

T: First thought: this is like a weaker version of Smuttynose's Durrty. It's a hoppy brown ale, no doubt, and a bigger version of a brown ale that tends towards a strong ale in some ways. There's toffee and caramel malt and toasty malt. Earthy, spicy and piney hops at the finish. Lingering bitterness over top of grainy malt. Touch of alcohol heat noted on the way down and in the belly.

M: Medium in heft, good carbonation.

O: A good beer. Doesn't really fit into any one category perfectly, however, as it's sort of like an American Brown Ale meets an American Black Ale (at least in hoppiness, though not color) meets an American Strong Ale.
