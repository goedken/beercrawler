BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Stillwater Artisanal Ales
Maryland, United States
stillwater-artisanal.com

Style: American Pale Lager

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 5.20%

Availability: Year-round

Notes / Commercial Description:
An evolution of the 'single hop' phenomenon. While Galaxy is featured on the label, the concept here is to showcase this headliner in it's greatest form possible, and if you ask me every hop is better when supported by others. So accents of Centennial & Sterling to widen the frequency and crank up the volume of bright tangerine, grapefruit, and fresh grass.

seedyone, Mar 10, 2016
4.06/5  rDev +2.3%
look: 4 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Picked this up a while back at the Whole Foods on Houston St in New York City, having never had this before. I love the other Contemporary Works by Stillwell and this held up well compared to those. Most American Pale Lagers tend to be light and watery but this had great balance and was easy to crush, even though I had only bought one bottle!

Nice pour to this as the beer had a golden hue with some haziness to it and very fluffy head that stayed intact for a while before dissipating into a web of rings around the inside of my pint glass. Lots of grass and lemon in the nose with sour grass, bread, and some herbal notes underneath in the taste. More juice from the citrus could be discerned as the pith and rind notes came out in the back. Quite wet, this went down very easily and almost felt like a session beer, even though this technically didn't qualify as one. Not a lot of hops could be felt here and this almost felt like an American Pale Wheat Ale, since there were traces of wheat here and a good amount of carbonation to round things out.

Not strong nor weak, this was perfect when it came to the booze and would have been fitting for a warm night outside under the stars. It's still cold outside but this beer is one that can be enjoyed year-round once the citrus notes settle down during later sips. A great example of what a Pilsener-style beer should be and what other American Pale Lagers wish they could be. Worth seeking out for those looking for a change from beers that are too-light top to bottom!

Tone, Oct 16, 2015
3.76/5  rDev -5.3%
look: 4 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 4

A- Pale yellow pour from the 12oz bottle into a tulip. Puffy head builds to just over a finger high displaying decent retention. Frothy, half-cm collar provides large lacing sheets at the top with ample spots and some drizzle down the glass.

S- Aroma starts out with lemon, grapefruit and tangerine citrus up front with a passionfruit and tropical fruit hint in support. Herbal, pale crackery barley and grassy hints beyond all of that. Hop-driven but not pungent. Delicate yeast; clean.

T- For me the Pilsner aspect of things comes into play a bit more obviously here than it did in the aroma. Dry, dusty and bready Pilsner grain, cereal grains and a note of yeast esters and spiciness. This all takes the backseat to the fruity, herbal and grassy hops. Passionfruit and grapefruit are the stars with tangerine, flowery, spicy and slight tropical juicy tones joining them.

MF- Crisp and highly carbonated low-medium thick overall body. Texture is both foamy and creamy finishing off with a slight herbal bitterness.

Not entirely sure on the freshness of this one but this bottle still displays some good hop tones atop a spicy, crackery Pils base. A shade more Galaxy might be nice but a solid lager nonetheless.
