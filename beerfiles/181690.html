Can purchased at a beer store in Brooklyn. Impressions below are regrettably brief:

Aroma has a tropical fruit bend with Simcoe-derived onion and some citrusy pulp. Aromatic intensity is average.

Tastewise, tropical fruit dominates the second half, with sweet tangerine dominating. Pulpy orange. Balanced albeit somewhat simple - especially for a beer billed as an IIPA (though its scant 8% ABV may indicate the IIPA designation is merely marketing).

Texture is medium to full-bodied, smooth, wet, and a bit overcarbonated. Not oily, gushed, hot, boozy, astringent, harsh, rough, or scratchy.

Overall, it's drinkable but not dazzling fare and I'm glad I tried it once, but I can't say it's really anything special. I'd sooner reach for Lagunitas Sucks.

B- / WORTHY

edthehead, Sep 25, 2016
4.58/5  rDev +3.6%
look: 4.75 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

Canned ___. Consumed 9/23/16.

A: Pours a pretty hazy burnt orange with about a finger of pillowy white head. Just the slightest hint of transparency, but almost entirely opaque. Alcohol legs for sure with some wasps of lacing on the glass.

S: Tons of sweet, sticky tropical fruit. Huge passionfruit notes, along with some sweet orange, apricot, and peach. Lots of bubblegum sweetness underlying the fruit-forwardness. Not really much dankness or earthiness to speak of... This is a candied fruit bomb on the nose.

T: Follows the nose pretty closely. Passionfruit, peach, and orange in spades; pineapple and a Juicy Fruit bubblegum flavor to a lesser degree. It's super sweet--almost sticky--but manages to avoid that "cloying" sweetness I get on some bigger DIPAs. It truly tastes like fruit-flavored candy.

M: Extremely soft and smooth on the palette, but not so thick as to feel syrupy. It's extremely sweet with just a bit of bitterness on the backend, but the lightness of the mouthfeel, as well as the pungency of the hop flavors, prevents it from feeling cloying. At 8%, there isn't a hint of alcohol. It packs a ton of flavor and weight with minimal abrasion on the feel.

O: Another fantastic offering from Grimm. Lambo is sweeter than Afterimage, but also packs a softer and fuller mouthfeel. It might be too sweet for some, but the flavor/aroma profile is so damn delicious. For an IPA, it almost tastes decadent, dessert-like. It kinda feels like the archetypal Grimm beer, one that takes their signature candied fruit and soft mouthfeel characteristics to an extreme. Overall, I'd say it's definitely worth trying, especially if you've previously enjoyed some of their other offerings!
