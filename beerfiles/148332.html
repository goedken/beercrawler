Notes / Commercial Description:
Dust off those glowsticks, and get ready to rave once again with this ripe funky old ale spiked with brettanomyces and aged for one year in a blend of bourbon, apple brandy and red wine Barrels. Yep.

50% aged in Bourbon Barrels

Thanks to Sanderson for dropping this extra into a fall trade, one that brought much joy & happiness to my town. 22 oz. bottle split with my wife as a celebration of coming home from a week in the distant coal mines of San Diego. Bottled 10/24/14.

The pour even looks funky, weathered red paint chips mixed in with clay court runoff & flatland earth. Surprisingly carbonated for a big, bourbon, odd old ale. The nose is mostly wine & sharp, which I would think was a lot of wine if I didnt know there was brett in this.

Ravers Old Ale is curiously mundane; expecting back-flips & a maltese cross, instead theres a handstand. The malty sweetness of the old ale is held onto by the barrels. The wine imparts some sharp vinous notes, & the bourbon a low heat. Apple brandy might be felt on the finish, but if it werent advertised, I wouldnt pick it out. The brett is surprisingly tame, adding a dry element & a sprinkling of green apple skin. Strangely drinkable, as I keep raising the glass inquisitively.

Interesting, but ultimately a pretty tame beer for what went into the making. Pretty good.

Smelled tons of sour notes up front (and pretty much the whole way through), getting cranberry, apple, Brett, sour bourbon, cherry, candied sugar (Smarties/Sweet Tarts) and grape. Smelled more like a dark sour/wild ale than an Old Ale. (Smell - 4.25)

Taste wasnt nearly as good as the smell. Similar flavors, getting boozy, sour cranberry, cherry, Brett, apple, Bourbon, red grape and grape juice. (Taste - 3.50)

Medium body. Oily texture. Average carbonation. Long, boozy finish. (Feel - 3.50)

This beer had its moments, but the smell was the best part by far. Was happy to get to try it though. (Overall - 3.75)

L - Pours a dark brown body that is very opaque and not pretty to look at. The one finger khaki head has great retention and lacing.

S - There's a mix of smells going on here. The red wine barrels along with the apple brandy barrels hit you first with the bourbon barrels bringing up the rear. After you wade through the barrels, you get dark fruits, malts and sugars. The smell is so in you face and a mix of things happening that for me, it was hard to pick out certain elements.

T - Okay, this is nothing like and so much calmer than the smell. The bourbon barrels are front and center along with some oak aspects. A little bit of caramel and malts from the base beer. The brett is there, but very subdued.

F - Medium body and carbonation. Has a smooth body and wet finish.

O - In my opinion, it's a mess of a beer. Every flavor is going in opposite directions and it doesn't meld together at all.

22oz bottle into a tulip
bottled on: 10/24/14

released at the brewery today


A: Dark Mahogany with ruby hues when held to light and 1 fingers width of light tan head which dissipates down at a moderate rate and leaves lots of lacing. 4.25

S: Wow, theres so much going on Im not sure where to begin. Apple brandy notes are the first to stand out, this is followed by some traditional old ale sweet flavors including dark fruits and candied sugar. There are tons of vinous red wine notes. Bourbon characters are the most subtle of the barrel notes, but are present with an oaky vanilla. I also get some funky almost pungent cheese-like notes from the Brett. This is one of the most complex aromas I have ever experienced in a beer, but miraculously it somehow pulls together brilliantly. 4.5

T: Interestingly, the first thing I get in the flavor is bourbon notes with charred oak and bourbony vanilla. There are apply brandy and red wine notes as well but they are more subtle. I dont get many base beer old ale notes, except caramel/toffee and burnt sugar. The Brett funk is certainly present and seems to come in waves. I would have liked more base beer old ale flavors to come through, but still very good and quite complex. 4.25

M/D: Medium body, but lighter than I anticipated for the style. Carbonation is on the higher end, but appropriate. ABV is very well masked, albeit fairly low for the style. A bit thin. Drinkability is good. 3.75

O: I was a bit skeptical that the Brett would throw things off in this one, but it amazingly did not. Very well done brew. 4.25
