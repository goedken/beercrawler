First time I tried 'Simmer Down' was on my way back from Bar Harbor. Not sure if it was the fresh ME air or that the lower ABV let me have one with lunch and still keep going, but I found this to be a great, easy-drinking beer. Even though it's on the higher end of a 'session' (when I think session, I think 3.5-4.5, but I guess under 5 is still in the "definition"), it certainly doesn't taste it. Also, the bitterness that comes on the back-end of some sessions simply isn't there. In all, I wish it was available in PA [ that's a hint to you folks at Sebago :-) ].

On-tap 5/31/2015 at Battleship Brewhouse, Fall River, MA served in a US tumbler pint glass.

A: The beer is a light golden brown color, with a short dense off-white head that fades slowly and leaves a thick lace on the glass.

S: The aroma contains floral hops, caramelized malts and fruity citrus.

T: The taste starts out with a nice burst of floral hops bitterness that is quickly followed by sweet flavors of fruity citrus and caramel. The malt character is hearty but not too heavy and brings a very good balance. The after-taste is slightly hoppy and slightly sweet.

M: Crisp and very smooth, medium body, medium carbonation, finish is slightly sticky.

O: Very tasty, goes down quite easily, not too filling, mild kick, very good representation of style, this is a very solid beer to pound down for a long time.

O: as I said I was really impressed with this beer at the start. My finer analysis curbed this slightly, but for what it is, it's a very solid offering in my opinion.

I rarely think that any beer is perfect, but this offering from Sebago is. The drinker is hit by prominent crisp floral and citrus hop aromas when the bottle is opened. The pour, carbonation level, and head are as they should be. The taste is bright and clean, well balanced hops with moderate malt (no caramel or diacetyl), and carries little to no after taste. Great burp. This is essentially a light IPA, and in my opinion the finest of its style.
