bound4er, Apr 26, 2016
4.21/5  rDev +6.6%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

I saw this for the first time in my local Trig's Cellar 70. This one was packaged about six weeks ago. Poured right from the fridge into my Founders pint glass.

L: Pours a hazy deep copper amber with about an inch-and-a-half of off-white head that sticks around for a good while and leaves considerable, even lacing on the glass. Considerable visible carbonation present.

S: Floral is probably the most fitting word to describe the nose. There are pine, citrus, and grassy hop notes present, but this one really has a flowery quality to it. There are also more restrained bread and caramel malt notes coming through, as well. It smells like I imagine a Hops Yankee Candle would...in a good way.

T: I'd like to echo another BA's comment that this one tastes memorable. Very unique, assertive flavor profile on this one. The opening brings cracker and more substantial toasted malt flavor. Right on its heels is the infusion of big floral and orange citrus hop notes. The middle brings a bit more earthiness and the sense of sweeter caramel malts. The back end brings the pine hop flavor forecast by the bouquet, along with more of the earthiness and sweeter caramel malt, which balances things out nicely.

F: This one falls pretty squarely into the medium-bodied category. It has a great level of carbonation that lends it some nice creaminess and helps to accentuate the great hop flavors. While it does supply some lingering bitter, it never feels syrupy.

O: I was a bit apprehensive about this one. Big breweries that try their hand at traditional craft brews don't exactly have a great track record. But Pabst has done a very nice job with this one. It's fairly rare these days that I come across a new IPA that I know I'll return to again. This one is that good--that unique. I don't know if the "bold" classification by other reviewers is the best adjective for it, but I do know that this one brings a lot of tasty hop and malt flavor. I definitely recommend giving it a shot.

Real neat IPA, very memorable as well.

PA-hunter, Oct 16, 2015
4.27/5  rDev +8.1%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

Really surprised by this IPA.

Yes that is a 4.27 rating, and yes, this ale is that good.

Poured into a 12 ounce snifter at 37F from the 22 ounce bottle.

Having had numerous hangovers and blackout nights in college from the older sister product Ballantine Ale in 40 ounce bottles, I had some reluctance with the brand.

But man, this is a really quality IPA. and for a 7.2% ale, I taste almost no alcohol. A telltale sign of a craft ale. Yes this is most certainly a craft beer product, despite my previous shortcomings with the brand in my formative years.

The color is a really impressive rich golden copper. Not quite as nice as Carton or Singlecut, but the coloring is up there with the best in the business.

Nose is slightly simcoe, maybe amarillo, and some other piney and resinous hops. Not too powerful, doesn't really seem like there was any dry hopping done, but for this product, the scent is pretty much spot on.

Taste is really well balanced. Pine resin, bready malt, ale yeast. Not a lot of malts and not a super bombardment of hops, but again, just really well balanced and no tasting note really outweighs the other. The yeast is really clean tasting. Maybe Safale US-05? I kind of wished they would up the malts and hops on the backend, more akin to Other Half or Singlecut, but hey, this is an awesome ipa for the price point, and Im a fan.

Very picky beer consumer here and I will absolutely repeat purchase Ballantine IPA.

My only gripe is that it was bottled 9 weeks ago, which for a hop forward ipa is slightly old. Distributors need to treat this product with more care and priority, despite the lower price point.

Ballantine also gets extra points for printing the bottling date on the bottle. Not some confusing julian dating code either.

O -- This is a pretty respectable take on an American IPA from a not-so-respected brew-house. It's nothing I (myself) would drink in great quantity at one sitting, but I'm not that sort of IPA drinker. On the other hand, I'd definitely pick this up if I was in the mood for a good IPA. Well worth a sample if you like IPAs.
