3.38/5  rDev -12.4%
look: 3.25 | smell: 3.25 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3 | overall: 3.5

Brief initial impressions:

APPEARANCE: 12 fl oz aluminum can pours a thick khaki colour head with nice creaminess but lacking in fullness and thickness. Retains well, though - easily for 10 minutes. Pretty impressive at 7.50%.

Body is an opaque dark black, but it may be a very dark brown which appears black...it's certainly not an ink-black or jet-black, anyway. No yeast particulate or hop sediment is visible.

AROMA: Coffee. A bit of roast. Fresh ground bean. Could be richer, could be more roasty, could be fresher, could be deeper, could be more multifaceted...but it's there. And that's more than I can say about the aromas of some purported coffee stouts.

Hints of nuts - hazelnut, I guess? Dark malts, a kiss of raisin. Cream. Maybe some brief chocolate malt.

It's a pleasant aroma of average strength.

TASTE: Coffee - some roast, ground beans, and java/espresso characteristics. Dark malts. Some pleasant hazelnut - subtle enough not to be bothersome, present enough to add complexity.

I find it simple and fairly one-note, but it's balanced and enjoyable. A coffee ale that brings the coffee...consider me satisfied.

Still, it could use more depth of flavour, especially where the coffee is concerned, and a little more intricacy could only help. Milk sugar, perhaps? Pecan? Walnut? Milk chocolate? More cream? There're ample opportunities for improvement; this tastes like a pilot batch beer awaiting further development.

TEXTURE: Well-carbonated, smooth, lightly coarse, and approachable. It's slick and creamy in the early second act, but then it gets a bit scratchy and drags on the palate from the late second act through the finish. If it kept that initial slickness, the beer would be much more drinkable and enjoyable.

OVERALL: A tasty coffee ale without much depth. It'll satisfy, but won't impress. Worth nabbing if you chance across it, but it's not world-class. I'm glad I picked up a 6-pack. I'll enjoy revisiting this in more depth. Very drinkable. Hides its ABV well to boot.

B-

***
03.31.15: Truncated 2nd impressions below from another CAN. Served cold into a pilsner glass.

A: Decent khaki colour head with ~4-5 minute retention and fair creaminess. Body colour is black.

Sm: Acidic coffee. Faint hazelnut. Odd brown malts with an unnecessary sweetness.

It's a bit weird for a coffee ale...

T: It brings the coffee, but it's not expressive or deep at all. And there's some kind of off-note vaguely redolent of pool water chlorination.

It's a shallow simple brew with a malt backbone mismatched to the coffee. I like it generally speaking.

Mf: Smooth, wet, miedum-bodied, and overcarbonated. Unrefreshing. The finish has an odd dryness to it.

Dr: Drinkable stuff from Deep Ellum, but the coffee just isn't interesting or evocative enough considering this is a coffee ale, and the malts are oddly chosen. I remembered liking this better.

C+

i really liked the one beer i had from these guys before this, so i was pumped to try this one. a coffee brown ale, thats actually pretty light on the coffee. the base brown is pretty big though, american brown, fuller bodied, strong, and with a definite hop bitterness in the finish. the coffee is an accent flavor here, nothing too strong, its not supposed to be. the coffee must have been cold pressed since the beer is minimally acidic. dark chocolate and char are present in the aroma and flavor, and the hops do cool things with the coffee and the chocolate malt, making a potent beer. carbonation is average and the body is pretty full, although i dont find this filling. i like it less as it warms up, the hops come out a little too much and the booze starts to develop too. pretty decent caffeine kick in here too despite the coffee being subtle in the taste. good way to get the spins. tasty and well made stuff here, a little sweeter than it needs to be, gets distracting as it finishes, but solid overall.
