Bottling date of 8/11/16

Appearance - Pours with a slight haze and is golden yellow in color with a white, finger width head. Fair retention, peeling back to a mostly barren surface with a thin ring collecting around the edge of the glass. Weak lacing, with just a few tiny specks and a broken lines found around the glass.

Smell - Aroma evokes thoughts of orange, lemon, pineapple and a touch of pine. Maybe a faint bit of bread buried well beneath.

Taste - Follows the nose. Citrusy flavors with a touch of pineapple and pine. Mild lemon peel and some cattiness mid-taste. Light, pithy finish with some distant grain found lingering in the aftertaste.

Mouthfeel - Extremely light in body with moderate carbonation. Goes down smooth and easy, with a slightly watery finish.

Overall - The label may say Hoppy Pale, but this drinks and tastes like a session IPA all the way to the bone, and a fairly average one for the style at that.

12 ounce bottle into pint glass, bottled on 7/1/2016. Pours lightly hazy/cloudy medium golden yellow color with a 2 finger dense and rocky white head with good retention, that reduces to a nice cap that lingers. Nice spotty soapy lacing clings down the glass, with a moderate amount of streaming carbonation retaining the cap. Aromas of grapefruit, tangerine, pineapple, peach, lemon zest, orange peel, mango, light pine, light honey, toasted bread, herbal, and floral/grassy earthiness. Nice and pleasant aromas with good balance and complexity of citrus/tropical/pine hops and moderate bready malt notes; with solid strength. Taste of grapefruit, tangerine, pineapple, peach, lemon zest, orange peel, mango, pine, light honey, toasted bread, herbal, and floral/grassy earthiness. Moderate pine/herbal/grassy bitterness on the finish; with lingering notes of grapefruit, tangerine, pineapple, peach, lemon/orange peel, pine, bread, and herbal/floral/grassy earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Very nice complexity, robustness, and balance of citrus/tropical/pine hops and moderate bready malt flavors; with a great malt/bitterness balance, and zero astringent hop flavors after the finish. Light-moderate dryness from bitterness, increasing through the glass. Medium carbonation and body; with a very smooth, moderately sticky, and fairly grainy balanced mouthfeel that is nice. Zero warming alcohol as expected of 5.2%. Overall this is a very nice standard pale ale. All around nice complexity, robustness, and balance of citrus/tropical/pine hops and moderate bready malt flavors; very smooth and easy to drink. A very enjoyable offering.

A hoppy pale ale. Poured from a bottle into a New Belgium snifter. The pour is fairly clear, gold in color, effervescent, and sports a finger of rocky white head. There are lots of hoppy notes on the nose. I get lemon, grapefruit, oranges, and a little tea.

Very hop-forward flavor with a delicate feel. Very little, if any, sweetness. You get hit by bitter notes of orange peel and lemon rind right from the start. As the beer travels across you palate, the flavors round out and the hops peter out a bit towards the back. Still, the beer finishes dry with a pleasant, lingering, citrusy hop flavor.

Overall, a good beer, but to me it seems a bit out-of-balance. I love the hop profile of this beer. The flavors obtained from the bitterness are spot-on and very nice, but there doesn't seem to be enough malt flavor to support it. Still, even though my feelings towards this beer seem a bit lukewarm, I think it's one I could really learn to love if I drank more of it.

This self-described hoppy Pale Ale is a looker: a large, pebbly ivory-colored head sits atop a crystal-clear, effervescent pale gold body; head retention is modest and lacing is good, consisting of sticky slabs and caverns. The nose is medium in intensity, leading with a whiff of bready malt that is quickly overrun by a delicious blend of assertive perfumey florals and more restrained grapefruit and citrus notes, with faint alcohol overtones. Up front, the palate is dry, medium-bitter pine and subtle grapefruit; mid-palate the grapefruit emerges a bit more and the florals from the nose peek out, along with a very slight bittersweet nutty character. There is a bit of bready malt foundation to be seen but, while just medium bitter for the style, this beer certainly lands on the dry, hoppy end of the balance. The finish is quite clean, showing just a bit of perfumey grapefruit rind, and ends quite dry; the linger is a modest resinous piney bitterness that stays around for a good while. The mouthfeel is on par with the light body, and the texture is medium-creamy and medium-peppery from the well-expressed carbonation. This crisp, tasty beer is a nice choice if you're looking for a drinkable low-ABV hoppy pale ale. (12 oz bottle, 20 days after pkg)

BeerDawgs, Apr 22, 2016
4.04/5  rDev +3.3%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4

330ml Bottle

Again, Will coming through like the Boss that he is! Thanks, dude! Napolean Complex is a dirty apricot orange with a respectable amount of haze throughout. This beer glows when backlit. The cotton white head is creamy and rich, growing to two fingers in height on the pour. The foam eventually recedes to a chunky island and ring around the glass leaving behind sticky sheet lacing on its way. That's purty!

The nose is perfume laden. Floral off the bat with strong undercurrents of mango and pineapple. As this beer warms it gets even juicier. Juicy Fruit gum and pithy grapefruit emerge. The hop profile is well played. It's even handed, complex and mouthwatering. A whisper of dank earthiness wraps it all up. the malt base is a simple canvas, crackery and softly sweet.

The flavor profile takes a bit of a different path, also good. It's big and pithy off the bat. Orange and grapefruit galore. Freshly squeezed juice of both with grapefruit inching out orange. The florals are buried citrus but the tropical notes I mentioned above play second fiddle. It's bright and lively, a squeeze of lemon even. Again, the malt profile is softly sweet and crackery, basically a canvas for the hop profile.

The body on this beer is where it's at! It sits between moderate and light in weight. It's effervescent and lively with a tongue tingling effect that seems to open the tastebuds. There's a decent bitterness that never rises above moderate which keeps this beer flowing. One after the other, I could put this away.

Wicked Weed rarely, if ever, disappoints. This is another damn fine beer from the folks down South. It's bright and lively. Drinkability is way, way up there. This is summertime drinking at its finest. Thanks, Wil!
