AgentMunky, Aug 25, 2015
3.83/5  rDev -0.8%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3 | overall: 3.75

Poured from a 12oz bottle into a Spiegelau IPA Glass.
LOOK: Pours with a finger's worth of off-white foam that halves itself within 30 seconds. Has a very dull, cloudy yellow color. Plenty thick lacing. There is a plethora of rising bubbles, so I am unsure as to why the foam head is not bigger. We have a mystery on our hands, folks.
SMELL: Has strong aromas of citrus hops that mix with mild bready malt, pale malt, lemon peel, and fruit (mango, peach, and apricot) aromas.
TASTE: The first flavor that strikes my palate is a combination of strong citrus hops and mild pale malt. Almost instantly, mild bready malt and salt flavors (yet another mystery because I did not smell any salt!) join in. Subtle fruit and lemon peel flavors enter immediately after act 2 as all flavors persist except for pale malts, which become hardly noticeable.
FEEL: Surprisingly light-bodied (no me gusta) with a subtle carbonation (si me gusta) and slightly creamy texture. And of course, since it is an IPA, it has a unpleasantly dry finish.
NOTE: This was a pretty good IPA. Not quite in the DFH category of IPAs, though.

12oz from Techichi (thanks!). Best by 8/30/15, I assume bottled 5/30/15. Served in my Phish snifter.

Pours a cloudy, pale orange with about a finger white head. Decent retention with some surface haze and a healthy halo that deposits some fairly sizable clumps of lace.

Muted nose with notes of mild pine, citrus peel and some cracker.

Flavors are not much more vibrant than the aromas. Citrus peel, slightly vegital, a little pine and a bit of crackery malt. Too mild to produce any memorable taste.

Medium light feel with adequate carbonation. Pine and peel linger longer than I'd prefer.

This bottle of Lost Gold was well within its freshness date but utterly lackluster. Unfortunately no aspects stood out as anything more than average.
