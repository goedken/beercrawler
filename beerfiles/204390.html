Poured from 12 oz brown bottle into a clear oversized wine glass. No obvious freshness date. Received as a gift from generous bud Erinn.

A: Black with beige 1-finger foamy head with decent retention and a nice solid stripe of lacing around the top of the glass.

S: Delicious cinnamon and cocoa aromas with softer dark fruit and light roast

T: Strongly bitter cinnamon note up front that doesn't meld with the cocoa and dark maltiness and roast that well - has an artificial chemical bitterness to me. This blends weirdly with the mild sweetness of the caramel malts.

M: A little astringent to me with the bitterness with a tacky feel in a medium-full bodied beer.

O: I liked the regular Drafty Kilt quite a bit better but give MN credit for their creativity and risk taking.

This is a very interesting, yet satisfying Beer by Monday Night. I was a very big fan of their Drafty Kilt, so I was very interested to try it. Comes in a standard 12oz, pour with a short, quarter-inch head of foam. Mild, seltzery carbonation. You're hit right away with that recognizable Drafty Kilt smokiness, but this time with a very noticeable cinnamon essence. The flavor again has that Smokiness punching through , but you definitely get a bitterness from the cinnamon that the regular Drafty Kilt doesn't have. The cocoa nibs hit you upon swallowing , and accompanies the smoky Scotch ale taste well as it sits on your pallet. Overall a very good beer, however I think if I had to choose I would stick with the regular Drafty Kilt. Not taking anything away, however, it's definitely worth trying.

4.16/5  rDev +3.5%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 4.25

This is a great-looking brew. It a almost black, with two inches of head on the initial pour. There is a ton of lacing on the glass, and the head takes a long time to settle. This might be the best-looking scotch ale I've ever seen.
The smell makes it seem like it's going to be a cinnamon bomb. Also, it has some of that soapy/potpourri smell like Xocoveza, even though this beer is not supposed to have any nutmeg or chiles.
The flavor is similar, but I'd say a little better. The chocolate is more noticeable on the finish. There is a ton of cinnamon mid-palate. Some of the sweet maltiness you'd expect of a scotch ale is detectable, but the cinnamon almost overpowers it.
Mouthfeel is fairly typical of scotch ale, but the carbonation gives it a light feel. It might benefit from a bit more chewiness.
If you like cinnamon and have a sweet-tooth, you'll love this beer. Personally, I'm just not a huge fan of cinnamon bombs. This is a well-made beer that delivers what it promises. I would have gone for more cocoa and less cinnamon, but that's just my palate. I respect opinionated beers, and that's exactly what we have here.

12oz bottle from Hop City (Atlanta, GA). 1.99. No bottling date but this was released earlier this month (January 2016). Served in a Monday Night snifter.

Pours a rich burgundy tinged mahogany, dark but transparent, with a finger and a half of dense sand hued head. The froth eventually settles to a thin veneer and healthy collar that leaves a few specks of lace behind.

Inviting aromas of cinnamon and cocoa virtually leap from the glass to greet my nose. Those are dominant but there is some subtle caramel and bread in there too.

Flavors of cinnamon, caramel, airy chocolate, toast and just a hint of alcohol. The cinnamon completely takes over but works well enough that I'm ok with that.

Mouthfeel is on the lighter side of medium bodied with lively carbonation. Cinnamon continues to drive the experience in the finish along with a little cocoa and toast.

Monday Night has a fun variant of their successful Drafty Kilt here. It might border on being a one trick pony with that commanding cinnamon but I must admit that the pony sure is fun to ride.
