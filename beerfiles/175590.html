Important note for anyone drinking this beer: This is the one you want to review. There is another Sierra Nevada KÃ¶lsch, which if you go to their website, is in their "vault." Meaning that it is retired. Same ABV but different beer. I am quite certain that the recent reviews over at that beer were intended for this beer, and not for the vault version of the KÃ¶lsch. I hate to sound patronizing, but we really need to pay attention. If not for yourself, for the brewers. If not for them, for us drinkers. Goodness, this may be my longest review yet. Now if I could get to the actual reviewing:

Clear and gold with bright off-white foamy head of medium-retention. In bottle, aroma is of dough or baking bread. In glass, strong but pleasant brewers' yeast aroma to the fore, with faintest fruitiness in the backdrop. Tastes at first like the driest white wine you ever did have. Less fruity than that, even. Crackery backbone, if you can call it that. Strong yeast character, touch of wheat nearly lost in the balance, and herbal and soapy hops in the back. Yeast spiciness with retro-olfaction. Dry, long lasting, light-metallic finish. Fuller end of light-bodied, lots of gentle suds, light tingling where teeth and gums meet. Moderately crisp.

Frankly, this is a different take on the American version of the KÃ¶lsch than what I am used to. I prefer the fruitier (as in distinct fruit flavors, not as in overly sweet) ones and I can't help but wonder that maybe people rated this one as highly as they did for either its brand or possibly its closeness to the original style (I've not had actual German KÃ¶lsch so I can't say anything conclusively, but I have always heard that these are supposed to be fairly fruity as well, so I think my first guess may be closer to the mark). Sorry for those remarks, to each his own!

I am not a fan of hopping up every style, but the subtle NW hops added make this a real treat!

Date: July 23, 2015... Glassware: Ayinger willibecker... Occasion: well pump replaced, and a tan replacement scheduled for tomorrow--time to drink... Appearance: sallow, pale straw yellow body that hides nothing; steady effervescence suggests healthy beer; white head is foamy, almost coral in its fragmented,, upward-reaching arms and oxygenated spaces; reasonable post-sipping lacing.... Aroma: lemony to start, and light, flowery hops--as per the style, not much else to pollute the promise of clean, easy drinking; a little cereal suggested, with a minimal yeast funk... Palate: thin and fizzy, with a swift exit and subtle, lingering aftertaste--again, the style dictate its drinkability, and this is agreeably so... Taste: hops are damp, recalcitrant but slightly flowery, and they do return late to a scaled flourish; malts are even more understated, with a soft sweetness piquing the taste buds; some bubblegum and bitterness that doesn't add to or subtract from the experience.... Overall: once more, reviewing a kolsch is like rating techno or describing the color white--and I am a fan of both in the right context. Most accomplish their goal of drinkability through quality water and the delicate application of ingredients; Sierra Nevada knows their stuff and delivers a beer commensurate to its style... proof is in the nearly empty glass next to the computer while I rate this, a simple sip-and-type ritual that exemplifies the functionality of this beer on a nice summer night ....
