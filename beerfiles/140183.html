Tripel_Threat, Aug 20, 2015
3.86/5  rDev +3.5%
look: 4 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

We pop the top on a bottle, pouring into our Great Lakes pint glasses. It shows a bright, uniform amber cherry coloring, and sporting a one finger head of manila envelope colored foam. Lacing is unilateral, large, and cratered following a quick retention. No haze or sediment is noted, and carbonation appears to be in intermittent streams. The aroma is simple and to the point, offering authentic red apple skin and juiciness, baked and buttery crushed Graham cracker crust, white wine vinegar, lactic creaminess, vanilla coffee creamer, raw and tannic pale and amber malts, heavy cinnamon, ginger, and dried nutmeg spice fig jam, plastic phenols, yeasty ale metallics, mineral water, and golden honey. The flavoring, however esoteric and admittedly curious, tastes like cold apple pie. You get balancing and blended notes of cooked brown sugar and fruity apple tartness and acidity, with an accompaniment of apple cider juiciness, raw cinnamon, cream cheese cake frosting, buttery pound cake, savory and metallic ale yeastiness, lemon sourness, vinegar, amber and wheaty malts, dried dog food, bile acidity, and pulpy cranberry and plum chutney. The body is medium and heavy with sugary, while the carbonation is light to medium. Each sip gives hearty slurp and smack, with easy creaminess and frothiness on the swirl around the mouth. The mouth is thick coated from top to bottom, leaving the palate spiced and salivating. A fine pucker comes with only the first sip. Abv is appropriate, and the beer drinks back fine.

Overall, this beer accomplishes what it sets out to do. Odd Side tends to bottle and distribute a lot of these goofy, highly specific and targeted, one-offs. At least in Michigan they do. Here, we find an initially tart, but easy-to-get-into apple fruit beer, that remains more beer than cider throughout. It honestly tastes like a cold slice of leftover pie, with all the spice and fruitiness of the mash, and baked flakey qualities of the crusty vessel. While it isnt amazingly robust or truly authentic, it again, hits the mark just enough to be drinkable and approachable. We hate to directly compare beers in reviews, but if you look at some other local Michigan beers going after a pies flavoring, this tops even some of its heavier hitting contemporaries.

Bottled 10/22 and poured 12/19. I think a single bottle was $2.49, maybe $2.29. Poured into a Sam Adams Perfect Pint.

Appearance: Aggressive pour threw up a nice head about an inch in height. Off-white head. The beer itself was mostly clear until I got the last of the bottle in the glass, in which case a ton of yeast fell in. Rusty brown and red color with a lot of floaters in it. Not really any lacing.

Smell: Not quite like apple pie, but actually pretty damn close. A lot of cinnamon with a graham cracker crust smell in there too. Under that is the apple. Really smells nice and almost exactly like described.

Taste/feel: A bit thin feel bothers me a bit, but I dig the moderate/high carbonation that really works well with the slight tartness from the apples. Definite apple pie qualities with the cinnamon and apples all over the place. The milk sugar is apparent in the way it adds that dairy sort of thing you'd get in a pie crust.

This is super tasty. It's far from cloyingly sweet -- instead, it's got a nice balance with a little apple tartness that makes this a very easy drinking fruit beer that could easily be sessioned... I feel like this should be re-released by Odd Side in Summer, because this would make a great Summer beer, in my opinion. Very impressed by this one.

12 ounce bottle into pint glass, bottled on 10/9/2014. Pours lightly hazy/cloudy orange/amber color with a 1-2 finger dense off white head with good retention, that reduces to a thin cap that lingers. Light spotty soapy lacing clings on the glass, with a moderate amount of streaming carbonation retaining the cap. Aromas of big tart green apples, cinnamon, nutmeg, graham cracker, toasted bread, light cream, and herbal earthiness. Very nice aromas with good balance and complexity of apple cider, bready malt, and spice notes; with good strength. Taste of tart green apples, cinnamon, nutmeg, graham cracker, toasted bread, light cream, and herbal earthiness. Minimal bitterness and light-moderate fruity tartness on the finish; with lingering notes of green apples, cinnamon, nutmeg, graham cracker, toasted bread, light cream, and herbal earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Very nice robustness, balance, and complexity of apple cider, bready malt, and spice flavors; with a good sweetness/tartness balance and zero cloying flavors after the finish. Medium carbonation and body; with a very smooth, crisp, and slightly acidic mouthfeel that is nice. Alcohol is well hidden with minimal warming present after the finish. Overall this is a very nice fruit beer style. All around good balance, complexity, and robustness of apple cider, bready malt, and spice flavors; and very smooth and crisp to drink. A very enjoyable offering.

TreyIsWilson, Nov 05, 2014
3.81/5  rDev +2.1%
look: 3.75 | smell: 4 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

I picked up a bottle of Oddside Ales Granny's Apple Pie last week for $2.19 at Holiday Market. I'm always down to try a new beer from Oddsides and this one sounds pretty interesting, so let see how it goes. No visible bottling date, 2014 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a tulip.

A- The label looks ok, it goes well with the name, but I don't think it's too eye catching and I think they could have came up with a better a label. It poured a slightly hazy amber color that took on more of an amber red color when held to the light with a finger and a half worth of bubbly off white head that died down to a thick bubbly ring that stayed till the end and it left some spotty lacing behind. This was a pretty nice looking beer, I have no complaints.

S- The aroma starts off with a medium amount of sweetness with the pie crust like aroma being the first to show up and it actually has a real pie crust like aroma that goes right into some apples which have a nice balance of sweet and tart apple natural apple aromas. Up next comes the spices which mix nicely with the apple and they impart a nice brown sugar and cinnamon like aroma with just a slight clove aroma showing up and there's a creamy vanilla aroma that shows up right in the end. This beer had a good aroma that was true to name, the spices had a similar aroma to the spices in Mayan Mocha Stout.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma and it starts off with a medium amount of sweetness with a slight malty taste being the first to show up and it mixes in with a slight bit of that pie crust aspect that I got in the aroma. Up next comes the apple cider which still has the nice balance of tart and sweet apples with the spices coming up right after and they impart the same aspects that they did in the aroma with the cinnamon sticking out the most. On the finish there's a little bit of that creamy milk sugar with a light tartness taking the bitterness's place with a tart apple and cinnamon like aftertaste. This was a pretty nice tasting beer, the apple cider wasn't overpowering and neither were the spices.

M- Smooth, crisp, not too acidic, on the lighter side of being medium bodied with a slightly higher amount of medium carbonation. This beer had a pretty nice mouthfeel that worked well with it.

Overall I thought this was a pretty nice beer and I thought it was a nice example of a dessert beer, it came close to the dessert it was modeled after, especially in the taste and I liked how just about every aspect of a Apple Pie showed up individually in this beer. This beer had nice drinkability, it was smooth, crisp, not too acidic, not filling and the taste did a nice job of holding my attention, I could drink a couple of these and it would make for a nice beer to enjoy after a good meal. What I liked the most about this beer was the aroma, it was good and it really did resemble a homemade Apple Pie. What I liked the least about this beer was that the taste wasn't more like the aroma, but that wasn't that big of a deal since it was still pretty close. I would buy this beer again and I would recommend it to anybody who enjoys a nice dessert beer or if an Apple Pie beer sounds appealing to you. All in all I was happy with this beer, it wasn't mind blowing or anything, but In the end I found myself enjoying it and i think they did a nice job at making a beer that tastes similar to Apple Pie. This isn't my favorite Oddsides beer, or my favorite dessert beer, but it's still a good example of both. Nice job guys , this was a tasty dessert beer, keep up the creative work.
