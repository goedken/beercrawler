It may be retired, but the backlog (especially bottles) here at Chez Woody knows no bounds. Plus, it is New Beer Sunday (Week 598), so it gives me a reason to participate.

From the bottle: "Nate and Erin, our resident cocktail mongers, love a good classic. And we believe that innovation - alongside healthy dashes of decadence and depravity - comes from within. Needless to say, this Brewhouse Rarities pitch was a ringer. Saddle up and sip on our dark horse."

A strong pour following the Pop! of the cap got me just under three fingers of dense, foamy, bone-white head with good retention. Color was a gorgeous Golden-Amber (SRM = > 5, < 7) with NE-quality clarity, prompting a visit from The Gelt Gang of Midas, Mammon & Croesus, all madly scrambling for a sip. Nose smelled like fresh mint, straight out of my neighbor's garden. Mouthfeel was medium-to-full, thanks to a lot of sparkling carbonation and a nice effervescence. It was a very clean-tasting ale, more like a lager, rally allowing the gentle mintiness and mild honeysuckle to shine through. This was wonderfully refreshing after a morning spent hosing off the front patio at Chez Woody. Located on a major thoroughfare, the soot and grime from years of exhaust needed rinsed off and away & so the neighbor & I had at it before the sun really got a chance to bake us. Finish was clean, crisp, minty, semi-sweet and very refreshing. I kept thinking that it was a lager. Unretire this one, put it in a CAN & you have a winner.

-Dated 09.15.16. Consumed 06.24.16.

A: Light orange, good clarity reveals a steady and fast paced rush of carbonation. Off white, creamy head is 0.5 inches and well retaining with very little window dressing.

S: Strong scents of honeysuckle and mint as advertised with some natural sweet tea hints. Light bourbon wiff, barrel wood, sweet malts and minty tea scents best sums this up. Good strength, very unique, props.

T: Light watery bourbon and natural tea leaves with mint on the open. Spicy with some lime and herbaceous notes. Nice strength, again as with nose, very unique with the mint being very noticeable, but also very well balanced with respect to the rest of the drink. Malt sweetness with some fruitiness perhaps from the yeast, apple, grapes, honeysuckle bourbon and wine like flair. Chardonay barrel notes, butterscotch sweetness and minty tea leaves on the finish.

M: Soft light to medium body mouthful, lightly creamer like texture, good carbonation crisp.

O: Props for a unique flavor and well balanced effort, the mint really highlights here with good results, fresh, with some fruity yeastiness that reminds me somewhat of a belgian style beer. Cleanly brewed and worth a try.

12 oz. bottle, enjoy by 09/18/16 printed on the label. Acquired a single bottle of this stuff from the local bottle shop - "Ale brewed with mint leaves, honeysuckle, and bourbon natural flavors".

Poured into Flying Dog stemware.

A - Pours a bright hazy golden body, lots of chill haze, and one finger of frothy white head that falls to a ring. Minor fluffy scattered random lacing. Appears adequately carbonated. Looks okay for what it is.

S - Aroma is comprised of earthy leafy menthol / kaffir lime leaf, with subtle notes of flowers & lemongrass (honeysuckle?), and a very prominent bourbon-soaked oak chip aroma. Maybe a touch musty / chalky. Smells like they achieved what they were going for, but it's not always entirely pleasant.

T - Taste follows the nose: earthy, leafy, minty, herbal, subtle notes of honey sweetness makes an occasional appearance, floral & grassy, with notes of cola, cherry, vanilla, oak wood, & bourbon soaked oak chips on the finish. The more this drinks on, the more in reminds me of the Spiteful Brewing Absinthe Barrel aged saisons that i tried a while back. Slightly grainy, papery, & semi-dry on the finish.

M - Feel is smooth & fluid. Crisp, clean, medium-bodied, with okay carb. Not particularly light or easy to put down. One of these is definitely enough.

Overall, glad to have been able to try it, but this is a definitely one and done.

smakawhat, Jun 04, 2016
3.43/5  rDev +3.9%
look: 3 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 2.5 | overall: 3.5

Bought this beer at a local discount liquor store on a whim. I'm a bit of a sucker for weird ideas, and this certainly qualifies. The bottle describes it as "brewed with mint leaves, honeysuckle, and bourbon". I have had two bottles of the six-pack so far: the first time I found quite disagreeable but upon giving it another try I can say I will certainly be keeping the remainder for myself.

Appearance: Light golden with a moderate head that dissipates rather quickly. Persistent carbonation bubbles continue to rise over the course of half an hour.

Smell: As expected, the first whiff smells overwhelmingly of mint, occluding any of the more traditional beer smells. Once the nose gets over the shock, there is a mild malt aroma underneath. No hops or yeast to be smelt anywhere near this beer.

Taste: Very unremarkable in the mouth, but produces a sweeping range of flavors once swallowed. An herbal sweetness hits first, followed quickly by the taste of mint, which gradually gives in to an almost smokey aftertaste. The combination of mint and smoke is unusual; I can't quite decide if I like it or not.

Feel: Creamy, with moderate carbonation that quickly fills the mouth. Upon swallowing there is a very mild herbal astringency.

Overall: Much better than I was expecting, which is not really saying much. The taste is truly an experience I've never had in another beer, and I'm impressed with the fact that Flying Dog managed to make such a beer palatable. That said, it takes a little while to get used to, and given that it's a limited release experimental brew, I'm not sure it's necessarily worth it.

Recommendation: If you can find it in singles, you've got to get one. Drink it only slightly chilled, and try to give it a shot. No guarantees you'll love it, but it's certainly worth the experience.

rrmills, Apr 16, 2016
1.45/5  rDev -56.1%
look: 3.25 | smell: 2 | taste: 1 | feel: 1.75 | overall: 1

I can't offer a complete review, because I was pretty disgusted by this beer and never bothered to pour it into a glass after a couple small sips from the bottle. To qualify, I like Flying Dog in general for being local to me, and having generally tasty and diverse beers. I got a single bottle of their Mint Julep Ale expecting it to be a so-so novelty beer that wouldn't be great, but would be drinkable. Boy, was I wrong.

I'm reviewing, since I was pretty curious about this one, and it is probably the worst beer I've ever tasted. Or at least the most disappointing, based on somewhat decent expectations.

The smell is pretty flat, slightly woody and leafy. Not the lively, herbaceous mint with rich, sweet bourbon that I'd expect.

The taste was quite frankly horrible. A mint julep should have a fresh mint taste, and light sweetness from simple syrup. This isn't sweet at all, which might work for most beers, but completely fails the experiment here. It also lacks any freshness from mint or any good qualities of bourbon. I literally almost gagged on the first sip, and after a second taste I couldn't go any further. The taste is bitter, mucky, and medicinal. There is a slight minty aftertaste, but it comes too late to make the beer enjoyable in any way.

I didn't pour the beer, so I can't say much about how it looked. But it was a dark gold or light amber when I poured most of the bottle down the drain. The label is simple for a Flying Dog Ralph Steadman design, which works for the experimental Brewhouse Rarities series. I'd say the look struck me as slightly above average, which is the best aspect of this beer.

The mouthfeel wasn't terrible, if you could detach it from the taste and aroma. It was very flat -- little to no detectable carbonation. It was smooth-flowing, and pretty thin. Nothing really remarkable here, although I think more carbonation would have helped since it would complement the lively mint flavor in a mint julep (or mimic a splash of soda into the cocktail). But again, this beer tasted awful, so the thin mouthfeel probably helped in that the flavor didn't linger.

Overall, this beer struck me as horrible, despite what I think were fair expectations and general appreciation for the brewery. So bad that it inspired my first review on here...which is long overdue, since I've relied on this site for advice for years. I'll try to be more active on here with beers in the future, nearly all of which are assured to be better than this one.

The bottom line: avoid this one, except maybe as a prank or to see an adult's yuk face like when you give a baby their first lemon.
