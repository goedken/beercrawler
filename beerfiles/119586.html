Brewed by:
Grimm Artisanal Ales
New York, United States
grimmales.com

Style: American Double / Imperial Stout

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 10.00%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
Double Negative pours jet black with burnt orange highlights. A long lasting tan head leaves abundant lacing. Aromas bursting from the glass include coffee, bitter chocolate, caramel, black currant and blueberry. Creamy and viscous on the palate, the flavor initially follows the nose. A pop of smooth bitterness comes after, and then a lingering, roasty finish.

Drank from a 1pt. 6 fl. oz. bottle purchased at Cambridge Wine & Spirits, Cambridge, MA
November 2015
Served in a snifter

Inky and viscous, near pitch black appearance. The thick, shimmery liquid was capped by a thin film of tan colored froth. Within seconds, the crown transformed into a small collar. Spotty drops of lacing trickled down the glass.

The nose was incredibly roasty and smoky. Dark and robust. Bit of anise. Decent amount of booziness. This almost had a subtle woody, bourbon-like quality to it. Some sweetness came forth as the beer sat. Brown sugar and chocolate syrup were both noticeable.

Big smoky, roasty notes appeared in the flavor profile as well. Rich and malty. Bakers chocolate appeared in the center. There was a metallic note on the back end and in the finish, but this subsided with time. Some acrid, char-like notes appeared too. Good amount of alcohol heat. All in all, a bit raw and rough around the edges. Like the nose, the flavor profile tuned sweeter and smoother with time. Vanilla was noticeable. On the second pour, some earthy hops were also detectible.

The mouthfeel was a touch thin for the style but still solid. Really creamy and slick. The liquid contained a super fine, frothy effervescence that turned fluffy and smooth on the palate.

I am a huge fan of Grimm's sour beers but this imperial stout was a bit of a letdown. The roastiness was a bit overdone and the mouthfeel seemed rather thin. As other reviewers have noted, this reminded me of a smaller version of The Abyss.

oberon, Oct 07, 2016
3.45/5  rDev -18.8%
look: 3.5 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.25

Bomber size bottle at a bar in Brooklyn. Cost was $21 (the same as Mexican Cake). August 2016 vintage. 10% ABV.

Shiny tan colour head. Creamy and soft in appearance, with 4-5 minute retention.

Body is, predictably, an opaque black. No yeast or sediment is visible within.

Chocolate, chocolate malt, dark malts, a blip of anise, some lovely roasted barley, hints of rich dark fruit (plum mainly). Sweet though balanced, promising a rich dessert stout. Aromatic intensity is mild. Shaping up to be really tasty stuff.

Tasty it is, with chewy notes of dark fruit/caramelized fruit (plum mainly, maybe dates or even figs or prunes). Plenty of chocolatey sweetness is present, but subtle roasted barley keeps it in check. Still, a bit more roast would be nice. (Coffee additions wouldn't hurt, either). Subtly integrated blueberry/bilberry is the highlight in this build, eclipsing the plum easily as the most fascinating element at play in terms of dark fruit and the beer as a whole.

The chocolate covered blueberry gives this a unique flavour not commonly observed in imperial stouts. Balanced to boot, with enough intriguing elements to separate it from the pack.

Texture is silky smooth, wet, chewy, and full-bodied without ever being too heavy or boozy on the palate. A softer creamier more leathery presence on the palate would help coax out a bit more depth of flavour from this only mildly expressive brew. Good carbonation.

Drinkable and approachable for a big bold imperial stout, though wanting in terms of intricacy, depth of flavour, and impeccable balance. Not among the greats in this oversaturated American style, but the blueberry note really pops in the midst of the chocolatey flavours.

High B- (3.45) / WORTHY

look: 4.5 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.25

2016 batch. Consumed 9/15/16.

A: Pours a dark brown; sits almost pitch black in the glass. Minimal transparency. About a finger+ of soft, pillowy light brown head.

S: Dark chocolate, raisins, and a hint of dark roast coffee. Doesn't smell overly sweet; rather, its very roasty, especially on the chocolate and coffee notes.

T: Follows the nose. Heavily roasted coffee with chocolate (sweet milk and bitter dark). Hint of caramel sweetness and dry, pithy raisin.

M: Medium plus to full. This is the best aspect of this beer, as it feels even bigger than its ABV would suggest. Very creamy, but not at all syrupy or sticky. It has weight on the palate, but the flavors glide away soon after the sip. It's very well carbonated, making for a perfectly balanced feel.

O: This is an extremely well executed imperial stout without any adjuncts. It hits all of the expected notes in a more 'traditional' brew in the style. It's not decadent, nor is it syrupy/sludgy/sweet. Rather, it strikes a fine balance between roasty coffee/chocolate, sweet caramel/chocolate, and dry/hoppy raisin that, combined with a superb mouthfeel, makes for an enjoyable and easy-drinking 10% imperial stout. Nicely done, Grimm!

kalosjakar, Sep 04, 2016
4.46/5  rDev +4.9%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.5

August 2016 bottle, pours spent motor oil black color with a foamy 1 finger tan head that was packed with tiny bubbles along with bubbles popping on top of the head as it dissipated very, very slowly, leaving really good spotty lacing and rings as it went down.

Smells of dark chocolate, roasted malts, charred wood, cocoa powder, anise, molasses, bakers chocolate, lactose sugar like a roasty milk stout, faint piney hops, coffee grinds, earthy notes and more chocolate. Some dark fruits peek through, but the nose is all roasted malts and chocolate. It's simple yet gives off a ton of complexities.

Tastes very similar to the nose with the astringent roasty and charred malt notes kicking it off with dark chocolate, coffee grinds, cocoa powder and bakers chocolate. The next wave of flavor is roasted malts, light anise or black licorice, lactose sugar, brown sugar, Hershey's chocolate syrup, burnt coffee, half and half or a chocolate milk sweetness, piney hops, lemon zest, mild peppery spices, brownie batter and faint dark fruit. A lot of char and ash seem to come out as it warms with more chocolate and coffee complexities that completely hide the 10% ABV. The bitterness leans towards the high side but for me is where it should be for an imperial stout that is chocolate and coffee driven.

Mouthfeel is creamy, almost buttery smooth, with a chewy viscous feel that has mild carbonation, a minor alcohol burn and a bitterness that resembles a Starbucks bold black coffee. The aftertaste is bitter, roasty and full of chocolate, anise, coffee and roasted grains allowing just enough malty and hoppy sweetness to keep it in check.

There is something so simple yet so delicious about this beer that made it a great slow sipper that got better as it warmed. Depth and rich malty complexities made each sip slightly different and I look forward to aging my second bottle and having it a year from now to compare the changes. I'm sure this will age gracefully since it's excellent tasting now.
