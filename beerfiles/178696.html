Brewed by:
Grimm Artisanal Ales
New York, United States
grimmales.com

Style: American Wild Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 5.00%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
Psychokinesis is a hoppy, tart American Wild ale fermented with a mixed culture of yeast and lactobacillus and conditioned on American white oak. Dry-hopped with Mosaic, El Dorado, and Jarrylo hops. This beer has never been boiled or filtered.

a weird one from grimm, surprised to learn its not a kettle sour, it certainly has that same acidity and tang to it, nowhere near funky enough to be bretty and oak aged right? its awfully mild on the yeast front, super clean, and with only a little real sourness to it. its incredibly light too, pale watered down yellow, nearly clear. the head is fizzy and white and only lasts a minute. major hoppy nose, fruity and new school, heavy citrus and pineapple, lime zest, really bright with the mouthwatering lacto tang. the flavor is also quite delicate, hoppy without being at all bitter, and soft on the acidity. lemon and lime notes to this, no trace of it being done with oak, and almost no funk, seems more bacterial than bretty by far. i like the hops, really the best feature of this. decent brew overall, but not the best these guys can do, and really pretty one dimensional for a wild ale. tastes like a quick simple souring process on the front end. good beer, the hops rule, but this lacks complexity overall.

look: 4 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

Picked this up a while back in New York and finally got around to it today since I had a chance to take some time off of work. Bottle had a nice design to it and was dated from April, which led me to wonder if this would taste fresh or not. Thankfully, this did as it was quite lively and tart, which was in line with how easy it was to enjoy thanks to the light amount of alcohol in body and taste.

This beer had a nice look to it as the pour was cloudy, straw, and lively. Lots of carbonation here though the big bubbles in the head quickly settled down and didn't remain as foam or lacing on the side of my pint glass. Not a strong aroma to this as there was just a hint of tart apple and lactobacillus to be found. More of those were present in the taste, as they accompanied an earthy funk, subtle hops, and underlying oak from the aging that gave this beer a wonderful but understated depth. Lots of carbonation on top of tht as there was a good amount of lemon and lime here, but it came out more in the mouthfeel than the actual taste. This almost felt like soda given how crisp the aftertaste was and how the citrus emerged more after the liquid went down, especially in the juicy slickness that coated my palate in between each sip.

This was as tasty, easygoing, and solid as a session beer could get since this was so easy to polish off. While not overtly full of yeast, there was enough to keep this lively while being in proportion with the hops and flavors that could be found here. A really enjoyable beer perfect for an early summer day such as this, that wasn't too funky for a Wild Ale. Easily worth the effort to find and obtain!

dalizzie, Jun 11, 2016
3.4/5  rDev -18.5%
look: 2.5 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.25 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

Pours a cloudy pale straw color - take away the turbidity, and it's at Bud Light levels of SRM. The head is big and bubbly, with three fingers of height. Retention, however, is absolutely horrid. Tiny specks of lacing are left behind on the glass.

The aroma really brings that dank-yet-citrusy tone Mosaic brings (when used right). There are also nice hints of mango, papaya, and passionfruit - it even seems juicy at times! On the other hand, the lacto aspect is very subtle. I get it, just not assertively. Still, everything comes together nicely with some richly defined notes.

This is far more sour tasting than the nose indicated. Big tartness combines with tropical fruit notes open it up well. Slowly but surely, some of the danker, grassier aspects of Mosaic show up - though it never devolves into sweet onion. I also get a little bit of oaky flavors near the back end. The hop profile has nice nuance to it, but the souring yeast unfortunately doesn't. There are some very good - nay, some great - elements to this beer, they just don't integrate spectacularly for my tastes.

At first, the feel seems light, fizzy, and refreshing. Problem is, a thickly lumpen sweetness lingers on the palate post-swallow. It does balance some of the tartness after a while, so it's not completely annoying. And the drinkability is off the charts, as well.

I like it, but there are some aspects of the flavor profile that seem strange to me. It's as if it doesn't seem perfectly balanced. Considering how much I paid for this bottle, I won't be buying it again.

3.4/5: Psychowhatsis?

FriedSlug, Apr 27, 2016
4.31/5  rDev +3.4%
look: 4 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

L: Pours a very turbid light yellow, almost incandescent like Gatorade, with practically no head.

S: Lots of mealy, sweet tropical and stone fruits, not so much the bright and acidic variety. Apricots, peaches, papaya and melon, particularly watermelon. There’s a clean lactic sourness in the background. There’s more influence from the Hüll Melon and El Dorado hops than the Mosaic in this one.

T: Medium tartness with lots of bright, fairly juicy fruity hop flavors and a strong StarBurst note. The hoppiness paired with the tartness creates a nice puckering, mouthwatering effect. As with the aroma, the Hüll Melon and El Dorado show themselves more than the Mosaic. I’m not getting much influence from the oak, which is probably good because it likely would have counteracted the brightness I’m liking a lot in this beer. Very dry, rounding out to a bit more malt in the finish. Lots of melon and papaya in the finish, with a touch of hoppy grassiness.

F: Medium-light bodied with abundant prickly carbonation and an acidic, slightly tannic mouthfeel.

Thoughts: Loved it. Really masterful selection of complimentary hop varieties in this one. Personally I love the dry-hopped sours we’re seeing more of these days, and this is one of the more memorable ones I’ve had. People are going crazy for Grimm’s DIPAs and Imperial Stouts, but I think the sours are my favorite part of their lineup.

Ciocanelu, Mar 20, 2016
3.99/5  rDev -4.3%
look: 3.75 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

Pail straw, and cloudy as an opal, it seems to glow beneath a thin short lived fizzy white head that leaves a ring of lace.

Powerful grapefruit whoops one in the olfactory, followed by tropical notes. Eyes closed this would equal any straight grapefruit juice at first impression. With a little time, one may notice a mustiness.

Opens with mild sourness and a good dose of tart grapefruit juice. The sweetness is a bit much, similar to packaged grapefruit juice. Pineapples comes out in the middle. Throughout the acid level is high, but bitterness is held in check until it warms and the pallet acclimates to the tartness. The finish is long, dry and tart.

The body is thin with a slick watery texture, while the carbonation is fizzy and the alcohol is imperceptible.

Intended or not, this is a hybrid sour IPA. This is an interesting and welcome trend, worthy of further exploration. However, it is difficult to consume more than 8oz of a style the assaults the senses as powerfully as this hybridization. Ultimately I suspect there will be a spectrum of these beers, with welcome representatives at the extreme edges, and the moderate middle. There is a place for beers that put forward flavor, another place for beers that put forward the bitter, sour, tart, aspects, and another for the blending of these sensations.
