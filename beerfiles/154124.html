Purchased as part of a pick six, 12 ounce bottle, "Enjoy By" date of 5/2/16. Chilled to 39 degrees, allowed to sit for 5-10 minutes before opening, poured into a large wine glass.

Look: Pours a nice orange/red amber color with a 1inch tan colored head. Noticeable carbonation held the head up longer than some other beers and continued until the end. When head finally dissipated it left a light foam coating with some lacing. Very promising visuals.

Smell: Hard to determine, some floral/grassy notes, bit of spice, hint of pine.

Taste: Hops and malt seem to be balanced at the start, lightly resinous, grassy but not totally unpleasant aftertaste.

Mouthfeel: Medium body, carbonation is noticeable throughout.

Overall: Drinkable but unimpressive. Cool looking label seemed to promise more than I got. It really held it's carbonation well, always a plus. Not a bad beer by any measure and would certainly like to try this again. Curious to know if draft would be different from bottled.

Schoeni, Jan 20, 2016
4.01/5  rDev +5%
look: 3.75 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 4.25

Victory brewing is quite a nice brewery. This Hop Devil I have seen many times on the shelf, but the label is a bit cartoonish and so it took me awhile to finally give it a try. This one is 6.7% ABV.

Look-It pours a beautiful dark copper amber that glints with hues of red in the sun. The lacing is sparse lending to the thin head that settles quickly.

Smell-It smells of malty breads, molasses, oak, and a bit of sweetness. The hops arent as noticeable as the malts. It is a bit fruity and very malty. A bit of worry overcomes me.

Taste-The worry fades away as I taste a medium to full bodied hoppy brew with excellent flavors of pine, grassy hops, caramel, some fruits, and some mild citrus. The malts can really be described as dark breads and oaky deliciousness, but the hops do come through nicely on the palate.

Mouthfeel-It is, as Ive said, medium to full bodied and has an oily hoppy finish. Nice lingering on the tongue that captures its hop essence.

Overall-It is a really nice beer. This IPA is going to sipped on again by myself in the future. Cheers!
