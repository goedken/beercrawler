Sampled back on 1/14/16 with a good friend. Comes in a 12-ounce bottle ($6.99 a 6-pack). Bottle(s) chilled down to 36 degrees F in my beer cooler and poured in to a "CHEERS-BOSTON" handled-glass stein.

Brew pours a clear reddish-amber with a thin off-white head. Really not much lacing is seen when sipped. Aroma is of grains with some honey sweetness. Pretty much can be said for the taste. The sweetness from the honey does stand out but is still a decent brew. No bitterness or harshness at all. Lighter-side of medium-bodied with lower carbonation. Overall a decent brew.

Very light for an amber, more yellow honey than reddish or brown. Also pretty incredible as to its clarity. Very small head that goes away in a hurry to go with somewhat-below average carbonation. Decent lacing, though. The smell is sweet and malty, but not particularly appealing. The taste is sweet, and there is more body and depth to the flavor than the visual would lead you to believe, but I wish there was a bit more bitterness to balance the honey. The feel is smooth, particularly after an initial blast of sharpness from the carbonation. Overall, this is a decent beer, and an interesting take on this style, but there's nothing about it that's particularly captivating that would make me want to revisit it. Still, for $6.50 a sixer (as an Aldi house brew), it's worth a try.

This brew breaks a rule - ambers are supposed to be pretty. Otherwise, it is surprisingly good.

It's dark yellow with just a bit of fizz. The smell is sweet and slightly spicey, but faint.

The taste is very nice. Sweet and malty with a touch of hops. Very good. The aftertaste is nice too - sweet. It all has a bit of bite too.

I was leery about ever buying beer at Aldi's after the bad European beers. I saw this a couple times before I picked these up thinking I would regret it, but they are pretty good, and I would definitely recommend them if you don't have time to get better - there were three in the sampler and they were all OK.
