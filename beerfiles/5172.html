oberon, Sep 10, 2007
3.44/5  rDev -8.5%
look: 4.5 | smell: 3 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

Lightly fogged rich amber with a splash and a half of orange juice. Slow, steady streams of carbonation can be seen plunking into the underside of a gorgeous, vanilla milkshake creamy, alabaster cap. The beer's best feature may just be the abundant, fine, filagreed lace that puts the appearance into the APA Hall of Fame.

For a beer brewed with (and dry-hopped with) 100% Cascade hops, Pale Ale doesn't smell like it. The grapefruity hops are nearly overcome by a funky, phenolic (Band-Aid-like) aroma that better go into hiding on the palate if this beer hopes to impress me. If I tell myself that I'm smelling a Belgian pale ale... nah, that won't work.

Things are a little better on the palate. For some reason, my taste buds are better able to (not completely able to) ignore the off-flavors and concentrate on the usual ones. The usual ones include a decent amount of pale malt and a more than decent amount of bitter orange, peach and grapefruit zesty American hops.

I have to believe that some whacked-out yeast strain was used to get the beer to taste this way. Since Jolly Pumpkin brews the beer that Grizzly Peak BC chooses to bottle, at least this one, maybe they decided to mix things up a little and throw us a curve ball. I prefer high, hard fastballs when it comes to hoppy beer.

The mouthfeel is on the medium to slightly full side for an American pale ale. It comes together nicely and becomes at least slightly creamy as it sits. I don't really mind the more vigorous than usual carbonation since it adds to the mouth interest more than it annoys.

Grizzly Peak Pale Ale is a distinctive offering, even if it isn't an amazingly delicious one. I'd be interested to see if the phenolic qualities of this bottle are present in all batches, or if they're unique to this one. There's potential here, but the beer needs a funkectomy before I'll be fully onboard. Sometimes simpler is better.

Brewed and bottled for Grizzly Peak by Jolly Pumpkin. Wonder if any of the wild microbes got in this batch?? One can hope!

Pours a brilliant deep golden with a faint haze. Solid two fingers of thick, creamy, off-white head that crawls the sides of the glass effortlessly. Nose is an intoxicating mix of buttery biscuit malt, apricot, floral perfume, lavendar and herbal, minty bitterness. I swear there is a slight tart twang too. The mixture of all these aromas is incredible!

Lemon-citrus bitterness up front. Biscuity malt, shortbread cookie, and tart fruit follow. Floral, herbal, and lemon hop bitterness cleans up sufficiently with vaguely tart overtones. Overall, a very perfumey, floral, lavendary tasting pale ale. Moderate body, with a soapy feel that turns dry and acidic and refreshing in the finish. A very nice summer quaffer.
