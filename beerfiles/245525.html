22 ounce bottle into tulip glass, bottled on 7/22/2016. Pours lightly hazy/cloudy medium golden yellow color with a 2 finger dense and rocky white head with great retention, that reduces to a nice cap that lingers. Nice spotty soapy lacing clings around the glass, with a moderate amount of streaming carbonation retaining the cap. Aromas of big lemon zest, orange peel, pear, apple, coriander, peppercorn, clove, light banana/bubblegum, wheat, biscuit, light honey, herbal, floral, grass, and yeast earthiness. Damn nice aromas with great balance and complexity of fruity/earthy yeast, spices, and pale malt notes; with great strength. Taste of big lemon zest, orange peel, pear, apple, coriander, peppercorn, clove, light banana/bubblegum, wheat, biscuit, light honey, herbal, floral, grass, and yeast earthiness. Moderate herbal/citrus peel bitterness and yeast spiciness on the finish; with lingering notes of lemon zest, orange peel, pear, apple, coriander, peppercorn, clove, light banana/bubblegum, wheat, biscuit, light honey, and herbal/yeast earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Damn nice complexity, robustness, and balance of fruity/earthy yeast, spices, and pale malt flavors; with a great malt/bitter/spiciness balance, and zero astringent flavors after the finish. Light-moderate dryness from bitter/spiciness, increasing through the glass. Medium carbonation and body; with a very smooth, moderately creamy/grainy/sticky, and slightly resinous balanced mouthfeel that is great. Minimal warming alcohol for 7.5%. Overall this is a great imperial witbier! All around great complexity, robustness, and balance of fruity/earthy yeast, spices, and pale malt flavors; very smooth and easy to drink for the ABV. Quite hoppy for the base style, but not enough to make it feel like an IPA. The traditional witbier flavors are the main showcase. A very enjoyable offering.

JayQue, Sep 14, 2016
4.12/5  rDev +9.6%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

I never got a chance to try the original, so props to Stone for doing an 'encore' series. Great idea!

Looks: like a Belgian pale ale or even a tripel. Great golden color with lots of clarity, and a nice head — no lacing.
Smells: again, like a tripel. Spice aromas come through, along with grass/hay and some fruity notes.
Tastes: like a strong hoppy witbier. I really dig it. Love the spices and hop bite. It could probably use a bit more maltiness to balance it out, but I don't mind.
Feels: great. I could drink this all day honestly. It's not the highest ABV but there's 0 boozyness to this. It's slightly dry, but very refreshing. It's been a very hot summer, so September is a perfect release time. This would be great for a hot summer lunch/dinner.

Overall: I would definitely buy this again. It's right up my alley in pretty much every aspect. Once again Stone hits all the marks i'm looking for and love. Props to Stone for releasing this as an 'encore' — even though this isn't the original i'm glad I got the chance to try it. I think they, and other breweries, should do this more often! If a beer was well received, why not release again for those that didn't have a chance?

Also, and many may disagree, but this reminds me of Victory "Golden Monkey" tripel. A lot of folks despise that tripel (overly sweet/boozy/etc) but i've always liked it. This beer hits the same spicy/hoppy/refreshing notes as Golden Monkey, but is definitely more refined.

3.34/5  rDev -11.2%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 3 | feel: 3 | overall: 2.75

Excited yet not really setting expectations high here. To be honest I was always told to not bother with seeking out the older vertical epics and I never did. I had a few chances to try the 03 and forward but passed. The 02 however was never on the table so I guess I'm pretty excited to see what all the fuse was about.

Poured into a stone vertical epic glass, the golden color gives hints or orange and looks like a summer beer with just a bit more protein and density than a lighter beer, but still in a pint glass I'd assume I was getting ready to have a nice summer beer. The head forms large and proud, but this is not a thick or dense head, and it fades after a few moments.

The nose instantly has me thinking about hefs, bananas, pears, grass, hay, lemon zest, light vanilla, cane sugar, wheat, yeast. The aroma is very rich and really nice.

The rich aroma might be a hint of higher abv, the taste leaves no doubt. Bitter herbal hop flavors with interesting grassy spices, Belgian spices and German ones perhaps . The flavors here are not mixing or playing nicely with each other. The hops are giving a truly bitter bite and there's not much malt to bring things back in line. I can see why they felt this would hold up to some age, a decade being excessive perhaps but a year might bring things together really nicely. As for the spices I don't know the primary spice flavor here, people say coriander but I haven't a clue what that is like. I am also picking up clove.

The abv pushes this to a dry wine like mouth feel. What initially smelled and looked a bit more of a summer beer, is tasting like something that I'd want to pair a nice dinner with. I'm not sure what though this certainly is a beer to pair with cheese.

I will point out this is not a go to style of mine and though this is not traditional for the style, I suppose that should be pointed out with my score. Overall it's an interesting beer. I'm not upset spending 9 dollars on it. There's a lot going on here, but they didn't bring it together.
