BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Neshaminy Creek Brewing Company
Pennsylvania, United States
neshaminycreekbrewing.com

Style: American Pale Ale (APA)

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 5.20%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
"It goes without saying that if you’ve spent any time in and around the Philly area you undoubtedly have heard your fair share of Philly slang and local colloquialism. Some things just scream ‘Philly’ so bad, it almost hurts. Anyone familiar with our Head Brewer knows he’s got a fondness for the linguistic arts as well. Whether it be his loud, booming Jersey/Philly accent cutting across the room, or his late night drunken German, there’s no doubting his love for the sound of the spoken word of any native tongue. He’s also got a fondness for easy drinking, hoppy beers. What do you get when you put the together? J.A.W.N. – Juicy Ale With Nugget. This 5.2% ABV American Pale Ale is malty, yet crisp, and is loaded with dank citrus, as well as tropical and stone fruit flavor and aroma from the gratuitous use of Nugget and Zythos hops."

Added by InspectorBob on 11-09-2014

12 oz can

Pours a slightly hazy amber golden, inch of white head retains as a sudsy lacing.

Smells of grapefruit, orange juice, pine, maple, and biscuit.

Taste is of citrus juice up front, orange and grapefruit, as well as citrus zest, followed by a mild piney and citrusy hop flavor over a dry biscuit malt.

Medium body with tingly carbonation on the tongue.

Solid juicy pale ale, decent balance of hop and juice over a more subdued malt base. Drinks easy, quenches thirst, worth trying and something I'd be willing to go back to.

MMOSNN, May 29, 2016
3.98/5  rDev +1%
look: 4 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 4

A: Pours a relatively clear golden amber in color with moderate amounts of active visible carbonation along the edges of the glass and some moderate honey orange highlights. The beer has a finger tall foamy tan head that slowly reduces to a large patch of mottled thin film covering a little more than 90% of the surface of the beer and a medium thick ring at the edges of the glass. Moderate amounts of lacing are observed.

S: Moderate aromas of dank, herbal, and citrus/grapefruit pith hops over the top of light to moderate aromas of biscuit + caramel malts.

T: Upfront there is a moderate flavor of biscuit + caramel malts with a light amount of caramel sweetness. That is followed by moderate flavors of dank, herbal, and citrus/grapefruit pith hops which contribute a light to moderate amount of bitterness which does not linger for too long.

M: Medium bodied with moderate amounts of carbonation. Crisp initially with a hint of dryness in the finish.

O: A pretty easy drinking APA with really nice hop aromas and to a lesser extent hop flavors. Pretty well balanced with well hidden ABV. Enjoyable.

Had this on 2015-12-16 in Columbia, PA from a growler and again on 2016-06-21 from a 12oz can.

A - Fairly clear golden-orange with a 1.5-finger white head that sticks around with decent lace.

S - Moderately dank, biscuit, touch of orange and pine.

T - Red grapefruit flesh, biscuit, light chocolate malt, lots of pine. The finish is on the bitter side for an APA but not a tongue scrapper and there's a bit of bread-orange-chocolate sweetness for balance in the aftertaste.

M - Light-medium body and medium carbonation. Quite dry and a bit prickly.
