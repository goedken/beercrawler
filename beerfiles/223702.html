Canned on 10/12/16, consumed on 11/10/16.

A: Poured into a Duvel tulip, the body is black. A quarter-finger light-brown head settles on top and recedes pretty quickly.

S: Not really a pleasant nose - heavy on the milk side of things, with milk chocolate and chocolate milk. Lactose. Artificial vanilla.

T: Improves here. Sweet malt - molasses, some charcoal, Yoo-Hoo, cocoa powder and brown bread - with a touch of nuttiness on the finish. Vanilla taste lingers on the aftertaste. Overall impression: sweet and chocolatey.

M: A little flat in feel; call it medium in heft.

O: Good. Not great, and honestly, this isn't a style I love, so YMMV.

Overall: A very good Imperial Milk Stout. Good milk chocolate sweetness balanced by some roast bitterness.

A- Can poured into a tulip with a motor oil, pitch black body and a dark mocha brown cap. The head makes it up to about half an inch displaying good retention. Spotty lacing in somewhat low quantities.

S- Luscious decadence. Quality dark chocolate opens up the aroma leading into cocoa nibs, coffee, heavily caramelized grains, roast, slight char, licorice and a milky sugar character. Clean fermentation and no alcohol here.

T- The roastiness that was folded into the chocolate really steps up its game but so does the sweetness which provides a balance between roasty acidity and rich dessert sweetness. The milk sugar part of this is very obvious and it plays well with rich dark chocolate, caramel, burnt barley, licorice, malted milk balls, coffee and vanilla sweets.

MF- Extremely big bodied and very creamy; beyond chewy. The carbonation comes in at a medium level and perhaps a slight bit towards the lower end which suits the richness of the experience. Silky, oily and extremely smooth.

The third can of the four pack for me and I really enjoy this rich milk stout. The ferment is very clean allowing the chocolate, roast and milk sugars to shine to the fullest extent. Sweet but not cloying.

From the bottom of the can: "BB NOV 2017 5/31/16 09:08"
First off, from the can this is listed as an "Imperial Chocolate Milk Stout."

Appearance: Black body with a fizzy light brown head. The head forms nicely at first but fizzes out to a ring and a few filmy islands. No lacing.

Aroma: There is a burnt aroma with a bit of a woody character.

Taste: While drinking this beer I never noticed any alcohol which is very notable for a beer that sits at a lofty 8% ABV. The beer has a bit of a sweet side but it feels very watered down. There just isn't a whole lot of flavor. No chocolate flavor except at the end where I got just a touch of cocoa from the aftertaste. The beer has a bit of an acrid metallic taste that is common in heavily roasted barley. Even this is very light but stronger than most near non-existent flavors throughout the beer.

Mouthfeel: Very thin for a milk stout, imperial or otherwise. The beer just doesn't have any texture. It has a lot of carbonation giving it a bit of a bite. The finish is dry. No bitterness. It is very easy to drink.

Overall: I don't see myself buying this again. As a chocolate beer it just doesn't have enough chocolate flavor and as an imperial milk stout it just doesn't have enough texture to make it enjoyable. Overall it is rather watered down and pretty flavorless. I wouldn't recommend it unless you like to collect pink cans or something.

justintcoons, Jun 03, 2016
4.02/5  rDev +2.6%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Poured from the can into a nonic pint glass. Best before Sept 2017?

Really dark body, nearly black with an almost red edge to it and not a single bit of light getting through this at all. Head creation is a classic mocha brown, on the lighter shade a bit with an easy foamy three finger and uniform creation. Little retention with bigger bubbles and dissolves to a good solid finger.

Porter smelling dry grains, hints of charcoal and mild ash, a small touch of roast and far less hinting sweetness if at all on first whiff. Mild sensing coffee bean and a little bit of molasses kicks in as well.

Very interesting milk stout angle on the palate. First sip is a pleasant light bodied roast, then a light sweetened hit of coffee and milk. Finishes with an almost whoppers candy sweetness but more toned down. Some of the alcohol while noticeably present manages to interestingly tone down the sweetness. There's a noticeable hop astringency that finishes as well, which brings out a nice dark bitter chocolate accent as well.

A very unique take on a milk stout that's more steered towards roast and chocolate flavors, with a hinting bitterness that lets sweet and lactose take a secondary seat. For this I am thankful.
