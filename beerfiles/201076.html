Brewed by:
Trillium Brewing Company
Massachusetts, United States
trilliumbrewing.com

Style: American IPA

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.00%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
Our first IPA brewed and packaged at our new facility in Canton, MA. El Dorado, Citra, and Columbus hops create a blast of citrus zest, pineapple, wet earth, and woody aromatics. The flavor brings a pithy bitterness and just a touch of toasted malt. Medium bodied with a dry, minerally finish.

MALT: American 2-row Barley, White Wheat, Flaked Wheat, Dextrine, C-15

HOPS: El Dorado, Citra, CTZ

JohnnyMc, Oct 05, 2016
4.37/5  rDev +2.8%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

Trillium Scaled 09/01/16

A: Pours basically full-pulp orange juice, like most Trillium does: completely opaque, bright, glowing orange. A pure white head of two fingers sits atop and remains for a moderate period. Lacing is very nice, however. Looks quite viscous. If NE IPA is a style, this is a near perfect-looking example. 4.5/5

A: Orange-heavy, grapefruit, citrus rind, creamy malt, a surprising pine aroma (for Trillium and for the style), plus plenty of stonefruit. Candied pineapple rounds it out. The biggest gripe is that it all feels a bit faint, toned down; it does not jump out of the glass like most Trillium beers. 4.25/5

T: It hits the same notes as the aroma on the palate, but everything is intensified. So much apricot and peach hits up front that it is almost tart. Then bitter orange rind picks up, helped along by orange juice, some crackery malt, and a surprisingly hefty dose of herbaceous, floral, earthy, pine bitterness. This is strange for the substyle, which usually traffics in fruit cocktail to the max; it truly comes off more like a hybrid of Coasts, in the best sort of way. Delicately balanced. The bitterness lingers a bit long and comes off a little vegetal, however, but that is a minor knock. 4.5/5

M: As in the appearance, this drinks a lot like carbonated, whole-pulp OJ. The carbonation is soft and welcome. Viscosity is medium (medium low for the style), but there is still that traditional creaminess that one expects from NE IPAs. That lingering astringency is, again, too much. 4.25/5

O: While this probably is not one of Trillium's best offerings, it is still an incredibly solid, well-put-together beer. This is also perhaps the one that could win over those West Coast haters given its moderate bitterness. I personally found it unexpected and a bit much, but it is still a treat to drink. Pair with an above-average release from an amazing band, like Opeth's "Deliverance." 4.25/5

Poured from a 16oz can into Artaic glassware, can dated 9/1:

Appearance: pours pretty much standard hazy IPA style. Not as murky and hazy as Headroom or some of Trillium's heavier beers, but still packs a super bright orange-yellow color, with a sudsy and sticky head.

Aroma: more subtle than Trillium's other beers in that it doesn't immediately pour out the can upon opening, but there's still lots of nice scents here. Light biscuit and dry malt, with some mildly bitter notes and some tangerine and citrus.

Taste: again, the fruit here is much more subtle and less potent than Trillium's other hop bombs, but it's still a solid IPA. Citrus, tangerine are present, but also light, crackery malt and a larger punch of bitterness at the finish than some.

Feel: medium body, moderately carbonated, with a semi-dry finish.

Overall: it's not my favorite Trillium brew, but definitely served as a nice base beer for Scaled Up and Scaled Way Up, both of which were amazing. Still, an excellent beer on its own.

larryi86, Aug 26, 2016
4.46/5  rDev +4.9%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.5

Making hoppy beer look easy, the masterful brewers at Trillium hit the IPA head on with the classic three-headed tandem of hops that ensure a gripping, dry bitterness but use advancements in hops to give a juiciness to its taste that's a rare treat for hop-heads.

Scaled pours with the familiar turbidity that denotes New England IPA. Muddy and golden, the beer slowly sheds a bubbly blanket of froth and a truly seductive tropical and citrus-laden scent. Its radiant fruity perfume leads to a candied malt flavor of honey, simple syrup and faint caramel- all taking the shape of angel-food cake.

Creamy sweetness drifts away across the middle palate while the hops continue to build upon its highly aromatic showcase. Pink grapefruit, mandarin orange, lemon, passionfruit, pineapple, gooseberry, honeydew and apricot all lay tracks of marmalade and sherbet. Its delicacy develops into a sharp but smooth bitterness that brings out the beer's more herbaceous, earthy and woodsy flavors.

Medium-dry and trending creamy but light, the beer's early fullness quickly tightens into a crisp, clean and resolutely bitter finish. Hints of plater, sassafras tea and fresh grassiness decorate the finish as its bitter grip grows in the gullet for a long sprucy, peppery linger.

750ml bottle, 5.12.2016 stamped on the side, poured into a tumbler glass.

The beer pours a super hazy and cloudy golden copper color with a finger of soft white head, very taut with good retention and nice lace. The aroma is pleasant, very citrus forward, juicy, dank with a bit of resinous character, light pale grain sneaks out as well offering some sweetness, but there is a lot of wildflower, juicy tropical fruit and pine that lingers behind after the initial citrus blast. The flavors are good, nice citrus fruit, bitter rind, a touch of pine, tropical fruits and a nice wildflower character mixes well with soft wheat and pale grain and a nice mineral water quality. Verdict: A really nice IPA from Trillium and actually, I prefer this to the double dry hopped version. There is a bit less bitterness and more balance to this beer, a bit more rounded than the all out hop attack that is the dry hopped bottle. Juicy hop goodness that is sure to please any hop head.
