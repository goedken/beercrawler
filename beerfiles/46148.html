3.89/5  rDev +14.4%
look: 4 | smell: 5 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

I found this single 12oz brown bottle at the Binny's store in Glen Ellyn, IL for $1.59. There is a freshness date chart along the left side of the label, but no one bothered to notch anything onto it, so I have no idea how fresh the beer is or isn't.

The beer poured a hazy golden/yellow color with a super fast rising head, so be careful on the pour. When finished pouring, fully of the glass was filled with fluffy white foam that lasted several minutes and left behind tons of lacing.

Huge fresh blueberry smell to this along with some yeast and pale malt. Definitely NOT a blueberry pancake syrupy smell. This was not sugary sweet at all. Of the few blueberry beers I've tried so far, this was by far the best smelling one of them all.

While the taste is also dominated by blueberry flavor, it is not at all as strong a flavor as the smell would suggest it might be. Mildly tart blueberry flavor along with some biscuity malt and yeast flavors. Not picking up much hop presence.

Light body, dry finish, and highly carbonated - almost as much as a soda.

I'm not a fan of blueberry beers, so I wouldn't be one to finish off 6 of these anyway, but if I had to chose one to drink - this would be it, Very drinkable and the alcohol is completely hidden.

scruffwhor, Oct 17, 2009
3.62/5  rDev +6.5%
look: 3.5 | smell: 4 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

Orange peel orange, not blueberry blue. A thick slice of manila colored foam fills the upper quarter of the pint glass and looks pretty good doing it. Chubby squiggles of suds should be around for the duration. No one would ever know that this is a blueberry ale by looks alone, which is as it should be.

The scent of bursting blueberries (with a slight perfuminess) was noted on crown cracking and continues to be appreciated... even though the glass is several feet away from my nose at present. With respect to aroma, all I ask of a fruit beer is plenty of natural fruit essence. Mission accomplished.

Even though Maiden's Blueberry Ale's taste doesn't match its aroma, this is a pretty good version. Failing to find the right sweet-bitter balance has tripped up many a fruit ale. MBA favors the bitter end of the spectrum more than I'd like. If hop flavor was present, it'd be okay. Instead, it's a flavorless, crushed aspirin-like quality.

On the other hand, it isn't that much of a negative because there's plenty of blueberry flavor. There might be a little German hop flavor in there too, it's hard to say for sure. Some of the bitterness seems to be abating as the beer warms, which is a good thing.

The body/mouthfeel is a tweener. I think I'll go with the lower score because... well, just because. It's light-medium, fades a bit too much on the finish, and has bubbles that are within the wide range of acceptable.

All things considered, Sherwood Forest Maiden's Blueberry Ale is a pretty good effort. It isn't the best blueberry ale that I've ever had (Town Hall Blueberry Hill), but it's far from the worst. Thanks to damienblack for the extra.

The fourth kind of beer in my monthly beer package from the Flying Noodle company, a gift from some colleagues at work and my regular opportunity to try some beers that I would never otherwise try -- such as this one.

The appearance is one of the best aspects of this beer -- swirls of lacing, with a body that is slightly cloudy and brassy. The head recedes to about an eighth of an inch and stays there.

The aroma - how to rate this? In terms of volume, you would be tempted to give it a 5. I opened the beer at the counter and reached up to get a glass, and before I could even grab the glass (never mind pour the beer into it) that corner of the kitchen suddenly smelled like blueberries. But, that dissipates quickly, and when you actually put your nose to the glass, you get an aroma that is more acrid than sweet, and I find the combination of hops and blueberries to be less than attractive as the beer warms up.

Gritty and raspy in the mouth, leaving an unpleasant, sourish fruity aftertaste. I cannot deny that there are blueberry notes in the flavor, but they seem overly sweet and artificial.

I will be interested to see if blueberry beer aficionados agree with my assessment; this is my first blueberry beer, and I am not impressed.
