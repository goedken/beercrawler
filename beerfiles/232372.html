Brewed by:

lagunitas.com

Style: American Pale Ale (APA)

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 5.50%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
Tropically Hoppy. Light, yet Full-Bodied. Bright and Citrusy. Word.

The magical, mystical 12th of Never is a blend of Old and New School hops that play bright citrus, rich coconut, and papaya-esque flavors, all on a solid stage of English puffed wheat. Tropically hoppy. Light, yet full-bodied. Bright and citrusy. The 12th of Never Ale is everything we’ve learned about making hop-forward beer expressed in a moderate voice. Pale, cold, slightly alcoholic and bitter. It’s all we know.

These 12oz mini-kegs (AKA cans) are an exciting new option for us, and we are stoopid stoked at the opportunity for y’all to take us to all those new, nelophobic locations…

I picked up a can of Lagunitas 12th OF Never Ale at Boyne Country Provisions. I'm in the mood for something hoppy tonight and I'm interested to see what Lagunitas can do with this one, so lets see how it goes. 2016 Vintage. Poured from can into a IPA glass.

A- The can looks good, it's got a good design to it and it's a little eye catching. It poured a nice yellow color that took on more of a golden orange hue when held to the light with over three fingers worth of very fluffy off white head that died down to a thick ring that stayed till the end and it left some nice patchy lacing behind. This is a very nice looking beer, I have no complaints.

S- The aroma starts off with a slightly higher amount of medium sweetness with the hops being the first to show up and they impart a big tropical fruit like aroma with some pineapple, mango and other tropical and citrus fruit showing up followed by some green hops and floral hops showing up in the background. Up next comes a little bit of doughy yeast that leads into the malts which impart a pale malt, grainy, bready, and light biscuit malt like aroma. This beer has a very nice aroma, I really like the hop profile and it's well balanced.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma and it starts off with a medium amount of sweetness with the hops still being the first to show up and they impart all the same aspects that they did int he aroma, this time more of the fruity aspects show up with not much of floral hops and just a little bit of green hops. Up next comes some doughy yeast and it's followed by the malts which impart all the same aspects that they did in the aroma, but they are just a little lighter this time. On the finish there's a lighter amount of bitterness with a nice hoppy aftertaste and just a little bit of malts showing up. This is a very nice tasting beer, I like the hop profile and the balance.

M- Smooth, crisp, a little refreshing, slightly prickly, on the lighter side of being medium bodied with a higher amount of carbonation. The mouthfeel is good and it works well with this beer.

Overall I thought this was a very nice beer and a very nice example of the style, it pretty much blurred the line between a pale ale and IPA, but as either one it was still a tasty bee with a good hop profile and just enough balance. This beer had good drinkability, I could drink a few of these with out getting tired of it. What I like the most about this beer is the hop profile and balance, they were great and the highlights of this beer. What I like the least about this beer is nothing, there isn't anything that set this beer back. I would buy this beer again and I would recommend trying it. All in all I was happy with this beer, I know Lagunitas has some skills with the hops, but this was still a little impressive with how well all the aspects came together. It's one of the better Lagunitas beers I've had and it's a great example of the style. Great job guys, this is one tasty beer, keep it up.

When you're everywhere, then you're nowhere. Pulling from both the new and old, as it pertains to hops and malt, is Lagunitas' attempt to bring several generations of beer together in one seamless taste in order to refute that notion that when you're everywhere, then you're nowhere.

Bright straw and with a faint haze, the scent of melon, herb, citrus and pine align on the nose while its taste is softly sweet of cashew, biscuit, caramel and honey. All things equal, the flavors carry a slight less-than moderate play on the early palate.

With the taste traversing across the tongue, the sweetness quivers and the hops gain steam for a flavors of candied pink grapefruit, lemon, orange and papaya. Tropical pineapple, passionfruit and mango show an exotic flavor as the taste dives into a moderate bitterness of pine, citrus peel and herb.

Slightly dank, grassy and floral, the finish leaves a medium-dry sensation behind along with mulled herbs that linger onward. Supported with a light honey balance the flavors of brewing worlds colliding is realized. Though noble in its fellowship, the beer's "old world" character leaves it limited.

Absolutely love this new pale ale and this has almost 2 months on it, so I can't wait to find it fresh.

Smells fruity. I get peach and citrus with some malt sweetness (doesn't smell "malty" though)
Taste follows nose - great bright citrus and some peach. Hop flavors take center stage, not having competition as their is very little malt presence (just cracker sweetness), and only moderate bitterness.
F- It is well carbonated which helps keep it crisp and the hops feel bright.
O- This is in the top 3 of Lagunitas hoppy offerings for me (though I haven't had Waldo's or Born Yesterday). This has the refreshment of a fine pilsner but with such a great new american hop profile. It reminds me a lot of Luponic, only I prefer this.
