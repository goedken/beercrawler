barczar, Aug 05, 2016
4/5  rDev -0.7%
look: 4 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

I picked up a bottle of Oddside's Nihilist a couple weeks ago for $4.59 at The Lager Mill. I've had El Dankerino many times, but I'm not sure how it would be bourbon barrel aged and I'm interested to see how this one turns out, so lets see how it goes. 2016 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a snifter.

A- The label is nice, it goes well with the name and it's e ye catching. It poured a nice darker orange color that took on more of a golden orange hue when held to the light with a little over two fingers worth of very creamy very light tan head that died down to a thin ring that eventually faded away and it left some patchy lacing behind. This is a good looking beer, I have no complaints.

S- The aroma starts off with a higher amount of sweetness with a fruity hop aroma being the first the first to show up and it imparts all those nice unique fruit hops aromas from El Dankerino with pineapple, citrus rinds and light coconut like aromas coming through. Up next comes a sweet and creamy aroma that goes into a noticeable caramel aroma that flows right into the bourbon barrel which is on the lighter side with more of a light bourbon, oak and vanilla like aspects coming through. Towards the end comes some sweet malts and a light alcohol aroma. This beer has a good aroma, the bourbon barrel works surprisingly well with it.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma, but lighter and it starts off with a higher amount of medium sweetness with the fruity hops still being the first to show up and they impart all the same fruity aspects that it did in the aroma with no single aspect sticking out the most. Up next comes some sweet malts that lead into the alcohol which is noticeable and a little warming, but it's not overly boozy and it's followed by that same caramel aspect that I got in the aroma that mixed with the bourbon barrel which impart all the same aspects that they did in the aroma. On the finish there's somewhat lighter amount of medium bitterness with some sweetness and alcohol also showing up and it had a fruity hop aftertaste along with some caramel and bourbon barrel flavors. This is a good tasting beer, it's easy to tell that it's still El Dankerino and the bourbon barrel just adds a little to it and it doesn't really take away from the base beer which is nice.

M- Fairly smooth, crisp, not too boozy, on the thicker side of being medium bodied with a higher amount of medium carbonation. The mouthfeel is good and it works well with this beer.

Overall I thought this was a good beer and good example of the style, it had all the aspects that I was looking for and I thought it was the balance that really helped this beer, if the balance was any different I don't think it would be as good. This beer had ok drinkability, one is enough for me, it might start to get boozy if I have more than one. What I like the most about this beer was the balance, it's just where it needs to be. What I like the least about this beer is nothing, it's fine just the way it is. I would buy this beer again and I would recommend it to anyone looking for a unique DIPA with a little bit of bourbon barrel to it. All in all I was happy with this beer and I was little impressed how well the bourbon barrel worked with it, I still prefer the original version, but this is still a nice take on it. It's not a favorite Oddsides beer and It's a good example of a bourbon barrel aged DIPA. Good job guys, this is a tasty and unique beer, keep up these creative brews.

CNoj012, Mar 21, 2016
4/5  rDev -0.7%
look: 4.5 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 4 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4

12oz bottle from LambicPentameter (thanks man!). Bottled 1/26/16. Served in a goblet.

Pours a clear burnt gold with two and a half fingers of fluffy ecrue head. The froth fades to a tall central island that thins as it disburses towards the edges. A frothy, jagged crown around the edges leaves wide swaths of lace in its wake.

The nose combines oak, pine, citrus, bourbon and a little caramel. There are some slightly floral hop notes that join the pine and citrus as a counterpoint to the boozey bourbon.

Flavors are a tad hot but there is an interesting blend here. Semi astringent pine, bourbon, citrus zest, wet wood, flower petals, vague tropical fruit, singed caramel, berry seeds, bread, a little peppery spice, faint vanilla and plenty of booze. Overall there is a good mix of bitter IPA and barrel character. Despite the alcohol heat this is quite tasty and not at all difficult to drink.

Full, almost creamy body with fairly light, pillowy carbonation. The lingering bitter finish contains pine sap, orange peel and bourbon soaked oak.

Oddside Ales offers an all around solid take on the increasingly popular barrel aged imperial IPA. They have mixed the elements well to allow both the base hoppy beast and the bourbon barrel elements to shine through. Well done and worth a try.
