This brew uses their Rebel, Freedom, Yaquina, and Alluvial Hops. I have a sweet memory of Rogue Farms for having literally stumbled on the place after taking a tiny ferry across the Willamette. A: Dense, darken orange, with a moderate and sporadic release of carbonation. Off white frothy head of 0.5 inches is well lasting providing a intrique lace pattern down the vessel of very good coverage. The coloring is really dark, juicy orange juice like.

M: Nice body, medium weight, lightly dry, creamy malts with a spicy bite of hops warming the inner cheeks.

O: Overall haven't been to impressed with Rogue lately and their experimental beers but this really did impress me and keeps pace with the dank, citrusy tropical oj like ipas that are sweeping the beer shelves. Dank citrus and tropical notes are prominent, with a very good rate of drinkability at 4.44% which has a lot of malt depth and tons of flavorful hops that drinks much bigger and can hang with most of the respected ipas on the shelves today. 6 pack worthy and very good overall.

beerthulhu, Jun 25, 2016
4.48/5  rDev +18.8%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.5

I like it when brewers do cute things, like do a four-hop beer that rings in at 4.44% ABV. But I also worry this will lead to further confusion among pretentious beer neophytes, who already have zero idea of what hops actually are. "This one's got, like, six hops, man. You can tell by the alcohol."

Oh why does beer make everyone stupid? Or, more direct, why does beer cause people to become so confident in, and sometimes viciously protective of, their own stupidity?

Us seasoned beer people--those of who read more than 3 books a year, at least--we ain't stupid so much as we're tribal. We used to love Rogue because they were one of the first brewers that 1) brewed high-ish IBU beers, 2) made great and accessible versions of classic styles, and 3) took risks with some big styles (which they released in 7 ounce bottles, because that was acceptable back then). Now, we switched, for disparate reasons: 1) some of their more recent risks have sucked, 2) they are overpriced even by the horrific standards of 2016, and 3) they're not eXtreme enough anymore and none of their IPAs taste like mango juice.

Personally, I think tribalism is fine--or at least unavoidable, and so we should try to temper it with self-examination and wordy self-recrimination. I realize I am a prejudiced and idiotic bastard, and my ponderous bleatings about as much serve to cleanse my opinion of the myopia which sullies lesser beersmen.

This--this tastes like my young adulthood, the first 3-4 years when I was first getting into good beer. That's why I like it quite a bit. It does not taste like much I've had lately, though, and so if your coming of age occurred more recently, after the point when brewers figured out how to make 8.5% ABV IPAs taste like hop soda, then your happybones will most likely not be tickled. You might even find it offputting.

It pours hazy and quite dark for an IPA, a gold bordering on amber. Very nice, retentive head and pleasant lacing.

Smells primarily earthen, with some sharp nodes of pine and biscuit. Old school and well-balanced. Tastes the same: mid-90s hoppy, very beery, no overt fruit. Great yeast and bitter hops finish things out.
