look: 3.5 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Thin head, barely half a finger of orange tinted eggshell tan color, the lacing has decent thickness but close to no stickiness. Cloudy yellow-orange liquid, opaque and holds light easily, hard to see any bubbles floating inside. The nose full of grains, wheat and sourdough, pepper, earth and grass, spoonful of molasses yet not a lot of pine, citrus or pit fruit, small dose of cocoa, kind of tough and gritty and without a sustained sweet component. Medium-bodied, the carbonation grinds things in place, not much spreading out. Cocoa, mocha, orange peel, pine and nectarine to pineapple fruit give it a nicely typical IPA profile, at the same time it stays within itself, no distracting burn. Credible malt presence, brings more sweetness than any citrus or flowers. Compact ending without seeming short. Lack of flash might tempt you to underrate it.

look: 4 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Pours a cloudy amber color with a finger plus of light beige head. Decent lacing and a bit of stick.

Smell has some piney hops and a bit of grapefruit, with lots of caramel and a biscuity malt aroma too. More malty than many IPAs.

Taste also has a significant malt component but also a good deal of hop flavor and bitterness, which I'm glad to see. Caramel malt is a very solid backbone, and like the smell it has a biscuity, almost toasty flavor as well. The hops have more character than in the nose though, with some earthy, pine and pithy grapefruit flavor. Bitterness is pretty average for an IPA; not overwhelming but definitely makes its presence known. This is a pretty balanced IPA, with plenty of hop character but not a tastebud killer.

Mouthfeel is medium bodied, pretty crisp and drying in the finish. The hop bitterness lingers in the finish a bit along with an earthiness.

This is a very solid IPA. It's more of a balanced IPA than just a hop bomb but that's ok by me, as there's enough hop flavor and bitterness to make it interesting. Solid brew I'd be happy to have again.

Another damn fine brew that I picked up with my great New England package that I recently acquired. This one was served cold and poured into a pint glass. Consumed on 10/13/2008.

The pour was very nice. Rich dark amber in color with a decent amount of haze to it. Some nice carbonation could be seen streaming up from the bottom of the glass and coming to head of about two inches in height and settling down to a thin layer a half an inch in height. Some light side glass lacing.

The aroma was your typical, good IPA fair here. Nice and deep resin like hops. Lots of floral and citrus notes that came flowing through right away. Sticky, with a nice fresh, smell to it. The flavor followed more of the same. Started with a nice and biting hop note that quickly followed with a slightly bitter, and bread like mid palate that gave up to the rich and sticky, dry mouth that is pretty damn fine for a good IPA. Nice medium body with a great carbonation profile and a really smooth follow through. Definitly sessionable, there was no way this one was anything but easy going down.

Overall this was a very solid IPA. One of the best I have had in recent times. Since I had never heard of the brewer before, I have to admit I was quite impressed. I would have no issue with trying this one again anyday!
