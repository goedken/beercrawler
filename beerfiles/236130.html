Well, here again is one of those Where Did You Score That? moments. My lips are sealed, but understand that here at The CANQuest (tm), we adhere to the idea that it pays to advertise. This was a lark over a decade ago, but it pre-positioned me to be at the forefront of the CANned craft beer explosion. People want to get their CANned beers into my hands and I am happy to accept. Plus, I get credit for the approved photo (another basic tenet of The CANQuest (tm)).

From the CAN: "Single hop Extra India Pale Ale".

The Crack! went off without a hitch, but I do not like to ChANce what CAN occur with an inverted Glug from a pint-sized CAN. Instead, a gentle Glug was the order of the day. I still wound up with three fingers of dense, rocky, foamy, bone-white head with great retention in the face of this evening's humidity. Color was a marginally hazy Yellow-Gold (SRM = 4 - 5). How did I know? The Gelt Gang passed up their ChANce, since they prefer their Gold to be pure. Oh well, Their loss. Nose had the cattiness that I have finally come to recognize. The term was foreign to me for a long time, but Todd actually clued me onto it. Here we have a single-hop varietal utilizing a hop with very distinct characteristics, not the least being its odor. Wow! Mouthfeel was medium, but rather soft, especially after some of the evening's previous IPAs. The taste was not as intense as I might once have described it. Yes, there was a definite "cattiness", but along with it was a really pleasant blueberry pancake flavor. I know that it should have also included pine notes, but I just could not parse them out. Dang, this was the best of the night! Let us chalk it up to best for last, since if I know that I may not be perceiving some intrinsic flavors, then it may be time to rack out. Finish was dry, but not puckering or outright unpleasant, both of which I have experienced. I still say blueberry pancakes with cat wee in place of syrup, but I also get a light lemon biscotti flavor at the extreme finish. My my. I really enjoyed this beer, but more as a one-off single varietal. One was plenty for me, but it also gave me a ChANce to test my palate. Bed time!

Can poured into flute.

Golden, yellow haze, thick foamy, large-bubbled head that pops itself out and forms a ring around the glass.

Big up-front splash of hop aromas instantly when you open the can. Big earthy, piney, resinous and the overused work dank. This moves into a juicy citrus and some spiciness.

The flavors are all similar to the aroma, but a bit more subdued. Smooth and effortless to drink, appealing sip after sip. Earthy hop flavors, subtle spiciness, and tropical/citrus. Just a bit of malt sweetness reminds you this is a DIPA, no sign of alcohol.

Medium bodied, moderate carbonation. Very smooth and easy drinking for a double.

My favorite offering to date from Sole. The aroma is the big player here. Every aspect was great, but flavors more subdued when compared to the aroma.

Had this on 2016-07-07 in Columbia, PA from a very fresh 16oz can.

A - Hazy straw-gold with a 1.5-finger white head that drops quickly leaving decent lace.

S - Orange flesh, mild dank, grassy/herbal. Smells fresh; not like cat pee. Pleasant but not an aroma bomb.

T - Mildly Simcoe-flavored hop forward, cracker, mango, melon, honeysuckle, apple, light pine. The good hop flavor on the finish blends nicely with the fruit flavors.. Not aggressively bitter, which I appreciate. Tasty but not complex.

M - Light-medium body and carbonation. Just a bit resinous but not prickly.

O - Pretty simple flavors. Easy to drink. Doesn't take advantage of the 7.5% ABV IMHO in that this level of flavor and aroma could be accomplished in an <5% ABV brew. In that sense, it's dangerous at 7.5%!
