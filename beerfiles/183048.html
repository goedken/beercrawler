Aroma of limes, lemons, sea water, herbs, and fruity coriander. Bright and complex. Minerally and a little funky. Acidity is notable. Almost has a lime soda kind of aroma.

Pours murky yellow/orange with a fizzy white head that fades quickly. No lacing. No head retention.

Tons of acidity and bright citrus upfront. Very juicy. Mouthwatering acidity. Mineral salinity and sea water follow and just a hint of bread dough. Acidity seems to be all lactic as it washes away quickly. Incredibly refreshing and thirst-quenching. So much fruit going on. Again, lime soda like without the sweetness.

Light bodied with high carbonation despite the lack of head. Dry and acidic. My mouth continues to water as I drink it.

One of the best gose's I've had recently. Solid acidity and balance. I have to add that this is my favorite beer label ever.

StonedTrippin, Nov 01, 2015
4/5  rDev +2%
look: 4 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Influenced by traditional Euro-brands but gaining confidence in the American boldness in brewing, the Dugges brewers link up with Baltimore's Stillwater Artisnal Ales for a clean and concise sour ale that sells well the character that's best about, or all things- a northern German sour wheat ale.

Bright straw and clear like chardonnay, those wine-like attributes continue with the beer's militant carbonation that fuels a spritzy head and racing perfume. Lemon, lime, sour white grape and green apple frame the aromas while the supple confectionate sugars of malt lay a powdered sugar underlay.

Malty sweetness and short-lived breadiness dissolves effortlessly on the middle palate and paves the way for the acidic white wine, honey mead, and fully fermented fruit to take turns panging away at the tastebuds. With a light aura of burlap the ale finds balance with a light peppery tilt and a soft rebuttal of concord grape.

Light and easy, the beer finishes with a saline roundness to keep the ale firm and of sustenance, and sprinkled with the soft perfume of citrusy coriander. Crisp and clean acidity reinforces the beer's sharp sourness to pucker the mouth and to cleans the palate of all thirst in a quick and snappy aftertaste of baking bread.
