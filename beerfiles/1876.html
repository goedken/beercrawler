Look:
Lower on the carbonation side and a very minimal head. The head that was there diminishes quickly. There is a bit of decent lacing that continues after. It is a nice copper/orange color that is clear enough to read through.

Smell:
Smells of ginger (of course), but not as powerful as expected. There is a faint fruity and caramel malt smell, but not very aromatic otherwise.

Taste:
Slight bitterness at first, but then the ginger comes in. It is laid back and not in your face as some ginger beers are. Some malty flavors come through and some earthiness. Overall sweet and well balanced.

Feel:
Lighter side of medium with an interesting tingle.

Overall:
It was ok, but I'm not a big ginger beer fan. It was not all that flavorful and not much on aroma. I probably wouldn't buy again -Left Hand has a lot of other great beers to choose from.
