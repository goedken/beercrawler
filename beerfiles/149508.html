Strong bourbon smell and taste. Good carbonation, not too much or too little. I like more bourbon forward beers and this definitely meets that preference. It's like a stronger, more bourbon-y backwoods bastard, though some of the complexity of flavors in bastard are probably overshadowed by the barrel here. Arcadia did well on this. I've had barrel aged shipwreck porter and while that was good it wasn't amazing and I wouldn't buy it again, but the loch down nailed it. To me this was one of the more delicious bourbon barrel aged scotch ales I've had and I wouldn't hesitate to pick up a few more at least for $9/per 12 oz bottle if I can find it again.

dropbombs25, Jan 23, 2015
3.78/5  rDev +21.9%
look: 4 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 3.5

12 ounce bottle into snifter; no bottle dating, but is a recent release. Pours hazy very dark brown color with a 1 finger dense light khaki head with good retention, that reduces to a thin cap that lingers. Light spotty soapy lacing clings around the glass, with a fair amount of streaming carbonation. Aromas of big raisin, prune, date, caramel, toffee, toast, molasses, bourbon, oak, licorice, brown sugar, brown bread, light chocolate, and herbal/yeast earthiness. Nice aromas with good complexity and solid balance of fruity/earthy yeast, dark/bready malt, and bourbon barrel notes; with good strength. A bit too fruity, but not bad. Taste of big raisin, plum, date, caramel, toffee, toast, bourbon, oak, brown sugar, brown bread, light vanilla, light chocolate, herbal, and yeast/oak earthiness. Minimal earthy herbal/oaky bitterness on the finish; with lingering notes of raisin, plum, date, caramel, toffee, toast, bourbon, oak, brown sugar, brown bread, light vanilla/chocolate, herbal, and yeast/oak earthiness on the finish. Very nice robustness and complexity of dark/bready malt, fruity/earthy yeast, and bourbon barrel flavors; with a nice malt/yeast/barrel balance and zero cloying sweetness after the finish. Light carbonation and medium bodied; with a very smooth and fairly creamy/bready mouthfeel that is nice. Alcohol is very well hidden with only a slight warming present after the finish. Overall this is a very good scotch ale style. All around good robustness, complexity, and balance of fruity/earthy yeast, dark/bready malt, and bourbon barrel flavors; and very smooth to sip on for the big ABV. A very enjoyable offering.
