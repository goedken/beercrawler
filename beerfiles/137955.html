iheartmyTHO, Dec 06, 2015
4.31/5  rDev +8.3%
look: 4.75 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.25

12oz bottle into snifter. 2014.

A: Pours a deep dark brown, near black, that sits heavy in the glass. The head is at least three fingers thick, light coffee in color, and extremely dense and thick. The head took forever to slowly collapse into a thick film that resembled melted chocolate ice-cream. Rocky chunks of lace were left down the glass.
S: Potent and rich nose with dominating notes of coffee and chili up front, followed by chocolate malt, boozy alcohol, slight raisin, and slight vanilla.
T: Rich flavors of chocolate, coffee, and chili with hints of molasses, cinnamon, vanilla, caramel, raisin, and an overall nutty and roasted quality. The finish is mildly bitter with a decent amount of spice, and the aftertaste has a hint of booze, thick fudge brownie, and toasted marshmallow.
M: Thick body, chew mouthfeel that is rich and potent, with medium-low carbonation. Alcohol is hidden surprisingly well.
O: Similar to the original but perhaps at tad more spicy. This went well on a cold winter evening.

notchucknorris, Mar 15, 2015
4.6/5  rDev +15.6%
look: 4.5 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.5

I liked bomb so I figured I should try their "Christmas" offering.

A - Pours a very dark brown and settles into a deep black with a small dark brown colored head with moderate lacing.

S - Full aroma with spices, roasted malt, and chocolate. The spices are traditional like ginger, nutmeg, and cinnamon but they don't overpower but blend nicely with the stout base.

T - Much like the smell but more stout balanced with the sweet stout flavors of cocoa, malt, and coffee on the front. The spices fill the middle with soft warming ginger, cinnamon, and nutmeg and maybe some of the "chili" from the base beer. The back of the tongue has a nice roasted character that lets the flavors blend together. Lots of flavors that sit well.

M - Big mouth-feel. low to moderate carbonation. Overall balanced but slightly cloying and the flavors just stick to the palate.

Overall, this is a very good "spiced" imperial stout. This beer is an improvement upon the base beer with the more balanced approach and harmonious flavors. The spices are there but only compliment and not overpower. I wouldn't want to drink this too often and I could see the spices changing over time and batch to batch but if you like the base beer I see no reason you wouldn't like this adaptation.

Poured from the bottle into a snifter glass.

Rich and creamy forming head. Thick two fingers and dark mocha brown color, looking rich and lively. Settles to a thick collar and nice thin whispy top. Body color is very dark into a dark walnut brown nearly black opaque body, with perhaps a few red to brown chestnut hues. Great looking stout.

Fun nose. Hints of spruce tips and chili powder. Cherry like sensing fruit and a bit of dry bitter cocoa, a little powdery smelling particularly from a chipotle like smokey sensing angle and large amounts of cinnamon bark. Very nice hints of spice and chili, not so much on the coffee angle but quite nice.

Palate washes clean but also a little wet. Dry sensing mid palate filling with lots of powdered like brown spices, hinting moderately to pepper to an almost bitter clove, nutmeg, and lots of drying cinnamon. Finish and aftertaste is noticeably astringent with an almost metallic twinge. Good hint of a solid dry roasted grain in the middle, but manages to not really linger after a sip. Moderate controlled boozy warmth in the stomach feeling, but easily drinkable.

It's not terrible, but seems a bit of a let down from the regular bomb and the cinnamon qualities really take over everything else. Still a solid spiced stout regardless.

February 14, 2015

12 oz bottle, snifter

A – Jet black, with mild viscosity in the pour. A thin layer, about a centimeter, of bubbly brown foam rises and then disappears quickly, leaving chocolate-tan lacing on top of the beer.

S – Chocolate and roasted coffee, somewhat bitter, and impression of nutmeg and other mulling spices. Some alcohol warmth and spice, perhaps light fruitiness related to the alcohol. Some minty, woodsy hop aroma.

T – Chocolate, vanilla, and roasted malt are first here, but I definitely get the minty, woody, earthy/herbal impression from the hops, very strong in the mix. Nutmeg, clove, other mulling spices are all present in the background. Some roasty malt and hop bitterness, medium-high in the finish.

M – Full-bodied and slightly oily/viscous. Definitely some malt sweetness but the roasty bite and hop bitterness take hold. Medium-low carbonation, a bit dry in the finish relative to the body.

O – This is a very uncommon aroma/flavor profile in beer, but very fitting for a holiday brew. I don’t love it, but it accomplishes its purpose, and does it pretty well.
