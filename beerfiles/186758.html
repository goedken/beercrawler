schoolboy, Jul 31, 2016
3.69/5  rDev +0.5%
look: 3.75 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

In celebration of six years in existence, the jovial Clown Shoes brewers show a bitter side. Their sixth anniversary IPA is a smooth malty ale with a dominant hop aggression and a twinge of earth and spice that puts a little twang on its taste- kinda like a whammy bar for the tastebuds.

Whammy Bar pours a turbulent and aggressively carbonated hazy bruised peach color. Billowing with eggshell white froth, its scent is of citrus peel, herbal tree leaf, stem, pepper, plastic and medicine. Biscuity sweet malts carry a honey and caramel taste with hints of shortbread in taste and texture.

Mango, pineapple, apricot, peach, orange and nectarine all align on the middle palate with a mild marmalade-like sweetness bringing the flavors together. The bitter rebuttal is smooth and creamy with a strong herbal presence of sassafras, verbena, lime leaf, citrus peel, pith and rib and with a soft pinecone finish.

Medium-bodied and trending dry, the beer's lasting sweetness buffers the palate against the brash astringency that the hops carry. Biting, peppery and with a spicy latex bitterness, the hops more aggressive, chlorophyl and phenolic character begins to show in much the same way that America's early hop bombs would do.

From the bottom of the can: CANNED 05/24/16

Appearance: The head of the beer is pretty large and both fluffy and creamy looking. It is slightly off-white, about an eggshell color, and it has very good retention. The body of the beer is extremely hazy. It is a dark burnished gold and copper color that changes depending on how the light strikes it.

Aroma: It has a very nice sweet smelling fruity tangerine aroma. There is a touch of peach in there as well. It is a very inviting aroma. It doesn't give off that "fresh hop" aroma but the fruity tangerine and peach aroma is very nice.

Taste: It has an interesting flavor up front. It is a bit like a sweet caramel sauce and then it is immediately followed by a grain flavor. There isn't a ton of bitterness at first but the finish and aftertaste bring in a good deal of bitterness that just kind-of hangs out at the end.

Mouthfeel: It has good carbonation and it has a nice bitterness that is a bit resinous yet not aggressive. The body is light-to-medium and it has a good dry finish.

Overall: I enjoyed the beer and I would drink it again. I would recommend buying it at least once if you enjoy IPA's because it is a good beer and it's good to know that for future reference. I don't think I would make this a regular purchase but if it was mixed in an ice chest with a bunch of my other favorite IPA's and I grabbed it at random I wouldn't put it back and fish out something else.
