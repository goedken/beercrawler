Nsottek, Oct 28, 2015
4.21/5  rDev +16.3%
look: 4 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

Perhaps nestled in the midwest Evansville, Indiana landscape, but their hearts couldn't be any more in Belgium as unleash a superior clean lactic sourness that's deeply woven into the foundation of apricot fruit.

Damascene pours with a bright golden-straw color. Its brilliant sparkle showcases the steady dance of carbonation that races to the brim. While casting more of a champagne-like zest, it fills the nose with the distinctive twang of light and sour fruit, dry white wine and earthen balsa, cork and burlap. Its supple sweet taste is light of powdered sugar and soft wheat.

While the confectioner's sweetness dissolves quickly on the tongue, the sourness ushers in. First, the beer's base lactic sourness charges in with citrusy lemon and lime. Then come the under-ripe variety of berry and crabapple, and lastly the apricot provides its own sweet, earthy and condensed tropical character that commands the bulk of taste from middle palate onward.

Challenging each and every tastebud, the strong sourness puckers the mouth, tightens the jaws and evokes the saliva glands with its strongly piquant but clean sour taste. Finishing with warhead candy sweet and tart interplay, the beer's finish is dry, vinous and lightly of rice-wine vinegar. The return of true apricot fruit, earthy burlap and whole wheat bread all gently roll over a fast-acting aftertaste.
