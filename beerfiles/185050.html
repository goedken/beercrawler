Tone, Dec 30, 2015
4.19/5  rDev +4.5%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

Brewed with passion fruit and grapefruit; hopped with a blend of Citra, Mosaic, Azacca, and Lemondrop...

I pour the beer into a Belgian Independence Day glass tulip at an angle so as to allow the liquid to gently fill the volume and not exhaust itself in blooming into a tall head. As I level the glass from its cant, the head is a delicate three-fourth's of an inch in height; slowly, as the white recedes back to the huajillo amber surface below, it leaves a delicate lace in its place. A few fine jolts to the stem of the tulip reveals the readiness of the effervescence to break and fill the circular surface with enough bubbles to look white.

The smell from the beer is pleasant: passion fruit, nectar, meyer lemon, and ruby red grapefruit are blended and balanced into a detectable bouquet.

The flavors, as they encounter the palate in sequence, can be described, for myself, as an initial cold, quenching citrusy flesh covering a maltier, rich mouthfeel that masterfully eludes to the flavor that I had worried would have been overridden: passionfruit. If you have not purchased one of those ~33oz. cartons of passionfruit at your local grocery store for consumption, or if you have not ever consumed local passionfruit from the vine, then this flavor might be lost on the tongue (I would like to credit Cigar City a bit for this). Lastly, the hops are bringing in the crispness of the finish without acting to overly dry out the mouth; I enjoy the presence of the Lemondrop hops.

Overall, it is a worthwhile pale ale to be had during an irregularly-regular warm weather winter in Austin, Texas; especially, if you can appreciate tropical, refreshing pale ales alone as a beer.

Cheers & Happy Holidays

madelfonatree, Dec 15, 2015
4.18/5  rDev +4.2%
look: 4 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.25

Who doesn't love Boulevard, or Cigar City for that matter? Even though they're hundreds of miles apart, I didn't know it when I ordered this up at the Cloverleaf the other night. While strong and nuanced for an American Pale Ale, I really enjoyed this and how nuanced the flavors were in it, as the hops too a back seat to the "tropical" that was in this beer's nomenclature. Pale Ales should be balanced by nature and of course, that was the case with this one as well!

Nice pour to this as the golden hue, white head and lacing, and subtle haze all combined for a pleasing appearance. Lots of tropical fruit in the nose with a strong up-front hop presence that hit me upon the first sip. That was quickly overcome by the resinous nature of the mouthfeel, the hop oils, and the citrus pith that combined for the hallmark taste of this beer. Sticky without being cloying and full without being heavy, there was wonderful balance here as the Lemondrop and Azacca Hops gave this a puckering effect unlike any other beer that I've had before. The aftertaste was unique in the sense that I could feel lime and citrus pith more than anything else, leading this to have a bit of a dry-hopped aftertaste to it.

Slightly strong in alcoholic content, this beer never felt it nor did the booze overshadow the slick consistency of this and the wonderful array of fruit flavors. I love collaborations and this was one of the best that I ever had, as the Pale Ale was on par with Sierra Nevada when it came to quality and level of enjoyment. Really impressed with Boulevard and thankful that I'm able to enjoy so much of their lineup in this neck of the woods, as this American Pale Ale stood up with the best of the others that I've had. Wonderful stuff!
