No label information. Velvet Evil is brewed in the style of an Old Ale brewed with chocolate, raspberries and coffee. Available in 22 oz. bottles and on limited draft.

Poured from a 22 oz. bottle to a snifter glass.

(Appearance) Pours a frothy fizzed copper cream head over a deep brown body with mahogany highlights and Retention is poor and there is no visible and lasting lacing. 3.25

(Smell) Potent chocolate-covered raspberry fruit character up front, incredible chocolate nose with subtle, smooth bread malt underneath. Nice balance of fruit tartness with the sweet richness of the chocolate aroma. Potency is moderate. 3.75

(Taste) Rich, smooth and decadent chocolate candy flavor (not nearly my favorite, but tootsie rolls in particular come to mind) up front with undertones of raspberry fruit, a bit more subtle but still very evident. I don't pick up much on coffee, but I honestly don't think this beer needs it and I couldn't fathom the potent edge of coffee playing out well in addition to this beer. The malt base is substantial enough as a support base for the additives of chocolate and raspberry, although it doesn't provide much character of its own, this beer is deceptively drinkable for a 13% ABV ale. 4

(Mouthfeel) Texture is slick, lightly chewy, smooth, mildly dry. Carbonation is light, generating a light frothiness and a softly crisp finish. Body is medium+ for the style, shy of heavy overall. Balance is slightly sweet and syrupy over bitter and grainy. Alcohol presence is mellow and there are no off characters. 3.75

(Overall) A very solid chocolate-laden beer that is a satisfying dessert beer with lots of rich chocolate character and an underlying, but very evident raspberry note. Notably, this is one of my favorites from the brewery and I think it's worth giving it a shot, especially if you like sweeter, candied ales. 3.75

I'm not sure why this beer is marked as "retired." I just bought one off the shelf on 2015-12-14.
It pours a beautiful, deep, dark mahogany, almost black in my snifter. It has a creamy layer of foam that settles into a ring.
The smell is immediately chocolate and raspberries, like a dessert treat. Then there is a slightly bitter note. I assume that's the coffee, although it doesn't smell specifically like coffee.
The taste is almost as well balanced as the smell. It starts out with a bit more bitterness than the smell, and the chocolate and raspberry are a little more subdued. The coffee flavor comes out more on the finish after the beer warms up a bit.
It has a smooth with a creamy finish.
I think I'm a fan of old ales in general, and this one is unusual, but I'm enjoying it. It's not much as caramel as something like North Coast Old Stock Ale, but the chocolate, raspberry, and hint of coffee give it an earthiness and slight bitterness that balance the sweetness.

22oz bottle from Sprayberry Bottle Shop (Marietta, GA). 13.99. No bottling date. Served in a Barrel and Barley teku.

Pours a deep crimson mahogany with two fingers of pale sandy head. A thin but persistent collar remains, depositing only a few tiny specks of lace.

The aroma blends coffee, raspberries, toffee, mild cocoa, some caramel sweetness and an almost merlot quality. It sweetens a bit as it warms but the coffee and raspberry remain at the forefront.

The flavors are a heavily booze laden, bizarre blend of coffee, raspberry, toffee, caramel, chocolate, dark fruits, tobacco, an almost apple-like yeasty quality and a mild earthiness. As mentioned, the alchohol is hearty but that is not to say that it is too much or out of place. It actually goes down much easier than a 13% abv brew should.

Full bodied with an understated carbonation. Coffee, yeast, fruit, toffee sweetness and booze dance on the palate for a complex finish.

Velvet Evil is a strange but delicious brew. It's a mixture of fruity, malty, yeasty, boozey and sweet. Another high quality offering from Straight To Ale.
