Poured from a 12 oz brown bottle into a clear glass tulip. 05.26.16 on bottle neck.

A: Barely hazy gold with slightly under 1-finger off-white head with average retention and minimal to no lacing.

S: Melon and guava with grapefruit-lime accents

T: Flavor is unique but hopping is a bit muddled with the lime-citrus not working as well with the melon characteristics. Given this is a Galaxy-Citra hopped beer, there is no tropical fruit in the flavor at all. The maltiness seems crystal-free, using Munich or another more colored base malt possibly or a character malt instead. It is overly bitter, especially in the finish. No yeast or alcohol character.

M: Dry but downgraded for the bitterness. High carbonation and light bodied -- would dial down the carb a bit.

O: Pretty good but hops need a different ratio -- they seem even and muddled -- and the bitterness could be dialed down about 10 IBUs to enhance approachability.

argock, Jul 31, 2016
3.84/5  rDev +3.8%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

Second bottle from the variety pack. Bottling date is May 26th, 2016, so this is just under two months of age.

The beer is light golden in color and mostly clear, with some haziness. There's a lot of little bubbles rising up at all times, and the foam on top forms a nice thick head when pouring, shrinking down to a still-fairly-thick cap after a few minutes with a thicker layer around the edges of the glass. Leaves pretty good lacing all around the glass as the beer is drank -- it's easy to see how big your sips(/gulps) were based on where the "rings" around the glass end up. Looks nice!

Smell is some bread-crusty malt mixing with hops, little bit of fresh-cut grassiness, little bit of fruit skins, and little bit of spicy. Oddly enough there's a bit of an almost savory sort of thing in the background. A little weird when that shows up. It's pretty nice-smelling when it leans more toward the "grassy fruit skins" thing, though -- I really like that feature of Galaxy hops and it is definitely present here. As it's warming and I empty out more of the glass, that smell I like so much is becoming more and more prominent -- pineapple skins in particular seem to be standing out here and it's really nice. This is why I don't give up on beers that use those "tropical" hops -- Galaxy in particular -- despite the way my taste buds always make them a little hit-or-miss. Definitely going to have to bump up my score here from what I had originally put down.

Taste is similar, but with a bit more of a sweetness and some fruity flavor showing up at the start. Weird savory thing from the smell is not present at all, which is probably for the best. Shifts into an herbal sort of thing after the first sip with less fruitiness, but that background sweetness is still there and it doesn't go full-on onion-herbal-spicy despite by taste buds' best efforts to mess things up for me. Not a lot of bitterness, just a bit of lingering leafy bitter on the end starting around the halfway point of the glass. As stated above, when the flavor leans toward that "freshly-cut grass and the smell of fruit skins walking through the produce section" thing, it's really nice! My taste buds seem to be shifting back and forth between tasting that and something that leans more toward onion-spicy, but fortunately, today it seems they prefer to taste the "grassy-fruitskins" thing more than the "oh crap it's turned all onion-spicy again" thing. It never gets to a point where it's quite as nice as the smell, but it is still very good, much better than the way the first bottle tasted to me despite being over a week older. Glad I got to experience this beer tasting the way it's supposed to!

I would not have guessed that this was under 5% ABV going by the flavor and mouthfeel -- it's fairly light and definitely pretty drinkable, but the hoppiness and maltiness combining make it feel more substantial than you'd expect 4.6% ABV to be. Mouthfeel is nice overall, not super prickly, not even remotely watery, about what I expect from a lighter IPA (note, not a "hop water" session IPA -- I'm talking ones with at least some malt presence) or a 5%-ish pale ale.

Overall, this is a nice pale ale that makes good use of the Galaxy hops -- anything that pulls off that "fresh-cut grass and fruit skins" thing is a success in my book, and this beer (especially in the smell, though it's present in the taste too) does indeed pull that off. I have to say I prefer this to most of the other hoppy beers I've had from Devil's Backbone (Eight Point is pretty good but somewhat harsh at times, Bravo Four Point is pretty good but feels a bit thin, and I wasn't too impressed by the Striped Bass Pale... though to be fair the cans I had may have been a little old, I should give it a second try); the only ones that I'd say have been clearly better were some of the previous collaboration beers.
