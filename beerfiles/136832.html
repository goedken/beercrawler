StonedTrippin, Mar 10, 2015
3.51/5  rDev -5.6%
look: 4.5 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3 | overall: 3.5

Bottling date rubbed off. Poured into a Sam Adams Perfect Pint. $2.49 bottle.

Appearance: Great looking beer -- a very hazy golden yellow color with a two finger head and a bunch of lacing.

Smell: Herbal and grassy hops with some underlying grapefruit. Doesn't smell super vibrant to me, and really has the aroma of a "meh" IPA. I hope the taste is better.

Taste/feel: The feel is very thin/watery with a moderate carbonation to make this feel like a very slammable IPA... that's the first thing I'm thinking. As for taste, it's really pretty much on-par with the smell. Some herbal and grassy hops with just a little bit of citrus. The finish also has a biscuity malt quality to add some depth. There's a bitter finish on this, but it's a little more balanced than I thought it might be

At 6%, it's probably not quite a session IPA to most people, but I would say that it is, in large part to the mouthfeel. On one hand I enjoy the fact that it's a "low" ABV and super easy to drink, but on the other I'd like more body. This is a kind of "time and place" IPA... if this were mid-July and I were on a boat, at a party, or cutting the lawn, I'd get the most out of it. As it stands being early March with snow on the ground, it's not quite doing it for me. Still a decent enough IPA... maybe just not at the price of $2.50 a single.

Thanks to Another6Pack for this one! 12 oz bottle into a mug.

Appearance: Pours a cloudy orange-amber color. The head is three fingers high and off-white in shade. While the texture is pretty rough, retention is good. Soapy streaks of lacing are left on the glass. (2.75)

Smell: The focus here seems to be on lemon, with lesser hints of orange peel, grass, and pine. It becomes more lemony as it opens up. Slight maltiness also appears...as well as a bit of must. On the plus side, the hop character stops short of being overly harsh (as certain lemony, Amarillo-laden IPAs can be). (3.25)

Taste: Amarillo hops enter the picture first, with plenty of lemony hops. Some flavors of bread, cracker, and cream finish it off, along with traces of grassy pine. The level of bitterness is rather high. It's really straightforward, almost to a fault, but the Amarillo is shown off decently. (3.25)

Mouthfeel: I always like the crisp snap of an unfiltered ale, and I really like it here. Combined with the dry finish, this goes down very easily. Carbonation pokes through the thin body fairly well. (3.5)

Overall: Not bad at all. Yeah, it's a simple and straightforward IPA, but what's here is well measured. A solid enough choice for those who like their IPAs dank and bitter. (3.25)

3.25/5: Not quite a party in my mouth, but hardly a snoozefest

PaulyB83, Feb 19, 2015
3.61/5  rDev -3%
look: 4.25 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

I picked up a bottle of Oddside's Jesse's Party IPA last week for $2.09 at The lager Mill. I had a small sample of the last week and I thought it was a good IPA and I thought I would get a bottle to review, so lets see how it goes. 2015 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a snifter.

A- The label looks nice, it's got a nice design to it that goes with the name. It poured a slightly hazy golden color that took on more of an orange color when held to the light with a little over two fingers worth of creamy and chunky light tan lacing that died down to a thin ring that stayed till the end and it left some sticky and patchy lacing behind. This is a good looking beer, I have no complaints.

S- The aroma starts off with a lower amount of medium sweetness with the hops being the first to show up and it's a nice mixture of light green hops, tropical fruit and citrus fruit hops with the fruits sticking out the most. Up next comes a slight bit of doughy yeast that goes into the malts which are on the lighter side and they impart some sweet malt, pale malt and biscuit malt aromas. This beer had a really nice aroma, but it was little lighter than I would like.

T- The taste seems to be pretty similar to the aroma and it starts off with a lower amount of sweetness with the hops still being the first to show up and they are lighter than they were in the aroma, but the fruity hops still stick out the most. Up next comes a slight bit of doughy yeast that leads right into the malts which impart the same aspects that they did in the aroma with the biscuit malts sticking out the most. On the finish there's a lighter amount of medium sweetness that doesn't really linger and it has just a generic hop taste and biscuit malt aftertaste. This is a nice tasting IPA, but I thought the taste was a little too light and I wish the hops were much bolder.

M- Smooth, crisp, pretty clean, on the lighter side of being medium bodied with a medium amount of carbonation. The mouthfeel was nice, but I wasn't expecting it to be so clean.

Overall I thought this was a nice tasting beer and I thought it was just a little bit above average for the style, it was a very easy drinking IPA, but I wasn't expecting the aroma to be a little on the lighter side and I really wasn't expecting the taste to be even lighter, so that threw me off a little, but it didn't have any off flavors. This beer had decent drinkability, it was smooth, crisp, pretty clean, not too filling and the taste didn't do a good job at holding my attention, the lightness of the flavor just doesn't hold my attention. What I like the most about this beer was appearance, it was a good looking beer. What I liked the least about this beer was how light the taste was, I was expecting much more flavor from an Oddsides IPA. I might try this beer again on tap to see if it's any better, bu t I don't think I'll be picking up any more bottles. I can't say I would really recommend it, but if you really like IPA's it might be worth a try. All in all I was a little disappointed in this beer, Oddsides has some serious skills when it comes to hoppy beers, but this one just didn't live up to my expectations and in the end I just wanted more out of the taste and aroma from this beer. It's not a favorite beer from this brewery and it's just a little above average for the style. I appreciate the effort, but I think with a little more work this could be a pretty good beer.

12 ounce bottle into pint glass; no bottle dating, but is a recent release. Pours lightly hazy/cloudy golden color with a 2 finger dense and fluffy off white head with awesome retention, that reduces to a nice cap that lasts. Nice dense soapy lacing clings down the glass, with a moderate amount of streaming carbonation. Aromas of grapefruit, tangerine, pineapple, peach, mango, lemon zest, orange peel, light pine, floral, grass, light honey, bread, and floral/grassy earthiness. Very nice aromas with good balance and complexity of citrus/tropical hops and light-moderate pale malt notes; with good strength. Taste of grapefruit, tangerine, pineapple, peach, mango, lemon zest, orange peel, light pine, floral, grass, light honey, bread, and floral/grassy earthiness. Fair amount of pine/grassy bitterness on the finish; with lingering notes of grapefruit, tangerine, pineapple, peach, mango, lemon/orange zest, light pine, light honey, bread, and floral/grassy earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Very nice complexity and robustness of citrus/tropical hops and light-moderate pale malt flavors; with a good malt/bitterness balance and zero cloying flavors after the finish. Medium carbonation and body; with a very smooth, fairly sticky, and lightly creamy/bready mouthfeel that is nice. Alcohol is very well hidden with no warming present after the finish. Overall this is a very nice IPA. All around good balance and complexity of citrus/tropical hops and light-moderate pale malt flavors; and very smooth to drink. A very enjoyable offering.
