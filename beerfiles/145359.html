BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Boulevard Brewing Co.
Missouri, United States
boulevard.com

Style: American Strong Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.30%

Availability: Limited (brewed once)

Notes / Commercial Description:
On November 17th, 1989, John McDonald loaded a keg of Boulevard Pale Ale into the back of his pickup and drove to a restaurant a few blocks down the street. Scarcely 24 hours later and 650 miles away, Doug Odell loaded a keg of Odell Golden Ale into the back of his pickup and... you get the idea. They were the first kegs of beer either of them ever sold, but they wouldn't be the last. This deep amber ale celebrates 25 years of brewing with an ample body of caramel-toffee malt balanced by piney hop notes. Here's a toast to working hard and sticking with it.

Added by LambicPentameter on 11-12-2014

This beer is retired; no longer brewed.

"This golden copper ale celebrates 25 years of brewing with an ample body of caramel-toffee malt balanced by piney hop notes. Here's a toast to working hard and sticking with it. Cheers!" Brewed in the style of an American Strong Ale, available in limited run 750 ml. bottles and on limited draft. Collaborative anniversary release between Boulevard and Odell Brewing Co.

Poured from a 750ml. bottle to a snifter glass.

(Appearance) Pours a frothy foam head of a caramel cream color over a mildly hazy copper-amber body with very fine sediment and moderate relaxed carbonation. Retention is average and lacing is light and spotty. 3.75

(Smell) A pretty simple but inviting nose of caramel biscuit malts with soft toffee undertones, very clean aroma. Faded but earthy and piney hop notes with a mild bitterness. Potency is mild. 3.5

(Taste) Caramel and biscuit toffee malt notes forward with a moderate bitterness, softly nutty and earthy bitterness with a low hop flavor and bitterness. Finishes chalky dry with a lingering biscuit graininess. 3.75

(Mouthfeel) Texture is lightly chewy, slick, smooth and very dry. Carbonation is moderate, generating a light frothiness and a fully crisp finish. Body is shy of medium for the style, medium overall. Balance is slightly slightly earthy bitter and softly grainy over sweet. Alcohol presence is mild and there are no off characters. 3.5

(Overall) An enjoyable but simple malt bodied ale that doesn't come off as too much more than a slightly enhanced body amber ale. Not much hop character and not much going for the malt flavor. Drinkable, but not worth an inflated price tag. 3.5

daijoobo, Apr 21, 2015
3.61/5  rDev -4.7%
look: 4.5 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 4 | overall: 3.5

First off - congrats to Boulevard on the big 2-5. I can't imagine what starting a brewing operation was like in 1989, let alone today in the crazy world that is craft brewing. I picked this up a few weeks back at the Bottle King in Livingston, hoping that I'd be able to help in the celebration of a brewery that I've come to admire in the short time that they've had their products in sale here in the Garden State. I love the design of their bottles as much as what's inside of them and hoped that this would be the case here.

As so many others on here have felt, this was an underwhelming beer. Not that I didn't like it - but when something is sold in a big bottle/bomber, I expect it to be special. The pour led me to believe that this would be the case here, as the radiant dark apricot hue, frothy head, and wondrous lacing were in line with my expectations...

...but the came the first sip. Booze, yeast, dark fruits, and a bit more booze all combined for an interesting taste profile. Why didn't it sync with the smell? Why wasn't there much of an aroma here to begin with? Why was this weak for an American Strong Ale once I realized that this wasn't a Belgian Pale Ale? All of these questions swirled through my head as I swirled this beer, hoping for some answers. There was some nuttiness underneath everything to lend depth, but not a solution to the tasteful problem that was vexing me.

I wasn't feeling the caramel-toffee malt and for certain, the piney hops were weak at best and only noticeable in the aftertaste for a brief moment. Maybe it was the high carbonation that did this beer in, as it felt like a soda with some booze and yeast thrown in for good measure. I wouldn't call this a drain pour as much as it an underwhelming let-down. Yes, American Strong Ales can vary in their flavor profile and strength but in this instance, I felt that this beer was categorized incorrectly. Fans of Boulevard should still give this a go, because it's a fairly solid beer from top to bottom. Where it falls in their portfolio is the bigger question...

Schimms, Jan 31, 2015
3.09/5  rDev -18.5%
look: 3 | smell: 2.75 | taste: 3 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

BOTTLE: Brown glass. Branded hood-and-wire cap over a cork. Uninteresting label design.

7.3% ABV. Brewed & bottled by Boulevard. Batch number SA14283-1. Best by: 07-2015.

Served cold into a pilsner glass at low altitude in Austin, TX. Reviewed live as a "golden copper ale" per the label. Expectations are high; I really like both Odell and Boulevard.

No bubble show forms as it's poured.

HEAD: Four fingers wide. Nicely frothy and full. 3 minute retention. Off-white colour. Leaves messy lacing on the sides of the glass as it recedes.

BODY: Dark murky amber-copper of average vibrance. No yeast particulate or hop sediment is visible.

Appears adequately carbonated. Overall, it's not much to look at, but it's passable for a copper ale.

AROMA: Caramel, bread crust, faint toast, brown bread, amber malts, toffee, and a wisp of piney hop character. Overall, the aroma seems muted and reticent. Aromatic intensity is below average.

I wish it were more evocative. It's an okay aroma for a copper ale, and does have some lightly doughy yeast characteristics which I like, but overall it's wanting.

No overt off-notes or booze are/is detectable.

TASTE: Caramel. Amber malts. Bread crust. Faint toast. Cream. A hint of toffee. Generic floral hop character. I don't really find the piney hops. Some doughy yeast surges on the climax - which is nice. Brown bread.

It's almost more of a bock flavour profile than anything else. It has a loose cohesion, an okay balance, and fair complexity, but it just never tastes like a gestalt whole, and it doesn't emphasize any constituent note, lending it a lack of focus. I'm not sure what they're going for with this, but it seems soulless and generic. I do like it in a general sense, but it isn't the quality work I'd expect from the breweries involved, and it's just not that evocative of anything at all.

Has average depth, duration, and intensity of flavour. There's fair complexity here, but it lacks the intricacy and subtlety needed for greatness.

TEXTURE: Smooth, wet, fairly creamy, medium-bodied, unrefreshing, and overcarbonated. It has an apt moderate thickness without ever coming off syrupy.

Not oily, gushed, hot, boozy, astringent, harsh, rough or scratchy.

This texture fails to elevate the beer, but it does complement the taste nicely and makes the beer more approachable. Coarseness and dryness would be ruinous, and thankfully both characteristics are avoided.

OVERALL: Not at all the quality I expected given the breweries involved. I definitely wouldn't buy this again at its unreasonable price point (relevant to its quality), but I'm glad I tried it once. For a copper ale, it's fairly generic, though it does satisfy style conventions. I wouldn't recommend this to friends or trade partners, and I expect it will soon be forgotten by craft beer fans. There's little here to interest the discerning drinker, but it's drinkable for its ABV and enjoyable for what it is. I like to see more copper ales on the market, but this is better suited to 6-packs than to special release bombers.

C+

stephens101, Jan 24, 2015

A good, muddy-copper color; a quick effervescence, cutting through a healthy turbidity; a solid, tiny-bubbled head at the top of the glass reflecting the solid, fine-dust coating of yeasty sediment at the bottom of it—this Silver Anniversary collaboration has a lot of inviting entrées beyond the twin names on the label.

Odell and Boulevard celebrate twenty-five years with a beer long on sweet and short on bitter, with an ale that features a nice, turbinado-tuned body, which almost nears the fringe of molasses territory, but boasts moreover chords of dry raisins, brandied cherries, caramel-butter biscuits, and some late-stage banana-bread richness. The hops—along with an ample blast of the beer's booze—are quite pronounced in the nostril, releasing wet-pine-bark and dry orange-peel notes; they are, nevertheless, not nearly as prevalent in the taste, which is mostly a maelstrom of the aforementioned sweet flavors—along with some neutral woodiness that help to elevate the deep, agèd textures of this strong-armed ale.

While it doesn't necessarily explode with venerable nuance—and though I enjoyed it much better when I had it on tap in Minnesota (where listed as brewed at Odell), versus out of a bomber bought in Kansas City (listed as brewed at Boulevard)—it's by no means a weak celebration of two quarter-centuries of brewing.
