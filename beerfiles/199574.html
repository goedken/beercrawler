Poured from the bottle into a Spiegelau IPA glass.

Very clear, nearly crystal with an excellent dark orange body color. Very good amounts of carbonation, slowly creeping up through the body, meeting to a big fluffy off white, almost banana tinged yellow in color head. Great retention and staying power, with lots of leggy lacing and soft topped puck. Great looking IPA.

Nice simple aroma. Slight notes of caramel with a much larger hop angle following. Hints towards ashen citrus oils of hop on the nose, sort of a woody character a bit as well. Hints at a bit of dry flour, almost powdery grain as well. Slightly odd, but decent and solid.

Palate manages the right texture and angles but has a bit of give. Sharp hop front with a bit of numbing pine needles at first. Soft caramel slowly taking over with some more hop character building up. Slightly juicy, but also gets a little fleeting slightly wet. Hop angles are present but manage to not have a real distinct defining quality to them. They all manage to have mild subtlety in earth, pine, a slightly bit of herbal tea, and a noticeable mouthwash almost minty finish.

A decent IPA, but doesn't seem to provide a real unique hop flavor and experience. Highly drinkable though and does feel larger than it actually is.

jzlyo, Feb 20, 2016
3.92/5  rDev +2.3%
look: 4 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 4 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 4

22oz bottle - I see now the connection between the number of hops, ABV, and the picture on the label. Neat, I guess!

This beer pours a slightly hazy, medium golden amber colour, with a handful of puffy, rocky, and somewhat creamy ecru head, which leaves some layered and sudsy continental drift lace around the glass as it slowly ebbs away.

It smells of fairly dank pine resin, a mixed forest floor detritus green bitterness, edgy orange and white grapefruit rind, loitering yeast, plain bready caramel malt, a twinge of hard water flintiness, and more earthy, musty, and dead floral hoppiness. The taste is bready and doughy caramel malt, leafy and piney wet garden paths, lemon, lime, and Texas red grapefruit peel, Rockets candy, a suggestion of phenolic warming alcohol, and more earthy, musty, and floral hop bitters.

The bubbles are decently rounded in their supportive and sort of challenging frothiness, the body a solid middleweight, and generally handicapped in its attempt at smoothness by the machinations of that sextet of ale (I mean hops - stupid sexy Simpsons!). It finishes off-dry, the malt and inherent fruitiness of the homegrown hops doing well to keep the heretofore antsy acerbic and alcoholic astringencies duly in check.

Overall, a generally pleasant and more than adequately bitter West Coast Yankee IPA, and even if the specified hops are unfamiliar, they play out like the descendants of those we do know. Easy to drink for any sort of avowed hop-head, and a nice reminder of why Rogue still matters - discuss!
