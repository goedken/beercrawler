Poured from a tallboy into a shaker pint, the beer is a cloudy, opaque, peach-orange coloring with a low profile, clingy, filmy, white head. Aromas of orange, peach, grapefruit, toasted grains, bitter rye notes, and subtle grassy notes. Very balanced aromas. Flavors are tart citrus forward, heavy on orange and grapefruit, with an accompanying acidity, all on a bitter and toasted rye grain backbone, with subtle grassy and piney hops characteristics at the end of the palate. Smooth and dry on the tongue, with a subtle slickness to it. The beer has a citrus and grainy aftertaste, lingering on past the slightly acidic, dry, crisp finish. Good beer.

MaineBeerDinner, Jul 23, 2016
4.75/5  rDev +11.8%
look: 4.75 | smell: 4.75 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 4.75

L - pours a gorgeous ripe tangerine. Perfect amount of haze. When held up to the light, the orange hue really starts a-glowing. Looks like Sunny D!

S - love the aroma on this one. Big juice up front with lasting power. Grapefruit pith, orange, peach, guava. Exotic and tropical. Deep and luxurious. Tangerine dream.

T - lots of rind acidity up front. Grapefruit, orange, pineapple. Awesome fruit flavors with the rye tingle supplementing nicely. Great complexity and transitions into crackery mosaic. Pine on the finish with a drying sensation. Transforms front to back with huge flavors, nothing lingering too long. The pacing on this beer is great!

F - full and fluffy. Crazy the kind of buoyant mouthfeel these guys can achieve with only 5.1%. Light enough to make it stupid drinkable.

O - really impressed with this one. Like, really really impressed. Still can't believe it's only 5%! How can so much flavor and body be packed into such a tiny package?! Lux stands with the best of them. If I could get this regularly, it would be my go-to "sit on the deck and watch the grass grow" kind of beer.

A super hazy, pale, dirty orange juice looking beer with a frothy white head. Upon closer inspection, it is not nearly as thick/murky as it looks. Just a bunch of debris.

Smells like spicy fruit, and yeast.

A lot of peppery kick. Until it warmed up I thought it was just more of a hop spice, but the rye really starts to come through. Some lemon rind, pineapple, grapefruit, and green grass. Plenty of eartht yeast and grainy malts. A nice balance of malt to hops flavor. I feel like I might be underrating the taste at 4.25 considering it is a 5% beer. Unbelievable amount of flavor.

The flaky/chewy body is fantstic. You would swear it was almost 8%. Pretty crisp. Nice amount of resin.

Easily one of the best low ABV hoppy beers I have ever had.
