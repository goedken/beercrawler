BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Trillium Brewing Company
Massachusetts, United States
trilliumbrewing.com

Style: American IPA

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.00%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
An intensified version of Scaled and the latest addition to our line-up of aromatic, juicy IPA's. The secondary dry hop of El Dorado and Citra results in an enhanced nose of citrus, pineapple, and wet earth. The flavor brings intense lime, refreshingly pithy bitterness and just a touch of toasted malt. Medium bodied with a dry finish.

MALT: American 2-row Barley, White Wheat, Flaked Wheat, Dextrine, C-15

HOPS: El Dorado, Citra, Columbus

ragnar3, Sep 27, 2016
4.77/5  rDev +6.5%
look: 4.5 | smell: 5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.75

L - classic Trillium. A solid block of pale OJ haze.

S - the nose on this one is insane. Getting wafts from the moment I cracked the can until I actually put it up to my face. Incredibly bright and grassy. So many hops. Orange, pineapple, and melon. It's not just the smell, it's the WAY it smells. Penetrating and expansive, aroma fills the air.

T - not as bitter and hoppy as I was preparing myself for! Loads more smooth and creamy. Has a sweetness to it along with doughy grains. Pineapple and nectarine. Juicy nectar with earthy undertones thanks to the dry hopping. Really like Trillium DDH brews because you can literally taste the hops. The raw, primal grassiness of eating straight hop pellets. Well-balanced which includes the extra hops. Starts strong and finishes strong with robust flavor throughout.

F - viscous and creamy. Only 7% you say? Drinks like a double.

O - just a well-done beer. Amazing how dry hopping can really take over the flavor and ingrain itself so deeply into the base beer. Usually dry hopping is a gimmick and tacks on some grassiness separate from the rest of the beer, but this feels natural.

M - Medium body and light-medium carbonation. Resinous, prickly and finishes dry.

O - A flavor bomb. Only knock for me is I'd have liked a bit more fruit sweetness in the finish to counter the lingering bitterness. I prefer their normal Scaled for this reason.

Bright hazy yellow-orange with a thin, white fizzy bubbly head.

Super bright tangerine on the nose paired with sweet apricot and bubblegum. This is just loaded with absolutely vibrant hops, with some spiced orange.

This beer has so much fresh orange flavor, I think it may be the most prominent of any other hopped brew I've ever experienced. All of this is attributed to the really fresh, bright hops, and not for a single instant does it seem like there is anything artificial. It's just big time, juicy, sweet tangerine, with some balancing but light green pine and some distant herbal dankness as it warms. Soft light oat or wheat develops as well, with some lemon.

Overall this is a very bright, light, tangy and tingly brew with nothing heavy, too bitter or overpowering at all. DDH Scaled is just so damn drinkable!

Addendum: I just recently had a 4 month old bottle of this that I kept around before they transitioned to cans, and it's absolutely crazy. I've never had a "stale" IPA that tasted and smelled this fucking good. It smells like a very dry, herbal-esque, essential oil, perfumey bouquet. I've never had an old IPA that didn't turn into a complete pine forest or grain showcase. Taste became apple, pear, honeysuckle--just a super floral meadow of birds and bees. Outstanding!
