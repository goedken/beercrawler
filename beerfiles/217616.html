BEER INFO

New Belgium Brewing

Style: American Wild Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 6.20%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
Thanks to Boulder’s finest for this unabashedly tropic take on Fat Tire. Avery took a healthy dose of Brettanomyces Bruxellensis Drie to create a Fat Tire spin with a sturdy malt backbone, fruity hop aroma and a tropical pineapple layer. Heed the call of the wild Fat Tire.

From the 12 oz bottle in a snifter----Thanks Randy!
This nice wild ale pours a ruby tinged amber with a firm head
of white foam that settles to a thick ring, nice pool, and features
some nice light lacing. Nose of orchard fruits, light pepper notes,
bread malts, honey malts, and citrus hops. Flavors follow the
nose with yeasty breaded malts touched by honey and dusted with
peach and pineapple notes and light pepper tinges on the rear of the
palate. Nice mouth feel, crisp and very dry with light carbonation.
One of the better wild ales I have tried....not a fan of the style, but this
is right up the alley of those who view the style as a bit unapproachable.

Cheers

The very pretty beer pours a brilliant copper to cherry amber (SRM 17) with moderately fast rising bubbles under a 1" cream colored foam cap that is moderately persistent. the aromas rising from the glass include tropical fruit, citrus, lemon, mild funk, bruised and over ripe fruit. The palate is fruity and lightly tart leading to a slightly astringent finish quickly followed by a lingering juicy hop bitterness. A tropical fruit cocktail of flavors fill the mouth seasoned by a restrained tartness and funk with a moderate hop bitterness. Nothing is overbearing or overstated with an interesting and very refreshing four way balance between malt, tart, funk and bitter. Fat Wild Ale captures the creamy rich Brettanomyces texture.

Poured into a tulip.

Appearance: since this is a Fat Tire grain bill, no need to does describe further. You know what Fat Tire looks like.

Aroma: really, a non-event. The Brett character dominates the nose - leather and faint old Juicy Fruit; little else, but again, this beer will give you a clear understanding of this Brett strain's characteristics.

Flavor: interesting. The Brett flavor definitely takes the lead. Overripe peach, leather and stale Juicy Fruit gum. Little, if any, hop flavor or bitterness. There is nothing I dislike about this beer, but then again, nothing wows me either.

Mouthfeel: light-medium. Again, youI've had Fat Tire.

Overall: this beer is definitely worthwhile to try. It will give you a clear understanding of the flavors that this Brett strain (Brux Drie) contributes. Technically, this is a very good beer; it just doesn't have the flavor. Had I bought this beer as a bomber for $8-10, I would be disappointed, but this was included in a Folly Collabeeration Pack, "Fat Tire and Friends".

Brief first impressions from a bottle:

Hoppier than expected, with a malt backbone redolent of bread crust, amber malts, and caramel. Possibly toffee too. Overly sweet for a beer billed as a wild ale. I don't get much brettanomyces bruxellensis character at all. Hop profile is indeed tropical as billed on the label, but is that really a good thing in a wild ale? I'd contend not - and I find the pineapple fruit flavour off-putting.

Seems like a beer that's trying to cash in on the wild ale craze without actually committing to the time and care needed to craft one well. More marketing than quality, but drinkable.
