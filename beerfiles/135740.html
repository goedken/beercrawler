Labatt Prohibition Series Bourbon Barrel Ale - Labatt Brewing Company Ltd. Poured from the bottle into pint glass, this brew's head is one finger thick off-white head, spottier lacing, and a clear medium-amber body.

Ozzylizard, Oct 16, 2015

Poured from bottle into a pint glass

Appearance The beer pours a medium golden color with a one finger head of fizzy white foam. The head has a lighter level of retention, slowly fading to leave a lighter level of fizzy lace on the sides of the glass.

Smell The aroma of the brew is strong of a sweet caramel and maple syrup smell mixed with a bit of a bready aroma. Along with these aromas comes some smells of sum of a fruity aroma of harvest fruits and some spice aroma of cinnamon and nutmeg along with a very light hint of an oak smell.

Taste The taste begins with a rather sweet flavor with a good showing of a breadiness. The sweet is a mix of caramel and maple syrup with some fruity tastes apple and pear. As the taste advances some of the light graininess detected in the nose is detected on the tongue with the caramel and the fruit transitioning to a mix of more maple syrup and a bit of brown sugar. A little bit of a woody taste comes at the very end leaving one with a sweet, but enjoyable, almost pancake like taste to linger on the tongue.

Mouthfeel The body of the beer is on the average side in terms of thickness and creaminess with a carbonation level that is rather average as well. A little thicker and chewier of a feel may have been slightly better for the rich syrup pancake flavors of the brew, but overall, the feel was rather decent.

Overall- I dont know what people are talking about here I am drinking this out of date (as I found it a friends house in the back of his fridge), but it has quite a bit of flavor and is very enjoyable overall. I havent had any of the other prohibition series Labett are rolling out, but I will try some if I get the chance.

3.48/5  rDev +35.4%

I understand that in some ways this is big business poaching dollars from craft producers and all of that, and that we aren't supposed to like beers like this, but if you close your eyes and taste it, its very good, and frankly I feel like if bells or someone made this, we would all be raving about it, so lets judge it for what it is, a tasty, affordable, and inventive beer from a big player. I like the idea of bourbon flavors in a blonde ale, and while I know its not barrel aged but rather brewed with staves in it, the flavor of the whiskey is really strong, notes of honey and vanilla, lots of sweetness, and the oak all come through the medium bodied highly carbonated pale base beer, which has redeeming characteristics of its own. I like the quantity of that special element too, more would make it too intense, less it wouldn't stand out quite enough, they dialed that aspect in. it may be a little sweet for slamming a bunch of, and it may be a little lackluster for those used to huge barrel aged stouts, but for what it is, I think its really excellent. I don't like that sometimes we don't really objectively review beer, but let things like politics and preconceived notions influence us so much. I am guilty of it too sometimes, but lets give credit where its due, this stuff aint as bad as it looks on here. id drink another one right now if I had it...
