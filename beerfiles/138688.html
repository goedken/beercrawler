Disappointing, as I am ordinarily a sucker for brandy-aged beers. Quite attractive, with a hazy golden-amber pour and a finger of fluffy white head that lingers but produces very little lacing. Smell is like a light scotch aged in sauternes barrels--reminiscent of the Glendronach 14-year with said finish, but with a bit of green apple and potpourri also in the mix. However, it's not quite so pleasant as that description conjures, as there is also a hint of rubbing alcohol and a level of dry heat undesirable in a beer. The taste has some of the same astringency to it, detracting from what otherwise might be quite nice. The less pleasant flavors are not overpowering enough for me to call them off flavors or to cause me to think anything is wrong; it would be better if they were absent, though.

Had a generous pour at Torst.

O: Very impressed by these guys. Very solid beer.

A: The beer is hazy yellow in color. It poured with a quarter finger high white head that died won, leaving a large patch of bubbles on the surface and a collar around the edge of the glass.
S: Moderate to strong aromas of apple brandy are present in the nose.
T: Just like the smell, the taste has lots of flavors of apples from the brandy but no traces of alcohol are perceptible.
M: It feels just about medium-bodied on the palate and has a moderate amount of carbonation. No warming from the alcohol is perceptible.
O: From the smell to the taste, this beer is all about the brandy, which completely overpowers the underlying triple. It's not bad if you like brandy but otherwise this beer could be considered as one dimensional.

4.36/5  rDev +4.6%
look: 4 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.25

Bottle purchased at Nathaniel Square Corner Store in Rochester, NY.

Pours a golden sunny looking orange, with a small half inch head, and a little bit of lacing.

Smells like honey, Belgian yeast, sweet brandy, oak, and a bit of vanilla.

Tastes even better. The honey flavor is sticky and sweet, without being cloying. The brandy is sweet, a little apply and nutty, and not too overwhelming. There's a bit of oak and vanilla from the barrels, as well as nice juicy citrus notes, mainly orange and lemon.

This one has a sticky, slightly syrupy mouthfeel, just actually perfect. There is a low amount of carbonation.

Grimm is legit, more people need to be made aware of them.
