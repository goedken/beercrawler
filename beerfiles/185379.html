BEERMILER12, Nov 30, 2015
3.77/5  rDev -0.5%
look: 4 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

As a style of beer that is in serious decline in popularity, Goose Island's American-style amber ale captures the essence of the fall season while showing their appreciators that this is a beer style that shouldn't fade away easily.

Medium amber and rounded with a creamy foam cap, the beer's bright clarity carries a handsome appearance while caramel malts and a bright citrus bouquet swirl toward the nose. Malty-sweet flavors greet the early palate with caramel, cashew and mild bread crust.

And as the ale opens up across the middle palate, it does so with tightening malt flavors that exhibit as much toasted grain as it does simple caramelized sweetness. This opens the door for hops to deliver their grapefruit, orange and bright herbal balance. And as the ale nears finish, its balance is even-keel and somewhat of British bitter character.

Medium-bodied and soft on the palate, the beer trends semi-dry, crisp and clean with an aftertow of malt in finish, a soft resinous pine-like bitterness and the evaporative cooling of mint. Its simply a beer that's well suited for all things autumn.

Goose Island "Autumn Ale"
12 oz. brown glass bottle, bottled on 11 August 2015

A solid American Amber ale with a more than respectable 6.7% abv. A frothy yellowed-white (almost tan) head tops a clear deep-copper body, and the head retention and lacing are both very good. The aroma is clean, bright and fresh displaying a dark caramel malt base and spicy and piney hops. As might be expected, the flavor follows suit with the addition of some hoppy, peppery spiciness. It's stiffly bitter, leaving it dry, but not so much so that the malt can't leave an edge of caramelish sweetness. Perfectly balanced. In the mouth it's decidedly medium with a dextrinous edge and a fine, moderate-median carbonation that leaves it delicately bristling and then creamy smooth once warmed a bit. Hard to argue with... nicely done!
