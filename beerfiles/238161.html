BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Southern Tier Brewing Company
New York, United States
southerntierbrewing.com

Style: Gose

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 8.30%

Availability: Limited (brewed once)

Notes / Commercial Description:
The finished beer is a striking, lustful shade of red with a persistent white head and durable lacing thanks to the large portion of wheat in the grist. The nose is defined by equal parts bright lactic acidity and cherry notes with a hint of coriander. On the palate, the strong lactic acidity is accentuated by the natural tartness of the cherry juice. Quantitatively, this beer is quite sweet, but we find that this level of residual sweetness is just enough to balance the acidity and that the overall perception of the beer is actually quite dry. The beer finishes very clean and in conjunction with the salt in the finish, it definitely makes you want to come back for more.

pH - 3.4
Total Acidity: 9.9 ml/100g

Pours a slightly hazy rose colored amber producing barely a finger of mineral water effervescent, shortlived, foamy, white head that leaves little lacing. This is in direct opposition to the head & lacing comments on Southern Tier's website. My bottle is dated 06/27/16 so perhaps the age is playing a role.?. Aroma is tart cherry with a doughy malt note making it faintly cherry pie like. I detect pilsner malts & wheat. There are also hints of grain, mineral, candy apple & coriander in a briney background. Taste opens with intense lactic acid sourness, tart cherry & mineral with brine developing at mid-sip & intensifying into the finish. These flavors largely persist & subdue the notes of malt, wheat, lemon & lime to mere hints in a peppery, crisp, dry finish. Aftertaste is salty cherry, mineral lemon, lime & tart green apple. While I can detect the coriander in the nose I cannot do so in the mouth. The alcohol is well hidden in both the mouth & nose. Mouthfeel is light bodied with moderate-higg carbonation that rapidly dissipates as the brew warms, prickly & dry.

12 ounce bottle into tulip glass, bottled on 9/8/2016. Pours slightly hazy reddish orange color with a 1-2 finger dense light pink head with good retention, that reduces to a thin cap that lingers. Light spotty lacing clings around the glass, with a light amount of streaming carbonation. Damn nice aromas with great balance and complexity of fruity/acidic yeast, cherry juice, salt/coriander, and moderate bready malt notes; with great strength. Taste of big tart/sour cherry, cherry skin, lemon, orange, green apple, sea salt, light coriander, wheat, biscuit, and light lacto funk. Good amount of fruity/acidic sourness on the finish; with lingering notes of tart/sour cherry, cherry skin, lemon, orange, green apple, sea salt, wheat, biscuit, and light lacto funk on the finish for a while. Damn nice complexity, robustness, and balance of fruity/acidic yeast, cherry juice, salt/coriander, and moderate bready malt flavors; with a great malt/sourness balance, and zero cloying flavors after the finish. Light-moderate dryness from acidity as well. Medium carbonation and medium-plus body; with a very smooth, moderately acidic/grainy/bready, and fairly saline balanced mouthfeel that is great. Alcohol is very well hidden with minimal warming present after the finish. Overall this is a great fruited Gose style. All around great complexity, robustness, and balance of fruity/acidic yeast, cherry juice, salt/coriander, and moderate bready malt flavors; very smooth and easy to drink with the balanced malt/sourness. A very enjoyable offering.

Had this recently up at the Cloverleaf as this was one of the few beers from Southern Tier that I never had a chance to enjoy before. Sours and Goses have been slowly gaining favor with me in recent months and this was one of the reasons why - it went down so smoothly, was true to form, and was beautiful to look at. At the end of the day, what else could one ask for?

Loved the pour to this as the beer had a light mango hue to it with lots of head that held it's own and moderately spotty lacing that slid down the side of my tulip glass. Tart cherry, lactic acid, sourness, and salt were in the nose with a surprising shot of alcohol in there to tie it all in together. Quite tart up front, the salt *really* kicked in not too long after that as everything faded to that in the aftertaste. Good amount of cherry juice but this tasted like the real thing and not the like the bright red ones that come in a jar. The ultimate sweet 'n sour beer mixed with fruit, the syrup could be tasted but not felt as this beer never came across as being too thick. This was thick because of the wheat but by no means did this feel like a wheat beer as this reminded me a bit of the Sam Adams Cherry Wheat that I loved many, many moons ago...

Without a doubt, this had to be the strongest Gose that I've ever had as one was more than enough for a sitting. An interesting take on the style, this wasn't fruity enough to be light and had enough body and alcohol to be enjoyed during outside of the warmer months. A fun beer that was serious enough to elevate this style past it's session-like connotation. Can't wait to give it again when the cherry blossoms burst out next year!

The label says "DOB 06/27/16 11:56AM"

Appearance: It is a bright red bodied beer that appears to be nearly luminescent as it sits on the table. There is a hint of a dark blood red in the center of the glass and a bit of an orange hue on the edges. The head is very short lived with a pink appearance. It looks very lovely even though the head is nonexistent after a few seconds.

Aroma: It has a light sweet cherry aroma like the way it smells when you open a Hostess Fruit Pie that is sold in gas stations. It doesn't have a very strong aroma at all.

Taste: It is a light flavored beer. There is a soft cherry quality to the beer and just a hint of tartness but it is not even to the point where I would call it sour. What is absolutely amazing is that this beer is 8.3% ABV and with such a light flavor profile I can't pick up any alcohol at all. It really is amazing for that fact alone. There really isn't a lot going on in the beer flavor-wise but it tastes great. It's just a mellow cherry flavor with the grains and hops very retracted.

Mouthfeel: Very light and airy on the palate. It drinks like a beer less than half its alcohol volume. Very clean on the palate and it finishes effortlessly smooth.

Overall: To a person who is very sensitive to sour beers I would highly recommend this beer. It is not very strong in the sour department. If you like cherries I would also recommend the beer. If you aren't a fan of fruit beers this beer doesn't have much to offer other than the cherry so while I highly recommend the beer I would also say that if you don't like fruit or cherries it is obvious that you stay away from it. If you like (or even don't mind) fruit or cherries then you will find an amazingly easy to drink higher alcohol beer made with cherries that feels a bit like a lawnmower beer. I think when most people see 8.3% ABV and cherries they think "cough syrup" and amazingly this beer pulls it all together and thankfully does not taste like cough syrup.
