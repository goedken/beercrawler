Another picture triumph for yours truly! I was recently reminded by BigBry why the necessity for an approved pic - in the early days of The CANQuest (tm), I was accused of cheating because some people were unaware that a particular beer was available in a CAN & they required photographic evidence. For me, it has carried over to bottles simply because I believe that people are visual learners & seeing a beer helps to preserve it, especially when out shopping for something new. There is nothing like the spark of recognition to entice me to open my wallet.

From the bottle: "Like trimming the tree, this beer should be part of every holiday tradition."

I have to agree with a previous reviewer in that Atwater Brewery is the most hit-or-miss that I have encountered. I kind of hold my breath with each review, hoping for the best, but not always succeeding.

That said, I Pop!ped the cap and began a gentle pour to see what would happen next. The simple answer is nothing untoward. I got a brief foamy finger of light-tan head that quickly fell back to wisps. Color was Brown to Dark Brown (SRM = > 22, < 27) with NE-quality clarity. The name translates to "gingerbread" from German and the nose certainly bore this out. Spicy, and underlain with molasses/treacle. It smelled like a loaf of gingerbread, rather than the sheets that one makes to build a house. Mouthfeel was medium-to-full, bigger than I expected in the mouth, perhaps due to its carbonation. The taste was more cinnamon-spicy than molasses/treacle-sweet, but then there was also a bready malt quality that also seemed to hold the sweetness in check. Finish was semi-dry, more dry than sweet, but not by much. It was largely due to the spices that had been added. I would not want a steady diet of it, but it was certainly not bad or "a miss" on Atwater's part. It was nice during the winter and next year, I will try to drink one closer to Christmas.

Atwater LebKuchen.

Alright I'll admit it. I'm not looking forward to this beer. The whole reason I'm drinking tonight is to review the beers that have me backed up throughout the holidays. Atwater has far more misses than hits. So whatever. This was given to me over a month ago so I guess we'll see what we get.

Look - From the twelve ounce bottle hard poured into a pint glass. This beer pours a deep, deeeeeeep copper with a two finger head that eventually dies down to a thick cap amongst the top of the drink leaving some rather attractive stick amongst the top of the pint. Carbonation appears to be thinly bubbled though moderate in nature. Spotty lacing is left all amongst the glass upon a finished brew.


Smell - The holiday spice here is kept in check rather nicely. Cinnamon and nutmeg really dominant but it is almost nothing like a pumpkin ale since that's where most people get those scents. There is just a touch of that whole wet cardboard thing going on as well but it isn't nearly as bad as it was in their Winterbock. Caramel is all there with a nice malt level.

Taste - The flavors translate rather well from the smell. Big dosage of cinnamon and nutmeg without the pumpkin ale aspects and once again just a smidge of the wet cardboard. Nothing else going on here.

Feel - The feel is rather thin with a low to moderate amount of carbonation. Things are quite cold and the beer is rather watery though a...thick watery if that makes any sense at all. The flavors upon a sit are completely the same as they were in the nose and the taste profile.

Overall - This brew is rather basic in its aspects though it is basic in a nice way. A solid winter warmer that would could easily throw back without much thought. This is pretty good for an Atwater beer. Nothing here for the hardcore enthusiast but a fine beer for even newbs for happen to grab it. I'd glady
have again if given to me..

This one is darker than many but with a bright, deep, wine-like red hue through its body. The head is tan, extremely creamy, and surprisingly strong. It never fades from a heavy half finger, where it rests the entirety of the session after topping just over twice that. Lacing is left immediately at the very top of the glass and continues thickly the rest of the way down.
There's lots of yeast in the nose, extremely earthy and dusty in its overtones, with a lot of clove and nutmeg in the front and carrying through as well. Underneath is an almost acidic wine and cider mix of dark fruits. Malts seem to contribute a lot of breads while some citrus comes out.
The taste is fairly complex and deep, with lots of earth balancing herb balancing citrus and tangier fruits balancing savory types of spices. Cinnamon seems to linger in the back while orange peel develops underneath as well. A sort of brown sugar or molasses taste lingers lightly without interfering too much with the savory and breadier notes and contributing well to the overall character.
It's at once robust and soft, giving way to the softness but with an easy, almost nitrous-like creamy smoothness. Crispness is just there and it finishes juicy then dry.

Brenden, Dec 31, 2014
3.58/5  rDev -1.4%
look: 3.75 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.5

I picked up a bottle of Atwater's LebKuchen Christmas Ale last week for $2.09 at The Lager Mill. I've never had the cookie this beer is named after, but I'm still interested in seeing how it turns out, so lets see how it goes. 2014 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a imperial pint glass.

A- The label looks nice, it goes with the season ans it has a Christmas Sweater like look to it, I think it's a little eye catching, It poured a hazy brown color that took on more of mohogany color when held to the light with a little over two fingers worth of bubbly tan head that died down to a thin ring that stayed till the end and it left a little bit of patchy lacing behind. This was a pretty nice looking beer, I have no complaints.

S- The aroma starts off with a slightly higher amount of medium sweetness that has a slight brown sugar aspect to it with the malts being the first to show up and they impart some toasted malt, sweet malt, bready, bread crust, cereal and caramel malt like aromas. Up next comes the spices with no single spice sticking out the most and it's followed by a fruit aroma that reminds me of dried fruits with dried cherries seeming to stick out the most. This beer had a pretty nice aroma that's a little unique for the style.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma and it starts off with a medium amount of sweetness that has just a slight brown sugar aspect to it and it's followed by the malts which impart the exact same aspects that they did in the aroma with no single aspect sticking out the most. Up next comes an English yeast flavor followed by the same amount of spices that were in the aroma. On the finish there's just a slight bit of those dried fruit flavors along with a higher amount of light bitterness with a pretty malty and slightly spicy aftertaste. This was a nice tasting beer, but I wish more of those dried fruit aspects from the aroma came through.

M- Smooth, a little crisp, slightly prickly, on the lighter side of being medium bodied with a medium amount of carbonation. The beer had a nice mouthfeel that worked well with it.

Overall I thought this was a nice beer and a nice and fairly unique example of the style, i tend to like it when a brewery takes a different approach to Winter Warmers, there's more than enough over spiced ales in the category so it's nice to see something a little different. This beer had ok drinkability, it's smooth, a little crisp, slightly prickly, not filling and the taste did an ok job at holding my attention, two would probably be my limit, anymore than that and I might start to get bored with it. What I liked the most about this beer was the appearance and aroma, to me those were the highlights of this beer. What I liked the least about this beer was the balance in the taste, it was ok, but I wish more of those spiced and dried fruit aspects came through. I might buy a bottle or two if they release it next year, but it's nothing I'm going to stock up on. I would recommend giving this one a try since it's unique for the style, but don't expect to be too impressed. All in all I liked this beer, but I didn't love it and I think if they worked on the balance in the taste a little that this could be a pretty nice winter seasonal. I think it's one of the better Atwater beers and i think it's above average for the style. Nice job guys, this was a solid and slightly unique Winter Warmer, keep up the solid work.

12 ounce bottle into tulip glass, bottled in 10/2014. Pours slightly hazy ruby red color with a 1 finger dense light khaki head with good retention, that reduces to a small cap that lingers. Nice spotty soapy lacing clings around the glass, with a light amount of streaming carbonation. Aromas of caramel, toasted bread, brown sugar, lemon zest, orange peel, apple, date, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, clove, herbal, and yeast earthiness. Very nice aromas with good balance and complexity of dark/bready malt, spices, and fruity yeast ester notes; with good strength. Taste of caramel, toasted bread, brown sugar, lemon zest, orange peel, apple, date, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, clove, herbal, and yeast earthiness. Light amount of herbal/spice bitterness on the finish; with lingering notes of caramel, toasted bread, brown sugar, lemon/orange zest, apple, date, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, clove, and herbal/yeast earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Very nice robustness and complexity of dark/bready malt, spices, and fruity yeast ester flavors; with a nice malt/spice balance and zero cloying flavors after the finish. Medium carbonation and body; with a smooth, crisp, and slightly dry/bready mouthfeel that is good. Alcohol is well hidden with minimal warming present after the finish. Overall this is a very nice winter warmer style. All around good robustness and balance of dark/bready malt, spices, and fruity yeast ester flavors; and very smooth and crisp to drink. A very enjoyable offering.
