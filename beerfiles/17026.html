kirok1999, Dec 08, 2007
3.36/5  rDev +0.9%
look: 4 | smell: 3 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3 | overall: 3.5

Review # 700!
The "Jubilee" Ale seemed only appropriate to celebrate today's special anniversary. It claims to be suited for a summer picnic but yet robust enough to stand up to a cold winter wind; this 12th day of April was flashes of both. The label also claims to make any season a time of joy and celebration, I think I know someone who does just that same thing...
650 ml bomber, pours a gorgeous sandy burgundy in colour, unique and attractive. An abundance of froathy, off white head. Leaves even webs of lacing all down the glass. Aroma ripened by crystalized caramel malts, lots of amber, lightly roasted. Hints of dried fruits, a poundcake like character. Very gritty, high mineral content too. Mouthfeel carries different degrees of dimension. Hits initially upfront with a pincing alcohol sting, mellows into a fairly packed (yet light bodied) set with fairly quenching finish. An almost astringent metallic bite. Flavours evolve throughout. Different malts peak at different points with accompnying varying degrees of roast. Light smoke character, works nicely as a winter warmer. I get the impression of over-ripened fruits (banana maybe) and nut mix. Hints of chocolate, spice. Hops leave a soft, lasting finish. Enough flavour here that it manages to keep you interested, but not overly satisfied.
A special thanks to Solomon420 for including this local treat as part of my Secret Bunny '07 package! Appreciated Damian!

A weird cross between a porter and a dubbel, although a dubbel with no belgian esters. Weird as hell....glad my sink was thirsty as I couldn't finish more than 1 pint of this.
