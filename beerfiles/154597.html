BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Stillwater Artisanal Ales
Maryland, United States
stillwater-artisanal.com

Style: Light Lager

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 4.20%

Availability: Year-round

Notes / Commercial Description:
Canned beer has made a serious return in the craft brewing world. Prior to this resurgence, the only beer found in cans were industrial lagers. So as a nod in both directions we present an elegantly crisp and hoppy new world lager. The light and refreshing pilsner malt body delivers an abundance of floral, spice, and grass driven hop flavors and aroma. Perfect for those luxurious cruises through the Caribbean.

Added by jhartley on 01-17-2015

Somewhat reasonably priced, for a tallboy craft can in 2016. And billed as a "dry-hopped session lager," which was interesting enough to catch my eye.

Pretty can. The beer jumps out violently when opened, however, which might mean trouble.

Pours fine. Hazy for a beer billed as a light lager. But a nice white head. The fizz isn't too aggressive.

Smells medium hoppy with some distinct pilsener malts against floral hops, but a little bit of hay in the back. It's pleasant, and I guess I can't judge too harshly since light lagers aren't known to be good, but this seems a bit too rough to run with the best of the category.

Tastes like it smells: nicely composed, but some esters hold it back from being great. Spicy and floral euro hops up front, pilsener malt in the middle, dry hops and american blonde ale nodes in the back. I'm not some kind of style snob, so this still gets a solid score, but I can't rank it among the best.

Finishes with a not-unpleasant lingering lemon rind bitterness.

M: Moderate carb married to a dry, mid-weight & crisp lager body. Ultimately very refreshing.

look: 4.25 | smell: 4 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

Picked this up a few weeks back at Carmine Street Beers in New York, never having any of the canned offerings from Stillwater before. I'm usually not a fan of this style as it tends to be bland and watered-down but that wasn't the case here. These guys nailed the essence of this style without sacrificing it at the same time, to the point where I could enjoy this on a cold winter's night such as this!

Nice blonde pour to this, with a frothy head that quickly settled and a few rings of lacing along the back side of my pint glass. Lots of grass and lemon in the nose with even more in the taste. Strong pilsner malt in the taste with floral and lemon zest in the taste, ad this was quite "bright" from the get-go. Loads of bubbles here which were great for scrubbing and a nice, biscuit backbone provided the right amount of body to keep this from falling into the watered-down trap that dooms so many other lagers. No cereal here but just a bit of cracker that stuck around at the end, giving me something to proverbially "chew on" before the next sip.

Want a session lager? Look no further. Shame that this was only brewed once because of how strong this was, when it came to mastering the style. I loved this and the hints of lemon pepper and sour grass really gave this a brightness that I wasn't expecting. An impressive beer that sneaked up on me - I may even go back and see if there are any left...

A- Silver tallboy into a snifter with a slightly hazy, uber pale yellow body. A large white head drifts upwards of a finger high with good retention and a frothy foamy consistency. A large belt at the edges leaves several thick rings of lacing.

S- Crisp without all that much going on besides a light-medium hint of floral and grassy hops. Tiny touch of cereal grains as well as pale fruit esters. Clean for a light lager.

T- Basically a carbon copy of what I get in the aroma with some more depth and complexity coming out as it usually does on the taste buds. The hops gain more flowery tones and end spicy, herbal and grassy. Mild sweetness, cracker, cereal and bready grains. Pear pale fruit and it feels a bit like a Pilsner.

MF- A carbonation on the high to very high side of the spectrum promotes a texture that is both fluffy and frothy, airy on the palate. Finishes with a spicy hop bitterness lingering for quite some time. Overall the body is on the light side and a shade towards medium.

Low ABV lagers are getting better and better with every craft brewer that decides to give them a go. Being from MA Jack's Abby spoils me but Yacht is very pleasant with floral hops and a clean fermentation; very much sessionable.
