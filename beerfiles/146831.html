Very nice aromas with good balance and complexity of fruity/spicy yeast, fruit juice, hops, and light-moderate pale malt notes; with good strength. Taste of lightly tart lemon, lime, mango, passion fruit, white grape, pear, apple, light banana, pepper, wheat, bread, herbal, grass, light funk, and yeast earthiness. Moderate amount of yeast spiciness and light fruity/yeasty tartness on the finish; with lingering notes of lemon, lime, mango, passion fruit, white grape, pear, apple, pepper, wheat, bread, herbal, grass, and yeast earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Very nice robustness and complexity of fruity/spicy yeast, fruity juice, hops, and light-moderate pale malt flavors; with a great yeast/hop/juice balance and zero cloying flavors after the finish. Medium carbonation and light-medium bodied; with a very smooth, crisp, and slightly acidic mouthfeel that is nice. Alcohol is very well hidden with minimal warming present after the finish. Overall this is a damn nice fruited saison style. All around great robustness, complexity, and balance of fruity/spicy yeast, fruit juice, hops, and light-moderate pale malt flavors; and very smooth and crisp to drink for the ABV. Golden with rather surprising clarity. A touch undercarbed by the looks of it, barely a skin of white foam with lacing slipping down the glass, must be due to the fruit.

Pungent mango and passionfruit are really focused leaving some white wine hop character, orange citrus and a steady earthiness humming in the background.

A clean pillar of tight cracker and bready malt with a touch of honey sweetness. Spices are bright, full of baby powder, coriander and lemon zest. The fruit juices also come through with a flurry of passion fruit and well overripe mango. As the beer warms the funkiness really comes out and makes this unpleasant, finishing with vinegar, compost and stomach bile. A tweener beer that is somewhat funky and somewhat acidic/sour but doesn't swing either way.

Medium-light body, carbonation is active enough. Alcohol well hidden, it's put together well but drink this one cold.

Stillwater is usually spot on whith this style but they got caught between elements here making a beer that sells itself short on spice, funk and sour but overdoses on the fruit a bit. In the end as the beer warms it really does get a bit vomit-like with the acidity and pungent/earthy tropical fruits. An interesting experiment, just not a winner.

rawfish, Mar 10, 2015
4/5  rDev +2.6%
look: 4 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

Stillwater's Ale "Remix Project" invites the brewers of Against the Grain to collaborate a hoppy, juicy, fruity farmhouse ale that captures the fermented soul of each brewery.

Rio De Sauvin pours the common yellow-straw of canary with faintly tinted haze and a casual swath of foam. It grazes the nose with plump fruitiness of citrus, orchard and especially tropical varieties. The initial palate is awash with softly powdered sugar, fruit and vinous-type spices. Something akin to sweetheart candy comes to mind.

As the natural tendencies of beer to lean spicy, this beer keeps the balance even-keel with a host of apple, orange, mango, passionfruit, melon, lemon and lime. As the taste explodes with the aid of sparkling effervescence, dryness ensues and gives the ale a decidedly riesling-like tilt.

Its medium-light palate has a creamy upstart but a zesty and racing finish, equipped with fruit tartness, light acidity and a jolt of carbonic bite to keep things lively. Delicate wheat, fruit and faint sweetness resonate in aftertaste, once again teasing the memory with those sweetheart candies.
