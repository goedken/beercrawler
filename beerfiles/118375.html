BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Old Bust Head Brewing Co.
Virginia, United States
oldbusthead.com

Style: English Pale Ale

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 5.60%

Availability: Year-round

Notes / Commercial Description:
Brewed in the tradition of an English Pale Ale, but infused with the spirit of new American craft brews, our flagship beer is drinkable, balanced, and enjoyable year-round. Additions of Goldings, Fuggle, and Cascade hops provide our pale ale with the aroma of floral hop tones, as well as imparting a refreshing, clean bitterness. Our use of caramel malt in the grain bill balances out our generous hop additions, and provides the beer with a smooth and bready backbone. Perfectly balanced, it’s our brewmaster’s favorite beer!

I don't see any bottle date.

Appearance: It is a dark shade of amber with very good clarity. The white head is very short and has a creamy consistency. It doesn't maintain a very large head and even that short head slowly fades out to a film but it leaves nice lacing on the glass.

Aroma: Oddly the aroma reminds me of toasted pumpkin seeds. When I first started pouring it I was picking up that aroma so I leaned in closer to get a whiff and sure enough I got an even stronger aroma of pumpkin seeds. Aside from that odd reference to pumpkin seeds it also smells the way that the residue smells on the outside of hop bines. Not the actual hop itself but just the bine. It smells very inviting.

Taste: There is this very light sweet biscuit flavor underlined by a perfectly balanced hop flavor and light bitterness. The hop flavor is this earthy woody stem flavor. It is very well made.

Mouthfeel: It teeters at the lower edge of a medium body. It has a light texture on the palate that gives the beer some substance without adding too much weight. It is smooth while drinking with a slightly raspy finish from the bitterness at the end.

Overall: I don't have a freshness date code so I have no idea how old this beer is but it tastes wonderful. It has this great earthy quality to it which goes well with the bready malt flavor. This beer would be perfect to drink in the Autumn and I will have to keep that in mind as I would absolutely drink it again and I would recommend it to others.

Beejay, Sep 06, 2015
3.59/5  rDev +2.9%
look: 3.75 | smell: 4 | taste: 3.5 | feel: 3 | overall: 3.5

I will start by saying that I haven’t had any beers until now by Old Bust Head. Although their name is peculiar and less than stellar, I think that this beer is a decent contender for some of the semi-giants out there. This Pale Ale has 5.6% ABV and a nifty label so let’s take a peek.

Look-It pours a partially cloudy amber copper color with a soapy foamy head that lingers. Its texture beckons and promises of tales untold within this amber elixir of joy. The lacing is thick, sticky, and white.

Smell-I smell the peculiarly labeled soapy hops which are far more delicious than this term will imply to those less initiated and narrow-minded beer drinkers who couldn’t tell a sprite from a Pale Ale if it hit them in the head. I get notes of grass, citrus, strong yeast components, and a bready malt backbone.

Taste-I taste tangerine and orange marmalade from the interesting interplay from the hops and malts. I also pick up on some fresh cut hay and spicy grains which makes for a nice pale ale. The hops take back stage, but manifest themselves through the aforementioned flavors. However, the first sip is slightly dusty or musty and I can tell that they used malt extract because it has a sort of fake sweetness and a syrupy flavor.

Mouthfeel-This slightly full-bodied brew, as I’ve said before, has malt extract that gives it a syrupy consistency which detracts from the experience. Also, the smell was excellent, but the taste let me down a bit. The bitterness was underwhelming, but as a pale ale, I shouldn’t take too many points off for this.

Overall-It was a pleasant drink, but not stellar. If you are looking for an amazing pale ale then I would refer you to the Pale Ale variety by Flying Dog. But if you can’t find it, then this variety should be a relatively decent replacement.

Rootdog316, Jun 16, 2014
3/5  rDev -14%
look: 4.25 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 2.5 | feel: 3 | overall: 3

12 oz bottle into a goblet.

A: Pours a very clear chestnut color. The head is a finger high with a pale yellow shade. No, retention isn't the best, but there's a nice ring of lacing left on the glass as the head recedes. This looks quite nice in the glass.

S: It smells...exactly what I'd expect from a typical EPA. English hops give it a nice earthiness to go with its apricot and orange marmalade esters. The malts bring a hefty character of caramel and toffee. EPAs are pretty low on the totem pole of "My Favorite Styles", but this seems to be well-brewed because of how well it hits all the basic notes I look for.

T: The first sip seems oddly musty...too much so, actually. It's trying to inject touches of orange and apricot flavors right before the swallow, but both seem too mild. Luckily, the must notes will fade into a backbone of caramel and toffee. It finishes with a hoppy touch - unfortunately, just a touch. At the end of the day, the malts overpower everything else: there's too much of that burnt caramel flavor I don't like in my beer.

M: While it's reasonably well attenuated, the malts linger too vigorously on the palate. It's not disgustingly sweet by any means, just that the finish is too long. That's normally a good thing, but not when I don't like how this tastes. Carbonation isn't that much of a factor.

O: Like I said, EPAs are pretty low down on my list of favored styles - and this does nothing to change that. I will concede, though, that's it's well made for what it is. It's enough to earn it a few more bonus points, but not enough for me to say "I like it". I won't return to this, most likely.
