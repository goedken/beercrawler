750ml bottle. Served in a Sante Adairius stemmed glass. Pours a lightly hazy medium yellow body with a light orange hue. One finger effervescent white head, with the perfect amount of retention, bits of lacing. Smells of lemon, grainy cereal, a fair bit of white wine, even a bit of white wine grape must and a dry aroma; light farmhouse funk, light peppercorn, very light citrus. Flavour is a bit of grainy bread, lots of lemon, light funk, medium amounts of white wine, black pepper, more citrus. This is a lot like a more peppery Le Sarrasin. Mouthfeel is medium bodied, creamy, light on the tongue but heavy on the lingering flavours. Very nice on the palate. Overall, a very good white wine barrel-aged saison that follows in the footsteps of Flora b5, although the wheat seems to be missing here. There is a bit less white wine and more of the grain in this one in comparison. Definitely a real treat.
