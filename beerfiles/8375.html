22oz draught at the brewpub.

Appearance: Light gold, good lacing.

Aroma: Lots of aromatic hops, very generous for the style.

Taste: Very hoppy for a pale ale. Tropical fruit notes come through from the hops. No ascertainable malt flavours until the beer warms; then a surprising sweetness comes through. Also a generic nuttiness that is not my favourite attribute. But pretty tasty overall.

Mouthfeel: Medium, hops can be felt a little.

Drinkability: Don't let it warm up. That way you avoid the slightly unbalanced sweetness. When colder, this is a nice hoppy pale ale overall.

A good, standard example of the Pale Ale style. Starts off with a sparkling copper color with golden highlights, abundant carbonation, formitable head formation and retention with light lacing on the glass. Aromas of spicy, citrus hops dominate with a back-side balance of true maltiness and barely sweetness. Flavors of light caramel, toast, and fruits (peaches and pears) arise from the sweet malts; while grapefruit, orange rinds and lemon-zest eminate from the hops. The feel is a bit thin, but does show some creaminess, but ultimately has a light, drying textrue that prevails in the aftertaste. A light hop-metalic taste persists after the malts fall off. But, overall very easy to drink, and desirable. Pairs well with the sushimi rolls.

Gavage, Apr 21, 2004
4.1/5  rDev +9.6%
look: 4 | smell: 4 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.5

Presentation: Sampled at the Main Street Casino, Brewery and Hotel during a recent Sunday Afternoon visit to Las Vegas. Dispensed from one of the 4 Taps at the Main Bar that overlooks the Slot Machines Floor and served in a straight sided Pint Glass. Cost less than $2 for a Pint.

Appearance: Incredibly inviting. The luscious amber color had some hints of sun-ripened Satsumas when held to the light streaming into the Main Bar area from outside. Off-white head is large and voluminous when poured, which causes some considerable wastage, but it does lend a fantastic classic IPA look to this offering when it was finally plonked on the Bar.

Nose: Amazing fresh hops leap out of the glass, some malts. Balanced Odors.

Taste: Floral hop leaves wipe the tongue, a strong malt backbone defends the even stronger hop-bitterness that impresses at the end. Lingering astringent bitter finish. Classic IPA profile. Very impressive.

Mouthfeel: Solid and incredibly wholesome for a beer that clicks in at 5.6%. I have had IPAs much higher than this with much lower mouthfeel and bitterness. It sits in the mouth and coats it with hops when swirled.

Drinkability: Its clean, refreshing and well put together. In short its a Quaffer; excellent fresh nose, mightily impressive tastes, definitive mouthfeel and a Drinkability to round of this very impressive Pale Ale cum IPA. Next time I am in Las Vegas I will return to the Main Street just to drink this one beer.

Overall: For a Pale Ale = this is a very good IPA!!! Maybe its a Las Vegas Pale Ale, its just that bigger and better than most. Even so, this would give a lot of other so-called IPA's a run for their money though. Sadly because it comes from a Brew-Pub in Las Vegas, many may find that hard to accept. But I stand by this one  and I would tell any Hop-Heads heading out to LV to put this at the top of their list. Recommended.
