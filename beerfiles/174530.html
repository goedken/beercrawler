BEER INFO

Brewed by:
Boulevard Brewing Co.
Missouri, United States
boulevard.com

Style: American Pale Ale (APA)

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 7.20%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
Combining CCB’s expertise with tropical fruit flavors and Boulevard’s love of pale ales, Collaboration No. 5 – Tropical Pale Ale begins with a base of soft pilsner malt layered with Maris Otter, Munich and caramel malts. Huge late hopping with a blend of Mosaic, Citra, Lemondrop and Azacca lends bright flavors of mango, pear, blueberry and citrus to a pale ale featuring slightly toasted biscuit and caramel/toffee notes. Infused with freshly sliced grapefruit at the conclusion of the boil, our tropical pale ale is briefly aged on passion fruit and grapefruit at the end of fermentation resulting in a refreshing acidity and tropical fruit flavor and aroma. At 7.3 ABV and 55 IBUs, Collaboration No. 5 – Tropical Pale Ale finishes slightly tart with bright citrus character from dry-hopping with Lemondrop, Azacca and Pacific Jade hops.

Just picked up a four pack of this, has a best by date of 2-13-2016, so guessing they sold the remaining inventory to distributors at a discount.

The pour is a nice copper color with about an inch of a light tan head. There is a little lacing at the sides of the glass, but now a large amount.

The smell is a but fruity, also can detect some yeast and a hint of funk.

I can mainly taste peach, apple, and pear. There is a tart note as well that may be pineapple. There is a bit of spice to this one, mainly pepper, leaving a bit of heat at the finish that lingers.

Fairly harsh going down, has a definite bite to it. This isn't a criticism, just an observation.

Actually a pretty good beer. It really reminds me if a triple, as it has the fruit and spice notes I associate with that style. In fact, it tastes more like a triple than some triples I have had. It is fruity, and the alcohol is well hidden. I don't know if the taste would be different if this was a bit fresher, but given the abv, I am guessing it wouldn't be much different. I'd say if you like Belgian triples, give this one a try, as it will remind you of one.

Boulevard Brewing Company "Collaboration No. 5"
12 fl. oz. bottle, "BEST BY 02/12/16"... sampled on 04/04/16
Purchased for $ $2.99 @ Total Wine & More, Cherry Hill, NJ on 03/19/16

Seeing as how this beer is past its prime you might expect it to be lacking in hop aroma or flavor, or even oxidized, but it's not. It is quite hazy in the glass with visible chunks of yeast in suspension (but I thought that was all the rage right now, no?).

The aroma expresses a blend of juicy tropical fruits (mango, melon, orange, apricot, grapefruit, pineapple, passionfruit) over a distinctively grainy malt base.

The flavor is even fuller, displaying some more straightforward fruit (berries, apple, pear) and some pine and grassiness. The malt is fuller as well, displaying a depth of character not normally found in hoppy pale ales. A solid bitterness anchors it, and it finishes dry with semi-tart citrus juiciness that fades to lingering bitterness.

At 7.2% ABV and 55 IBUs it's certainly up in the IPA range, although it's actually labeled a "pale ale". And I have to wonder how much of its juicy fruitiness is due to "natural flavors" as opposed to hops, and how much of those "natural flavors" come from the "passionfruit and grapefruit" that it's brewed with. Regardless, it certainly delivers a balanced, on-point juicy tropical ale. Interesting, refreshing, and satiating.

AgentMunky, Jan 14, 2016
4.59/5  rDev +15%
look: 4.75 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.5

12 fl oz bottle poured into my Sixpoint Resin snifter.

L: Pours a hazy bright apricot color with a fair amount of bone-white head that recedes to a continously-bubbling ring. Regarding the haze, the beer itself is actually nearly clear but it's chock-full-o-fruit! Big, rude chunks of juicy fruit pulp permeate the brew, defying gravity for some time before eventually settling toward the bottom of the glass. Yeah, now that's what I like to see in a fruit ale! Sticky, disorganized lacing that tends to stay put.

S: Big nose of grapefruit and, most prominently, passionfruit, riding on a wave of expressive hop aromas including notes of citrus and earthy pine. Wonderful aroma. Can't wait to take a sip...

T: Yum! Tart grapefruit hits the tongue first, the mild acidity drifting to the sides of the tongue as juicy passionfruit flavor settles in for the long haul amidst vibrant hop notes of lemon, tropical fruit, and dank pine. It's difficult to distinguish much in the way of specific hop details amongst the big fresh fruit additions, but there is definite complexity in the hop bill. Citra and Lemondrop in particular are duly noted as present, and there are more tropical fruit flavors than the passionfruit could deliver alone: I'm detecting mango, pineapple, and perhaps even a bit of blueberry (that last one isn't exactly tropical but definitely welcome). Moderate hop bitterness, a bit more than is typical for an APA but not quite IPA territory. Malts hinting of biscuit and sweet caramel provide the backbone to this delicious tropical fruit concoction.

F: Medium-light and refreshing with just a touch of alcoholic bite and carbonated prickliness. Pretty much exactly what you'd want for a tropical pale ale. If I could change anything I would carbonate it a bit more heavily, but it's certainly fine the way it is.

O: This is a fine brew, excellently flavored and eminently refreshing. Sipping it transports me to a tropical island in the sun, which is most welcome during these cold months of the year though I bet this would be even better in the summer. The passionfruit flavor in particular is absolutely spot on, showing the Cigar City influence in this collaboration. I'm glad I took the chance on a four pack, and will not hesitate to buy another if I see it again.

MattyG85, Jan 05, 2016
4.21/5  rDev +5.5%
look: 4 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

Its like when you live in the midwest and then your cool cousins from Florida come to town. They bring a tropical vibe with a refreshing attitude, a sunny demeanor and a youthful confidence that's hard to deny. That's what happens with Boulevard's collaboration ale with Cigar City.

Hazy amber and with golden tethers, the beer's lofty head pops with vibrant tropical and citrus fruit. Seemingly like hawaiian punch, the caramel and honey seeps into the tastebuds like the fructose of grapefruit, orange, pineapple and apricot.

But the ale simply blossoms on the palate as its broad swath of carbonation pops on the tongue and lifts the sweetness. What's left is an aura of fruit sherbet as those ruby reds, mandarin, pineapple, mango and passionfruit flavors begin to shift the balance toward the hops. Its smooth bitterness is playfully acidic and grassy as tones of citrus pith and softly muddled pine enter the fray.

Finishing medium-bodied, the pale ale retains enough malt sweetness to support the hops and to keep their juicy, radiant and vibrant fruit character selling convincingly to the taste, the texture and to the mind. Lingering with fruit pectin and a rounded citrus bite the ale trails with smooth bitterness and mild alcohol warmth.
