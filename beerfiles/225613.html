mikeg67, Tuesday at 02:41 PM

look: 3.75 | smell: 3.5 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.5 | overall: 3.75

I picked up a bottle of Samuel Adams Maple Ale in their new fall variety pack. It's a cool rainy day and this one sounds good, so lets see how it goes. 2016 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a imperial pint glass.

A- The label looks nice, it goes well with the beer and it's just a little eye catching. It poured a amber brown color that takes on more of a red color when held to the light with a little half a fingers worth of creamy tan head that died down to a thin ring that eventually faded away and it left some patchy rings of lacing behind. This is a pretty nice looking beer, I have no complaints.

S- The aroma starts off with a lower amount of high sweetness with the maple syrup being the first to show up and it impart a little maple syrup like aroma with the sweetness I've come to expect. Up next comes a slight dark fruit aroma that goes into a little of doughy yeast. Towards the end comes the malts which impart a caramel malt, caramel, light bread crust and sweet malt like aroma. This beer has a nice aroma, it's what I would expect from the style.

T- The taste seems to be fairly similar to the aroma and it starts off with a lower amount of high sweetness with the sweetness and the malts being the first to show up and they impart all the same aspects that they did in the aroma. Up next comes a little bit of doughy yeast with no dark fruits this time and towards the end comes the maple syrup which shows up more than it did in the aroma and it impart a nice rich maple syrup like flavor. On the finish there's pretty much no bitterness with some sweetness taking it's place and it had a maple syrup and light malty lije aftertaste. This is a pretty nice tasting beer, it's got all the flavors that I was looking for.

M- Smooth, slightly cloying, pretty crisp, a little prickly, on the slightly thicker side of being medium bodied with a higher amount of carbonation. The mouthfeel is nice, but I thin the carbonation could be toned down a little.

Overall I thought this was a pretty nice beer and I thought it was a good example of the style, it had all the aspects that I was looking and it was on the rough around the edges just a little, other than that it seemed to be a well done maple syrup ale. This beer had ok drinkability, i think one or two would be my limit, the sweetness and carbonation would start to wear one me. What I like the most about this beer is the taste, it's the highlight of this beer and it lest the maple syrup show up just enough. I would buy this beer on it's own and I would recommend it to anyone looking for a maple syrup beer. All in all I was happy with this beer and out of all the maple syrup beers I have had this one might be my favorite since the balance is right where I would want it. It's not a favorite Samuel Adams beer, but it is one of the best maple syrup beers I've had. Good job guys, this is a tasty bee, keep it up.

12 ounce bottle into signature pint glass, best before 1/2017. Pours crystal clear reddish copper color with a 2 finger fairly dense and rocky khaki head with great retention, that reduces to a large cap that lasts. Dense soapy lacing clings down the glass, with a moderate amount of streaming carbonation retaining the head. Fantastic appearance. Aromas of caramel, brown sugar, maple, and toasted brown bread; with light nuttiness, toffee, herbal, wood, dark fruit, and toasted earthiness. Nice and pleasant aromas with good balanced and complexity of dark/bready malt, maple, and light earthy hop notes; with solid strength. Taste of big caramel, brown sugar, maple, and toasted brown bread; with light nuttiness, toffee, herbal, wood, dark fruit, and toasted earthiness. Light herbal bitterness on the finish; with lingering notes of caramel, brown sugar, maple, toasted brown bread, light nutty/toffee, and toasted earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Damn nice complexity, robustness, and balance of dark/bready malt, maple, and light earthy hop flavors; with a nice malt/bitterness balance, and zero cloying sweetness after the finish. Minimal dryness. Medium carbonation and body; with a very smooth, creamy/bready, and slightly syrupy mouthfeel that is great. Zero warming alcohol as expected of 6.4%. Overall this is a very nice amber ale. All around nice complexity, robustness, and balance of dark/bready malt, maple, and light earthy hop flavors; very smooth and easy to drink. Rich and complex on malt flavors, and never feels too sweet. A very enjoyable offering.
