Poured into a taster glass. Thanks, Mike.

Bottled 09/29/15. Tasted 21 Oct 2015.

Pours a slightly hazy creamy orange. Just a thin layer of white head, gone pretty quick, okay lacing.

Smell is good, but rather faint. I get some juicy fruits, some faint spice, and an acidic twang.

Taste is not so good. There's something off-putting about it, about the way the hops combine with something else in the beer. It's chalky, and bitter in an unpleasant, medicinal, aspirin tablet way. Not liking this.

Feel is fine. Medium body, prickle-tingly carbonation. But I want it gone faster!

This is the second Cowiche Canyon variant I've had, and I've liked both of them much less than the standard version. Wish I could find some more sixers of that stuff.

Poured into a Fremont pint glass. Pours a medium golden amber with a fine two finger white head with great retention and thick lacing. Aroma of citrus hops, overripe tropical fruit, grapefruit and lemon; hints of pine. Flavor is similar, with lemony citrus hops, grapefruit rind, tropical fruit, slight earthiness, lightly caramel malt. Lingering spicy citrus rind hop finish with a little pine resin. Medium bodied with good carbonation. A refreshing fresh hop ale with nicely complementary hops; not overly complex, but flavorful. Good lively herbal flavors from the hops that make fresh hop ales such a treat this time of year. Malt stays in the background, but supports the hops well. This has enough refreshing lemon that it reminds me of the the passing of summer; I won't be sipping this one on a hot day. Looks great and tastes great.

bottled 9/29/15
Pours a crisp clear amber with a modest white head that settles in nicely to it's soapy little colonies and window lacings.

Nose is a gorgeous bouquet of citrus and tropical fruits that I always associate with Mosaic (I'm not as familiar with Equinox) that explodes as soon as the cap pops. Neutral malts provide support but don't stand out on their own.

Flavour is bright and clear with a soft lead in of pastel tropical fruits and juicy citrus. Gives way mid palate to juicy bitter citrus which grows into a wave of pith that crashes into a long receding finish with that underlying veneer of sweet juicy fruits. Subdued but omnipresent carbonation helps build the experience.
This is another example of the artistry of fresh semi-local ingredients and talented visionary craftsmen (and women).
