i liked the other one better, the peach gose, but this is tasty too in its own right, quite apart from what we think we know about sour red beer. this has almost none of the vinegar sharp lacto we see in the flanders red types so often, instead subbing in a cool mix of bretty funk as well as berry flavors. i dont believe there is fruit in here, but it sure has some taste like it does, red currant and blackberry come to mind, sweet in the center but also tangy, going nicely with the funk. the grain is more flavorful than it is in a lot of these, nutty and deeply toasted, hints of toffee and caramel and butterscotch with the fruit, but there isnt much sugar left by the end. tart rather than sour for me overall, there isnt a ton of acid going on. it also lacks life to me, not as carbonated as it should be for the style, and that drags it down a little. dried red berries in the finish, mild brett, and a curious tang that is somewhat new to me. i think this one might develop nicely in the bottle over time, but for now it feels young and not quite all the way brought together. good not great, but given the breweries involved, its probably picking one up to cellar awhile and take the chance...

500 ml bottle into tulip glass, bottled on 3/1/2016. Pours hazy dark ruby red color with a 1-2 finger fairly dense light khaki head with good retention, that reduces to a thin spotty lace cap that lingers. Light spotty lacing clings around the glass. Aromas of lightly tart cherry, cranberry, raisin, plum, red grape, caramel, brown sugar, toasted biscuit, nuttiness, light cocoa, pepper, oak, hay, straw, light red wine vinegar/funk, and yeast/toasted earthiness. Nice aromas with good balance and complexity of fruity/funky/spicy yeast, oak barrel, and dark/bready malt notes; with good strength. Taste of lightly tart cherry, cranberry, raisin, plum, red grape, caramel, brown sugar, toasted biscuit, nuttiness, light cocoa, pepper, oak, hay, straw, light red wine vinegar/funk, and yeast/toasted earthiness. Light fruity/acidic tartness and yeast/oak spiciness on the finish; with lingering notes of cherry, cranberry, raisin, plum, red grape, caramel, brown sugar, toasted biscuit, nuttiness, light cocoa, pepper, oak, and yeast/toasted earthiness on the finish for a good bit. Very nice complexity, robustness, and balance of fruity/funky yeast, oak barrel, and dark/bready malt flavors; with a nice malt/tart/spiciness balance and zero cloying sweetness after the finish. Light-moderate dryness from acidity/spiciness as well. Medium carbonation and medium-full body; with a very smooth, moderately creamy/grainy, and lightly acidic/sticky/chalky balanced mouthfeel that is nice. Alcohol is very well hidden with minimal warming present after the finish. Overall this is a very good Flanders red/brown ale style. All around good robustness, complexity, and balance of fruity/funky/spicy yeast, oak barrel, and dark/bready malt flavors; and very smooth and easy to drink with the lighter acidity. A very enjoyable offering.

tbryan5, Mar 31, 2016
3.81/5  rDev +8.2%
look: 3.75 | smell: 4 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

Brewery versus brewery, the heads that butt actually come together with a beer of sour tendencies but with a maltier edge and a sweetened toasty backbone.

The Prairie vs TRVE matchup pours a turbulent rusty brownish red and carries a froth that's rife with sourness, pungeny and fruit. Its taste is gently sweet and toasty with candied pecan, caramel and grape-like sweetness.

With the middle looming, the beer's sweetness shows a meandering fade as the sourness and fruit begins to intensify. Cherry, currant and grape play a winey tone as the taste of wheat, peanut skins, and medium toast round out the bulk of flavor. Trailing bitterness is woodsy with weathered balsa, earthen with damp burlap and delicately oaken for a taste that's somewhat of red wine and that of vinegar.

Medium-bodied for a sour ale, the beer's rich nutty, toasty and sweetened heft offers a cola-like taste with a creamy-dry body that trails with cherry, date and grape in a medium-length aftertaste
