Pours a clear golden color with some small yeast floaties sneaking out of the bottle. And a one finger white head that does leave some light lace behind.

Aroma is lightly pungent, wet hay big ripe banana notes, lighter light citrus mostly lemon, tangerine, pear. Also some light peppery and earthy spice notes to round it out. Big Hefe likeness with a light tart farmhouse fruity funk.

Taste is bready malts with some of that horse blanket funk mixed in. Good fruity notes banana but not as strong as the nose, Citrus lemon, tangerine and pear mingled with some more tartness. Finish is nice peppery spices, some clove and little more funk.

Mouthfeel is good for the style lighter side of medium with fairly peppy carbonation.

Overall not a bad beer, definitely noticing some Hefe likeness in this one with touches of funk.

A: Pours a hazy to cloudy golden honey orange in color with moderate amounts of active visible carbonation along the edges of the glass and faint copper highlights. The beer has a two finger tall sudsy foamy off-white head that reduces to a large patch of thin film covering about 85% of the surface of the beer with a medium thick ring at the edges of the glass. Moderate amounts of lacing are observed.

S: Moderate aromas of wet straw and freshly cut grass over the top of light fruits and spicy Belgian yeast.

T: Upfront there is a moderate flavor of pale malts with a light amount of bready malts. That is followed by moderate flavors of wet straw/barnyard and hints of light fruit sweetness (apple/pear). Finally there is a moderate flavor of spicy Belgian yeast and light flavors of grassy hops. The hops impart a light amounts of bitterness which fades away pretty quickly.

M: Medium bodied with moderate amounts of carbonation.

O: A decent offering from a new brewery (for me). A decent and approachable farmhouse ale which is pretty easy to drink. Would consider getting this one again and trying others from this brewery.

Freehouse Brewery "Ashley Farmhouse Ale"
750 ml brown glass bottle, no apparent freshness dating
$8.99 @ Harris Teeter #277, Charleston, SC

Appearance: Surprisingly crystal clear deep gold-light copper body beneath an inch thick head of white foam. The head retention and lacing are good but far from great.

Aroma: Malty with an edge of wet straw, cut grass, and some yeasty "yeast" and fruitiness... candy sugar, and perhaps a touch of anise... a hint of minerals... slightly vegetal as well, like cut carrots, dried beans soaking, and the inside of a green bell pepper... earthy, spicy, peppery, and floral. I also get a bit of the 'wood floor in a gymnasium' scent which probably suggests wood and lemon.

Flavor: As the aroma dictates, the flavor follows. The vegetal note is distracting. It's perhaps a bit more malty than it could be. I love the mineral character from the yeast, and it's fruitiness as well, but there's not enough to fully shroud the beer and it ends up allowing the malty/vegetal components found in the nose to enter. Perhaps those come from spicing? And perhaps not... ? Some light clove lends occasional respite along with a bit of sugary sweet malt... bell pepper bubblegum anyone?

Mouthfeel: Medium bodied and gently effervescent. I'd have expected more bite.

Overall: I truly believe that this could be a great beer, but without any dating information on the bottle I have no idea of how old it is, and that disappoints me. I feel let down by the brewery as even a simple ink stamp on the label with the date it was bottled would help. On the other hand,maybe I'm a little bit to blame as perhaps I should have let it stand chilled for a longer time to allow the yeast to sediment more completely.

Davepoolesque, Jan 20, 2015
4.1/5  rDev +4.1%

Crawling off the banks of Charleston's Ashley River, a connection with the farmhouse of Belgium seems to resonate harmoniously with the brewers at Freehouse as a much more authentic saison flavor than is usual for American brewers is both realized and welcomed!

Its hazy honey hues of amber and gold permeate through the yeasty cloud. On top of it all, spice and earth play out with a peppery, grassy and fruity character above a bready-sweet and yeasty taste.

As the beer opens up, more honey, doughy and confectioner's tastes come to life. While the fruitiness of orange, apple and pear build, so does the spices that calder from below- white pepper, cinnamon and ginger all playing significant roles will quickly balance the fruity-sweet middle in the absence of hop flavor.

Its lightly acidic finish is slightly wine-like and favors a peppery balance. Its medium body lightens considerably as the finish looms, allowing the fruit to dissolve and promoting the prickle of spice and warmth of alcohol to dry the palate with ease. Only the spicy and grassy hop bitterness persist to accompany its distinctive acidity.
