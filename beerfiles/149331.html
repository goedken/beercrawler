Expectedly dark brew with a purpleish highlights, not unlike cola. Decent sized dark head, with poor fizzy retention.

You can smell the liquor as soon as you pour the brew into the glass. Lovely and strong, it hints strongly of Brandy, oak, and chocolate.

Upfront, the chocolate is the most significant flavor. The booze from the brandy his present throughout, but is far most dominant in the back half. Coffee, toffee, and syrupy Caramel are also present in spades. The brandy it really takes over in the end, carrying the brew to a lovely, slightly hot finish.

Exceptionally smooth for the sky high ABV. You can tell it is boozy, but it is never remotely unpleasantly so.

Like drinking Christmas candy, this is a delightful, complex, and delicious brew. Unique.

SerialTicker, Oct 02, 2016
4.21/5  rDev -5.4%
look: 4 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4 | overall: 4.25

I picked up a bottle of Prairie's Apple Brandy Noir at The Lager Mill. I've been wanting to try this one since I first heard of it and I'm very interested to see how it turns out, so lets see how it goes. 2016 Vintage. Poured from a brown 12oz bottle into a snifter.

A- The label looks good, it has a nice design and it's eye catching. It poured a nice black color that didn't let any light come through and it had a thin rings worth of bubbly tan head that quickly faded away and it left a little bit of spotty lacing behind. This is a good beer, but I wish had better head and retention.

S- The aroma starts off with a lower amount of high sweetness with the apple brandy being the firs to show up, it has a distinct brandy aroma with a noticeable apple cider like aroma to it and it goes right into the malts which impart some dark malt, milk chocolate cocoa, roasted malt, brown sugar and light bready aromas. Up next comes a dark fruit aromas followed by a noticeable alcohol aroma and a little more sweetness. This beer has a very nice aroma, it lives up to it's name.

T- The taste seems to be similar to the aroma and it starts off with a lower amount of high sweetness with the apple brandy still being the first to show up and it imparts the same aspects that it did in the aroma with that same noticeable brandy, apple and apple cider like aspects that show up just a little more than they did in the aroma. Up next comes the malts which imparts lots of sweet malts, roasted malts, dark malt, chocolate, cocoa and this time it imparts some very nice caramel flavors. Up next comes a noticeable alcohol flavor that's a little warming and towards the end it goes into a little of dark fruit flavor with more caramel coming through. It pretty much has no bitterness with some sweetness and booze taking it's place and on the finish there's some chocolate, apple brandy and caramel showing up in the aftertaste. This is a very nice tasting beer, the combination of flavors grew on me and enjoyed it.

M- A little smooth, a little velvety, sticky, a little boozy, a little cloying, on the thicker side of being medium bodied with a lower amount of medium carbonation. The mouthfeel is good and it works well with this beer.

Overall I thought this was a very nice beer and a very nice example of the style, it had all the aspects that I was looking for, plus a couple that I wasn't expecting and when it comes down to it the combination of flavors and aromas made for a pretty unique beer and memorable beer. This beer had ok drinkability, one is enough for me, anymore than that and it might start to wear on more. What I like the most about this beer is how unique it is, the apple brandy seems to take the this beer to the next level. What I like the least about this beer beer is how sweet it is and i think the booze could have been hidden a little better, bu t know that comes with the territory. I would buy this beer again, if I see it and I would recommend it to anyone looking for a big, complex and unique beer. All in all I was happy with this beer and a little impressed with it, Prairie shows they have some major skills when it comes to beers like this beer. It's one of the better beers I've had from them and it's a very nice example of the style. Great job guys, this is one tasty, complex and unique beer, keep up the hard work.

Dactrius, Aug 07, 2016
4.84/5  rDev +8.8%
look: 4 | smell: 5 | taste: 5 | feel: 4.5 | overall: 4.75

Drank from a 12 fl. oz. bottle
Served in a snifter
Big thanks to my friend Bill for sharing this with me.

The thick, oily, near pitch black body was capped by a small, frothy, mocha colored crown that quickly fell to a tiny, bubbly ringlet. No lacing unfortunately.

The aroma was amazingly rich and decadent. Brown sugar, raisins and plums came through big time. Intense barrel presence. Not sure I would necessarily peg this as apple brandy barrel aged per se, but I definitely picked up on the sweet, boozy fruitiness. Nice and punchy without being hot. Loads of dark chocolate. Super fudgey. Hint of maple syrup too.

Like the nose, the flavor profile file was outstanding. Super rich, sweet and complex. Intense malty notes on the front end. Loads of brown sugar. Big chocolate presence too. Rich and somewhat fudgey. The apple brandy barrel notes came through strong on the back end. Similar to the nose in that this was quite boozy without being hot. The beer finished with a nice mix of roasted malts, brandy barrel and some lingering sweetness.

The mouthfeel was rich, lush and oily. Fuller bodied for the style. The liquid contained a super fine, supple, tongue blanketing effervescence.

Prairie makes a number of terrific beers, but this was definitely their most impressive offering I have tried to date. With such a rich and decadent base, it is no surprise that the apple brandy barrels upped the complexity to another level of awesomeness.

Pours pitch black with a thin light khaki head that dissipated to thin patches and slight lacing. Aroma of sweet, dark toasted malt, molasses, toffee and brandy, very inviting. I was still uncertain if I would like it as a barreling treatment, but no need to worry. While an average stout with just a little apple brandy becomes much more drinkable, a great stout like this one in brandy barrels is just plain delicious. Great chocolatey malt flavors and a very well proportioned brandy component. This is superb. Kudos.
