On tap at Taco Mac Duluth. BOOYAH!! 100th review of this beer! Reviewed from notes.

(Appearance) appears a deep golden amber tone, very cloudy in appearance, with a thick froth head, solid white color. Good retention. Looks pretty solid for a pale and a really good presentation. 4.5

(Smell) Pale malts and a mild crisp hop aroma, mild but balanced. Not much else beyond the predictable but decent for what's there. Higher potency and it would be gold. 3.5

(Taste) Malt with a medium bitter brace on the end. It's largely going for a bitter hop approach, and does pretty well on those grounds. However, there's not much substance that comes through with the bitterness, and the beer lacks a lot on flavor with hardly any middle ground. Not the most watery thing I've had but could improve on body a bit! 3

(Mouthfeel) finds a middle ground between smooth and crisp, there's a bit of perkiness from the carbonation. Texture wise it's very slick, pretty good for emphasizing the difference between the IPA and pale ale aspect. Body is very mild. Alcohol presence is low as well. 3.5

(Overall) not a bad pale ale from Red Brick and certainly fits in with the category, although the emphasis on hop bitterness takes over much of the brew without a body to balance it with. It's going away soon so get it when you can because its worth a shot for the price paid. 3.5

My second favorite Georgia brew, though the quality can vary, depending on issues at the brewery I suppose. For example, I occasionally get bottles I have to dump, and sometimes the draught tastes oxidized, cardboard-y. But I am rating it high based on when it is good, it is a very acceptable pale ale, maybe I would call it an IPA. A little too high in gravity to be a session beer, I wake up with a headache when I do that! The hopiness is high in both aroma and flavour, and it gives it an addictiveness.
Malt is balanced and flavorful, its not too sweet. Hops are citrus-y.
Note to everyone! This is what an unfiltered pale ale should look like! It is almost clear, its not full of yeast and bits, just has a slight haze. Your taste is ruined when too much yeast gets through! I can't believe the haziness in some unfiltered pale ales, its just about brewing skill!

Part of my "box of bricks" sampler. I took this down to the beach hoping for a nice dose of hops to make the humidity bearable.

The beer was terribly overcarbonated, a 25/75 split beer to head. After 10-15 min. waiting for the head to release there was nice lacing, but I'm not sure it was worth the wait.

The color was typical of a pale ale, mostly sunny with some haze, and the hops were very fragrant with a lemongrass note. This was the highlight for sure. If hops are your thing this is definitely a pale ale for you.

I would like to have this via draught because the carbonation made the mouthfeel soapy and the drinkability very poor.

Taste: Lots of hops to start off though it is a bit dry and tea-like which puts me off a little but it is offset but a grassy character which i like. Not as much fruit as in the aroma to start out with but as it warms there's a lot of grapefruit that comes through.

Mouthfeel: Very full-bodied and robust for a pale ale yet it manages to not be too heavy. Pretty good.

Drinkability: Putting aside the initial dryness, this is a very good pale ale that i would buy again.

Overall: A solid pale ale. I seem to be rating higher than previous raters but i really dug it and i think the key here is freshness as the girl at the store said they had just gotten this in. Seek it out for sure.

A - Opens with a vigorous burst of carbonation and pours with a huge head of white foam on top of a clear, copper colored body. The head settles slowly and leaves thick lace, while streams of bubbles rise through the body indicating high carbonation.

S - An aroma of sweet, grainy malt with a hint of red apple yeast. Little in the way of hops.

T - Light, bready malt up front that is quickly washed away by harsh bitterness and husky, tannic grain. Finishes with a little bit of pine hop flavor and more harsh bitterness that lingers in the aftertaste with an asprin-like astringency.

M - Medium body, very high carbonation, and a dry, extremely astringent finish.

D - Practically non existent. The aroma is bland but inoffensive, while the flavor is harsh and almost entirely unpleasant. There is a hint of malt and a brief moment of enjoyable hop flavor, but the bitterness is soapy and there is a profound, lingering astringency. Even the carbonation is excessive to the point of hurting the overall perception of the beer as it emphasizes the bitterness and astringency. This was a drain pour.

12oz. bottle received as an extra in a recent trade with GatorBeerNerd.

I was really pleasantly surprised by this beer. It was much hoppier than I expected and very well balanced. Recommended.

12 oz bottle into a Duvel tulip.

A: First off, this one gushed all over the place as soon as I cracked it. Then, even a very careful pour almost overfilled the oversized tulip, resulting in over 3" of head - way overcarbonated. That said, the head was dense, creamy, and extremely persistent - all very nice characteristics.

S: Not surprisingly, it smells slightly infected, with a touch of lactic tartness preceding the citrus hops and caramel malt. Could be much worse.

M: A little on the light side of medium bodied, and hugely carbonated. Definitely over the top with the bubbles, but not horrendously so.

D: The crisp bitter finish helps a lot, but the slight bit of funky sourness really doesn't help matters. For what it is, though, it's not a tough beer to drink.

Notes: Considering the infection issues, this really wasn't a bad beer in any way. I'll be on the lookout for a fresh bottle (I bought this one about 4 months ago, and just didn't get around to drinking it until now) the next time I'm down south, and will re-review as necessary. This one definitely has potential, and I'd like to see what its hop bill is like without the impediments of age and infection.
