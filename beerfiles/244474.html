NickSMpls, Aug 28, 2016
3.65/5  rDev -6.9%
look: 3 | smell: 3 | taste: 4 | feel: 4 | overall: 3.75

Pours a clear golden in color -near excellent clarity- topped with a barely off-white head.

Nose is light and lively -- a touch of malt, some floral hop, but fairly neutral and toned down altogether. Hop and malt notes are a blend of sweet grass and lightly herbal hp, along with ripe, golden pear.

Richly aromatic on the first sip, with an underpinning effervescent quality. Bright notes of melon (some wheat malt?) and honey, along with a slightly sharp and bitter hop, which quickly subsides before lingering politely.

Reasonably well carbonated --not overcarbonated-- the body and carbonation remind me of the "Sparkling Ale" (Australian style?) which Summit brewed many, many moons ago. Good balance overall, with the lightly sweet malt, more judicious biscuit malt, and not-quite assertive hops playing off one another.

A lighter bodied ale, this one is exceptionally drinkable.

Overall, this is a very good beer, and a really nice "session" style ale.

As to the style -- is it a pale ale, a session ale, or some Ur-classic from which the "American Pale Ale" style was derived? To be honest I was not familiar with the style as it was described by Summit in advance of the release, but feel comfortable with the way that they've approached this beer in hindsight. We'd probably call it a "Session Pale Ale" in modern American beergeek parlance, and that's okay.

It's a nice, easy-drinking ale with enough character to delight the palate of more experienced ale drinkers while also inviting our more casual drinker friends to partake without making them suffer through another hope bomb (Amber or IPA), Barrel-Aged boozer, or aggressive Sour.

And isn't bringing a variety of drinkers together in a common experience that what this is all about?
