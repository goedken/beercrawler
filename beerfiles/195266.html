dbl_delta, Sep 10, 2016
2.92/5  rDev -24.2%
look: 3 | smell: 2.75 | taste: 3 | feel: 2.75 | overall: 3

6 pack ran me $14 in Brooklyn - sweet Jesus, I usually get Sculpin for that!

12 fl oz brown glass bottle with a white branded pry-off crown cap and a generic label design.

Pale ale hopped with Molsaic and Cascade. Brewed with caramel malt. 45 IBUs. 5.20% ABV.

HEAD: Pours ~5cm of foam in my nonic pint glass. White colour. Decent thickness and froth. Retention is good - ~8 minutes. Leaves no lacing as it recedes.

BODY: Dull clear copper colour. Clean, lacking any visible yeast or sediment.

It's a decent look for a pale ale. No characteristics stand out as unique or special.

AROMA: Muted citrus and floral notes are all I get hopwise; I hoped for the subtle blueberry notes Mosaic is known for. Pale malts keep it balanced, but it's already shaping up to be a reticent brew if the taste matches the aroma. Some biscuit malt.

Aromatic intensity is below average; this is quite restrained for a pale ale.

No overt yeast character or booze.

TASTE: Fruity and floral, with a hint of lemongrass and a nod towards blueberry when I look for it (but no vivid evocative notes thereof). Hints of spiciness. Estery ale yeast. Pale malts keep it balanced, and luckily any caramel malt character is restrained; it isn't too sweet by any means...but more hop flavour would be very welcome.

It's a simply built pale ale with a pleasant flavour, but the hop profile won't impress discerning drinkers. Limited depth of flavour. Average flavour duration. Below average flavour intensity.

TEXTURE: Slightly oily. Overcarbonated. Unrefreshing, in spite of being smooth and wet. Medium-bodied.

A lighter more refreshing feel would really help this ale, as would a softer texture.

Lacks a harmony of texture and taste, but this mouthfeel gets the job done I suppose.

OVERALL: I hoped for the best given Tröegs is behind this, but it isn't stellar stuff. I definitely wish I'd reached for the Perpetual IPA offered at the same price. This is competent but underwhelming fare, and I've had hundreds of pale ales with more flavour and hoppy punch than this one. I wouldn't recommend it to others, but it's decent for what it is. I'd like to see them improve the mouthfeel, amp up the Mosaic, and maybe play around with some Citra. Cascade is a classic pale ale hop (going all the way back to New Albion's groundbreaking brew), but sometimes it feels tired.
