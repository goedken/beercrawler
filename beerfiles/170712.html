The aroma has a strong amount of cherry - but also a lot of buttery diacetyl. Orange peel and subtle oak also show up, but hey, so does the diacetyl. It tastes like a liquefied Werther's Original laced with cherry juice and orange peel at first, but the finish redeems things (slightly) with a nice herbaceous kick. It's pretty simple, and that's disappointing for 10% - still, I find it fairly likable. It has a medium body, yet there's a tongue-tingling coarseness to the texture that allows it to go down easily. Yeah, it's a bit boozy, but who cares? It's dangerous, and I like that in a big-ass beer. For what it is, it isn't bad at all - I'd drink it again, but not buy it again.

3.07/5: I think I'm sloooooowly coming around to these Alltech BA beers...

Treyliff, Jan 15, 2016
4.05/5  rDev +7.7%
look: 3.75 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4

A: Pours a clear amber with a fizzy whitish head that dissipates quickly.

S: Aromas of ripe cherries, bitter orange peel, caramel, vanilla bean, toasted coconut, charred oak, honey, and woody spice subtly express themselves on the nose.

T: Complex notes of caramel, honey, toasted coconut, charred oak, honeysuckle, woody spice, Angostura bitters, maraschino cherries, vanilla bean, and orange peel fill the mouth. The finish is delicate, dry, and warming, offering a pleasant mixture of caramel, vanilla, cherry, orange peel, honey, and bitters underscored by mellow alcohol.

M: Full-bodied and somewhat dry, though an impression of stickiness is left after the finish. Carbonation is surprisingly active and firm. Alcohol is apparent, but very restrained and well-integrated. Though the strength is readily apparent, this ale is quite easy to drink.

O: This is a very interesting strong ale. It is also the kind of beer that tends to grow on you over time-at least that was the case for me. When I first tried it, I really did not like it, but after giving it a second chance, I can really appreciate what the brewers were going for and think they more or less hit the nail on the head. If you are a bourbon and bitters fan, then I would say that there is a good chance you will like this ale. If you also happen to be a fan of quirky flavor combinations, then you will probably like this ale as well. All in all, I recommend it rather highly to fans of barrel-aged ales.

KYGunner, May 23, 2015
3.84/5  rDev +2.1%
look: 4 | smell: 3.75 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 4 | overall: 4

12oz bottle poured into a small snifter.

A: Red-orange color with great clarity. Fine-bubbled off-white head settles after a good while. Little to no lacing.

S: Corny sweet bourbon up front. Sweet candy apple shell malts. Woody barrel aromas dance with scents of oaky vanilla and caramel. Plump, sweet cherries and flashes of orange peel ride to the forefront as the bourbon and barrel flavors become more of a backbone. Just a hint of tobacco and herbs in the background.

T: Corny sweet bourbon and caramel/candy apple shell malt sweetness open things. Cherries are bold and have a decidedly tarter edge than they did on the nose. Flashes of orange peel. Barrel flavors ride up with drying oak, vanilla, and a spike of bourbon. They help to curb the initial sweetness. Hints of earthy, herbal, and tobacco-like flavors linger in the background, perhaps coming from hops or bitters or both. These flavors fade into the drying finish with bourbon and oak.

M: Medium bodied with a very round and sturdy feel. A bit coating. Soothing warmth all the way through.

O: I absolutely love this concept. Creative as hell, and the cocktail influence really shines through. The corny bourbon and malty sweetness rides very high initially, but thankfully the wood tannins and herbal/bitter flavors are able to reign it in and bring it into balance. Still a much sweeter and less potent experience than drinking an old fashioned cocktail, but remember that this is a beer after all.
