Style: American Double / Imperial IPA

Notes / Commercial Description:
Simcoe is the fourth release in our double IPA series that showcases our favorite hop varieties, one by one, with each consecutive release. This hop was a game changer when it was released 14 years ago because of its bright layers of overripe pineapple, guava, and sage. Look forward to many upcoming releases within this series of single hopped double IPA's.

1. That's a nice, simple name for a beer. The "bobby" or "jane" of beer names. That's a lost art, what with the recent onslaught of "Spaytlins" and "Cressicars."

2. Nice understated bottle art, too. H-have I become a 60-year-old in the last few weeks?

Pours a bright, very vibrant orange with a soapy and retentive white head. Smells herbal but not very cannabis-y, with some sweet honey malt and a little bit of pineapple. Not quite the tropical fruit bomb I was expecting from a beer with "simcoe" in its name, but still interesting and well balanced.

Tastes like it smells, albeit a bit more hop-heavy. Thick simcoe nodes dominate throughout, and while they are nominally well balanced/accentuated by sweet malt, the hop profile itself could use some balancing. I guess a straight-simcoe IPA isn't completely by cup of tea, but I can appreciate the craftsmanship here. The fact that it was first released 14 years ago, back when proto-beersmen were locked in their basements drinking homebrewed shoe leather-tasting ESB's, this is quite an impressive beer.

The newest beer in the Noble Showers series, Simcoe Showers presents itself as a clear corn/gold brew with a very small half finger head of white following a smooth pour. The retention while average coats the entire glass in a dense sheet of attractive lacing.

Solid orange citrus and pine gets a boost from some dank tropical mango, ripened banana and earthy herbs laying down a complex batch of hops. The malt is bready providing just enough sweetness to be unobtrusive to the nature of the hops. In this beer they are slightly acidic and stale with a tortilla chip and slight alcohol finishing aroma.

Bready malt is a bit stale and grainy but provides adequate sweetness to balance the Simcoe which proves itself to be a bittering and aroma hop style. Flavor is limited to orange peel with a steely pine and bright herbaceous flavor that ushers along the bitterness and finishing flavors. With a soapy and drying finish comes copious bitterness and a floral aftertaste laced with herbs. Alcohol has a middling presence.

Medium body and adequate carbonation. As said earlier, finish is soapy, clean and drying with sensible alcohol.

Limited to a single hop variety this version of 'Showers' flaunts Simcoe's abundance of aromas and clean finishing bitterness and drying while attempting to play off the lack of flavor with a early entering, clean finish. Noble really has the single hop DIPA down to a science but at this point, I wish Noble would shake things up with another multi-hop beer like Tongue Tickles (or a high hype hop like a Mosaic or Galaxy Showers) before continuing on their single hop tangent.
