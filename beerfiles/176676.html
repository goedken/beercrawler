L: Had this beer on-tap at House of Brews, poured into a tulip glass. Had a golden-orange color and a hazy consistency. There was an inch of foamy head that lasted an average amount of time. Fair lacing.

S: A very nice aroma of coffee, hops, and some light fruit as well.

T: Tasted of a little bit of hops, a tinge of pale malt, a good amount of coffee, citrus fruit (grapefruit), other light fruit, a creamy quality, and a sweet sugary element. Not quite as aggressive a taste as some coffee-flavored beers, but still pretty interesting. Well-balanced.

F: A good amount of carbonation with a smooth finish. Light- to medium-bodied.

O: This isn't a perfect beer, but it's certainly an unusual take on a tired style. Worth a look.

12 oz. bottle into a Pilsener glass.

Looks like a pretty standard Pale Ale, clear gold/amber, lots of fine carbonation, slight head is a slightly dirty white, surely from the coffee.

Smells like sticking your nose into a coffee grinder; light/medium roast, freshly grinded, maybe a touch of biscuity malts, but maybe not. Fresh cold brew dominates. No detectable hops.

Taste follows the nose; some malt backbone, but fresh, roasty cold brewed coffee is front and center. A touch of hop bitterness.

Feels like a standard Pale Ale; medium-light body, crisp carbonation, pretty clean & dry finish. No real sweetness, & what little bitterness there is fades before the roasty coffee flavors do.

Lighter coffee beers are less common than coffee stouts or porters, but the few that I've tried, I've generally enjoyed quite a bit, probably because I love coffee about as much as I do beer. The Pale Ale base exposes the coffee character much more prominently than a big dark style would, making this beer unquestionably all about the coffee. I wonder if the cold brew in here is the same that was used in the Sumatra Mountain Brown, which is one of my favorites in recent memory. You've got to at least like coffee to appreciate this one, but if you're a coffee junkie like me, this is a great way to get your fix at night!

Pours a hazy golden color with a thick pillowy white head. Head slowly dissipates leaving tons of white sticky lacing and a residual one finger head. Nice looking APA.

Smell is coffee. Roasted coffee. Its a lovely coffee aroma with hints of vanilla and coffee beans. As the bouquet develops you get more of the hoppy aroma with a touch of citrus, and a floral note. Its certainly a pungent coffee aroma as you'd expect from a Founders coffee beer, but the hoppy aroma mixed in there is different.

Taste has the coffee upfront, with some strong coffee and an almost vanilla note coming through. Slight hop character on the backend. The beer takes on more of a coffee infused creme soda like flavor all together.

Feel is perfect for this beer - nice carbonation, smooth creamy mouthfeel.

Overall, I'll be honest here, I don't like coffee infused pale ales. I've had a few and hated each one. I picked a single of this one up because its Founders and I was curious. And I was blown away. I still am not a fan of the style, but this beer really showed that you can do a coffee infused APA and get it to work. The coffee in this is incredible (I'd expect nothing less from Founders). The beer takes on a creamy taste and feel that plays nice with the coffee. Not one I'd pick up again because Im just not a fan of the style overall, but a tip of the cap to Founders for pulling off the best example I've had.

98green, Oct 08, 2016
3.87/5  rDev -2.5%
look: 3.75 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 3.75 | feel: 3.75 | overall: 3.75

Been a few months since I've had a coffee-infused pale ale... I think the last one that I enjoyed enough to write about was Sixpoint's C.R.E.A.M. actually. This is a sub-style/trend that I am ever-dubious about but I also try to keep an open mind about it in general, so without further ado...

Pours a very pale (4-6 SRM) color with a compact head that seems to leave very little in the way of lace. Though the foam that's there retains somewhat decently, the medium-translucent body doesn't feel far enough in either direction for me. With this kind of beer, I really would like either full opacity or very clean and clear... this seems to occupy a middle point that doesn't satisfy me too much, to be honest.

Nose is wondrously coffee-forward, with a nutty complexion and a whiff of ripe stone fruit among some slight notes of caramel and citrus peel. Earthy and bold, this is what I expect from Founders' coffee beers considering their fantastic Sumatra Mountain Brown ranks as easily one of my top 5 coffee infused beers of all time. Love the complex and balanced profile here. Slight, crispy acidity from the coffee and a svelte malt profile rule the day in the nose. Great stuff.

Taste is a bit less impressive, carrying a slight cardboard flavor that feels a bit like the coffee fell off quicker on the palate than it did olfactorily. Still has some semi-bold citrus with a slight hoppy/bitter twang that reminds me of Cascade and Centennial hops. The malt base is pretty nutty and basic feeling, but that cardboard-ish coffee flavor is a bit too forward for me and the acidity doesn't help the feel here either. This is a bit too light/thin in body for its own good, in my opinion. Not my favorite example of a lighter-colored coffee ale. Glad I only got a single.
