grimmales.com

Style: American IPA

Alcohol by volume (ABV): 6.40%

Availability: Rotating

Notes / Commercial Description:
Magnetic Compass prominently features NZ Rakau hops, bringing a unique mix of candied orchard fruit, stone fruit, and tropical aromas with some light dank elements. Background notes of Citra, Mosaic, and Columbus. Very hazy and juicy. Roll your can to suspend hop particles and enjoy.

TheBlueJerzzzz, Aug 27, 2016
4.55/5  rDev 0%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.25 | taste: 4.75 | feel: 4.75 | overall: 4.5

Picked this up a while ago in New York, as this beer had a date of 6/7 on the bottom. Even without rolling, there weren't a lot of particles suspended in this and even more amazingly, this was the best beer from Grimm Ales that I had so far as this went down so smoothly and had everything in near-perfect proportion, down to the last sip!

Great pour to this as the beer had a dank, golden hue to it with *no* large particles suspended in the liquid, a frothy head, and wonderful rings of lacing left on my pint glass as I worked my way through this. Not a powerfully strong aroma but there was lots of stone and tropical fruit in the nose along with the array of hops underneath that. The taste was unreal - the person below me said that this was fruit salad in the nose but that's what I felt in the taste - kiwi, kumquat, mango, papaya, grape, tangerine, melon, honeydew, and lots of lively yeast. The malt was the trademark Grimm bread with hints of spice and yeast in it, but not enough to overpower the juice that was here from start to finish. The light carbonation and smooth finish ensured that the flavors would shine through as the slick and dank pine mouthfeel was just enough to complement everything else going on here.

The alcohol was light enough here to be felt at the end but not to be detected in the taste, as should be the case with most IPA's. So smooth, juicy, and beautiful to behold - this one's a winner, even by the lofty standards of Grimm. A must-try, for sure!

Murky deep gold leaning towards a bit of copper, totally translucent.

Really potent aromas of peaches and other stone fruit as well as more tropical notes that lean towards mango. Citra hopes quite noticeable.

Taste is just lightly bitter, and even the bitterness comes off as smooth. Alcohol is noticeable but not off-puuting. Plenty of stone fruit here as well with a bit of tropical sweetness on the mid-palate and finish.

Mouthfeel is medium-full bodied. Fruit gives an impression of sweetness while remaining dry. Impression of creaminess.

This is an amazingly well balanced beers and a nearly perfect example of an IPA (if you're willing to accept haziness). Mouthfilling and fruity but still crisp.

L: Pours an incredibly murky dark hay with some orange and golden hues. Good sized head with very nice foam retention.

S: Super bright and tropical! Apricots, passionfruit, guava, some cumquats. A good deal of malt which really propels the sweet fruit notes.

T: Lots of Southern Hemisphere hop notes of apricot, melons, orange juice and sweet mango. On the malty side of the IPA spectrum, with plenty of bisquity/bready malt balancing the hoppiness and encouraging the perception of fruit flavors. Medium-length finish with sweet mango and a decent amount of herbal bitterness at the backend.

F: Medium body with soft yet adequate carbonation. Very pillowy mouthfeel with a nice moderately silky texture. The liquid has a way of staying suspended on the tongue; its like sipping clouds and

Thoughts: Really superb perhaps my favorite Grimm IPA to date. At times the sweetness came across as a bit much, but it really complimented and propelled the fruity hop notes. Awesome aroma and the high percentage of oats in the mash really gave this beer an idiosyncratic and delightful mouthfeel.
