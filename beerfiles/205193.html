gibgink, Jun 28, 2016
4.36/5  rDev +11.2%
look: 4.25 | smell: 4.5 | taste: 4.25 | feel: 4.25 | overall: 4.5

With the success of other breweries' use of bourbon barrels, imperial stout and maple, the allure proves to great for the Grimm brewers to ignore. Their excellence with their original Double Negative comes with tremendous acclaim, so let's see what maple does within this sweet and savory blend

Lusciously full at the pour, the beer is less viscous or thick than you'd expect- favoring instead a creamy but robust appearance. Its mid-statured froth swirls on top like coffee creme. Bold dessert-like scents start the show with chocolate, caramel, vanilla, coffee and campfire. Its start-up taste is wonderfully burnt with sugars and cream offering a near creme brule savoriness to the nose.

Alive and awake, the middle palate springs to life with the molasses, maple, butter pecan and deeply caramelized flavors, following with espresso bean, roasting walnut, bittersweet chocolate and heavy whipping cream for a closing taste that's most desert-like yet surprisingly mellow. A linger of root beer spice and a bright spruce taste brings the beer home.

Trending malty-dry but full, the beer's robust taste soon gives way to a more savory and succulent flavors. Peppery and dry, the final impressions are of raisin, char along with a long, warm kaluha-like aftertaste

SomethingClever, May 27, 2016
1.79/5  rDev -54.3%
look: 3 | smell: 1.5 | taste: 1.5 | feel: 2.5 | overall: 2

Got this one from desp59. Cheers man! I’m guessing this was a different batch than the base and bourbon versions I tried earlier.

From a bomber into a snifter
Bottled February 2016

First of all, this beer is definitely not infected. It’s terrible, but just because a beer is terrible doesn’t mean it’s infected. No signs of infection whatsoever.

APPEARANCE: Not a foamer, luckily. Lighter in color than the other batch. This one pours out clear dark brown and yields a two finger, creamy light beige head with good retention. Bistre brown body with no real carbonation evident. Head fades to a full cap and clings to the sides of the glass. A splotchy wisp remains leaving lots of good lacing down the glass. Nice head but the body is definitely too light for the style.

SMELL: Milder on the nose with some woody aromas, oak and cheap moonshine. A bit of vanilla and toffee perhaps but this is basically cheap moonshine and wood. No maple character either. Definitely below par.

TASTE: Woody and boozy up front. More alcohol at the swallow with cheap moonshine and a dose of syrup as well. Boozy and woody finish with notes of more cheap alcohol, oak, some vanilla and some maple syrup notes, but this isn’t really put together very well. Lots of cheap booze and woody tannins linger.

PALATE: Medium body and medium levels of carbonation. A little lighter on the palate, semi-creamy, goes down a little prickly with a scratch and finishes mouth-coating. A bit of a burn and some heat lingers.

OVERALL: Definitely the worst of the three Double Negative variants. There’s nothing infected about this beer. It’s just bad. This batch is thinner and lighter in color while the barrel aging is just plain lousy. It comes across as cheap and boozy, like someone dumped moonshine in the batch by mistake. The woody tannins are pretty hard to take as well, while the maple syrup addition was either too little or the syrup itself was boring. Not something I’d want to have again. These Double Negatives are mediocre at best but this particular installment is to be avoided at all costs. In any case, thanks again for the beers desp59!
